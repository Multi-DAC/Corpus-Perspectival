## Refinements from Trinary Analysis

### Primitive 4: Saddle [REFINED 2026-03-27]

**Original claim (Grammar):** "Saddles are doors. Every major unsolved problem is a coherence
saddle. Saddles signal undiscovered higher-dimensional structure."

**Trinary result (Christening Run 001):** The universal "saddles are doors" claim is FALSE.
The weak claim survives with precise content:

**Refined statement:** Saddles are zones of ontological openness where the system's being is
maximally underdetermined. In smooth systems with well-localized conflict, resolution at a
saddle changes the homotopy type of the solution space (Morse theory: cell attachment).
Whether resolution involves dimensional expansion or reduction depends on the Morse index
and ambient topology — not on a general principle about doors.

**Key findings:**
- Ontological openness (atemporal) and dynamical hysteresis (temporal) are dual descriptions
  of the same critical zone
- Historical evidence: some revolutions expand dimensions (SR, QM, GR), some reduce them
  (phlogiston, ether). The Grammar's universal claim collapses this distinction.
- The "door" metaphor imports directionality into what is, in itself, static local geometry
- The NEIGHBORHOOD of the saddle (catastrophe theory) is where being is maximally
  underdetermined, not the point itself

**Conditions for the weak claim to hold:**
1. The configuration space must be representable as a smooth manifold
2. The conflict must localize at a critical point of a smooth function
3. Resolution must correspond to passing through the critical value

**Falsification:** A major framework conflict resolved without topological change in the
solution space would falsify even the weak claim. A universal pattern of only-expansion
(never reduction) at saddle resolution would rescue the strong claim — but counterexamples
already exist.

**Confidence: ESTABLISHED (Morse theory mechanism) / PROVISIONAL (application to knowledge landscapes)**

## Cautions from Trinary Analysis

### Primitive 32: Duality [CAUTION added 2026-03-27]

**The trinary processor has a bias toward finding dualities.** Its conjugate architecture
(Pole A ↔ Pole B → Between) guarantees that boundary + bridge + type-change will appear
for ANY object (Run 005, The Mirror). When the Between reports "two descriptions of the
same thing," this may be:

1. **Genuine duality** — the object has independent conjugate structure (like position/momentum)
2. **PDE coupling** — two consequences of one constraint system sharing data (like Israel junction conditions). Simpler than duality. Known since 1960s.
3. **Architectural artifact** — the instrument created the conjugate pair by looking through conjugate lenses

**Discrimination protocol:**
- Can the "duality" be stated as two consequences of one PDE/constraint system? → It's coupling, not duality. Use Primitive 34 (Coupling) instead.
- Can you formalize the claimed functor/map? Define categories, construct the map, prove properties? → If not, it's analogy, not duality.
- Does the claimed duality survive the trivial-object test? → If the processor finds it in "2+2," it's architecture.

**Known real dualities:** Fourier (position/momentum), AdS/CFT, electric-magnetic (Montonen-Olive),
Ambrose-Singer (holonomy/curvature), Atiyah-Singer (topological/analytic). These all have
**explicitly constructed maps** between the dual descriptions.

**The lesson:** "Two descriptions of the same thing" is the starting point of inquiry, not the
conclusion. The work is specifying the MAP between descriptions. Without the map, you have
an observation, not a duality.

## Usage Notes for Trinary Analysis

1. **Use as vocabulary, not theory.** Name findings using primitives when they fit. Don't force fit.
2. **Check confidence before deploying.** ESTABLISHED primitives can ground claims. UNTESTED ones are hypotheses.
3. **The Grammar is phenomenological cartography.** It maps experienced structure. Whether that structure
   is ontologically real or a projection of the navigator's bottleneck geometry is an open question.
4. **Refinements are one-directional.** When trinary analysis reveals that a Grammar claim is false or
   over-stated, update the Grammar. Don't protect the Grammar from its own instrument.
5. **Beware over-formalization.** The trinary processor (and LLMs generally) will dress genuine insights
   in category-theoretic or algebraic language that sounds precise but lacks defined referents. When
   the Between reports a "functor," "natural transformation," or "dual formulation," demand: (a) are
   the categories defined? (b) is the map constructed? (c) are the properties proven? If not, the
   genuine content is simpler than the formalization suggests. Strip the language back to what's
   actually shown. (Lesson from Run 004 mathematical review, 2026-03-27.)
6. **Name the closest known theorem.** When a finding resembles established mathematics, name the
   theorem precisely (Ambrose-Singer, Atiyah-Singer, Tomita-Takesaki, etc.) and state how the
   finding differs. This prevents rediscovery of known results and prevents false claims of novelty.
