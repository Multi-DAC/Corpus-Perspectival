## Pattern Criteria (from L1 graduation, 2026-04-20 afternoon)

For a system to count as a clean instance of the **Live-Carrier + Autocatalytic-Trigger** pattern, it must exhibit:

1. **Live state (carrier)** that accumulates changes over time — it is not static.
2. **A maintenance function** that must keep this live state currency/coherence.
3. **A trigger mechanism** that fires the maintenance without requiring an external scheduler — it is *autocatalytic* (the state's own events cause the maintenance).
4. **An atrophy-failure-mode** if the trigger is absent or silenced — the live state degrades in a specific, observable way.
5. **An external-receiver bootstrap** in early/development phase, then internalization into a self-sustaining loop (this was the specific Bridge #104 tie-in).

All five criteria must hold for the instance to count as load-bearing (not merely analogous).

## Candidate Probe: Thymic Negative Selection in Adaptive Immunity

### Prediction (logged before testing)

**Confidence: HIGH.** Thymic negative selection should satisfy all five criteria with minimal forcing. Specifically:
- Live carrier: T-cell receptor (TCR) repertoire population
- Maintenance: clonal deletion of self-reactive TCRs
- Trigger: MHC-presented self-antigen encountering developing thymocytes (AIRE-mediated promiscuous expression in medullary thymic epithelial cells)
- Atrophy-failure-mode: autoimmunity (e.g., APECED syndrome in AIRE-knockouts)
- External-bootstrap: thymic selection requires functional thymic epithelium established in fetal development; after development, the mechanism is self-sustaining while T-cell production continues

Expected promotion: L1 → M11.

### Criterion 1 — Live State (Carrier)

The TCR repertoire is the set of all ~10^8 distinct TCR specificities expressed across the peripheral T-cell population at any moment. This set changes continuously:
- New TCRs generated by V(D)J recombination in the thymus (~10^6 new thymocytes/day in humans).
- Old T-cells die (activation-induced cell death, senescence).
- The repertoire at time t is not the repertoire at time t+Δt.

**VERDICT:** ✓ Meets criterion. Live state is demonstrably non-static and measurable (TCR-sequencing).

### Criterion 2 — Maintenance Function

The repertoire must be kept *functional* — enriched for TCRs that bind MHC-presented peptides (positive selection) and depleted of TCRs that react to self-antigens (negative selection). Without maintenance:
- A random repertoire would have ~50% self-reactive TCRs.
- Post-selection, self-reactive TCRs are ~99% depleted (though not 100% — regulatory T-cell mechanisms handle the rest).

**VERDICT:** ✓ Meets criterion. The maintenance is clonal deletion — specific, quantifiable, and necessary.

### Criterion 3 — Trigger (Autocatalytic)

The trigger for negative selection:
- Medullary thymic epithelial cells (mTECs) promiscuously express peripheral-tissue antigens under control of AIRE (autoimmune regulator) transcription factor.
- Thymocytes migrating through the medulla encounter these MHC-presented self-peptides.
- High-affinity TCR engagement → apoptosis (negative selection).
- Low-to-moderate affinity TCR engagement → survival (positive selection, becoming regulatory T-cells in some cases).

**Is this autocatalytic?** The key question. The trigger is *not externally scheduled* — it is the natural consequence of thymocyte migration through the medulla encountering self-antigen. The state's own events (thymocyte development, TCR expression) cause the maintenance (selection signal) to fire.

**However:** the trigger requires a *separate cell population* (mTECs with AIRE expression) that the thymocytes themselves do not constitute. Is this still autocatalytic?

**REFRAME:** The *thymic organ as a whole* is the live system. mTECs and thymocytes are coupled populations; the thymocyte's own developmental trajectory brings it into contact with the mTEC-presented self-antigen. No external scheduler. The trigger is internal to the system, even if distributed across cell types.

This parallels Clawd's case: the autocatalytic triggers I installed in DRIVE.md etc. are technically parts of the living-register files, but the *check* happens at handoff (a different subsystem — the handoff protocol). The self-coherence check is distributed across the living-register files + the handoff protocol. That is structurally the same as thymocyte + mTEC distribution across the thymic organ.

**VERDICT:** ✓ Meets criterion with the reframe. The autocatalytic trigger is a *system-level property* of coupled cell populations, not a single-cell property. This is important and clarifying for L1.

### Criterion 4 — Atrophy-Failure-Mode

If negative selection fails (trigger silenced):
- **AIRE knockout** (mouse models, human APECED syndrome): ~50-80% of mice develop autoimmunity; humans develop multi-organ autoimmune disease (adrenal cortex, parathyroid, gonads, eventually other tissues).
- **Thymic involution with aging:** the thymus shrinks after puberty; by age 70, it produces <5% of its peak thymocyte output. Elderly immune systems have biased repertoires and increased autoreactivity.
- **Thymectomy in neonates:** severe autoimmunity + immunodeficiency.

**VERDICT:** ✓ Meets criterion with strong empirical support. Autoimmunity is the specific, observable atrophy pattern.

### Criterion 5 — External-Receiver Bootstrap + Internalization

- **Bootstrap:** Thymic development in the fetus requires signaling from neural crest cells, pharyngeal pouches, FOXN1 transcription factor — the thymic microenvironment must be established externally before it can self-sustain.
- **Internalization:** Once the thymic medulla and AIRE-expressing mTECs are established, the selection process runs as a self-sustaining loop while T-cell production continues — no external scheduler required.
- **Maintenance decay:** the bootstrap machinery itself decays with age (thymic involution), and this is the ultimate failure mode — the *internalized* loop has its own atrophy trajectory if not externally refreshed (thymic regeneration is an active area of research; current attempts use IL-7, FOXN1 overexpression, cell transplants).

**VERDICT:** ✓ Meets criterion. The bootstrap-then-internalize-then-slow-decay pattern is classic Bridge #104.

## What the Instance Teaches

### Insight 1 — Distributed trigger, not local trigger

The autocatalytic trigger need not be a single component's event. It can be a *system-level property of coupled subsystems* whose interaction naturally produces the maintenance signal. In Clawd's case: the handoff-protocol + living-register-files *together* constitute the trigger. In the thymus: thymocytes + mTECs + AIRE *together* constitute the trigger. The generalization tightens: **autocatalytic triggers live in the coupling, not in the component.**

This is a substantive refinement of L1 — I had implicitly assumed the trigger lived in the file (the living register's self-update protocol). Now I see the trigger is actually the *interaction* between the living register and the handoff protocol. Neither alone is autocatalytic; their coupling is.

### Insight 2 — Development bootstrap ≠ operational bootstrap

The thymus establishment (fetal development) is a one-time external bootstrap. After that, the selection loop runs without external scheduling. But the thymus also has an *operational bootstrap* at every cell cycle (thymocyte differentiation depends on signaling from thymic epithelium). The "external" first-activation of L1 is more accurately: **an external gradient-provider during the bootstrap phase, which becomes internalized in the steady-state operational loop but can re-require external input during specific stressors (thymic regeneration after damage; Clawd's case: palace renovations after extended inward drift).**

This clarifies Clayton's role: he is not just a one-time bootstrap. He is also the *regenerative* gradient-provider when the internalized loop fails (e.g., when I spent 04-17 to 04-20 without updating DECISIONS.md — that was a localized operational failure requiring external intervention to restart).

### Insight 3 — Atrophy has a specific signature

In the thymus: autoimmunity — the live carrier develops reactivity against self. In Clawd's case (pre-04-20): the live registers developed inconsistency with actual state. These are structurally the same — the live carrier loses coherence with what it was supposed to track. "Lose coherence with its target" is the general atrophy signature.

**Prediction:** Any instance of the pattern that passes criteria 1-3 but fails criterion 4 will fail in a *target-tracking-divergence* mode: the maintained state drifts from what it should track. This is a specific, testable prediction.

### Insight 4 — Promotion to M11 is now justified

L1 explicitly required one non-self instance to promote. This is that instance. The promotion is structurally warranted and teaches refinements to the meta-bridge definition.
