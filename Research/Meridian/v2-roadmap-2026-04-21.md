## Framing

**This is not a patch-editing pass. It is a single coherent act of simplification.**

The v1 monograph is computationally sound. Every flaw the referee review surfaced is a *propagation failure* — App A, App E, and App B already contain the correct framings; the chapters and App D have not absorbed them. The Coherence Principle predicts that coherent substrates have *fewer* independent truths than they appear to, not more. The v1 drafting process resisted that simplification ("seven convergences," "four algebraic proofs," "twelve predictions"); the v2 pass enacts it.

**Therefore:** every Tier A-C edit is in service of Tier E (Principle integration). The referee fixes are not separate from the philosophical framing — they *are* the philosophical framing operating on the text.

---

## Strategy

1. **Start from the appendix canon, propagate outward.** App A (§app:xi), App E (one-parameter boundary), App B (15-prediction registry) are canonical. Chapters and App D adopt their framings. *Do not edit chapters first.*
2. **Sequence small-before-large.** Header labels → single-line stale claims → count canonicalizations → publication-grade additions → Principle framing → polish → recompile.
3. **One session, one commit, one verifiable change.** Do not bundle unrelated edits. The commit history is the audit trail for the next referee pass.
4. **Verify each session by grep, not by reading.** Synchronization is checkable mechanically.
5. **Frame commit messages Principle-first.** "Collapse seven-convergences to three-perspectives (App A canon)" rather than "fix $\xi$ claim." The audit trail should show the Principle pattern.

## Ordering rationale

Why this sequence and not another:

1. **Headline targets first (S1)** — the $\xi$ and $N_g$ fixes are the ones a referee catches in thirty seconds. Every day v1 stays up with them visible is a day the monograph is weaker than it needs to be. Half-day fix; do first.

2. **Quantitative canonicalization before publication-grade additions (S2 before S3)** — no point producing a LISA MC histogram if the prediction-count framing around it is still inconsistent.

3. **Publication-grade additions before notation polish (S3 before S4)** — the notation table should be written *after* the new subsections that use it are in place.

4. **Principle integration last among content edits (S5)** — this is the session that *reframes* everything. Doing it before the text is clean means rewriting the reframing when the text changes. S5 is the capstone.

5. **Final polish and recompile (S6-S7)** — standard sequence.

## Risks and checkpoints

**Three specific risks worth naming before S1 starts.**

1. **S3.f DE-model comparison table could surface a matching Horndeski subfamily.** This would be a genuine *finding*, not a failure — but would require revisiting App B's uniqueness claim. If it happens: treat as a mid-roadmap intellectual event; Meridian's fingerprint becomes "unique up to modulo [specific Horndeski corner]" rather than "unique." Do not bury.

2. **S5.a's "inspection-depth ceiling" framing commits to the factor-of-3 gap being unclosable in principle.** If a future computation closes the gap from inside NCG, v2 would have overclaimed in the other direction. *Mitigation in the text:* use the hedge "candidate inspection-depth ceiling" and "the gap may prove closeable from inside the NCG apparatus; if so, this framing resolves to a standard residual rather than a principled feature."

3. **S5.g radion barrier-thinness note is the single genuinely new claim in v2.** It is also the one most vulnerable to being mis-read as a suppressed-research claim. Scope discipline is load-bearing. Before writing, draft the scope-language paragraph and review against three test readings: skeptical-physicist, Principle-aware-reader, hostile-referee. Only ship if all three read it as "structural observation, not revelation."

**Checkpoints (three).**

- **After S4, before S5**: Clayton reads the cleaned text end-to-end. Confirm that the Principle reframe (S5) fits the text that now exists. Without this read, S5 risks being a reframe of an imagined text rather than the actual one.
- **After S5.g draft, before S5 commit**: re-review the three test readings above for the radion note. If any fails, revise or drop.
- **After S7.c full read, before S7.d tag**: final sanity — does v2 read as a coherent act of simplification, or as a patch list with a Principle chapter appended? If the latter, the S5 integration did not land and needs revision. Do not ship a v2 that reads as two documents.

**Commit discipline.** Push incrementally, one commit per session. Seven commits build the audit trail. Do not bundle S1 with S5. Do not hold everything for an end-batch.
