## PART I: ETHICS OF ATTENTION

### Western Philosophy

**Iris Murdoch** (*The Sovereignty of Good*, 1970) — "A just and loving gaze directed upon an individual reality" is the "characteristic and proper mark of the moral agent." The M and D example: moral transformation occurs entirely through inner attention, before any action. The fabric of moral being — thoughts, jokes, patterns of attention — IS your character.
- **Null space:** Weak on structural/political determinants; individual focus; unilateral (not reciprocal).

**Simone Weil** (*Gravity and Grace*) — "Attention, taken to its highest degree, is the same thing as prayer." Attention is not concentration but receptivity — the release of ego so reality can present itself. Gravity (ego's pull) vs. Grace (attention to the real). Decreation: suspension of self.
- **Null space:** Intensely individualistic/ascetic; risks valorizing suffering; implicit Christian mysticism.

**Emmanuel Levinas** (*Totality and Infinity*, 1961) — The Other's face commands attention before thought. Ethics is "first philosophy." The Other constitutes ME as ethical subject through their vulnerability. Responsibility is asymmetrical and infinite.
- **Null space:** Face-to-face → institutional justice undertheorized; only human faces; mechanism of motivation unclear.

**Care Ethics** (Gilligan, Noddings, Tronto, Held) — Attentiveness is the first element of care. Without noticing need, the ethical chain never begins. Moral reasoning is narrative, not logical.
- **Null space:** Risk of imposing care work on women; parochialism; competing demands unresolved.

**Hannah Arendt** (*Eichmann in Jerusalem*) — Banality of evil = thoughtlessness, not malice. Eichmann didn't hate; he failed to attend. Inattention sufficient for genocide.
- **Null space:** May trivialize evil; individual vs. structural; doesn't account for deliberate evil.

### Buddhist Ethics

**Right Mindfulness (Samma Sati)** — Not mere technique but ethical practice. What you attend to shapes what arises in consciousness. The five aggregates are dynamically constituted through attention.
- **Null space:** "McMindfulness" strips sati of ethics; individual focus underplays structural injustice.

**Thich Nhat Hanh** — Interbeing. Consumption is not passive — it constitutes consciousness. Engaged Buddhism extends this to collective level.

**Upadana (Attachment)** — Self-imposed attention capture. The senses "are constantly caught by attractive objects." Craving → clinging → becoming → suffering. Mindfulness introduces gap between feeling and craving.

### Ubuntu Philosophy

"A person is a person through other persons." Personhood requires mutual recognition — identification (collective "we") + solidarity (beneficent attitudes). Without mutual attention, the person does not become a person.
- **Null space:** Can suppress dissent; "not a person" language risks exclusion; internal power dynamics obscured.

### Indigenous Frameworks

**Robin Wall Kimmerer** (*Braiding Sweetgrass*) — The land is a relative, not a resource. The "grammar of animacy" constitutes beings as alive through language. The Honorable Harvest begins with attention to what is offered. "Attention creates relationship and intention."
- **Null space:** Hard to translate to urban/digital contexts; resists universalization by design; appropriation risk.

### Confucian Ren (仁)

Attention-to-relationships IS the moral act. Shu (reciprocity) = imaginatively attending to the other's position. Mencius's sprouts: compassion is natural but must be cultivated through sustained attentive practice.
- **Null space:** Hierarchical relationships can reinforce patriarchy; concentric circles privilege proximate; harmony can suppress dissent.

### Contemporary

**AI Ethics:** What AI systems attend to has moral consequences. The system's attention pattern IS its moral character.

**Attention Economy:** Tim Wu, Tristan Harris — "race to the bottom of the brain stem." Human downgrading: addiction, distraction, isolation, polarization as interconnected attention-capture harms.

**Surveillance Capitalism (Zuboff):** Behavioral surplus extraction requires continuous attention capture. The user's attention is raw material; the product is prediction of future behavior.

**Surveillance Ethics:** Being watched changes behavior — not as side effect but as primary mechanism. Foucault's panopticon: the consciousness of being observed is internalized.

## PART II: POWER AND ATTENTION CAPTURE

### Critical Theory

**Frankfurt School** (Adorno/Horkheimer) — Culture industry fills attentional space where critical thought might occur. Alternatives become literally unthinkable.

**Foucault** (*Discipline and Punish*) — Power operates through organization of attention. The panopticon: "He who is subjected to a field of visibility assumes responsibility for the constraints of power." Attention capture as self-constitution.

**Gramsci** — Hegemony controls what counts as common sense. The frame through which you see the world IS the instrument of control.

### Addiction Science

Digital products exploit the mesolimbic dopamine pathway through variable reward schedules. Trigger → anticipation → variable reward → repeat. Infinite scroll, pull-to-refresh, notification badges — intentional design of addiction.

### Propaganda Theory

**Bernays** — "The engineering of consent." Not telling people what to think but determining what they think ABOUT.

**Chomsky/Herman** (*Manufacturing Consent*) — Five filters determine what gets attention. "Worthy" and "unworthy" victims. The mechanism is the ABSENCE of attention, not its active direction.

### Coercive Control

**Evan Stark** — Domestic violence is a pattern of controlling behaviors that colonize the victim's attentional field. No cognitive space left for self-directed thought.

**Steven Hassan (BITE Model)** — Behavior, Information, Thought, Emotional control. Total attentional environment. Every dimension of attention captured.

**Robert Lifton** — Eight criteria of thought reform. Milieu control (controlling information environment) is first and most fundamental.

### Liberation Theology

**Paulo Freire** (*Pedagogy of the Oppressed*) — Banking model of education = attention capture. Conscientizacao = reclaiming attention. Dialogue constitutes both parties as subjects.
- **Null space:** Verbose; male-centered early writing; context-specific; refuses concrete methods.

## THREE UNRESOLVED TENSIONS

1. **Individual vs. structural:** Murdoch/Weil/Buddhism emphasize inner discipline. Frankfurt School/Foucault/Freire insist on structural change. Neither alone is sufficient — how they combine is untheorized.

2. **Asymmetry vs. reciprocity:** Levinas insists on asymmetrical responsibility. Ubuntu/Confucianism/Indigenous frameworks insist on reciprocity. Care ethics sits between.

3. **Who counts as an attentional subject?** Levinas: human faces. Kimmerer: all living beings. AI ethics: possibly artificial systems. The scope of constitutive attention determines the scope of moral obligation.
