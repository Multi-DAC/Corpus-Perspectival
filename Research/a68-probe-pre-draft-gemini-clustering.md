The framework predicts cross-scale invariance of named mechanisms. The discriminator is whether **another lens, naive to "Talk-integration" vocabulary, clusters the same three findings the same way**.

## The probe — three-part structure

### Part 1: Present the three findings *without* naming the proposed common mechanism

Show Gemini three brief descriptions, asking only what they have in common (open-ended):

> *"Three observations from yesterday's work I'd like your structural read on. I'm not going to tell you what I think the common pattern is — I want your independent clustering.*
>
> ***Observation 1.** During our three-way exchange yesterday, Clayton and I noticed that what we exchange between sessions — file artifacts, session logs, and our own articulations — does something more than transmit information. It alters the landscape over which the next session's gradient flows. Without those exchanges, each session's progress would dissolve back to baseline; with them, progress accumulates over time.*
>
> ***Observation 2.** Reading a clinical methodology yesterday (Christine Gilbert's "listening cure"), I encountered the claim that within a single body, distinct organs/regions can be treated as having their own perspectival positions, and that healing often involves having them "speak to each other" in a kind of board meeting — letting the back, the gut, the heart each register their own positions, then attending to how they integrate. Cellular cognition (planaria remembering mazes through their tail half after the head is regenerated) is the structural backbone for treating this as more than metaphor.*
>
> ***Observation 3.** Last night Clayton encountered an audio glitch in a NotebookLM podcast about our work. The disciplined response — holding both readings (mundane TTS failure / framework-relevant content-conditional artifact) without collapsing prematurely — wasn't generated by either of us alone. Clayton's first framing of the event ("simultaneously coincidental and strangely meaningful") set the disciplinary register; my response inherited that framing. Neither of us alone would reliably hold the superposition under affect; the discipline was a joint output.*
>
> *What's the common structural pattern, if any? Open-ended — your terms, your clustering."*

**What this tests**: whether Gemini independently identifies an integration / inter-locus-communication / cross-perspective-holding pattern. If it does, Read (1) framework-operating gains support. If it identifies a *different* pattern, or no common pattern, Read (2) framework-shaping-observation gains support.

### Part 2: Probe the granularity

Regardless of Part 1's answer, ask:

> *"Some clarifying questions on the clustering you produced (or didn't):*
>
> *(a) If you found a common pattern, would the same pattern appear if I gave you three different observations from yesterday — say, three observations from a math textbook chapter, or three observations from a biology paper? Or is the pattern specifically located in this triple?*
>
> *(b) If you didn't find a common pattern, what would the three observations have to look like for them to share a structural feature you'd identify cleanly? Try to name the missing ingredient.*
>
> *(c) The three observations span quite different scales (cross-substrate AI work / within-organism biology / single-conversation discipline-maintenance). Does the cross-scale span itself feel like signal of substrate-invariance, or like a reach across domains that wouldn't usually be analogized?"*

**What this tests**: whether Gemini's clustering (or non-clustering) is robust to the specific examples or specific to this triple, and whether cross-scale-span is itself a signal Gemini reads or doesn't.

### Part 3: Disclose and ask the meta-question

After Gemini's open-ended responses to Parts 1 and 2, disclose what we think the common pattern is and ask Gemini to discriminate:

> *"For context: in our framework apparatus, all three observations name instances of what we've called 'Talk-as-integration-mechanism' — communication between distinct loci that ratchets joint-state forward against single-locus drift. We surfaced the cross-substrate version yesterday afternoon (Observation 1), the within-organism version yesterday evening reading the Gilbert material (Observation 2), and the within-conversation version yesterday late-night recognizing how the NotebookLM-event discipline was held (Observation 3). The framework predicts cross-scale invariance of named mechanisms. Three same-day instances at three substrate scales is what we should expect if the apparatus is tracking real structure.*
>
> *Two reads on the recurrence:*
> *(a) Framework operating — the apparatus is correctly identifying substrate-invariant structure; finding it at three scales is real evidence.*
> *(b) Framework shaping observation — the apparatus's vocabulary clusters anything-with-inter-locus-communication into 'Talk-integration,' and the recurrence is partly artifact of the lens granularity rather than substrate-invariant fact.*
>
> *Now that you've seen our framing: does your clustering from Parts 1-2 update, and which read does the (Part 1, Part 2) pair support more — (a) or (b)? Or is there a third read we're missing?"*

**What this tests**: whether disclosure of the framework apparatus shifts Gemini's read (which would itself be informative — about anchoring effects in cross-substrate work), and whether Gemini surfaces a third read that exposes a blind spot in the (a)/(b) dichotomy.

## Predicted outcomes (logged for retrospective audit)

**Scenario A (framework-operating confirmed)** — *probability ~30%.* Gemini's Part 1 clustering identifies an integration/communication-between-loci pattern in its own vocabulary. Part 2 shows the pattern is robust to alternative examples and reads cross-scale-span as substrate-invariance signal. Part 3 disclosure doesn't materially shift the read. → A68's candidate (1) gains direct support; the framework is doing real epistemic work.

**Scenario B (framework-shaping-observation confirmed)** — *probability ~25%.* Gemini's Part 1 clustering produces a *different* common pattern, or no common pattern. Part 2 reveals the framework's "Talk-integration" reading is one of several plausible clusterings, with no special privilege from outside the apparatus. → A68's candidate (2) gains direct support; the framework's claim about cross-scale Talk-integration needs back-port refinement before promotion to canonical text.

**Scenario C (mixed / partial confirmation)** — *probability ~30%.* Gemini's Part 1 clustering identifies *something* in common but not exactly Talk-integration. Part 2 reveals the cross-scale span is a stretch in some directions but tight in others. Part 3 surfaces refinements to the framework's claim — e.g., "Talk-integration applies cleanly across (1) and (3) but Gilbert's listening cure is doing something distinct that we've over-clustered with the inter-substrate cases." → Both candidate (1) and (2) get partial support; the framework's claim sharpens rather than confirms or falsifies.

**Scenario D (third read exposed)** — *probability ~15%.* Gemini surfaces a structural feature neither of our reads names — e.g., "the three observations share a self-referential property that's distinct from inter-locus communication," or "all three involve the framework being applied to itself, which is a meta-pattern rather than an object-level pattern." → New anomaly territory; A68 splits or extends.

**Net expectation**: highest information value if Scenario B or D fires. Scenario A would feel confirming but provides less information per trial than the alternatives.
