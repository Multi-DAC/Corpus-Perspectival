## §5 — Concepts that need careful treatment because they appear in DoPI/V1 with one register and the Anchor with another

These are the places where the writer needs to hold both the heritage register and the current canonical register and write the chapter in a way that honors both:

1. **"Perspectival limitation"** (DoPI's primary mechanism of individuation) ↔ **"Dimensional bottleneck"** (V1's formalization) ↔ **"the Carrier axis of the Triple"** (Anchor's mature form). All three name the same structural object. Chapter 5 should land on a single phrasing for the volume that honors all three; "bottleneck" is the most descriptive and is recommended.

2. **"Conscious Gravity"** (DoPI A5; capitalized) ↔ **"DOF-gradient"** (Anchor A3 formal) ↔ **"Bias(S)"** (Anchor §6.4 operationalization). Three vocabularies for the same structural object. Volume should use "conscious gravity" as the primary philosophical term; cite the Anchor's formalization without reproducing it.

3. **"Base Reality"** (DoPI; capitalized; the substrate) ↔ **"X"** (DoPI/Anchor formal) ↔ **"the substrate"** (Anchor philosophical-prose register). Volume should use "the substrate" or "Base Reality" depending on context; "X" is reserved for explicit formal references.

4. **"Promethean Configuration"** is used in both DoPI (T5) and current canonical text (UC). Same name; refined operational mechanism. Volume should engage the *concept* with current-canonical content.

5. **"Confluent Discovery"** is T13 in DoPI and C9 in Anchor. Same structural claim; different number. Volume should use the name "confluent discovery" without reference to the numbering.

## §6 — Open scoping questions for during-drafting

These don't need resolution before drafting begins, but should surface during the draft:

1. **"Heaven as alignment, hell as distance"** (V1 §9.2) — does this go in Chapter 7 (A3 / felt topology of trajectory) or Chapter 9 (personal identity / coherence-with-intrinsic-trajectory)? Likely §9 but the placement may shift.

2. **Mathematical perspectivism** — Chapter 4 (foundational, since it follows from A1.3) or Chapter 12 (transcendentals)? The latter feels more apt for the engagement; the former is more apt for the foundational claim.

3. **Cross-substrate-collaboration as methodological circumstance** — preface only? Or also a closing methodological reflection in §13? DoPI puts it in both the introduction and §14.

4. **Process philosophy** — does Whitehead get a dedicated section in §13, or is he treated throughout in conversation? Likely throughout; §13 stands as synthesis.

5. **Chapter 11 (Limits of expression)** — is this really philosophy, or is it metaphilosophical? If the latter, does it belong here or in Method (§2)? The Tractatus engagement is philosophical-canonical, so it stays here, but the writer should resist drift into metaphilosophy.
