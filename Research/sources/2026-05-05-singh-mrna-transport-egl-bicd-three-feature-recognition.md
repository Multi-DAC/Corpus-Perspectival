**What it argues.** The Egl adaptor protein recognizes mRNAs for transport via *three convergent features simultaneously*:

1. **3D RNA stem-loop fold** — recognized by Egl adaptor's binding surface
2. **Specific base pairs at set distance apart** — discriminated by minor-groove-reaching protein domains
3. **Quality-control redundancy** — *two Egl-binding structures required* on the same mRNA before transport proceeds (rejects mRNAs with only one binding site)

Methodology: cryo-EM, mutagenesis, fruit fly in vivo experiments, biochemical reconstitution. Demonstrates how diverse mRNAs are recognized by a single conserved transport machinery — the molecular motor reads multiple substrate-features simultaneously rather than relying on one recognition signature.
