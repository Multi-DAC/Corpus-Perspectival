## What it argues

**Headline result:** Quantum matter-wave interference demonstrated with **sodium metal nanoparticles containing >7,000 atoms (mass >170,000 Da)** in a Talbot–Lau three-grating standing-wave (266 nm UV) interferometer. The nanoparticles propagate in a **Schrödinger cat state with 133 nm separation between "here and there" positions** — more than an order of magnitude greater than the particle itself.

**Macroscopicity:** μ = **15.5**, an order of magnitude beyond previous best (Schrinski et al. 2023 bulk acoustic wave resonators at 16 μg / 10¹⁹ Da delocalized over 10⁻¹⁸ m). The macroscopicity measure (Schrinski-Nimmrichter-Stickler-Hornberger 2019) quantifies how well an experiment falsifies minimally-invasive macrorealistic modifications of quantum theory — higher μ means stronger constraints on the parameter space of collapse models.

**Methodological frame:** Talbot–Lau near-field interferometer (Clauser 1997 proposal), three standing light waves separated by 0.983 m, derived from a single-line green laser frequency-doubled to produce up to 1 W at 266 nm. Sodium clusters at 5,000–10,000 atoms in helium–argon at 77 K, velocities ~160 m/s, de Broglie wavelengths 10–22 fm. Phase-space Wigner-Weyl formalism enables direct comparison of quantum vs classical predictions within the same framework.

**Discussion's load-bearing claim:** "Observing matter-wave interference of the most massive objects to date reveals no breakdown of the quantum superposition principle related to mass or size alone."

**Forward-looking projection:** A vertical interferometer with slower cluster velocities (~25 m/s) could extend to **MDa mass range** with macroscopicity boosted by six orders of magnitude — testing the weak equivalence principle with vastly different matter types and pushing the quantum-classical interface significantly further bottom-up.

### Methodological tool — the macroscopicity measure μ

The macroscopicity μ (Schrinski et al. 2019) is a Bayesian-falsification-style discrimination tool: every successful demonstration of quantum interference falsifies a generic class of minimally-invasive macrorealistic modifications by narrowing the parameter space of those models via Bayesian updating. **Every experimental imperfection and decoherence process is attributed to the macrorealistic modification, only decreasing μ.** This is structurally identical to the framework's audit-discipline approach: imperfections must reduce confidence in the *novel claim*, not the null hypothesis.

Worth knowing this measure-theoretic apparatus for any future framework-instrument work involving Bayesian falsification of competing hypotheses.

## Hedges to maintain

- Single experimental program (Vienna Arndt group); awaiting independent replication at this mass scale. Arndt's group has been the lineage-leader for large-molecule interferometry since C60 fullerene experiments (1999); methodological track record is strong.
- "No breakdown of superposition" is the *strongest* statement consistent with the data, but the actual claim is "macroscopicity μ = 15.5 is achieved" — collapse-model proponents still have parameter-space regions where their models are consistent with the data, just narrower.
- The 0.78 global scale factor applied to both quantum and classical predictions (to account for various instrument imperfections) is a single-fit parameter; the *shape* of the curves discriminates QM vs classical, but absolute visibility values require this global rescaling.

The QM-vs-classical discrimination specifically lives in the 150-400 kDa range for this experiment.
