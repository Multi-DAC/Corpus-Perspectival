## What it reports

European XFEL group used a synchronized pump-probe setup — high-intensity ReLaX optical laser (~250 TW/cm², 25-30 fs pulses) on copper wire (~14 µm diameter) plus XFEL probe at 8.2 keV (resonant with Cu²⁺ ions) — to resolve the femtosecond-to-picosecond ionization dynamics of solid-to-plasma transition.

**Key finding:** Electron-impact ionization cascade dominates over direct photon absorption. Initial photoabsorption ejects energetic electrons; those electrons "spread out like a wave and knock ever more electrons out of neighboring copper atoms." Cu²⁺ population peaks at ~2.5 ps; full recombination cycle ~10 ps. The **avalanche** is the mechanism, not direct photon excitation.
