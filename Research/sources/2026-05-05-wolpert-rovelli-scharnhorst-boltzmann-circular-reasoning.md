**What it argues.** Identifies circular reasoning in standard treatments of the Boltzmann brain paradox: physicists' assumptions about the past are used to justify conclusions about memory reliability, which then support those same initial assumptions. The paper develops a formal framework analyzing how time-related assumptions affect entropy/memory conclusions; clarifies hidden logical structure rather than resolving the paradox. Key insight: laws of physics don't specify whether to treat current universe state OR Big Bang's low-entropy beginning as fixed point — interpretive flexibility is irreducible.
