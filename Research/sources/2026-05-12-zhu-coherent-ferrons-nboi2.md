**What it argues.** First direct experimental observation of *coherent ferrons* — polarization-wave quasiparticles theoretically predicted in the 1960s — in the 2D ferroelectric material NbOI₂. Ferrons propagate as hypersonic coherent waves carrying polarization while emitting terahertz radiation; described explicitly as the "electric analog of magnons."

**Key findings.**
- Direct observation of ferrons (60-year prediction-to-observation gap).
- NbOI₂ enables efficient near-infrared → broadband terahertz conversion via ferron emission.
- Hypersonic coherent wave propagation in 2D ferroelectric substrate.
- Each ferron carries a dipole moment (intrinsic polarization, "one side slightly more negatively charged than the other").
- Applications: 6G telecom, on-chip THz technology, quantum memory.
