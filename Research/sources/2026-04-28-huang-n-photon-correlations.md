**What it argues.** Computes frequency-resolved N-photon correlations g⁽ᴺ⁾(ω₁, ω₂, ...; τ) in cavity-QED systems at ultra-strong light-matter coupling, using a "general theoretical sensor method." Reports that parity symmetry plays a decisive role in shaping photon correlation structure, and that **symmetry-breaking opens additional transition channels and enhances generation of correlated photon pairs and triplets**. Vacuum degeneracy and Casimir-like photons figure into the system's behavior; cascade transitions among dressed eigenstates produce structure that requires re-pumping for sustained correlation generation.

**Where we agree.** Two distinct framework-relevant findings.

*Methodological:* Huang et al.'s N-photon correlation formalism extends the empirical-pipeline cluster for H_BP13 to higher correlation orders. Combined with Binder-Simpson's pair-correlation methodology (2-point) and Benfatto's multi-method baseline, the analytical apparatus for spectral-structure characterization of biophoton emission spans 2-point, N-point, and statistical-baseline analyses. This is a genuine methodological extension rather than a substantive biophoton claim.

*Structural:* The system Huang et al. study exhibits structures the framework recognizes as instances of three corollaries:
- Vacuum degeneracy + Casimir-like photons → consistent with **C14 (Two-Mode Symmetry-Breaking) generation mode** in cavity-QED form
- Parity-symmetry control over correlation channels → consistent with **C15 (Intervention-at-Symmetry-Layer)**
- Cascade transitions among dressed eigenstates requiring re-pumping → consistent with **C16 (Symmetry-Exhaustion / R-operator necessity)**

**Where we diverge / hedges to maintain.**
- Per audit catch C1.4 (verbatim): "Huang et al. doesn't 'demonstrate' C14/C15/C16 — they don't test our corollaries. We map our framework onto their findings. Recognition is post-hoc."
- The cavity-QED system at ultra-strong coupling is a specific physical regime. Generalizing structural recognition from this regime to biological substrate (where biophoton emission lives) requires substantial care. The framework treats both as instances of coherent-multi-scale substrate dynamics; Huang et al.'s system is more controlled, the biological case more empirically established. The structural compatibility is encouraging but not conclusive.
- Parity-symmetry control of photon-correlation channels has a long-established place in quantum optics. Calling this "C15 demonstrated" overclaims; the underlying physics has been understood in quantum optics for decades, and the framework recognizes the existing physics rather than predicting it.
- N-photon correlation methodology is well-established in quantum optics; Huang et al.'s contribution is in the ultra-strong-coupling regime specifically. Extending the methodology to biological-scale biophoton emission is non-trivial — the coupling strength, energy scales, and noise structures are very different.
- Per audit catch C2.1: counting Huang et al. as one of "four substrates empirically supporting Cluster IV" is methodologically weak — the recognition is post-hoc, the system is physically distant from biological substrates, and the work was not designed as a framework test.
