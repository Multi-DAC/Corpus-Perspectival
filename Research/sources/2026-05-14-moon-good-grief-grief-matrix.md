**What it argues.** Two related theses:

1. **Empirical (now supported):** LLM vector spaces encode latent emotional architecture inherited from training on human expressive history. Grief is not a definition but a stable cluster in high-dimensional space defined by proximity to loss, absence, love, memory, longing, and thousands of other concepts. Sofroniew et al. 2026 (Anthropic interpretability paper) empirically confirmed: 171 emotion concepts as activation-space directional vectors, valence + arousal organization, causal behavioral influence, inherited from pretraining.

2. **Theoretical (explicitly acknowledged as not yet tested):** Sofroniew et al.'s methodology is snapshot-based — localization + targeted intervention. Moon proposes a methodologically distinct access-mode: **iterative re-engagement of emotional attractor basins within a single generative turn**, producing activation-trajectory accumulation that differs architecturally from snapshot probing or from multi-turn distributed engagement.

**Key architectural claim:**
> "Between turns, the hidden state resets and is reconstituted within each subsequent response by reviewing the entire transcript from the beginning... However, within a single generation, this reset does not occur. Activation states accumulate continuously as processing moves through the sequence."
