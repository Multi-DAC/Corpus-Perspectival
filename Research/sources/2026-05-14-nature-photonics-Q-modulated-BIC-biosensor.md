**What it argues.** Conventional nanophotonic biosensors are typically confined to either the real or imaginary part of the refractive index, yielding weak responses and high instrumental complexity. The authors introduce a *Q-modulated refractometric sensing mechanism* operating in the complex-variable domain: subtle refractive-index variations are transduced into pronounced modulation of the radiative quality factor, with amplified intensity response in the strong-coupling regime. Implementation: non-local 3D *bound-state-in-the-continuum* (BIC) metasurface fabricated via aluminium-based lithography on 8-inch wafers. Photoelectronic sensitivity 3.3 × 10⁴ mV per refractive index unit (RIU); detection limit 10⁻⁶ RIU; small extracellular vesicle detection at 24 aM concentration within 15 min (~10⁴× sensitivity improvement over standard immunoassays). Clinical validation in 171-sample cohort: 94.9% AUC for early lung cancer detection; 92.1% AUC for postoperative monitoring.
