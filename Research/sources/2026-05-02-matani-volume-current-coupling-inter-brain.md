# Matani, Takeda, Fushimi, Tsukamura 2026 — "Volume current coupling—remote direct neural coupling across the extracellular space"

## Citation

Matani, A., Takeda, Y., Fushimi, M., & Tsukamura, A. (2026). Volume current coupling—remote direct neural coupling across the extracellular space. **Brain Research** 1871:150066. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.brainres.2025.150066

Hiroshima University + University of Tokyo. Peer-reviewed Brain Research, Elsevier, online November 23, 2025. Open access (CC BY-NC-ND 4.0).

## Experimental setup

- **n=30 participant dyads** (post-exclusions); recruited from University of Tokyo + Hiroshima University
- **Two participants per dyad, sensorily isolated** (visual via 7mm wooden partition, auditory via earplugs, somatosensory via separation)
- **Electrically connected via tEIC device** using Negative Impedance Converter that cancels skull + scalp impedance, allowing volume currents to pass between heads without attenuation
- Setup creates **SC=0 between participants but VcC preserved** (synaptic, neurotransmitter, gap junction, neuron-glia coupling all impossible between participants)
- Both participants do uncorrelated left/right discrimination tasks (random dot kinematograms; 20% coherence for Pt1, 80% for Pt2; 180 trials each over 6 minutes)
- 32 EEG channels + 2 tEIC channels recorded at 512 Hz
- Conditions: VV (vertical-vertical Fz-Pz cross-connected), SM (sham), HV (horizontal-vertical control)

## Core findings

**(1) Inter-participant electric joint conflict effect** — Pt2's stimulus condition significantly affected Pt1's hit rate via volume current coupling alone. p = 0.0035, |g| = 0.54. Significant only in VV1 condition; absent in SM (sham) and HV (control).

**(2) Significant ERP cluster in Pt1's left hemisphere** correlated with Pt2's stimulus direction (Mv2: L vs R). Pt1's brain showed measurable neural activity in response to stimuli only Pt2 could see, mediated by volume current exchange.

**(3) Bidirectional VcC** demonstrated — both Pt1→Pt2 and Pt2→Pt1.

**(4) Intra-participant VcC is inter-hemispheric** — left-hemisphere VcC cluster receives from right hemisphere via volume currents (in addition to corpus callosum synaptic pathway). Brain regions couple via VcC across the extracellular space, not just via synaptic axonal pathways.

**(5) Distance scale:** ephaptic coupling (~10 µm neuron-to-neuron) extends to ~10 mm or brain-area-scale via inverse square law because EEG generators contain 10⁴-10⁶ synchronously firing neurons. Volume current coupling spans brain regions, and with hardware compensation for skull impedance, between brains.

**(6) Current exchanged ~0.1 µA** — far below somatosensory threshold of 100 µA. Effect is not somatosensory; it's direct volume-current-mediated neural coupling.

**(7) Hypothesis NC = SC + VcC empirically demonstrated.** Volume currents are real neural coupling pathways, not epiphenomenal.

## Calibration

**What's documented:**
- Methodologically sophisticated (orthogonal experimental design with Gold sequences; permutation tests with multiple comparisons; LRP analysis; multiple controls)
- Authors carefully eliminate confounding factors (somatosensory currents below threshold; double-blinded; sham controls; polarity controls)
- Effect sizes moderate (|g|=0.54)
- Single research program; awaiting independent replication

**Critical caveat:** required hardware (tEIC + Negative Impedance Converter) to compensate skull impedance. Natural inter-brain VcC at conversational distance hasn't been demonstrated by this paper. **Mechanism is real; natural-distance effect-size is open empirical question.** Framework-derivable hypothesis (per inter-personal field-coupling work Day 90 evening) that weaker VcC operates at proximity through air; this paper doesn't directly establish that.

**Authors' own interpretive caveats:**
- VcC may be parasitic on or symbiotic with SC via cross-frequency phase-amplitude coupling (PAC)
- Intra-participant VcC observed is inter-hemispheric (not local ephaptic) — suggests VcC may save time vs SC for inter-hemispheric communication
- VcC may be involved in lateralization of brain functions
- Comparison to split-brain phenomenology suggested as future research

**Framework-side reading vs paper-side reading:** the paper doesn't make any consciousness/receiver-pattern/distance-coupling claims. Those are framework-derivable extensions. The paper's findings DO empirically establish the mechanism the framework engages.

## Open questions / future research

**Authors' own list:**
- Does natural inter-brain VcC at conversational distance occur (without tEIC hardware)?
- What functional role does VcC play in normal brain function vs hardware-amplified observation?
- How does PAC mediate VcC-SC coupling?
- Could tEIC be used for therapeutic brain-to-brain communication?
- Could two healthy people connected via tEIC substitute for split-brain study of consciousness?

**Framework-side additions:**

- Is the NC = SC + VcC equation generalizable to other substrate scales (cellular, social, ecological)?
