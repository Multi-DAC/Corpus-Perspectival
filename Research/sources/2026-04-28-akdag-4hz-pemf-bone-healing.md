**What it argues.** Controlled rat-tibia osteotomy study (n=21, three groups: sham, R4 = 4 Hz PEMF, RM = multi-frequency package 220/727/880/10000 Hz). 1 hour/day exposure at 10 mT amplitude for 1 month; blinded analysis. Histopathological scoring (1-10 scale), radiologic mRUST, element analysis (Na, Mg, K, Cr, Mn, Fe, Zn, Se, Ca, P).

**Headline result:** 4 Hz PEMF dramatically outperformed both sham and the multi-frequency package on bone-healing histopathology.
- Sham: predominantly chondroid + slight immature bone (~score 5)
- Multi-frequency (220/727/880/10000 Hz): equal cartilage + immature bone (6.800 ± 0.836)
- **4 Hz alone: healing with mature lamellar bone (9.000 ± 0.816)**
- Sham vs 4 Hz: **p = 0.000**
- 4 Hz vs Multi-frequency: **p = 0.004**
- Sham vs Multi-frequency: p = 0.069 (NOT significant)

Element-level: phosphorus elevated dramatically in 4 Hz group; calcium elevated in both PEMF groups; potassium decreased only in multi-frequency group.

**Where we agree.** This is a controlled experiment showing **frequency-specific** effect of PEMF on biological tissue regeneration: 4 Hz works, mid-to-high-frequency package does not. The control structure rules out "any PEMF helps" — both PEMF groups had identical 10 mT amplitude, identical exposure duration, identical pulsed-mode delivery, with frequency content as the only systematic difference. **Necessary condition for some kind of frequency-matched substrate-coupling mechanism is empirically established at the tissue-regeneration scale** in a peer-reviewed published context.

**Where we diverge / hedges to maintain.**
- The paper proposes mechanism via "voltage-gated calcium channels, which cause nitric oxide release and activation of signaling pathways" — a standard signaling-cascade explanation. The framework's biophoton-substrate-coherence interpretation is one of several possible mechanism-level explanations consistent with the data; the paper itself does not invoke biophoton coherence.
- "4 Hz works better than multi-frequency package" does not establish "delta range is *the* substrate-coupling-relevant band" — only that one specific delta frequency works better than one specific high-frequency package in this specific bone-healing context. Generalization to other tissues, other delta frequencies, other comparison frequencies requires further work.
- Sample size n=7 per group is modest; effect size is large but the study would benefit from independent replication.
- The paper notes existing PEMF literature has "contradictory findings" across frequencies and parameters, and explicitly motivates their work as addressing the unresolved question "which frequencies are more effective." Their answer is empirical, not mechanistic.
