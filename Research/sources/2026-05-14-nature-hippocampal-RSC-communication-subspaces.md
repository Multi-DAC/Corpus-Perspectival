**What it argues.** Large-scale (up to 1,024 channels) extracellular recording across dentate gyrus / CA3 / CA2 / CA1 / retrosplenial cortex in behaving mice. Using partial canonical correlation analysis, the authors identify low-dimensional communication *subspaces* between region-pairs while accounting for influences from a third region. The subspaces capture distinct input-output transformations in CA1, linking upstream hippocampal activity (DG, CA3, CA2) to downstream cortical targets (RSC). Subspace memberships are constrained by intrinsic firing properties and anatomical location (deep sublayers of the CA3-CA1-RSC axis), and the same subspaces operate during both spatial and non-spatial tasks. Subspaces *recombine overlapping neuronal pools* to support distinct interareal interactions across changing experiences and brain states. Reactivation patterns of CA1-CA3 subspaces (but not CA1-RSC) during post-experience sleep correlated with replay, reflecting a *plasticity-stability balance* in the input-output transformation. Authors' conclusion: *"hippocampal-neocortical communication reconfigures predetermined circuit motifs to flexibly encode experiences."*
