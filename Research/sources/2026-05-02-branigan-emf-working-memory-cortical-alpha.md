# Branigan, Saroka, Corradini, Larivière, Dotta 2026 — "Electromagnetic field stimulation modulates working memory and cortical alpha oscillations in healthy adults"

## Citation

Branigan, K.S., Saroka, K.S., Corradini, P.L., Larivière, M.A., & Dotta, B.T. (2026). Electromagnetic field stimulation modulates working memory and cortical alpha oscillations in healthy adults. **Scientific Reports** 16:8660. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-026-42063-4

**Laurentian University group — same lab as the Persinger lineage that produced the 2016 whole-body biophoton 3.93 Hz finding (Vares/Saroka/Dotta).** Saroka and Dotta are the through-line; the lineage continues active research in cognitive-modulation territory.

Peer-reviewed Scientific Reports, Nature Portfolio. Open access (CC BY 4.0).

## Experimental setup

- **n=98 healthy adults** (35 male, 65 female; mean age 23.99 ± 5.45)
- Between-subjects design with 4 conditions: Theta-Burst (5-pulse bursts at 100 Hz) / Theta-Gamma (mimicking hippocampal coupling) / 40 Hz gamma / Sham
- **Field strength: ~4 µT** (extremely weak — orders of magnitude below regulatory exposure limits; comparable to natural ambient EM)
- 30 min EMF exposure preceding cognitive assessment
- WAIS-IV subtests: Digit Span Forward (DSF) + Digit Span Backward (DSB) + Digit Span Sequencing + Arithmetic + Letter-Number Sequencing
- Three spatial configurations: bilateral left / bilateral right / bilateral temporal
- 19-channel EEG before and after exposure

## Core findings

**(1) Pattern-specific cognitive targeting:**

| EMF Pattern | Effect | Mechanism observed |
|---|---|---|
| **Theta-Burst** | Impaired Digit Span **Backward** (working memory + manipulation; p=0.018, Cohen's d=0.76) | Increased high-alpha (10-12 Hz) in left IFG (p=0.002) + right SFG sLORETA effect |
| **Theta-Gamma** | Impaired Digit Span **Forward** (passive recall; p=0.047, Cohen's d=0.74 exploratory) | No detectable EEG change |
| **40 Hz** | No significant cognitive effect | — |

**(2) Subtest-specific targeting** demonstrates pattern selectivity. Theta-Burst targets manipulation/executive function; Theta-Gamma targets passive retention/encoding. **Different EMF patterns target different cognitive processes.**

**(3) Composite scores DIDN'T show effects** because they aggregate across multiple subtests with different demands. Compensatory processes mask targeted deficits. Authors note: *"EMF-related modulation can be localized and functionally selective, and may be overlooked when using broad aggregated measures."*

**(4) No spatial placement effect** — frequency dominates location. Authors interpret: differences driven primarily by frequency characteristics of EMF exposure rather than spatial placement.

**(5) Disruption direction in healthy adults.** Theta-burst REDUCED working memory in healthy participants. The same theta-burst stimulation (TMS form) IMPROVES outcomes in DEPRESSED patients per Li et al. 2014 (cited).

## Calibration

**What's documented:**
- Peer-reviewed Scientific Reports
- n=98 (reasonable sample for behavioral neuroscience)
- Between-subjects design appropriate
- Within-pattern effect sizes moderate (Cohen's d 0.66-0.89 for working-memory effects)
- Mechanism (high-alpha-as-inhibitory-gating) is established from prior literature

**Limitations / caveats:**
- Group-size variation (Sham n=20, 40Hz n=36) is moderate confound
- Single-site study; replication needed
- Theta-Gamma DSF effect labeled exploratory (omnibus ANOVA didn't reach significance; only post-hoc Tukey did)
- Authors note: "source-projected results should be interpreted as regionally constrained summaries of cortical activity rather than precise anatomical localization"

**Authors' own framing:** "Optimizing stimulation parameters may allow such fields to be harnessed for cognitive enhancement or targeted neuromodulation." This is therapeutic/translational framing aligned with framework's substrate-coherence-medicine paradigm.

**Framework-side reading vs paper-side:** the paper makes empirical pattern-selectivity claim + mechanism specification; the framework reads this through H_BP10 + substrate-state-dependence + LC9 lens. The framework lens isn't paper-supported but is consistent with paper's findings and aligned with authors' translational framing.
