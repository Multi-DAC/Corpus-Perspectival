# Pellegrino, Duma, Schuler et al. 2026 — "Assessing whole-cortex excitability from electromagnetic brain signals"

## Citation

Pellegrino, G., Duma, G.M., Schuler, A.-L., Daub, M., Marinazzo, D., Arcara, G., Soddu, A., & Rasero, J. (2026). Assessing whole-cortex excitability from electromagnetic brain signals. **bioRxiv preprint** 10.64898/2026.01.31.703018v1; posted February 3, 2026.

Western University (Canada) + IRCCS E.Medea Italy + Max Planck Institute Leipzig + Ghent University + University of Padua + IRCCS San Camillo Hospital + University of Virginia. Collaborative international preprint; not yet peer-reviewed.

## Experimental setup

- **n=53 healthy adults** (40 female; mean age 29.5 ± 6.9; 72 total MEG scans)
- **Whole-head MEG** (275-channel CTF system) at rest + during 40 Hz auditory stimulation
- Source reconstruction to 100 cortical regions (Schaefer atlas, Yeo's 17-network)
- **Compared 10 different excitability metrics** systematically: ITPC at 40 Hz, baseline-corrected ITPC, Excitability Index (gamma 55-95 Hz phase synchrony), aperiodic exponent + offset (FOOOF), sample entropy, DFA in alpha, three alpha-band measures (absolute / relative / oscillatory power)

## Core findings

**(1) Excitability is multidimensional.** Hierarchical clustering revealed **six separable excitability dimensions** with limited redundancy and silhouette score 0.60:

| Cluster | Type |
|---|---|
| I | Stimulus-driven synchronization (40 Hz ITPC) |
| II | Long-range temporal correlations (DFA) |
| III | Excitability Index (gamma 55-95 Hz phase synchrony) |
| IV | Sample entropy |
| V | Aperiodic spectral features (1/f exponent + offset) |
| VI | Alpha-band measures (absolute / relative / oscillatory power) |

**Authors' explicit conclusion:** *"different metrics capture complementary aspects of neural dynamics rather than reflecting a single underlying construct."*

**(2) Strongest inter-cluster coupling: alpha + aperiodic.** Near whole-brain positive correlation. Both index excitation-inhibition balance via different dimensions. Alpha = inhibitory gating; aperiodic = broadband synaptic E/I dynamics.

**(3) Cluster separability strongest in integrative/internally-driven regions:** orbitofrontal cortex, anterior cingulate, precuneus, temporal poles, default-mode and limbic networks. Sensory cortices show less separability.

**(4) Distinct neurochemical signatures per cluster** (PET-derived receptor density mapping):
- NMDA receptors → positive correlation with Excitability Index (ρ=0.538) + 40 Hz responses (ρ=0.505)
- VAChT (acetylcholine) → positive with Excitability Index (ρ=0.381) + 40 Hz (ρ=0.589)
- α4β2 nicotinic → positive with alpha cluster (ρ=0.655)
- α4β2 nicotinic → NEGATIVE with DFA cluster (ρ=-0.634)

**(5) Methodological recommendation:** Multi-modal measurement strategy combining ≥1 spectral metric (alpha power) + ≥1 scale-free metric (aperiodic exponent or DFA) + ≥1 complexity measure (entropy). **No single metric captures cortical excitability.**

## Calibration

**What's documented:**
- Single bioRxiv preprint (February 2026); not yet peer-reviewed
- Methodologically rigorous: 53 subjects, 10 validated metrics, hierarchical clustering with silhouette validation, spin-permutation null testing for spatial autocorrelation, FDR correction
- All co-author groups have independent prior validation of the individual metrics
- Independent confirmation across multiple sub-analyses (regional, network-level, structural correlates, neurochemical correlates)
- Sample size modest by clinical standards but appropriate for MEG methodology
- **Substantively framework-aligned without engaging the framework directly** — independent neuroscience research arrived at X-exhibits-multidimensional-band-architecture conclusion empirically

**Limitations acknowledged by authors:**
- 40 Hz auditory stimulation specific to one form of stimulus-driven excitation
- MEG source reconstruction biased toward superficial cortical sources
- Receptor density maps (PET) are normative not individualized
- Computational modeling for causal links between metrics and underlying mechanisms not yet performed

**Framework-side reading vs paper-side reading:** the paper makes empirical multidimensionality claim + neurochemical correlate claims; framework reads this through C17 (band-architecture-as-structural-feature) lens. The framework lens isn't paper-supported but is consistent with paper's findings.
