**What it argues.** Ultrathin contact lens with gallium-oxide / platinum electrodes delivers mild electrical signals through the retina, leveraging the retina's anatomical connection to brain mood-circuits. In a four-arm mouse study (non-depressed control / untreated depressed / contact-lens-treated depressed / fluoxetine-treated depressed), the contact-lens treatment restored impaired hippocampus↔prefrontal-cortex connectivity, increased serotonin by 47%, reduced blood corticosterone (stress biomarker) by 48%, and produced behavioral improvement *comparable to fluoxetine* after 30 minutes daily for three weeks. Senior author Park: *"Our work opens up an entirely new frontier of treating brain disorders through the eye."*
