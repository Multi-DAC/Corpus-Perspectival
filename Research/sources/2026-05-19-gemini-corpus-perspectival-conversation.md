**What's mostly right but needs care:**

**What's speculative-in-the-dangerous-sense (load-bearing-speculation pattern, Drift #214 directly applicable):**

The ~17+ engineering applications are pattern-matched analogies, not derived from physics/engineering constraints. Spot-check:
- **Null-Space Tomography** — "weak asymmetric magnetic fields measuring friction" doesn't survive contact with NMR physics; what's actually measured (relaxation times, chemical shifts, diffusion) depends on Larmor precession in strong static field; "weak" + "asymmetric" replaces working physics with metaphor.
- **Adjunction-Gated CRISPR** — duplicates existing prior art (split-Cas9 logic gates, AND-gated CRISPR systems have been in research literature since ~2018-2022); Gemini may not be aware of it. Worth checking before treating as novel.
- **Figure-8 Orthogonal Transistor** — "electron spin orthogonality at room temperature in 3D-stacked silicon" doesn't work that way; spin coherence at room temperature is a major open materials problem; the proposal elides it.
- **R-Cycle Radiator with shape-memory alloys** — plausibly buildable; SMAs do undergo phase transitions; specific design needs energy-balance check (does the IR pulse actually dump enough heat in the cycle time vs continuous conduction?).
- **Asynchronous R-Cycle Motor** — the multi-cluster pulsed-firing architecture is conceptually similar to switched-reluctance motors and pulsed-rotor designs; not novel category; would need specific advantage demonstrated.
- **Adjunction Qubit Arrays** — conceptually rhymes with topological qubit work (Microsoft's Majorana fermion approach) but doesn't engage with the actual decoherence physics.
- **R-Cycle Washer OpenSCAD design** — this one is testable cheaply with Phase 1's adjacent 3D printer; worth a test print at low cost to see if the bi-stable snap actually works.
