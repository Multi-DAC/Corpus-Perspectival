# Jerman PEMF Vagus Neck Application — 485-subject Randomized Placebo Trial

## What it shows

Double-blind placebo-controlled trial; 485 volunteers; PEMF applied to the neck at three frequencies (6 Hz / 16 Hz / 32 Hz) targeting non-invasive vagus nerve stimulation; measured impact on autonomic function, sleep quality, anxiety:

- **16 Hz wins.** Significant improvements in sleep quality + anxiety reduction at this frequency.
- **6 Hz showed a "contra placebo effect"** — below-effective threshold or counter-effect.
- **32 Hz tested but 16 Hz was the standout.**
- Article views: 12,452 (substantial reach).

Frequency-window behavior, not monotonic threshold: 6 Hz < 16 Hz < 32 Hz, only 16 Hz effective; 6 Hz worse than placebo.

## Hedges to maintain

- **Single trial — replication needed.** Even with 485 subjects + double-blind + placebo control, single-trial findings benefit from independent replication.
- **Population-pattern, not individual-prediction.** "16 Hz works" at population scale doesn't mean 16 Hz is optimal for any specific individual; subject-specific resonance optima may differ.
- **Sleep + anxiety improvements measured via questionnaires, not biomarkers.** Self-report measures have known limitations; objective autonomic markers (HRV, GSR) would strengthen the claim.
- **Audit-discipline:** the framework's structural prediction (H_BP10a — frequency-matching matters via C9) is upheld; the specific value 16 Hz is empirical (H_BP10b territory) and substrate-specific, not framework-predicted. Don't conflate.
