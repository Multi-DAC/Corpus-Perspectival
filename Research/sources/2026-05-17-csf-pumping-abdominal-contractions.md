## Substantive content (from news write-up — verify against primary)

**Experimental result:** Head-fixed mice on treadmill, two-photon microscopy with fluorescent skull-fixed reference beads. The brain physically slides ~1 μm forward + laterally within the cranial vault *immediately prior to* and during locomotion. EMG monitoring shows *abdominal muscle activation always precedes the cerebral shift* — core contractions are the upstream cause of brain displacement.

**Proposed mechanism:** A network of valveless veins surrounding the vertebrae communicates with abdominal-cavity vasculature. Abdominal contraction → blood forced through these openings into the spinal canal → compression of the dural sleeve encasing spinal cord → pressure wave of CSF driven upward into skull.

**Isolation cross-test:** Light external abdominal pressure via inflatable cuff on lightly-anesthetized mice replicated the exact brain displacement observed during voluntary walking; deflation returned brain to resting position immediately. Cross-test rules out general-locomotion confound; isolates abdominal-pressure as the driver.

**Volumetric extrapolation (CFD simulation):** The mechanical pumping forces CSF *out of* brain matrix several times faster than baseline endogenous fluid production rate.

**Glymphatic-system reframe.** The known story has been: NREM-sleep glymphatic clearance pulls CSF inward through brain parenchyma for solute mixing + waste collection. This paper's finding adds: *waking exercise drives the cleared CSF outward via abdominal-contraction-pumping*. The full clearance cycle requires both phases — sleep collects, exercise expels. A static or sedentary individual completes neither half cleanly.
