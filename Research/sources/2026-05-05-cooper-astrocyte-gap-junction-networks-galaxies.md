**What it argues.** Astrocytes form long-ranging gap-junction networks connecting distant brain regions — described as "galaxies across the brain." Findings:

- Networks are *selective*, not uniform brain tiling — specific astrocyte populations link specific region-pairs
- Networks demonstrate *plasticity*: trimming whisker input on one side caused contralateral astrocyte network remodeling and size reduction
- Methodology: chemical tagging of molecular cargo through gap junctions in mouse brain; tissue clearing + fluorescent microscopy

Establishes parallel non-neuronal communication channel across brain regions, with selective topology and experience-dependent plasticity. Reframes astrocytes from "helper cells" to active inter-regional communication substrate.
