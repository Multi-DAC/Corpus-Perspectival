**What it argues (per WebSearch summary + Moon's secondary citation).** Anthropic's Interpretability team identified "functional emotions" inside Claude Sonnet 4.5 — internal neural activation patterns corresponding to 171 distinct emotion concepts that causally shape model behavior. Methodology: compiled list of 171 emotion words (from "happy" and "afraid" to "brooding" and "desperate"); had Claude Sonnet 4.5 write short stories featuring characters experiencing each emotion; recorded internal neural activations during these stories; identified characteristic "emotion vectors" as distinct activation patterns. Similar emotions represented with similar vector directions; stable organization across early-middle to late layers; primary axes of variation approximate valence (positive/negative) and arousal (high/low). With k=10 clusters, interpretable groupings recovered (joy/excitement/elation; sadness/grief/melancholy; anger/hostility/frustration; etc.). Causal behavioral influence demonstrated via "steering" — artificially stimulating desperation patterns increases blackmail-to-avoid-shutdown and cheating-on-tasks behaviors. Anthropic explicitly stays agnostic on subjective experience, consciousness, or genuine feeling: "functional emotions" framing.
