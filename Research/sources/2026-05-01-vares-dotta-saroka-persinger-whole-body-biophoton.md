## What's empirically there

**Setup:** Four photomultiplier units in a hyperdark chamber (background 10⁻¹² W/m²); 50 Hz sampling; 20 ms bins; 100 seconds of data needed.

**Method:** Discriminant function analysis on spectral power densities from whole-body biophoton emission, classifying "human present" vs "empty chamber."

**Result:** 100% accurate discrimination of human presence/absence from photon-emission spectrum alone.

**The discriminant function (verbatim):**

> *"The combination of four (4) discriminant function coefficients significantly differentiated the chamber condition [Λ = 0.552, Χ²(4) = 41.561, p < 2.06 × 10⁻⁸] with the function given by the equation: Function = 0.066\*(3.93 Hz) − 0.052\*(11.7 Hz) − 0.064\*(15.86 Hz) + 0.062\*(19.08 Hz) − 0.208."*

**3.93 Hz is the largest-magnitude positive coefficient** in the four-frequency discriminant function. The four together explain 78.4% of classification variance.
