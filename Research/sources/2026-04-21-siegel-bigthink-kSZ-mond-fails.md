## What it reports

A **9.3σ detection of the pairwise kinematic Sunyaev-Zel'dovich (kSZ) effect** by cross-correlating 913,286 Luminous Red Galaxies from DESI DR1 with ACT DR6 and Planck CMB temperature maps. The pairwise kSZ statistic probes the large-scale velocity field of cosmic structure — a direct observable of gravitationally-induced peculiar velocities.

Key finding: the observed pairwise velocities **match ΛCDM + cold dark matter predictions** and **significantly deviate from MOND (Modified Newtonian Dynamics) predictions**. This is a new, independent empirical test of the dark-matter-vs.-modified-gravity alternatives to explain galactic dynamics, now extended to cosmic scales.

Framing by Siegel (Big Think): dark matter passes; MOND fails. The conventional ΛCDM + DM explanation of structure formation and velocity dynamics is reinforced. Gravity-modification alternatives are further constrained.
