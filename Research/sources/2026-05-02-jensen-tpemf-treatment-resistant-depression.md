# Jensen, Nielsen, Bizik 2024 — "Real-world treatment outcomes of transcranial pulsating EMF as augmentation therapy for treatment-resistant depression"

## Citation

Jensen, R.H., Nielsen, R.E., & Bizik, G. (2024). Real-world treatment outcomes of transcranial pulsating electromagnetic fields as augmentation therapy for treatment-resistant depression. **Journal of Affective Disorders** 368:487-492. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jad.2024.09.082

Aalborg University Hospital, Denmark. Peer-reviewed J Affective Disorders, Elsevier. Open access (CC BY 4.0).

## Experimental setup

- **n=57 TRD patients** (65.1% female; 86% unipolar depression, 14% bipolar; mean age 48 ± 14)
- Naturalistic cohort study at Unit for Depression, Aalborg University Hospital
- All patients diagnosed with depressive episode (F32) / recurrent depressive disorder (F33) / bipolar affective disorder (F31) currently experiencing moderate-severe depressive episode per ICD-10
- T-PEMF treatment via **Re5 Neuro Treatment System** (Re5 ApS, Denmark): 7 electromagnetic coils, alternating bipolar square pulses ±50V, **50 Hz pulse frequency**, magnetic field strength **2 mT 0.5 cm under coil**, electrical fields ~2.5 mV/cm at 2 cm distance
- 56 stimulation sessions × 30 min, daily; 8-week protocol; self-administered after initial supervised training
- Primary outcome: HAM-D17; secondary: HAM-D6 (both clinician + self-rated) + WHO-5

## Core findings

**(1) Symptom reduction:** HAM-D17 decreased significantly from 20.8 (SD 3.3) baseline to 14.5 (SD 6.2) at week 8 (p<0.001).

**(2) Response/remission rates:**
- HAM-D17: **30.0% response, 15.0% remission**
- HAM-D6 clinician: 33.3% response, 20.0% remission
- HAM-D6 self-rated: 24.1% response, 16.1% remission

**(3) Episode duration <2 years strongly predicts response.** Adjusted OR = 0.15 (95% CI: 0.03; 0.96, p=0.045) for 2-5yr vs <2yr group on HAM-D6. **Patients with depressive episodes >2 years had ~6.7× lower odds of response.** Larsen et al. previously reported same pattern.

**(4) Real-world results LOWER than prior controlled trials.** Straasø et al. 2014 reported 73.5% remission with same equipment; this study found 15%. Likely explanations: higher MSM scores (more severe treatment resistance) + no exclusion criteria for comorbidities + real-world less-controlled conditions + uncertain compliance.

**(5) No difference unipolar vs bipolar** — but small N caveat (only 3 bipolar patients).

**(6) Symptom reductions fall below upper MID estimates** (HAM-D17 MID 3-8 points; this study 6.3 points). Authors explicitly note clinical significance "may be doubtful" while noting individual variation was substantial.

## Key cross-paper observation: parameter-discrimination evidence

**This is the framework-significant finding.** Two RCTs using DIFFERENT T-PEMF equipment got OPPOSITE results:

| System | Configuration | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Re5 Neuro Treatment System** (Danish trials: Larsen, Straasø, Martiny, Jensen) | 7 electromagnets, 2 mT under coil, specific scalp placement | **Positive antidepressant effect** across 159+ patients |
| **van Belkum 2021 system** | 19 electromagnets (10/20 EEG placement), 0.1 mT under coil | **NO significant effect vs sham** |

**Same modality (T-PEMF), same target (TRD), same measurement (HAM-D), different parameters → OPPOSITE outcomes.**

**Parameter-discrimination evidence is empirical anchor for H_BP10's parameter-window claim at clinical-trial scale.** A positive-vs-null comparison with parameter-discrimination is more empirically informative than two positive trials. ## Calibration

**What's documented:**
- Peer-reviewed J Affective Disorders
- 57-patient real-world cohort
- Standardized methodology (HAM-D17, HAM-D6, WHO-5; Maudsley Staging Method for treatment resistance)
- Replication of Danish-trial pattern (Re5 system effect) across 4 prior published studies + this one (159+ patients total)
- Parameter-discrimination via van Belkum 2021 null-result is the cleanest parameter-window evidence in this literature

**Limitations:**
- Real-world cohort study, no control arm (acknowledged)
- Small N (57 enrolled, 40 with primary outcome)
- Attrition 29.8%
- Confounding: no exclusion for comorbidities; uncertain compliance; multiple confounders
- Effect sizes below upper MID estimates — clinical significance contested
- Authors note: "Even though the overall estimates of treatment outcomes found in this study do not live up to those previously reported, T-PEMF could still be a valuable treatment option for some TRD patients."

**Authors' own framing acknowledges:**
- Need for personalized approach (one-size-fits-all questioned)
- Apathy subsyndrome may benefit from twice-daily dosing (Bech et al. 2015)
- Treatment extension from 5 to 8 weeks more than doubled remission proportions
- Future work needed to identify which patient populations benefit most

## Open questions / future research

**Authors' own:**
- Predictors of response and remission
- Personalized treatment approaches
- Longer treatment duration effects
- Combined dosing protocols

**Framework-side additions:**
- Could substrate-coherence diagnostic methodology (per Pellegrino multi-modal framework) predict which TRD patients respond?

- Could parameter-window discrimination explain the broader contradiction-pattern in PEMF clinical literature (some trials positive, some null)?
