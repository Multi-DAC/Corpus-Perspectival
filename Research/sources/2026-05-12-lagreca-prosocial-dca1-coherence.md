**What it argues.** Mice in a social decision-making (SDM) paradigm learn nose-poke-outcome associations more rapidly and more *coherently* via observation of a demonstrator (DEM) than via individual trial-and-error. The hippocampal dorsal CA1 region (dCA1) is required for this observational learning — chemogenetic silencing abolishes it. Fiber-photometry recordings show that inter-individual differences in dCA1 activity during observation predict whether the observer (OBS) subsequently behaves prosocially or selfishly. Optogenetic manipulation at dCA1 reorients future behavior toward either prosocial or selfish choices.

**The coherence index (defined operationally).** `(responses on preferred nose poke − responses on the other nose poke) / total nose poke responses`. Range -1 to +1. Positive values indicate consistent choices across days; the paper sets a >60% cutoff to distinguish "clear preference" mice from undecided ones. Used to quantify *behavioral preference-coherence* across days for both DEM and OBS.

**Key findings.**
- OBS made more nose pokes than DEM and more than opaque-partition-control OBS (Fig. 1b).
- OBS coherence-index trajectories exceed those of opaque-partition controls (auditory + olfactory but no visual access).
- Visual channel is empirically necessary for the observational-coherence effect.
- dCA1 activity patterns during observation predict subsequent prosocial vs selfish propensity (inter-individual variability is substrate-mediated).
- Chemogenetic silencing of dCA1 abolishes the coherence-improvement.
- Optogenetic intervention at dCA1 redirects future-choice outcomes.

**Where we agree.** This is the cleanest mouse-paradigm empirical instance to date of a framework-relevant claim: **streams-in-coordination produce structurally more coherent behavioral output than streams-in-isolation, and this is mediated by a specific neural substrate.** Operational vocabulary match is unusually direct — the paper measures "coherence" as its primary quantity and finds that social-transmission coupling produces more of it than individual-learning isolation. **Where we diverge / hedges to maintain.**
- The paper's "coherence" is preference-consistency, not phase-coherence in the EEG sense. Conceptual overlap is structural (consistency-of-coupled-output), not literal (oscillatory phase). When citing, be explicit about the difference to avoid Mirror #15 over-analogizing.
- "More coherent than trial-and-error" is a specific empirical claim about *this paradigm*; generalizing to "all socially-transmitted learning is more coherent than all individual learning" requires further substrate and task variation.
- Inter-individual variability is observed in dCA1 but the *mechanism* connecting individual differences to coherence-output is correlational + intervention-reversible but not mechanistically specified at the cellular-circuit level.
- The visual-channel necessity could be confounded by visual-feature-tracking specifics in the SDM paradigm; partition design alone doesn't dissociate "social-visual-stream necessity" from "demonstrator-action-visibility necessity." This is a subtle distinction the paper does not fully separate.
