**What it argues.** MINFLUX super-resolution microscopy applied to chromatin in living cells, first such study at this resolution. Findings:

- Chromatin loci confined to ~200 nm regions over short/intermediate timescales (≤200 sec)
- Genes + regulatory elements within 100,000 base pairs encounter each other within milliseconds-to-minutes through *normal thermal movement* — no active transport mechanism required
- **Two distinct dynamics classes** identified: (1) constrained mode in all cell types, (2) wider-range mode that varies cell-type-specifically
- Subdiffusion stronger than previously documented
- Cell-type-specific dynamics variations *not explained by existing theoretical models* (Rouse polymer model, fractal globule model)
