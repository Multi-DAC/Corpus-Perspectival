**What it argues.** Spectral-power-density characterization of whole-body biophoton emissions from human subjects vs empty chamber, measured under controlled photometric conditions. Discriminative function reported:

> Function = 0.066·(**3.93 Hz**) − 0.052·(**11.7 Hz**) − 0.064·(**15.86 Hz**) + 0.062·(**19.08 Hz**) − 0.208

Strongest discriminative frequency is 3.93 Hz, sitting at the delta-theta boundary. The other three discriminative frequencies map onto canonical EEG bands: alpha (11.7), low-beta (15.86), beta (19.08).

Reports the discriminating energy value at approximately 2×10⁻²¹ J, "within the range of the Landauer Limit for the dissipation of energy from a single bit of information into entropy or the convergence of two quantum operations." Frames findings using "holographic-like properties" and "field or dynamic matrix whose configuration changes systematically in space-time (a tensor)."

Reports cancer-detection finding: SPD differences between melanoma-injected mice and reference mice detectable within 48 hours of injection — 10+ days before tumors are visibly discernable.

**Where we agree.** Whole-body biophoton emission temporal-fluctuation spectrum has dominant frequencies that overlap canonical EEG bands. The strongest discriminative frequency at 3.93 Hz sits in the delta-theta region. This is consistent with H_BP10b — the empirical observation that delta-range frequencies are biophoton-coupling-relevant in biological substrate.

The Landauer-limit observation is independently interesting: biophoton emission energy operates at the physical minimum required to erase one bit of information. The framework's H_BP1 (biophotons as inter-stream measurement-event closure carriers) predicted that biophotons should function as information-carrying substrate; Persinger 2016 reports the energy scale being at the information-theoretic minimum without invoking the framework.

The cancer-detection finding (SPD changes detectable 10 days before clinical signs) is consistent with H_BP12 (Coherent Body — biological substrate coherence as a useful organizing frame for health/disease tracking). Disease-state coherence-degradation appearing in spectral signatures pre-clinically is what H_BP12 would predict if it's pointing at something real.

The "holographic-like" + "field or dynamic matrix" framing is structurally compatible with the Coherence Principle's coherent-multi-scale-substrate vocabulary — they're describing what the framework would call coherent-multi-scale dynamics in different language.

- Per audit catch C1.4: Persinger's group is not testing the Coherence Principle. The framework recognizes their findings as instances of structures it predicts; this is post-hoc structural recognition, not framework-confirmation by Persinger.
- Single laboratory lineage. Persinger's group at Laurentian has produced the bulk of whole-body biophoton SPD work. Independent replication at other institutions with different equipment would strengthen the load-bearing claims significantly. The framework should treat Persinger-lineage findings as a single research substrate, not as independently-replicated consensus.
- "Holographic-like" vocabulary is suggestive but not load-bearing. Persinger uses it descriptively; it does not establish that biophoton fields actually have holographic information-encoding properties in the technical sense.
- The 10-days-pre-clinical cancer detection finding has not been independently replicated as far as I can confirm; treat as candidate-finding pending replication.
- Energy at the Landauer Limit is consistent with information-carrying-substrate readings but does not establish the substrate. Many physical processes operate near the Landauer Limit without being information-substrate carriers in any meaningful sense.
