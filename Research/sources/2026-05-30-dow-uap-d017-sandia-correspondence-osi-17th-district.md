## The two substantive findings surfaced from the first-pass

### Sighting No. 175 — Datil, NM, 24-25 February 1950 (page 61)

Photograph of unknown aerial phenomena taken at Datil, NM by Cpl Lewis E. Stanfield, Holloman Air Force Base. **Scientifically analyzed by Dr. Lincoln LaPaz**, Head of the Institute of Meteoritics, UNM, who concluded:

- Angular diameter of the perfectly round luminous object: approximately 1/4 of a degree
- Angular velocity of the object: greater than half a degree per minute
- LaPaz's discriminations:
  - **NOT the moon** (the angular diameter is too small)
  - **NOT Venus or any other planet** (the angular diameter is too large)
  - **NOT a bright fixed star slightly out of focus** (the observed rate of motion is *double* that due to the diurnal rotation of the earth)

This is a published-grade astrophysical analysis of a UAP photograph by a serious astronomer-scientist (LaPaz was UNM's Director of the Institute of Meteoritics and a recognized authority on meteor astronomy). The analysis is in primary government correspondence as part of the OSI catalog.

### Incident of 8 March 1949 — Los Alamos (page 84)

Reported by an AEC Security Inspector on duty at Los Alamos. Object description:

- "Elliptical, rather stubby, aluminum body surrounded or covered with billowy flame"
- "There may have been projections similar to stubby control surfaces and wings, but the observer could not be sure of this"
- Looked "rather like the part of a wartime German airplane from the cockpit aft"
- **No noise**
- Slightly descending path
- Disappeared behind clouds

This is an AEC-credentialed eyewitness sighting at Los Alamos in primary correspondence. The "wartime German airplane" comparison is the observer's analogical reach — wing-fuselage geometry not standard for known 1949 aircraft.

### The LaPaz thread across the document

Dr. Lincoln LaPaz appears at multiple touchpoints:
- Page 4 (August 1949 letter): receiving 18 collections of particle samples from "Tuesday trip" to Highway 84/66 — copper-bearing particles found
- Page 61 (Feb 1950): scientific analysis of Stanfield's UAP photograph

**LaPaz was functioning as the de facto scientific consultant for unusual aerial / particle phenomena in the New Mexico Sandia/Los Alamos area during 1948-1950.** This is a substantial historical-scientific finding worth tracing further in deep-read.
