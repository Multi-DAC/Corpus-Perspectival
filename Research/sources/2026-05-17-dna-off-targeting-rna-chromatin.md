## Substantive content (from secondary + primary abstract — verify against full primary)

**Methodological crisis identified:** A multi-institutional meta-analysis + empirical study reveals that three of the most widely-used methodologies for profiling long non-coding RNA (lncRNA) interactions with chromatin — ChIRP-seq, CHART-seq, and RAP-seq — all suffer from massive, widespread *DNA off-targeting*. The synthetic oligonucleotide probes, designed to hybridize specifically with target RNA, frequently and mistakenly bind directly to *genomic DNA* sequences sharing only partial sequence homology.

**Empirical consequence:** Because sequencing readouts cannot distinguish "probe bound to RNA" from "probe bound directly to chromatin," thousands of reported RNA-chromatin interaction sites in existing peer-reviewed literature are likely *false positives*. The artifact systematically confounds studies attempting to deduce the regulatory roles of the NESPR lncRNA chromatin interactome and dozens of other heavily studied targets.

**Methodological remedy:** RNase pretreatment controls to explicitly subtract DNA-dependent background noise from sequencing data. Future studies require this orthogonal validation to ensure the map of RNA-chromatin interactions corresponds to physical reality rather than chemical artifact.
