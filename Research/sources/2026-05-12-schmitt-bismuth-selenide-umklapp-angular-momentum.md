**What it argues.** Using terahertz laser pulses on bismuth selenide (BiSe) crystals, the team directly observed angular-momentum transfer between two lattice-vibration modes. During the transition, the *direction* of angular momentum reverses. The reversal is forced by the crystal's rotational symmetry — formally an Umklapp process, summarized colloquially as "1+1 = −1": two combined angular momenta produce rotation at twice the frequency but in the opposite direction.

**Key findings.**
- Direct experimental observation of inter-mode angular-momentum transfer at the phonon scale.
- Symmetry of the substrate forces sign-reversal at the Umklapp boundary.
- Bridges the Einstein–de Haas century-old question linking magnetic and mechanical rotation in a solid.
