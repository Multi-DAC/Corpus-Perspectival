**What it argues.** First experimental signature of an η′-mesic nucleus — a bound system in which a neutral η′ meson is held to an atomic nucleus (¹¹C) by the residual strong force alone, analogous to how electrons orbit atoms electromagnetically but with the strong interaction as the binding mechanism. Proton beam on ¹²C ejected neutrons and produced excited ¹¹C states; a novel tagging method discriminated the η′-mesic signal from background ~100-1000× larger. The η′-meson effective mass dropped approximately 60 MeV inside nuclear matter relative to vacuum — supporting theoretical predictions about chiral-symmetry-breaking behavior in QCD at finite density. Tanaka (RIKEN): *"Spectroscopy studies of η′-mesic nuclei provide direct experimental access to the η′-meson mass in nuclei."*
