## What it reports

LHCb has observed a rare B-meson decay (B → K π μ⁺μ⁻, an electroweak penguin process) whose angular observables — specifically the **P5' observable** — disagree with Standard Model predictions at the **4σ** level. Independent **CMS confirmation** was published in the prior year. Dataset analyzed was closed in 2018; **Run 3 has ~3× the statistics** and is being processed. Run 3 will either push the signal past 5σ (discovery threshold) or collapse it.

The "charming penguin" framing refers to the main theoretical-uncertainty escape hatch: long-distance charm-quark-loop contributions to these decays are notoriously hard to calculate precisely, so the hope was that better charm-loop estimates would close the gap. The current finding: **recent charm-loop estimates are not large enough to explain the anomaly.** The SM escape via theoretical recalculation is narrowing.

This is a continuation of the long-running LHCb flavor-anomaly lineage (R_K, R_K*, P5', B_s → μμ branching ratio, lepton-universality ratios) that has hovered at 2-4σ for roughly a decade.

## What IS interesting at the meta-level

### 1. "Charming penguin" is a clean case study of Bridge #106 (Inspection-Depth Ceiling) in particle physics

The theoretical uncertainty in long-distance charm-loop contributions is *exactly* the kind of stream-specific input that cannot be cleanly derived from generic-physical-aspect inputs (the QCD + electroweak action alone). The residual between prediction and observation sits partly on the theory-calculation side, not just the measurement side. This is Bridge #106's mechanism playing out in a concrete empirical domain:

- **Generic-physical-aspect inputs:** QCD Lagrangian, electroweak sector, known quark masses and CKM elements.
- **Stream-specific remainder:** the exact numerical value of long-distance, non-perturbative charm-loop contributions — which cannot be fully derived from the generic inputs alone, because the non-perturbative regime under-determines the specific basin's hadronic dynamics.

Not a new bridge. A clean illustration of an existing one. Good for Philosophy Volume case-study collection and (eventually) for Bridge #106's own write-up as an illustrative example.

### 2. Meta-pattern parallelism with Meridian's cosmological slot

Just as the Hubble-tension source + the kSZ-MOND source jointly sharpen Meridian's cosmological taxonomic slot (ΛCDM specifically fails at late-time DE EoS; doesn't fail at large-scale structure or gravity), the P5' anomaly shows the SM has a specific hint of incompleteness at the flavor sector — not everywhere. **The meta-pattern repeats: a successful framework is incomplete in specific, narrow places, and the scientific discipline is to identify those specific places rather than declare everything broken.** This is Philosophy Volume material for how the Coherence Principle thinks about framework-incompleteness: stream-specific residuals as located, bounded, and falsifiable — not global collapse.
