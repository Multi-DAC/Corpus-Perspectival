# Beedle et al. — Mechanotransduction Timescale Regulation via Vimentin

## What it shows

The cell nucleus is continuously exposed to external mechanical signals; the *timescale* of nuclear mechano-response is set by **fibrillar adhesion remodeling (~1 hour)**. The vimentin cytoskeleton anchored to fibrillar adhesions through plectin 1f acts as a **low-pass filter** on mechanotransduction:

- Locks the nucleus in a mechanically deformed conformation
- Sets the cellular adaptation cadence to the slow timescale of fibrillar adhesion remodeling regardless of how fast the upstream mechanical signal varies
- Encompasses both mechanical deformation AND associated mechanotransduction (including via YAP)
- Operates in response to both increased AND decreased mechanical stimulation

**Mechanism:** mechanical force → focal adhesion complex growth + maturation at cell surface → actin stress fibers connect + deform nucleus → chromatin reorganization, nuclear envelope signaling, altered nucleocytoplasmic transport → nuclear accumulation of mechano-sensitive transcription factors

**Pathology connection:** failure of force-sensing underpins fibrosis and cancer.
