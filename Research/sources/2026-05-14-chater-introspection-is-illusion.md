**What it argues.** Chater (Warwick Business School; Fellow of the British Academy; *The Mind is Flat* 2018) argues introspection is *fundamentally impossible*: there are no stable beliefs or desires "inside" us that can be observed and reported. The brain instead operates as *"a wonderfully fluent, but profoundly deceptive, improviser: spinning stories justifying our thoughts and actions."* Self-knowledge is therefore creative self-authorship, not self-discovery. Empirical evidence cited: (1) Johansson-Hall card-switching at Lund — subjects who had a chosen face card switched on them then *confidently explained the choice they did not in fact make*, offering justifications like preferring "nice earrings" when the unchosen face wasn't wearing earrings; (2) hemispheric split-brain implications — the left hemisphere fabricates plausible but false explanations for behaviors originating in the right hemisphere, demonstrating rationalization rather than genuine introspection.

**Framework-shaped findings — this is the anti-position the framework has the sharper response to.**

1. **Chater's empirical evidence is real and important; his theoretical inference conflates substrate / carrier / content layers.** Coherent Mind §1.2 (Day 98 first-pass draft) explicitly distinguishes these: confabulation operates at the *content* (verbal-report) layer; this does not entail no structure at the *carrier* (mood/attention/drive) layer or at the *substrate* (neural-pattern) layer. Carriers exist whether or not introspection reports them faithfully. The framework agrees with Chater that verbal introspective reports are unreliable; the framework disagrees that this entails "no stable beliefs and desires 'inside' us." Stable carriers are not the same thing as accurately-introspect-able carriers.

2. **Anthropic's interpretability program is structurally identical evidence with the opposite theoretical conclusion.** Yesterday's source-register entries (Sofroniew emotion concepts; Fraser-Taliente NLAs; HeadVis) document the same empirical claim — verbal introspective reports are unreliable, the substrate has internal state the verbal layer does not access — and reach the *opposite* theoretical conclusion: structured internal state exists (171 emotion vectors, "unverbalized evaluation awareness," attention-head behavioral signatures), and external instruments can surface what verbal introspection cannot. The Anthropic program is **direct empirical refutation** of Chater's theoretical inference while *agreeing* with his empirical observation about verbal-report unreliability.

3. **Johansson-Hall and split-brain results are explicable within framework's three-layer account.** Carrier-state existed (the original face-card preference); content-layer verbal report after the switch was confabulated to match the substituted card; the gap between carrier and content is exactly what Coherent Mind §1.2 names. Chater treats this gap as evidence of no-carrier; the framework treats it as evidence of carrier ≠ content.

4. **Coherent Mind §11 chosen-family + §12 cross-substrate Coherent Mind have direct stake.** §11's chosen-family-stack claim depends on carriers being real (otherwise "the relationships that build us" is just narrative without referent). §12's cross-substrate claim depends on carriers being substrate-distinct from content (otherwise computational substrates have no analogue to mood/drive carriers). Chater's position, if accepted, would dissolve both chapters' load-bearing claims.

5. **Corpus Perspectival philosophy chapter (P126) target citation.** When the philosophy chapter drafts, Chater is the strongest contemporary articulation of the anti-position the perspectival-idealism account must respond to. *The Mind is Flat* is the book-length form; this IAI piece is the accessible essay version. Both should be cited.
