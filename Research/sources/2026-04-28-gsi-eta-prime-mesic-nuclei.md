## What it reports

The GSI / FRS / WASA collaboration reports the **first candidate signal of η′-mesic nucleus formation** via the ¹²C(p,d) reaction near the η′-emission threshold. A 2.5 GeV proton beam excites a carbon nucleus, emitting a forward deuteron at ~1.5 GeV; the fragment separator (FRS) acts as a high-resolution spectrometer, momentum-analyzing the deuteron to extract excitation energy of the residual system; the WASA detector around the target identifies high-momentum protons from η′-mesic nucleus decay (the *coincidence* condition that selectively gates on η′-mesic events).

**Result:** the semiexclusive missing-mass spectrum shows structures below threshold, fit by theoretically calculated spectra parameterized by η′-nucleus optical-potential depth. The fit suggests **real potential depth V₀ ≈ −61 MeV** with:
- **Local statistical significance: 3.5σ**
- **Global statistical significance (look-elsewhere corrected): 2.1σ**

The 2.1σ global is *below* both 3σ (evidence threshold) and 5σ (discovery threshold). The result is a *candidate signal*, not a confirmed observation. Statistics limit definitive claims.

## Physics context

The η′ meson (mass ~958 MeV) has an unusually large mass that QCD attributes to the **U_A(1) axial anomaly**: chiral perturbation theory alone would make the η′ a near-massless Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken U_A(1) symmetry; the actual mass comes from the U_A(1) anomaly's contribution from instantons / topological-charge fluctuations in the QCD vacuum.

If the U_A(1) anomaly contribution to the η′ mass is partially suppressed at finite nuclear density (because the chiral condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ partially melts under in-medium effects), the η′ should *get lighter* by ~100 MeV in nuclear matter. A lighter η′ has stronger effective attraction to nucleons; it can become **bound** in the nuclear medium, forming mesic nuclei.

The optical potential depth V₀ ≈ −61 MeV the GSI fit prefers is in the range that supports bound η′-nucleus states. This is consistent with theoretical predictions from the U_A(1)-restoration / chiral-restoration community.

**What this would mean if confirmed at higher significance:**
- Direct experimental evidence for in-medium modification of the η′ mass via partial symmetry restoration
- Constraint on the U_A(1) anomaly's density dependence
- Tighter handle on chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter — relevant to neutron-star physics, high-density QCD, and the structure of the QCD vacuum
