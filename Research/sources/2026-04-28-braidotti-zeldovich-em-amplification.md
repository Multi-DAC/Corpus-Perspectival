**What it argues.** Experimental confirmation of Zel'dovich's 1971 prediction that electromagnetic waves scattered by a rotating absorbing cylinder can be amplified, gaining energy from the cylinder's mechanical rotational kinetic energy. The Zel'dovich condition: ω - ℓΩ < 0, where ω is wave frequency, ℓ is angular momentum quantum number, Ω is cylinder rotation frequency. When this condition is satisfied (rotation faster than wave frequency in the relevant frame), the absorption coefficient changes sign and the medium loses energy to outgoing waves.

For 53 years considered unobservable due to technological difficulty (rotation rates required were too high for practical devices). The authors achieve it by working at low RF frequencies in a specialized configuration: aluminum cylinder rotating in the gap of a toroidal LC circuit. They measure a **negative resistance** induced by the cylinder when the Zel'dovich condition is met — the experimental signature of EM amplification at the expense of cylinder rotational energy.
