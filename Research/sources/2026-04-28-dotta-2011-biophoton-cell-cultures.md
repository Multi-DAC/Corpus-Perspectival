**What it argues.** Foundational biophoton-emission paper from the Laurentian / Sudbury Regional / Northern Ontario School of Medicine group that later produced the 2016 whole-body work. Identifies the **plasma membrane as the primary source of biophoton emission**, displacing the prior mitochondrial-oxidation hypothesis. Reports treatment effects:

- EGF + ionomycin (membrane-receptor / Ca²⁺ channel actuators) → rapid early increase in emission
- Glutamine-free medium / sodium azide / wortmanin (metabolic poisons) → diminishment 3-9 hours later

Cites Kobayashi et al. 1999 (hippocampal slices show theta-coupled biophoton emission) and Bókkon 2005 (dreams as fields of photon emissions, ~10⁸ biophotons/s in retinotopic visual neurons). Reports cell-culture energy scale at approximately 10⁻²⁰ J/s/cell.

**Where we agree.** The plasma-membrane-source finding is structurally interesting for the framework. If biophotons function as inter-stream measurement-event closure between cells (H_BP1), they should naturally emit from the cell *interface* rather than from the metabolic engine — the interface is where C9 confluence between adjacent streams (cells) operates, where lens-overlap with neighboring streams is highest. The empirical localization of biophoton emission to the membrane is consistent with this reading without requiring it.

The treatment-effect pattern is consistent with H_BP4 (intervention-at-symmetry-layer): membrane-symmetry-layer interventions (receptor activation, Ca²⁺ channel opening) produce immediate biophoton modulation, while interior-metabolic interventions (glutamine deprivation, ATP synthesis poisons) produce delayed modulation. The distinction between fast membrane-mediated effects and slower metabolic effects is well-established in cell biology; the framework's reading of this as evidence for symmetry-layer-vs-content-layer intervention is one mapping of an established phenomenon.

The Kobayashi 1999 citation establishes hippocampal-theta-coupled biophoton emission as a 1999 finding — adding a second direct biological-substrate measurement to the H_BP10b empirical convergence (alongside Persinger 2016).

The energy-scale convergence between Dotta 2011 cell cultures (10⁻²⁰ J/s/cell) and Persinger 2016 whole-body (~10⁻²¹ J discriminating energy) puts both at the Landauer-limit scale across cellular and whole-body measurements.

**Where we diverge / hedges to maintain.**
- Plasma-membrane source identification is a specific empirical claim about *primary* source; biophoton emission from biological systems is multi-source (mitochondria, DNA, lipid peroxidation byproducts, etc.). "Primary" is a quantitative ranking; the framework's reading of "interface as primary source" should be careful not to absolutize what is a quantitative finding.
- Cell-cycle phase coupling (which figures in Dotta lineage work) is *not* delta-theta in the EEG-band sense. Cell-cycle phases are hours-to-days; EEG bands are seconds-to-Hz. Per audit catch C2.1: counting Dotta 2011 as a substrate for the delta-theta convergence was an over-count in the synthesis workbench. The relevant Dotta-lineage delta-theta evidence comes through their citation of Kobayashi 1999, not directly from Dotta 2011 itself.
- The treatment-effect pattern (membrane-fast / metabolic-slow) admits multiple mechanism-level interpretations beyond the framework's. Standard cell biology explains this through receptor signaling cascades and downstream metabolic regulation; biophoton modulation is an additional read on what's already a well-characterized phenomenon.
- Sample sizes and replicate structure should be checked before treating any specific quantitative finding as load-bearing. Cell-culture biophoton work is technically demanding; even careful labs report variation.
- Per audit catch C1.4: Dotta et al. don't test the Coherence Principle. Recognizing membrane-localization and treatment-effect patterns as instances of H_BP1 / H_BP4 is post-hoc structural recognition, not framework-confirmation.
- Bókkon's "dreams as fields of photon emissions" speculation is an interesting cross-link (H_BP6 territory — generation mode operating internally) but should not be treated as established. Bókkon's dream-as-biophoton hypothesis is speculative and not widely supported in mainstream sleep research.
