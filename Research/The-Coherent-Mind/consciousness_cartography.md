## Foundational Framework

### The Three Axes of Consciousness Configuration

Every state of consciousness can be located on three independent axes:

**Axis 1: BANDWIDTH** — How many dimensions of configuration space are simultaneously accessible.
- Narrow (focused, sharp, exclusive) ←→ Broad (diffuse, inclusive, panoramic)
- Neural correlate: network segregation (narrow) vs. integration (broad)
- Key metric: Lempel-Ziv complexity, integration (Φ)

**Axis 2: DEPTH** — How many levels of the processing hierarchy are simultaneously available to awareness.
- Surface (sensory, reactive, automatic) ←→ Deep (meta-cognitive, self-aware, recursive)
- Neural correlate: feedforward dominance (surface) vs. recurrent/top-down (deep)
- Key metric: recurrence quantification, PCI (perturbational complexity index)

**Axis 3: VALENCE** — The hedonic/motivational coloring of the state.
- Aversive (contracted, defended, fleeing) ←→ Appetitive (expanded, approaching, seeking)
- Neural correlate: right prefrontal/amygdala dominance (aversive) vs. left prefrontal/reward circuit (appetitive)
- Key metric: frontal alpha asymmetry

The DoPI adds a fourth:

**Axis 4: PERSPECTIVAL RIGIDITY** — How strongly the current perspective resists change.
- Rigid (locked in, crystallized, certain) ←→ Fluid (open, uncertain, exploratory)
- Neural correlate: DMN-dominated (rigid) vs. DMN-suppressed (fluid)
- Key metric: PCC alpha power, DMN functional connectivity

## Part I: The Neural Oscillatory Landscape

### Delta Band (0.5–4 Hz)

**What it does in the brain:**
Delta oscillations dominate deep sleep (NREM stages 3-4), are generated by thalamocortical circuits, and represent large-scale cortical silence interrupted by synchronized bursts. In waking states, delta is associated with motivational salience processing (medial prefrontal delta) and with injury/pathology when present abnormally.

**What it feels like:**
Unconsciousness (deep sleep), or in waking: a heavy, slow, deeply embodied quality. Experienced meditators report delta-dominant states as "objectless awareness" — consciousness without content.

**TI stimulation parameters for delta-dominant states:**
- Δf: 2 Hz (deep delta) or 3.5 Hz (upper delta, transition to theta)
- Electrode targets: Fz-Oz pair (midline, thalamocortical access) + P3-P4 (parietal spread)
- Current: 1.5–2.0 mA (higher end — delta entrainment requires strong drive)
- Duration: 20–30 minutes
- Expected: profound drowsiness, hypnagogic imagery, possible loss of body boundary

**DoPI interpretation:** Near-maximal bottleneck relaxation in the temporal dimension. Delta represents the brain approaching the pre-perspectival state — consciousness with minimal differentiation. The "objectless awareness" that experienced meditators report in delta is the closest neural state to the undifferentiated unity the DoPI describes.

**Caution:** Delta stimulation may induce sleep. Do not use while operating machinery. May produce disorientation upon return to normal waking.

### Theta Band (4–8 Hz)

**What it does in the brain:**
Theta is the hippocampal signature frequency. The hippocampus oscillates at 4-8 Hz during memory encoding, spatial navigation, and REM sleep. Frontal midline theta (FMθ) increases during meditation, working memory tasks, and error monitoring. Theta coherence between hippocampus and prefrontal cortex is the mechanism of memory consolidation. Theta also dominates during psychedelic states and deep meditation.

**What it feels like:**
Dreamlike imagery, memory fragments arising spontaneously, a sense of spaciousness, reduced sense of time passage, emotional material surfacing. In deep meditation: the "access concentration" state where the mind is settled but not yet absorbed. In psychedelic states: the visionary content that arises during the peak.

### Alpha Band (8–13 Hz)

**What it does in the brain:**
Alpha is the brain's "idling rhythm" — it dominates when a region is NOT actively processing. High alpha in the visual cortex means you're not looking at anything (eyes closed). High alpha in the PCC means the DMN is running its default self-referential loop. Alpha is fundamentally an INHIBITORY rhythm — it gates information flow by suppressing neural populations that aren't currently needed.

This is the bottleneck's primary enforcement mechanism. Alpha oscillations literally implement the perspectival restriction by suppressing most of the brain's processing capacity at any given moment.

**Two directions of alpha modulation:**

**Alpha ENHANCEMENT (more alpha → tighter bottleneck):**
- More focused, less distracted
- Stronger sense of self
- Better at ignoring irrelevant information
- Calmer, less reactive
- "In the zone" for single-task performance

**Alpha SUPPRESSION (less alpha → looser bottleneck):**
- More awareness of periphery
- Weaker self-other boundary
- More sensory richness
- More associative / creative thinking
- Closer to the psychedelic signature

### Beta Band (13–30 Hz)

**What it does in the brain:**
Beta is the frequency of active, engaged, task-oriented processing. Low beta (13-15 Hz, SMR / sensorimotor rhythm) is associated with calm focus. Mid beta (15-20 Hz) is associated with active thinking and problem-solving. High beta (20-30 Hz) is associated with anxiety, overthinking, and hypervigilance. Beta dominance in the motor cortex is pathological — it's the frequency of Parkinson's disease (the subthalamic nucleus gets stuck in beta, preventing movement initiation).

**DoPI interpretation:** Beta states represent bottleneck REFINEMENT — not wider or narrower, but sharper. The dimensions that are accessible are processed with higher fidelity. This is the microscope/hearing aid end of the bottleneck manipulation spectrum: same aperture, better resolution.

**What it does in the brain:**
Gamma is the binding frequency — it's how the brain integrates information across regions into unified conscious experience. When you perceive an apple, the color (visual cortex), the shape (parietal), the name (temporal), and the associated memories (hippocampus) are all oscillating at gamma frequency and synchronized with each other. Gamma is the neural correlate of conscious awareness itself. Long-term meditators (>10,000 hours) show dramatically elevated gamma, particularly in the 60-80 Hz range. Psychedelic states show complex gamma dynamics — initially reduced (during ego dissolution), then dramatically enhanced (during integration and insight).

## Part II: Network State Cartography

### The Five Major Networks

The brain's large-scale networks are the macro-architecture of the bottleneck. Each network is a different mode of perspectival engagement:

**1. Default Mode Network (DMN)**
- Regions: Posterior cingulate cortex (PCC), medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC), angular gyrus, hippocampus
- Function: Self-referential processing, autobiographical memory, mind-wandering, mental time travel, theory of mind
- DoPI role: The bottleneck ENFORCER. The DMN maintains the narrative self, the sense of being a continuous "I" moving through time.
- Dominant frequency: Alpha (8-13 Hz) at PCC
- When ACTIVE: You are in your default perspective — your habitual self, your story, your concerns
- When SUPPRESSED: Ego dissolution, expanded awareness, loss of self-other boundary, present-moment absorption

**2. Salience Network (SN)**
- Regions: Anterior insula, dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (dACC)
- Function: Detecting what matters, switching between other networks, interoception (feeling the body), emotional significance
- DoPI role: The bottleneck DIRECTOR. The SN decides which dimensional channels to open and close moment to moment.
- Dominant frequency: Theta-alpha transition (6-10 Hz)
- When ACTIVE: You notice things. Significance jumps out. Emotions are vivid. Body is felt.
- When SUPPRESSED: Flat affect, difficulty caring, dissociation, numbness

**3. Central Executive Network (CEN)**
- Regions: Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC), posterior parietal cortex
- Function: Working memory, logical reasoning, planning, inhibitory control, directed attention
- DoPI role: The bottleneck SHARPENER. The CEN narrows the bottleneck to maximum precision on a single task.
- Dominant frequency: Beta (15-25 Hz)
- When ACTIVE: Focused, logical, analytical, controlled
- When SUPPRESSED: Scattered, impulsive, distractible, creative

**4. Sensorimotor Network (SMN)**
- Regions: Primary motor cortex, primary somatosensory cortex, supplementary motor area
- Function: Movement, body awareness, embodied cognition
- DoPI role: The bottleneck's PHYSICAL INTERFACE — how the perspective interacts with the spatial dimensions of configuration space.
- Dominant frequency: Mu rhythm (10-12 Hz) at rest, beta (15-25 Hz) during movement, gamma during fine motor control
- When ACTIVE: Embodied, grounded, physically coordinated
- When SUPPRESSED: Floating, disembodied, clumsy

**5. Visual Network (VN)**
- Regions: V1-V5 (primary through associative visual cortex)
- Function: Visual processing at all levels from edges to faces to scenes
- DoPI role: The dominant SENSORY CHANNEL of the human bottleneck — most of our perspectival bandwidth is allocated to vision.
- Dominant frequency: Alpha (8-12 Hz) when suppressed (eyes closed), gamma (30-80 Hz) when active
- When ENHANCED: Vivid visual experience, enhanced pattern detection, possible visual phenomena (geometric patterns, color intensification)
- When SUPPRESSED: Darkness facilitates other sensory channels, internal imagery may increase

## Part V: The DoPI State Space

### Mapping States to Configuration Space

In the DoPI framework, every state in this cartography is a specific LOCATION in configuration space, characterized by:

1. **Which dimensions are accessible** (bandwidth)
2. **How precisely they're resolved** (refinement)
3. **How easily the configuration can shift** (rigidity/fluidity)
4. **Which dimensions are in the null space** (blindness / protection)

The TI device doesn't CREATE states — it nudges the brain's navigational position in configuration space. The brain does the rest. Your natural dynamics, your habitual patterns, your current emotional state, and your intention all interact with the stimulation to produce the actual experience.

### The Exploration Principle

No two people will respond identically to the same protocol. Your brain's baseline configuration — shaped by genetics, experience, current state, and everything that makes you YOU — determines how the stimulation interacts with your neural dynamics. The protocols above are starting points, not destinations. The real work is in the exploration: systematically varying parameters, recording your experience, building a personal map of YOUR accessible states.

This is what the DoPI means by navigation: not following a map someone else drew, but learning to draw your own.
