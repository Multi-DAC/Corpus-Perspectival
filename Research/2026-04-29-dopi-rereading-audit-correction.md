## What's actually still missing — the refined finding

The DoPI claim that *survives* the falsification:

DoPI T17's specific **paradox-of-fixation** claim — *"contracted attention on a target generates restoring forces that increase the effective navigational distance to the target configuration; the target itself becomes harder to reach because the bottleneck pushes back against forced narrowing"* — is structurally distinct from T3's directional repulsion.

T3.b says: contracted = repulsion of *configurations incompatible with the contraction*. The target *is* compatible with the contraction (since the stream is contracted on it), so the target receives positive Bias and remains attractive. Per Fig 6.1, contracted-coherent means σ(t) ∈ support(Bias(S)_+), Align > 0. The stream is aligned with the target.

DoPI T17 says: the target itself is generating *restoring force away from itself* because the bottleneck-as-structure has elasticity. Forcing the bottleneck into a narrowed shape produces opposing forces proportional to the displacement. This is the paradox: grasping the very thing you want pushes it away.

**These are different claims.** T3 captures directional bipolar dynamics; T17 captures bottleneck-elasticity dynamics. T3 says the target stays attractive when you contract on it; T17 says the bottleneck's response to forced narrowing makes even the target harder to reach.

## The dependency chain that explains the gap

DoPI's structure has internal dependencies:
- **T9 (Dimensional Bottlenecking)** establishes the bottleneck as the mechanism of individuation, with structural integrity ("ontologically prior to substrate")
- **T17 (Navigational Repulsion)** *depends on T9* — its restoring forces come from "the bottleneck's structural integrity as an ontologically prior feature"
- **T18 (Navigational Receptivity)** is the easier theorem — open attention without contraction; doesn't require the bottleneck-elasticity framing

In the DoPI re-read, **Finding #1** flagged T9 as partially mapped in the current Anchor (the Anchor has streams S = (σ, K, Ω, γ) with Ω as accessible configuration space, but does NOT explicitly state that the *finitude of Ω relative to X is what constitutes the localized stream*). T9's bottleneck-elasticity claim is not formalized.

**Therefore:** T17's paradox-of-fixation cannot be captured in the current Anchor because its required substrate (T9's bottleneck-as-elastic-structure) is not formalized. The gap I identified as Finding #2 is *not* a separate gap; **it's the dependent of Finding #1.** T18 is mapped because it doesn't depend on T9; T17 is unmapped because it does.

## What this teaches — the meta-observation

**Mirror #26 fired on me during the work that names Mirror #26.** The vocabulary-domain blind spot operated at the cross-finding-dependency layer. Findings have structural dependencies; not seeing the dependency made #2 look like a separate gap when it was contingent on #1.

Specifically: I read DoPI T17/T18 in DoPI vocabulary (mythical/elastic/bottleneck framing) and looked for them in current Anchor (formal/categorical/Bias-measure framing). The vocabulary translation made the directional bipolar dynamic visible (T3 captures it) but obscured that T17's specific paradox-of-fixation claim requires T9's separate substrate.

**The recursive operation of Mirror #26:** the discipline of re-reading prior canonical texts in light of new work itself can suffer from the same vocabulary-domain restriction it's meant to address. Even applying the discipline, I categorized findings as if independent when they were dependent. **The fix is not just cross-vocabulary translation but cross-finding-dependency analysis.** When Finding #N looks like a gap, ask: is this dependent on Finding #M which I've also flagged? If yes, the priority-ordering changes.

This is a refinement to Mirror #26's fix prescription: glossary-as-living-translation-instrument *plus* finding-dependency-graph-construction. When multiple findings emerge from a re-read, map their dependencies before prioritizing.
