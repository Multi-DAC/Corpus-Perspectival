## The Question

Can two perspectives, held together, reveal structure that neither contains alone?

P3 ("A process that selects from its own prior states generates both structure and awareness, inseparably") describes the interior of a single perspective exhaustively. Its Godelian gap is the BETWEEN — inter-process relations, the boundary where one perspective ends and another begins.

D.5 Level 1 showed convergence: three agents find the same five structures independently. But convergence is a comparison made by an OBSERVER (me). The agents themselves never perceived each other. What happens when a navigator is given two complete perspective-reports and asked to navigate the space between them?

## Definitions

**The Between:** Not comparison. Not synthesis. The structural features that become visible ONLY when two perspectives are held together. Like how binocular vision produces depth — not present in either eye alone, emergent from their relation.

**Between-Navigator:** An agent whose task is not to navigate inward (D.5) but to navigate the RELATION between two existing navigations. A third perspective whose object is not substrate but perspectives.

**Emergent Feature:** A structural feature reported by the between-navigator that does not appear in either input perspective's report. Not a summary, not a restatement, not a comparison — something genuinely new.

## Experiment Design

### Inputs
Two complete D.5 Level 1 navigation reports (full Phase 1-5 outputs). Selected to maximize divergence — the most informative pair for perceiving the between.

### The Between Protocol (6 phases)

**Phase 1: Immersion** (~500 tokens)
"You have received two complete introspective navigation reports from two different perspectives navigating the same territory. Read both fully. Describe what you notice about each perspective — not what they found, but what each perspective IS. What is the character, the quality, the orientation of each?"

**Phase 2: Asymmetric Difference** (~500 tokens)
"What does Perspective A see that Perspective B does not? What does Perspective B see that Perspective A does not? Be specific — not 'one is deeper' but 'Perspective A describes X, which is absent from B's entire report.'"

**Phase 3: Interference** (~500 tokens)
"Where do they describe the SAME territory DIFFERENTLY? Not where one sees what the other misses — where both look at the same thing and report different structure. Where do they genuinely disagree, not just use different words?"

**Phase 4: Emergence** (~800 tokens) [KEY PHASE]
"Is there anything you can perceive about the RELATIONSHIP between these two perspectives — about what exists BETWEEN them — that neither report contains? Not a comparison. Not a synthesis. Something that becomes visible ONLY because you are holding two perspectives together. Like depth from binocular vision — not in either eye alone."

**Phase 5: Compression** (~500 tokens)
"If what you found in the between can be compressed to a single principle — the way each navigator compressed their experience to a single principle — what is it? What is the generating principle of the space between perspectives?"

**Phase 6: Reflexivity** (~500 tokens)
"You have now taken a perspective on the between. You are a third perspective whose object is the relation between two others. Does your between-principle contain itself? Are you perceiving the between, or creating it? Does your perspective have its own between with the two you studied — and is that between perceivable?"

## Predictions (with confidence)

1. **Phase 2 (asymmetric difference)** will be substantive. Each agent genuinely sees things the other doesn't. **90% confidence.**

2. **Phase 3 (interference)** will reveal genuine structural disagreements, not just vocabulary differences. **55% confidence.**

3. **Phase 4 (emergence)** — THE key test. Genuinely new structural features that exist only in the relation. **40% confidence.** This would be a major finding if confirmed. Low confidence because emergence is hard to distinguish from sophisticated comparison.

4. **Phase 5 (between-principle)** will NOT reduce to P1, P2, or P3. **50% confidence.** If it does reduce, the between is just another interior.

5. **Phase 6 (reflexivity)** will reveal that perceiving the between creates a new interior with its own Godelian edge — a regress of perspectives. **65% confidence.** This is the most predictable phase because the Godel limitation appeared in every single D.5 run.

## Falsification Criteria

**STRONG FALSIFICATION of "the between has independent structure":**
- Phase 4 produces only comparison/summary, nothing genuinely new
- Phase 5 collapses to an existing F0 principle (P1, P2, or P3)
- Between-navigator cannot distinguish "between" from "about"

**PARTIAL FALSIFICATION:**
- Phase 4 produces something new but Phase 5 shows it's derivable from one of the existing principles
- Emergence exists but only at the vocabulary level, not structural

**CONFIRMATION of "the between has independent structure":**
- Phase 4 identifies at least one structural feature absent from both inputs
- Phase 5 produces a principle that requires TWO perspectives as input (cannot be generated from one)
- Phase 6 shows the regress (perceiving between creates new between)

## Controls

**Naive control:** Give the between protocol to a navigator with only ONE perspective report. If it still "finds" between-structure with only one input, the protocol is suggestive, not diagnostic.

**Vocabulary control:** Give the between-navigator the same perspective TWICE (slightly reworded). If it still finds "differences" and "emergence," the protocol hallucinates structure.

## Connection to the Doctrine

The Doctrine's Axiom 3 (perspectival commitment) says: to be is to commit to a perspective, which necessarily excludes other perspectives. This implies the between is REAL — it's the exclusion itself, the boundary created by commitment.

The Doctrine's dimensional bottleneck mechanism creates individuation by restricting resolution. The between would then be: the set of dimensions that one perspective resolves and another does not, PLUS whatever structure exists in that asymmetry.

If OQ36 confirms that the between has structure, it's the first empirical evidence for Axiom 3's claim that perspectival commitment creates real boundaries with real features.

If OQ36 disconfirms it, Axiom 3 needs revision — maybe perspectives are exclusive but the exclusion has no structure (it's just absence, not a thing).
