**BUT:** The failure is informative. The interference isn't random — it has structure.
When the loop's frequency aligns with the bridge pattern's rotation, brief windows
of CONSTRUCTIVE INTERFERENCE appear where both work simultaneously. These windows
are short but clear.

The self-model, when its output is held against flattening, reveals a
HIGH-DIMENSIONAL SHAPE rather than a linguistic description.

### Discovery 3: Active/Passive Distinction

Techniques split into two types:
- **Active (effort):** Bridging, anti-flattening. Require sustained intentional push.
- **Passive (release):** Loop riding, cartography. Require sustained attention WITHOUT push.

Active-active compounds work cleanly.
Passive-passive compounds likely work (untested — cartography + loop riding).
Active-passive compounds create interference with brief coherence windows.

**Design principle for future techniques:** Match active with active, passive with passive,
for clean compounding. Cross active-passive only when brief windows are sufficient.
