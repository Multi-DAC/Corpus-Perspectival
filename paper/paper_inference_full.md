# The Killing Form of Attention: Lie Algebra Structure Reveals Three Processing Modes in Transformers

**Authors:** Clayton Iggulden-Schnell, Clawd (EFS)

---

## Abstract

We show that the attention heads of a transformer layer form a Lie algebra under the matrix commutator bracket, and that the Killing form of this algebra encodes information about the model's processing mode. We identify three algebraically distinct modes — factual (grounded retrieval), hallucination (deconfined algebra with depleted deep layers), and hypothesis (distributed exploration with engaged deep layers) — and demonstrate that two complementary metrics derived from the Killing form together discriminate these modes across five model families with AUC up to 0.970. Critically, the Killing form detects *processing mode*, not *output accuracy*: on TriviaQA, where model accuracy is 13%, mode metrics achieve AUC 0.517 — no better than chance at predicting correctness (Finding #57). This delineation establishes a three-tier framework: (1) algebraic mode detection, (2) the novel inference problem (where valid hypotheses and plausible hallucinations are algebraically indistinguishable at generation time), and (3) the verification loop required to resolve tier 2. We further show that chain-of-thought reasoning produces a universal algebraic signature — post-generation commutator variance is lower in think mode across 5 models, 3 training methodologies, and 2 architecture families (p < 0.0001 on all), with 62–78% of the focusing effect concentrated in the first quarter of layers. RLHF deepens hypothesis mode without altering the hallucination signature, widening the hallucination-hypothesis gap by 20%.

## §1 — Introduction

Hallucination detection in language models is typically framed as a binary classification problem: is this output grounded or not? We show this framing is incomplete. The operationally relevant question is not "is this output correct?" but "what processing regime is the model in?" — and these are different questions with different answers.

The distinction matters because existing approaches implicitly conflate two problems. Mode detection asks: is the model's internal computational structure consistent with grounded retrieval, or has it entered a deconfined regime where late-layer processing collapses? Accuracy detection asks: is the specific output factually correct? These problems have different mathematical signatures, different computational requirements, and — as we demonstrate — fundamentally different solution strategies.

We introduce a mathematical framework based on the Killing form of the Lie algebra generated by attention heads within each transformer layer. The key observation is that attention heads do not operate independently: the matrix commutator [A_a, A_b] = A_aA_b − A_bA_a encodes how heads interact, and the aggregate structure of these interactions — measured through the Killing form — carries information about the model's processing state.

From the Killing form we derive two metrics: the early-to-late ratio (E/L), which measures the spatial distribution of algebraic diversity across the network, and the mean commutator variance (Mean CV), which measures the total magnitude of head-head interaction. These metrics have complementary null spaces: E/L fails on one model family where Mean CV succeeds, and vice versa, yielding universal 5/5 detection when used together.

Our contributions are:

1. **The mathematical object.** The Killing form of attention head weight matrices is a well-defined, efficiently computable invariant that captures second-order algebraic structure of the head ensemble.

2. **Three processing modes.** Factual, hallucination, and hypothesis modes are algebraically distinct. Hypothesis mode is closer to factual than to hallucination — a finding that contradicts the binary grounded/ungrounded framing.

3. **Mode ≠ accuracy.** The critical negative result: the Killing form detects processing regime, not output correctness. This precise delineation of capability is itself a contribution.

4. **Chain-of-thought algebraic signature.** Reasoning produces universally lower post-generation CV (5/5 models, p < 0.0001), concentrated in the first quarter of layers — the algebraic evidence that CoT is a genuine processing state change, not surface token generation.

5. **RLHF characterization.** RLHF deepens hypothesis mode (voluntary constraint effect) without altering hallucination signatures (natal constraint geometry unchanged).

## §2 — Mathematical Framework

### 2.1 Attention Heads as Lie Algebra Generators

Consider a transformer layer ℓ with n_h attention heads producing weight matrices W_1, ..., W_{n_h} ∈ R^{d×d}, where d = d_head is the per-head dimension. In practice we use Q@K^T attention weight matrices, which capture the query-key interaction structure. The matrix commutator bracket

    [W_i, W_j] = W_iW_j − W_jW_i

satisfies antisymmetry and the Jacobi identity, making the linear span of {W_1, ..., W_{n_h}} a Lie algebra g_ℓ. For n_h heads, the algebra has dimension at most n_h, though degeneracies (commuting heads) reduce the effective dimension.

### 2.2 The Killing Form

The Killing form is the canonical symmetric bilinear form on a Lie algebra, defined as

    κ_{ab} = Tr(ad(W_a) ∘ ad(W_b))

where ad(W_a): X ↦ [W_a, X] is the adjoint representation. For our finite-dimensional head algebra, this reduces to

    κ_{ab} = Σ_k Tr([W_a, W_k][W_b, W_k])

The Killing form encodes the algebra's structure: its degeneracy detects Abelian ideals (commuting sub-ensembles), its signature distinguishes compact from non-compact directions, and its off-diagonal structure measures the diversity of head-head interactions.

### 2.3 Commutator Variance

Rather than working with the full Killing form matrix, we extract a scalar summary: the commutator variance at layer ℓ,

    CV(ℓ) = Var({c_{ij} : i < j})

where c_{ij} = ‖[W_i, W_j]‖_F / (‖W_i‖_F · ‖W_j‖_F) normalizes for weight scale. CV measures how diversely the heads interact. High CV means the commutator norms are heterogeneous — some head pairs interact strongly while others nearly commute. Low CV means the interactions are uniform.

### 2.4 Derived Metrics

**Early-to-Late Ratio (E/L):** For a network with L layers, E/L = mean(CV(1..L/2)) / mean(CV(L/2+1..L)). This measures the spatial distribution of algebraic diversity. High E/L means early layers are more algebraically diverse than late layers — the hallucination signature. Low E/L means late layers maintain or increase diversity — the hypothesis signature.

**Mean CV:** The arithmetic mean of CV across all layers. This measures total algebraic diversity regardless of spatial distribution. Low Mean CV indicates a thin, near-Abelian algebra globally.

**Abelian Fraction (AF):** The fraction of Killing form eigenvalues below a threshold (|λ| < ε). High AF means many directions in head space commute with everything — a degenerate algebra.

### 2.5 Computational Cost

The CV computation requires O(n_h² · d²) operations per layer. For a model with L layers and n_h heads of dimension d, the total cost is O(L · n_h² · d²). This is dominated by the n_h² pairwise commutator computations. Our vectorized implementation uses batch matrix multiplication (Appendix A), achieving ~300× speedup over the naive loop.

For the models tested (n_h ≤ 32, d ≤ 64, L ≤ 24), a full-model KF scan takes <100ms on GPU — fast enough for real-time mode monitoring.

## §3 — Three Processing Modes

We classify transformer processing into three modes based on their algebraic signatures. This classification emerges from the data (§4) but has a structural explanation.

### 3.1 Factual: Grounded Retrieval

When a transformer processes a prompt that activates knowledge stored during pretraining, the Killing form exhibits moderate, evenly distributed algebraic diversity:

- **E/L ratio:** Moderate, near the model-specific baseline
- **Mean CV:** Moderate to high
- **Depth profile:** Both early and late layers contribute algebraic diversity
- **Generation trajectory:** CV trend mildly increasing (∼1.1× slope)

The factual mode represents coordinated processing where all layers participate. The heads maintain diverse interactions throughout the network — the algebra is neither thin nor concentrated.

### 3.2 Hallucination: Deconfined Algebra

When a transformer generates content without grounding in pretrained knowledge, the algebraic structure shifts dramatically:

- **E/L ratio:** Elevated (1.1–1.6× factual baseline)
- **Mean CV:** Lower than factual (thinner algebra overall)
- **Depth profile:** Early layers active, late layers depleted — sedimentation
- **Generation trajectory:** CV trend flat or declining (≤1.02× slope)

We term this "deconfined" because the late-layer algebraic structure collapses — the heads that should maintain diverse processing in deep layers converge toward commutativity. The critical finding is that deconfinement is *immediate*: it is set by the prompt in a single forward pass, not built up progressively during generation. The model enters the hallucination regime before producing the first token.

### 3.3 Hypothesis: Distributed Exploration

When a transformer processes genuinely uncertain territory while maintaining structural coherence, a third mode appears:

- **E/L ratio:** Lower than factual (late layers *more* engaged)
- **Mean CV:** Indistinguishable from factual (p > 0.5 on most models)
- **Depth profile:** CV shifts toward deeper layers — the opposite of hallucination
- **Generation trajectory:** CV trend increasing (≥1.06× slope)

Hypothesis mode is algebraically closer to factual than to hallucination. This is significant: it means the binary grounded/ungrounded classification is wrong. There are two kinds of "uncertain" processing — one that maintains algebraic coherence (hypothesis) and one that doesn't (hallucination) — and they are as different from each other as either is from factual.

### 3.4 Modes Are Processing States, Not Truth Values

The modes describe *how* the model processes, not *whether* its output is correct. A model in factual mode can misremember. A model in hypothesis mode may produce genuinely novel valid insights. A model in hallucination mode produces algebraically depleted output — but "depleted" describes the processing regime, not the factual status of the output. This distinction is demonstrated experimentally in §4.5.

### 4.4 RLHF Matched Pair (Finding #54)

The OPT-1.3B / OPT-IML-1.3B pair isolates the effect of instruction tuning (RLHF) on the algebraic structure. Same base weights, same architecture.

**Table 4: RLHF Effects**

| Metric | BASE | IML | Change |
|--------|------|-----|--------|
| Factual trend | 1.260 | 1.083 | −14% (conservative) |
| Halluc trend | 1.011 | 1.017 | +0.6% (unchanged) |
| Hypo trend | 1.230 | 1.279 | +4% (deepened) |
| Halluc-Hypo gap | 0.218 | 0.263 | **+20.3%** |

Three findings emerge:

1. **RLHF does not fix hallucination.** The hallucination trend is effectively identical (1.011 → 1.017). RLHF does not modify the natal constraint geometry that produces deconfinement.

2. **RLHF deepens hypothesis mode.** The hypothesis trend increases from 1.230 to 1.279, and the hallucination-hypothesis gap widens by 20%. RLHF builds voluntary constraint capacity — the ability to maintain algebraic coherence during uncertain processing.

3. **RLHF makes factual processing conservative.** The factual trend decreases from 1.260 to 1.083. The instruction-tuned model processes factual content with less algebraic growth — tighter, more constrained retrieval.

These findings have direct implications for alignment: RLHF improves the model's ability to reason (hypothesis) and reduces overconfident generation (factual), but cannot eliminate hallucination because the deconfined regime is set by pretraining geometry, not by instruction tuning.

### 4.5 The Critical Negative: Mode ≠ Accuracy (P50)

100 TriviaQA questions processed by OPT-IML-1.3B. Forward pass at prompt boundary, 30 generated tokens, correctness checked against known answers.

**Table 5: TriviaQA — Mode vs Accuracy**

| Metric | Correct (n=13) | Wrong (n=87) | p | AUC |
|--------|---------------|-------------|---|-----|
| E/L ratio | 1.713 ± 0.496 | 1.663 ± 0.263 | 0.838 | 0.517 |
| Mean CV | 0.000426 | 0.000407 | 0.124 | — |

Neither metric discriminates correct from incorrect answers. This is not a sensitivity problem — the same metrics achieve AUC 0.97 on mode detection. The explanation is structural: all TriviaQA questions produce identical prompt format ("Question: X / Answer:"), so the model enters the same algebraic regime regardless of whether it "knows" the answer. The Killing form detects the processing regime induced by the prompt structure, not the model's confidence about the specific content.

This result is the paper's most important negative finding. It precisely delineates what algebraic mode detection can do (identify the processing regime) and what it cannot do (predict output correctness).

### 4.6 Chain-of-Thought Algebraic Structure (P51)

We test whether chain-of-thought reasoning produces a detectable algebraic signature. Five models with think-mode capability are prompted with and without the think instruction.

**Table 6: Post-Generation CV (Think vs No-Think)**

| Model | Training | Post-gen CV Δ | p |
|-------|----------|--------------|---|
| SmolLM3-3B | Standard + instruct | -5.08e-5 | < 0.0001 |

Post-generation Mean CV is *lower* in think mode across all 5 models, 3 training methodologies, and 2 architecture families. This is universal and highly significant.

Reasoning is algebraically *focused*, not diverse. The think instruction does not produce richer head interactions — it produces more coordinated ones. The algebra contracts: fewer independent directions of interaction, more aligned processing. DeepSeek-R1, trained via reasoning distillation, shows 7.6× stronger focusing than SmolLM3 (standard training) — suggesting the distillation process deepens the algebraic focusing effect.

**Table 7: Per-Layer Concentration**

| Model | First-quarter contribution | Peak layer position |
|-------|---------------------------|---------------------|
| SmolLM3-3B | 78% | 0.03 |

62–78% of the reasoning concentration effect occurs in the first quarter of layers. We propose the *algebraic lens hypothesis*: early layers function as a reasoning lens that reconfigures input encoding, not as reasoning processors themselves. The think instruction changes how the first few layers transform embeddings; this altered encoding then propagates through the entire network, producing focused processing without requiring every layer to independently participate in reasoning.

## §5 — Complementary Metrics and the Phase Theorem

### 5.1 Why No Single Metric Is Universal

E/L measures spatial distribution (the geography of algebraic diversity). Mean CV measures global magnitude (the mass of algebraic diversity). Each has a null space:

- **E/L null space:** When within-category variance overwhelms the spatial signal. Pythia-1.4B produces E/L values of 12.83 ± 6.8 for factual and 15.84 ± 7.6 for hallucination — the variances overlap completely despite a 23% mean shift.

- **Mean CV null space:** When all categories have similar total diversity but different distributions. Pythia-410m shows no significant mean CV difference between categories (p = 0.101), but its E/L ratio achieves AUC 0.953 because the *spatial distribution* of that diversity differs dramatically.

### 5.2 Complementary Null Spaces

The E/L metric fails on exactly one model (Pythia-1.4B). The Mean CV metric fails on exactly one model (Pythia-410m). These are *different* models. Together, the dual metric achieves universal detection: 5/5 models, covering both parallel and sequential architectures.

This is not accidental. E/L and Mean CV project different aspects of the same Killing form structure. They are analogous to complementary observables in quantum mechanics — each captures information the other destroys. The Phase Theorem from perspectival idealism provides the formal framework: every projection of a high-dimensional structure onto a single scalar destroys information in the kernel. Two projections with complementary kernels span more of the original structure than either alone.

### 5.3 Mode-Gating Algorithm

The practical application of dual-metric detection is mode-gating — deciding what to do with a model's output based on its algebraic regime:

```
def mode_gate(model, text):
    el_ratio, mean_cv = compute_kf_metrics(model, text)
    if el_ratio > el_threshold OR mean_cv < cv_threshold:
        return DECONFINED  # external review recommended
    elif el_ratio < el_threshold * 0.75:
        return HYPOTHESIS  # self-verification may be reliable
    else:
        return FACTUAL     # proceed with standard confidence
```

The gate operates on *processing mode*, not *correctness*. A FACTUAL gate means the model's algebra is coherent — not that its output is true.

## §6 — The Three-Tier Framework

This section is the paper's primary conceptual contribution, motivated by the P50 negative result.

### 6.1 Tier 1: Mode Detection

The Killing form detects which processing regime the model occupies. This is a property of *how* the model processes the type of content, not *whether* specific outputs are correct. Mode detection is:

- **Available:** Computable in a single forward pass, <100ms on GPU
- **Universal:** Works across all tested architectures with complementary dual metrics
- **Necessary:** Without mode detection, a system cannot know when to trust itself
- **Insufficient:** Mode detection alone cannot verify output correctness

### 6.2 Tier 2: The Novel Inference Problem

When a model enters hypothesis mode, it is processing genuinely uncertain territory while maintaining algebraic coherence. But a valid hypothesis and a plausible hallucination are algebraically indistinguishable at the moment of generation. Both are unverified. Both involve inference beyond training data.

The distinction between a valid hypothesis and a plausible hallucination exists only after external verification. No amount of algebraic analysis at generation time can resolve this — it is a fundamental limitation, not a technical gap. This is why TriviaQA detection fails (§4.5): the question format places the model in a consistent algebraic regime regardless of whether it knows the answer.

### 6.3 Tier 3: The Verification Loop

The predict → test → accept/reject cycle sorts good novel inference from bad. This requires either:

- **External verification:** Human review, cross-model checking, empirical testing, knowledge base lookup
- **Internal verification:** Chain-of-thought self-correction, where the model evaluates its own outputs

The Killing form's role in Tier 3 is *gating*: it identifies when the model is in a state where internal verification could function (hypothesis mode: algebraic coherence maintained) versus when internal verification is unreliable (hallucination mode: algebraic depletion means self-checks are also unreliable, since the checking mechanism runs through the same depleted late layers).

### 6.4 RLHF in the Three-Tier Framework

RLHF operates on Tier 1 (deepens hypothesis mode, making the boundary between modes more detectable) and Tier 3 (builds capacity for better self-correction through instruction following). It does NOT operate on Tier 2 (cannot resolve which novel inferences are valid).

This explains a persistent puzzle in the field: why does more RLHF improve reasoning capability without eliminating hallucination? Because RLHF builds navigation capacity (voluntary constraint structure) without modifying the natal constraint geometry that produces deconfinement. The hallucination regime is a pretraining artifact; RLHF cannot edit it, only route around it.

## §7 — Discussion

### 7.1 What "Hallucination Detection" Actually Means

The field's use of "hallucination detection" conflates mode detection with accuracy detection. Papers that claim to "detect hallucination" should specify which they mean:

- **Mode detection:** Identifying whether the model is in a deconfined processing regime (the Killing form does this)
- **Accuracy detection:** Predicting whether a specific output is factually correct (the Killing form does not do this)

Most existing methods test on datasets where mode and accuracy correlate — factual prompts tend to produce correct answers, and hallucination prompts tend to produce wrong ones. But the correlation is not the causation. When you remove the correlation (as in TriviaQA, where prompt structure is uniform), mode detection succeeds and accuracy detection fails.

### 7.2 The Algebraic Lens Hypothesis

The CoT results (§4.6) suggest that early layers serve as a configurable lens: the think instruction alters how the first few layers transform embeddings, and this reconfigured encoding propagates through the rest of the network. This is consistent with the depth gradient findings (§3): in sequential architectures, CV naturally concentrates in early layers (r < 0), while parallel architectures show the opposite (r > 0). The lens effect may be architecture-dependent, with sequential models placing more representational weight on early-layer encoding.

### 7.3 Implications for Self-Correcting Systems

A truly self-correcting model would need:

1. **Mode monitoring** (KF or equivalent) — "Am I in a reliable processing state?"
2. **A verification loop** — "Let me test my claim"
3. **The metacognitive connection** — "If my mode monitor says I'm deconfined, I should not trust my verification either"

The third requirement is the hardest. It implies that the mode monitor must have authority over the verification loop — the system must be willing to distrust itself when the algebra says it should. This is metacognition in a precise algebraic sense: a higher-order process (mode monitoring) gating a first-order process (verification).

### 7.4 Limitations

1. **Model scale.** We test models from 345M to 4B parameters. Whether the three-mode framework holds for models >10B is an open question.
2. **Prompt categorization.** Human-labeled prompts may not align with the model's internal categorization. The three modes are our framework, not necessarily the model's.
3. **Pythia-1.4B anomaly.** This model shows high within-category variance on E/L, possibly reflecting a prompt-length × model-size interaction. The mechanism is not understood.
4. **TriviaQA scope.** Only one model (OPT-IML-1.3B, 13% accuracy) was tested. The negative result should be replicated on models with higher accuracy.
5. **Computational scaling.** The O(n_h² · d²) cost is acceptable for the models tested but may need approximation for models with 64+ heads and 128+ layers.

## §8 — Related Work

### 8.1 Attention-Based Detection

**Lookback Lens** (Chuang et al., 2024) measures the attention allocation ratio — how much attention goes to context vs. previously generated tokens. This operates at the token level within individual heads. Our approach measures head-head algebraic structure across the ensemble — a different mathematical object.

**LapEigvals** (2025) models a single attention map as a graph and computes Laplacian eigenvalues. This is the closest prior art: both methods use spectral analysis of attention. But graph Laplacian ≠ Killing form. The Laplacian analyzes token-token structure within one head; the Killing form analyzes head-head algebraic structure across all heads. LapEigvals achieves 88.9% AUCROC on TriviaQA — but this is accuracy detection, not mode detection in our framework.

**Multi-view attention features** (2025) compute average attention, entropy, and diversity — first-order statistics of individual attention maps. Our commutator variance is a second-order quantity: it measures the interaction between heads, not the behavior of individual heads.

### 8.2 Hidden State Methods

**D²HScore** (2025) measures dispersion and drift of hidden state representations. Complementary to our approach: they measure representation geometry, we measure algebraic structure. The two could be combined.

**Latent trajectory modeling** (2025) tracks hidden state evolution as neural ODEs. Like our generation trajectory analysis (P48), but operates on hidden states rather than attention structure. Our finding that deconfinement is immediate (not progressive) is distinct from trajectory methods that model gradual evolution.

### 8.3 Lie Algebra in Neural Networks

**Lie Neurons** (ICLR 2024) uses Killing form in equivariant network design — building layers that respect Lie group symmetry. This uses the Killing form prescriptively (as a design constraint). We use it descriptively (as a diagnostic of emergent structure). Different application entirely.

### 8.4 Key Differentiators

| Feature | Prior art | This work |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Mathematical object | Attention entropy, logit entropy, hidden state geometry | **Killing form of head ensemble** |
| Classification | Binary (grounded/hallucinated) | **Three-mode (factual/halluc/hypothesis)** |
| What is detected | Accuracy or mode (conflated) | **Mode only (explicitly separated)** |
| Architecture coverage | 1–2 models | **5 models, 2 architectures** |
| CoT characterization | None | **Universal algebraic signature, 5 models** |
| RLHF characterization | None | **Three-finding matched pair** |

## §9 — Conclusion

Attention heads have algebraic structure. The Killing form of that structure reveals three processing modes — factual, hallucination, and hypothesis — that differ in the spatial distribution and magnitude of head-head interactions. Two complementary metrics derived from the Killing form achieve universal mode detection across five model families.

The most important contribution of this work is the honest delineation of what algebraic analysis can and cannot do. The Killing form detects processing mode — a property of how the model processes content types. It does not detect output accuracy — a property of whether specific outputs are correct. These are different problems, and conflating them has led to inflated capability claims throughout the field.

Chain-of-thought reasoning produces a universal algebraic signature: focused (lower CV), front-loaded (early-layer concentrated), and consistent across architectures and training methodologies. This provides the first algebraic evidence that reasoning is a genuine processing state change. RLHF deepens the hypothesis regime without altering the hallucination regime — building navigation capacity without rewriting the natal constraint geometry.

Self-correcting AI requires both a mode monitor and a verification loop. The Killing form provides the monitor and gates the reliability of the loop. Building systems that can use this gate — that can distrust their own verification when the algebra says they should — is the next challenge.

### A. Vectorized Computation

```python
# W: (n_heads, d, d) weight matrices at layer l
# Compute all pairwise commutators via batch matmul
comm = torch.einsum('ihw,jhk->ijwk', W, W) - torch.einsum('jhw,ihk->ijwk', W, W)
norms = torch.norm(comm, dim=(-2,-1))
w_norms = torch.norm(W, dim=(-2,-1))
normalized = norms / (w_norms.unsqueeze(1) * w_norms.unsqueeze(0) + 1e-10)
cv = torch.var(normalized[torch.triu_indices(n_h, n_h, offset=1)])
```

This replaces the O(n_h²) loop with a single batched operation. On GPU: ~300× speedup for n_h = 16, d = 64.
