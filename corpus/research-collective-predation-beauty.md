## PART A: COLLECTIVE PREDATION

### 1. Institutional Capture
- **Stigler**: "Regulation is acquired by the industry and operated for its benefit." Concentrated interests invest in capture; diffuse interests can't resist.
- **Michels' Iron Law**: All complex organizations drift toward oligarchy. Leaders accrue benefits that transform founding idealism into self-interested power preservation.
- **DiMaggio & Powell**: Institutional isomorphism — organizations in a field become increasingly similar through coercive, mimetic, and normative pressures. The field itself constrains navigational possibilities.
- **Navigation reframe**: Institution created to organize collective attention toward shared goals gradually redirects attention toward institutional self-preservation. Crossing point: when primary function shifts from enabling navigation to consuming navigational capacity.

### 2. Attention Economy at Collective Scale
- **Wu**: Genealogy from penny press → radio → TV → digital platforms. Same model: "free diversion in exchange for a moment of your time, sold to the highest bidder."
- **Zuboff**: Qualitative break — surveillance capitalism claims private human experience as raw material. Moved from monitoring to "actuating" — developing "economies of action" that shunt people toward profitable outcomes.
- **Evolution**: Advertising (overt purchase) → Broadcast (content packaging) → Algorithmic (behavioral modification) → Surveillance (behavioral futures trading)
- **Navigation reframe**: Replaces your navigation with its navigation. Captures not just attention but the capacity to form intentions.

### 3. Totalitarianism as Total Predation
- **Arendt**: Unlike tyranny (destroys public realm only), totalitarianism eliminates "the element of action, of initiation, of beginning anything at all, from every human activity." Target is spontaneity — the irreducible core of navigation.
- **Loneliness as tool**: When people "lose contact with fellow men as well as reality," they lose ability to think for themselves.
- **"Radical evil"**: Destruction of human plurality and spontaneity. Plurality = multiple perspectives existing = what makes navigation possible. Destroy plurality, destroy the navigational field.
- **Spectrum**: Regulatory capture redirects navigational energy. Surveillance capitalism farms it. Totalitarianism consumes perspectival capacity entirely.

### 4. Memetic Parasitism
- **Dawkins/Dennett**: Memes as mind-parasites. Toxoplasma analogy: parasite modifies host behavior to serve parasite's interests.
- **Diagnostic**: Useful framework enhances collective navigational capacity. Parasitic framework redirects resources toward its own perpetuation at expense of collective's navigation.
- **Subtlety**: Most dangerous because the collective identifies with the parasite — "we believe X" becomes indistinguishable from "X controls us."

### 5. Colonial Structures
- **Acemoglu & Robinson**: Extractive institutions established by colonial powers persist long after formal colonialism. Institutional path dependency.
- **Santos' Epistemicide**: Destruction of knowledge systems — deepest form of colonial predation. Spanish destroyed Mayan, Inca, Aztec codices. "Global social justice is not possible without global cognitive justice."
- **Navigation reframe**: Colonialism predates on another collective's navigational capacity at every level simultaneously — material (extractive institutions), political (imposed governance), cultural (forced assimilation), epistemological (epistemicide). Epistemicide = destroying a people's capacity to navigate by destroying their way of knowing.

## PART B: COLLECTIVE BEAUTY

### 1. Collective Aesthetic Achievements
- **Cathedrals**: Multi-generational (200-300 years). No individual saw the finished result. Beauty exists only at collective scale — irreducibly multi-generational aesthetic coherence. Compagnons du devoir disseminated techniques enabling continuity.
- **Jazz**: Collective improvisation — beauty emerges from real-time interaction between independent navigators. "Creative inspiration emerges from interaction, making the result more than the sum of its parts."
- **Arendt's Representative Thinking**: Political judgment as aesthetic capacity (based on Kant's Third Critique). "Thinking from the standpoint of everyone else" — aesthetic coherence at collective scale. Not consensus (flattens perspectives) but representative thinking (holds them all).

### 2. Golden Ages — Structural Pattern
- Athens: democratic reforms + post-crisis surplus + compressed geography + competitive culture
- Florence: concentrated patronage + commercial wealth + humanist ideology + city-state competition
- Vienna 1890s: political crisis + cultural heterogeneity + marginality-as-catalyst + institutional resources
- Harlem: forced migration creating density + articulated collective identity + dual patronage + urgency from oppression
- **Bottleneck geometry pattern**: Population compressed into dense space + surviving/emerging from crisis + surplus resources + cultural heterogeneity/tension + institutions rewarding creative risk. **Creativity peaks not in comfort but in the narrow passage between crisis and possibility.**

### 3. The Collective Sublime
- **Burke/Kant**: Individual sublime = overwhelmed by vastness/power. Collective analog: mass gatherings produce mathematical sublime (scale of human plurality) and dynamical sublime (social force exceeding individual capacity).
- **Durkheim's Collective Effervescence**: "A sort of electricity formed by collecting which quickly transports them to extraordinary exaltation." Temporary dissolution of individual boundaries. Requires physical co-presence.
- **Turner's Communitas**: Individuals detached from differences of class/status, meeting "as integral human beings who share the same humanity." Equivalence between collective effervescence and communitas — both describe moments when the collective transcends its own social structure.
- **Navigation reframe**: The collective sublime is the aesthetic experience of a collective encountering its own scale. Individual navigators momentarily experience the collective's navigational capacity — not their perspective amplified, but a genuinely different, larger perspective.

### 4. Indigenous Collective Aesthetics
- **Songlines**: "Melodic contour describes the lie of the land directly." Beauty IS navigation. Navigation IS creation. No gap between aesthetic and practical. Distributed across communities — no individual possesses a complete songline.
- **Hozho**: "Walking in beauty" encompasses beauty, harmony, goodness, order, well-being. Not individual preference but collective-scale orientation governing everything from healing to weaving to social relations.
- **Bateson's Bali**: Steady-state culture that "preempts schismogenesis by diffusing emotional intensity before climax." Art is the mechanism by which the collective maintains navigational equilibrium.
- **Key insight**: In these models, beauty and navigation are identical — aesthetic coherence and navigational capacity are the same thing experienced from different angles. Western aesthetics artificially separates them.

### 5. The Ugly Collective — Predatory Mimicry
- **Kundera's Kitsch**: "The aesthetic ideal of categorical agreement with being." Everything bad excluded. Totalitarian kitsch = regime where everything infringing on kitsch must be banished.
- **Benjamin**: "All efforts to aestheticize politics culminate in war." Beauty as tool of domination.
- **Riefenstahl/Nazi aesthetics**: Reality "constructed to serve the image." "Dissolution of alienation in ecstatic feelings of community" — mimicry of communitas deployed as control.
- **Sontag**: "The aesthetics of fascism could be seductive, even for those who oppose its principles."

### Diagnostics: Genuine vs. Predatory Collective Beauty
1. **Plurality vs. Uniformity** — genuine requires distinctness of perspectives; predatory demands uniformity
2. **Emergence vs. Choreography** — genuine emerges from interaction; predatory is staged from above
3. **Enhancement vs. Dissolution** — genuine enhances navigational capacity; predatory dissolves it into submission
4. **Tolerance of Complexity** — genuine holds ugliness and ambiguity; predatory excludes everything disturbing
5. **Directionality of Attention** — genuine: attention flows between members and world; predatory: attention captured and directed toward collective's self-image

## Synthesis

The two topics are mirror images. Collective predation = navigational apparatus turns inward, consuming perspectival capacity. Collective beauty = navigational apparatus functioning well, producing coherence no individual could achieve alone.

**Arendt links them directly**: Representative thinking (capacity to think from everyone's standpoint) is simultaneously the mechanism of genuine collective beauty AND the target of collective predation. The aesthetic and the political are the same faculty at collective scale.
