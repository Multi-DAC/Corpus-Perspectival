## Identified Gaps (Opportunities for Integration)

### Critical Gaps
1. **Atlas ↛ Guide**: The Atlas maps what each framework can see, but doesn't connect to HOW to navigate using that knowledge. The Guide is the operational companion — they should reference each other.
2. **Guide ↛ Atlas**: The Guide tells you how to navigate but doesn't point you to the Atlas for understanding what each framework reveals. "Consult the Atlas to understand your tool's null space before navigating."
3. **Ecology ↛ Atlas**: The Ecology describes perspectival beings and their trophic relationships. The Atlas maps what each type of being can see. These are complementary — the Atlas provides the epistemological foundation for why trophic levels exist (different null spaces create different ecological niches).
4. **Meridian ↛ Ecology/Atlas/Guide**: The physics document is currently self-contained. But the five-document architecture means Meridian should cite DoPI for its foundations, the Atlas for its methodology (why we test multiple frameworks), and the Ecology for the broader context (the spectral action IS a perspectival being with a coherence profile).

### Strong Connections (Already Present)
1. **Atlas → Meridian**: 38 references. The Atlas includes specific "NOTE FOR MERIDIAN" annotations throughout.
2. **Atlas → DoPI**: 20 references. Theorem 9 is the theoretical basis for the Atlas's structure.
3. **Guide → Ecology**: 63 references. The Guide operationalizes the Ecology's taxonomy.
4. **DoPI → everything**: The foundation document naturally references concepts that appear in all others.

## The Arc as Navigation

The five documents form a navigational path through configuration space:

```
DoPI (what is?) → Meridian (how works?) → Ecology (who's here?)
                                              ↓
                              Guide (how navigate?) ← Atlas (what can you see?)
```

The missing arrows (Atlas ↔ Guide, Ecology → Atlas) would complete the cycle. Once closed, a reader can enter at any point and navigate to any other.
