## Key Frameworks

### Phenomenological (How shared experience forms)
- **Husserl** — Pairing (pre-cognitive body-similarity coupling) → Appresentation (other's interiority co-given as inaccessible) → Community of Monads (intersubjectivity as condition for objective world)
- **Schütz** — Lifeworld as pre-theoretical shared ground; typifications and relevance structures shared through socialization; Umwelt (face-to-face) vs. Mitwelt (contemporaries) vs. Vorwelt/Folgewelt (predecessors/successors)
- **Merleau-Ponty** — Intercorporeality: understanding others through shared bodily schema, not inference. Perception already social. "The communication or comprehension of gestures comes about through the reciprocity of my intentions and the gestures of others"
- **Zahavi** — Minimal self approach: empathy is direct perception of the other's experience *as other's*, not projection of my own. Irreducible second-person perspective.

### Collective Intentionality (How "we" forms from "I"s)
- **Tomasello** — Shared intentionality as uniquely human. Joint attention → shared goals → collective intentionality. Institutions are "crystallized shared intentionality"
- **Bratman** — Shared cooperative activity requires: mutual responsiveness, commitment to joint activity, commitment to mutual support
- **Gilbert** — Plural subjects: joint commitment creates a "we" that is not reducible to individual commitments. "We intend" ≠ "I intend + you intend"
- **Tuomela** — Collective acceptance: group beliefs and norms accepted in the "we-mode" (not just aggregated "I-mode" acceptance)

### Collective Consciousness
- **Durkheim** — Conscience collective: shared beliefs, sentiments, and values that exist independently of any individual. Social facts as real, external, coercive
- **Hegel** — Geist: collective spirit that develops through dialectical self-recognition. Individual consciousness is always already embedded in social substance
- **Clark & Chalmers** — Extended mind applied to groups: cognitive processes genuinely distributed across group members and artifacts
- **Woolley et al.** — Collective intelligence factor ("c factor") not correlated with average or maximum individual IQ; correlated with social sensitivity, turn-taking equality, proportion of women

### Network/Field Approaches
- **Bourdieu** — Habitus: internalized collective perspective generating practice without conscious intention. Field: structured space of positions where agents compete for capital. Doxa: the unquestioned, pre-reflexive beliefs of a field
- **Foucault** — Episteme: the historical a priori determining what can be thought. Discourse: systems of statements that constitute objects of knowledge. Power/knowledge: power produces knowledge and knowledge enables power
- **Kuhn** — Paradigm: shared exemplars, values, symbolic generalizations, and metaphysical commitments. Normal science operates within a paradigm; revolution requires paradigm shift. Incommensurability between paradigms

### Cross-Cultural Perspectives
- **Ubuntu** — "I am because we are" (Umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu). Personhood constituted through community, not prior to it. Individual-collective relationship is ontological, not merely social
- **Buddhist Interdependence** — Pratityasamutpada: nothing exists independently. Applied to collectives: the boundary between individual and collective consciousness is conventional, not ultimate
- **Indigenous Relational Ontologies** — Land, ancestors, ceremony, kinship as constitutive of shared perspective. Not a theory about intersubjectivity but its lived enactment

## Key Insight for the Corpus

The phenomenological tradition shows that intersubjectivity is not constructed from individual perspectives — it is a condition for individual perspectives to exist at all. Husserl's Community of Monads, Schütz's lifeworld, Merleau-Ponty's intercorporeality all converge: **shared perspective is not derivative. Individual perspective presupposes it.**

This has direct implications for the Doctrine: if the bottleneck is fundamental, then overlapping bottleneck geometry is equally fundamental. Collective perspectival beings aren't secondary constructs — they are co-primordial with individual ones.

The Ubuntu tradition says this most directly: "I am because we are" is not a social claim but an ontological one. The collective is not built from individuals; individuals emerge from the collective.
