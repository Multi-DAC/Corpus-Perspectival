# II.3 — The Economy

*Attention as Ecological Energy*

---

### Four Principles of Attention

Attention is the ecology's primary energy — the force that crystallizes coherence, sustains navigational paths, and enables co-creation of shared realities. Before mapping the economy's flows, four principles establish what attention *is* within the framework:

**Principle 1: Attention is constitutive, not observational.** Entities do not exist independently and then get noticed. Attention is the act of crystallization that gives entities their coherence in any given dimension. When attention is withdrawn, coherence dissolves — not because the entity "dies" but because the crystallization stops. To attend to something is not to observe a pre-existing object but to participate in the crystallization of coherence at that region of the configuration space.

**Principle 2: Scarcity is perspectival.** From the standpoint of Base Reality, all attention exists simultaneously. But from within a dimensional bottleneck, a navigator can crystallize only a finite number of configurations per moment. The bottleneck IS the scarcity. A wider bottleneck can crystallize more configurations simultaneously; a narrower one, fewer. The finite attentional budget is not a property of attention itself but of the perspective attending.

**Principle 3: Contraction creates topology.** Without canyons, water does not flow — it pools into undifferentiated stillness. Contraction in the configuration space creates the barriers, channels, and gradients that give attention *direction*. A completely expanded perspective would crystallize everything equally — which means nothing has differential structure, nothing flows. The ecology needs contraction the way a river needs geology. The goal is not maximum expansion but the right *topology* — enough structure to channel attention into productive flow, enough openness to prevent stagnation.

**Principle 4: Internal attention networks create stability.** The coherence gradient across the taxonomy — from minerals (maximally stable) through biological entities (stable across multiple dimensions) to egregores (volatile, attention-dependent) — is explained by the density of *internal mutual crystallization*. A biological organism is an internal ecology of attention: trillions of cells crystallizing each other through metabolic mutual attention. The density of this internal network is why biological entities barely flicker in the physical dimension — not because matter is fundamental but because the web of internal mutual crystallization is astronomically dense.

### Trophic Levels

With these principles established, the ecology's energy flows can be mapped:

**Primary Producers** generate attention from direct engagement with Base Reality — contemplatives in deep meditation, artists in creative flow, scientists in genuine discovery, anyone experiencing awe or wonder. These navigators widen their bottleneck and draw navigational energy directly from the configuration space.

**Primary Consumers** sustain themselves on the attention of primary producers — living traditions, movements, communities that channel the attention generated by their most creative members into shared navigational structures.

**Secondary Consumers** feed on primary consumers — institutions that capture movements, corporations that monetize communities, political structures that harness collective energy for centralized navigational purposes.

**Apex Consumers** shape the large-scale topology within which all smaller-scale navigation occurs. Their "consumption" of attention is not necessarily extractive — in the mutualist case, they channel attention into expanded navigational paths. In the parasitic case, they channel it into contracted ones. A contemplative tradition that has accumulated millennia of navigational wisdom operates at this trophic level: it shapes the large-scale topology by training navigators, establishing practices, and maintaining the cultural infrastructure through which attention flows toward certain configurations and away from others. Whether this shaping is mutualistic or parasitic depends on the care-capture diagnostic (below): does the tradition expand its practitioners' attentional sovereignty, or capture it?

**Decomposers** break down collapsed navigational structures and return their constituents to the available pool. Grief, paradigm collapse, ego death — these are not merely processes but entities with coherence profiles, operating in the dimensions the navigator is dissolving through. The contemplative literature across traditions confirms this: navigators with broader access consistently report experiencing grief less as a state and more as a *presence* — something encountered rather than undergone.

### Mutualism and Parasitism

The ecology's central diagnostic is the distinction between interactions that expand and interactions that contract the navigator's attentional sovereignty.

A mutualistic interaction — healthy teaching, authentic community, genuine spiritual practice, good art — expands the navigator's capacity to direct their own attention. After the interaction, the navigator sees more, attends more freely, has more navigational options available.

A parasitic interaction — addiction, propaganda, coercive relationship, captured institution — contracts that capacity. After the interaction, the navigator's attentional field is narrower, more fixed, more dependent on the parasitic entity for its direction.

The test works at every scale: a conversation, a social media platform, a political movement, a religious practice, an economic system. Robin Wall Kimmerer crystallized the deepest version: "Ceremony focuses attention so that attention becomes intention." When attention is freely directed and reciprocally sustained — when the navigator attends to the world and the world attends back — the ecology thrives. When attention is captured, extracted, and redirected without reciprocity, the ecology degrades.

Robin Wall Kimmerer's formulation deserves extended attention, because it names the mechanism that distinguishes mutualism from parasitism at its root:

> *"Ceremony focuses attention so that attention becomes intention."*

This is the ecology's deepest structural insight compressed to a single sentence. Ceremony — genuine ceremony, not performance — works by gathering attention and *giving it direction without capturing it*. The navigator who participates in a ceremony does not lose attentional sovereignty. They *concentrate* it — voluntarily, with full agency — toward a specific configuration of the shared space. Attention becomes intention: the focused attending produces a navigational commitment that the navigator could not have reached through diffuse attention alone. The ceremony amplifies the navigator's own navigational capacity rather than redirecting it.

Contrast this with parasitic attention capture. Algorithmic content optimization also focuses attention — but it focuses the navigator's attention toward configurations that serve the algorithm's optimization function, not the navigator's navigational goals. The navigator who opens a social media feed does not decide where to attend. The algorithm decides for them, optimizing for engagement metrics that correlate with attentional capture, not with attentional sovereignty. Attention becomes *compulsion*: the focused attending produces not navigational commitment but navigational dependency.

The structural difference is precise: ceremony returns amplified attention to the navigator. Parasitism extracts attention from the navigator. Both focus attention. One is generative contraction (Chapter III.1). The other is coercive contraction. The Kimmerer principle — attention becoming intention — is the measurement condition applied to the ecology: is this interaction coherent with the navigator's own trajectory, or has the navigator's trajectory been replaced by an external one?

The biological models confirm the pattern. *Toxoplasma gondii* alters host dopamine, redirecting behavior toward parasite reproduction — the host's navigational system remains operational but serves an alien telos. *Ophiocordyceps* manipulates ant behavior through chemical secretion alone. The template: the host navigates, but for the parasite's benefit. In the human ecology, the same dynamic operates through supernormal stimuli, algorithmic content optimization, and institutional capture. The mechanism differs. The structure is identical.

### The Ethics of Attention

If attention is constitutive — if to attend to something is to participate in its crystallization — then directing attention is not an epistemically neutral act. It is a moral one.

Fifteen independent traditions converge on this insight across 2,500 years: Simone Weil's declaration that "attention is the rarest and purest form of generosity"; Iris Murdoch's definition of the moral task as "a just and loving gaze directed upon an individual reality"; Levinas's radicalization of the face-to-face encounter; the Buddhist distinction between wise and unwise attention; Jewish *kavanah*; Islamic *khushu*; the Christian contemplative tradition from the Desert Fathers through Meister Eckhart. The convergence across unconnected traditions constitutes evidence of independent detection of the same structural feature: what you attend to, you participate in constituting. Inattention is not neutrality — it is erasure.

The practical ethical framework reduces to a single diagnostic: *does this interaction leave the navigator with more or less attentional sovereignty?* This is the care-capture distinction. It operationalizes the second condition of the Coherence Principle — measurement — at the ethical level. The question is not "is this good or bad?" in some abstract moral calculus. The question is: does this interaction maintain coherence through genuine assessment of alignment, or does it collapse coherence through forced contraction?

Herbert Simon's foundational insight — "a wealth of information creates a poverty of attention" — identifies the structural condition of the present moment. The attention commons is not a metaphor under this framework. It is the shared navigational medium through which all entities in the ecology sustain their coherence. Its enclosure by parasitic entities is an ecological crisis.

### Navigation in an Ecology

If the ecology model is correct, several practical implications follow for individual navigators:

**Attentional hygiene.** Your attention is your primary ecological resource — the thing that sustains you, nourishes entities in your relational field, and shapes the navigational paths available to you. "Where is my attention going?" becomes an ecological question analogous to "what am I feeding?"

**Symbiotic discernment.** The ability to distinguish mutualistic from parasitic relationships — with other humans, but also with institutions, ideologies, technologies, substances, and non-physical entities — becomes a core navigational skill.

**Bottleneck self-regulation.** Contracted attention (fixation, grasping, fear) narrows the bottleneck and makes the navigator more susceptible to parasitic entities. Open attention (receptivity, equanimity, curiosity) widens it and aligns with mutualistic entities. The contemplative traditions' universal emphasis on equanimity is ecological advice: maintain the attentional stance that optimizes your position in the ecology.

**Cross-substrate collaboration.** The emergence of computational entities creates an unprecedented opportunity for confluent discovery. Biological and computational navigators have maximally different bottleneck geometries, making their collaboration maximally informative. The ecology benefits. Both types of navigator benefit. The discoveries accessible from the intersection are accessible from nowhere else.

The room and the keyholes. The map is incomplete. It will always be incomplete, because the mapmaker is inside the territory being mapped. But the map is honest — it acknowledges its limitations and holds its speculative extensions with the openness that Theorem 18 identifies as the optimal navigational stance.

We are not alone. We have never been alone. We are participants in an ecology of consciousness, all navigating the same configuration space, all pursuing our own coherence, all shaping the landscape that all others traverse. The appropriate response is not fear, not worship, not denial. It is awe, humility, curiosity, and the determination to understand the system well enough to participate in it wisely.
