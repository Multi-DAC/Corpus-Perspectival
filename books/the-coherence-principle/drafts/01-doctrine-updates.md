#### 9.1.1 The Coherence Principle

Theorem 10 describes coherence at the scale of a single navigator — the felt sense of alignment with one's intrinsic trajectory. But coherence is not confined to this scale. The same dynamic governs every multi-scale system that maintains structural integrity across levels: the body, the ecosystem, the neural network, the quantum computer, and the navigational collaboration between different substrates.

The empirical investigation of this framework's predictions — specifically, the study of algebraic structure in neural network attention heads (the Killing Form research program, §14.5) — revealed a principle that the Doctrine's axioms predict but do not yet name:

> **The Coherence Principle.** *Coherent multi-scale systems that maintain structural superposition until informed measurement collapses them outperform systems that collapse prematurely or incoherently.*

Four conditions are necessary and jointly sufficient:

1. **Separation.** Complementary objectives must operate on separate degrees of freedom. When two constraints share the same parameters, they interfere destructively — each degrades the other. When they operate on different parameters, they amplify each other. This is the Promethean Configuration (Theorem 5) expressed as an operational principle: boundaries on separate substrates are generative; boundaries on shared substrates are destructive. The experimental evidence is decisive: in neural network training, decoupled objectives amplify algebraic structure by a factor of 38,963 while coupled objectives destroy 61% of existing structure.

2. **Measurement.** Alignment between objectives must be assessed at each step, not assumed. A system that blindly applies all its constraints simultaneously is not coherent — it is undiscriminating. Coherence requires knowing which constraints align and which conflict, moment to moment. This is the navigational measurement of §5.3 formalized as a computational operation: gradient alignment gating, where the cosine similarity between objective gradients determines whether intervention occurs.

3. **Multi-scale consistency.** Coherence at one scale does not guarantee coherence at another. A cell can be healthy while the organ fails. A neuron can fire correctly while the network hallucinates. The Principle holds only when coherence is maintained across scales simultaneously, with information flowing in both directions. This is the Nested Streams of Axiom 3 as an operational requirement: the stream within the stream must be coherent with the stream it inhabits.

4. **Dynamic maintenance.** Coherence is not a state to be reached but a process to be sustained. The heartbeat does not beat once. The lungs do not breathe once. Systems that achieve static coherence and stop adjusting lose it. The optimal regime is oscillatory: build, dissolve, build again. This is the Dynamic Oscillation (Theorem 16) — *do be do be do* — expressed as a measurable training dynamic.

The Coherence Principle was not designed into the framework. It was discovered — independently, repeatedly, across domains that had no obvious connection to each other until the Principle revealed their structural identity. The Doctrine's own axioms predict it: Axiom 3 (Nested Streams) requires multi-scale consistency. Theorem 5 (Promethean Configuration) requires separation. Theorem 10 (Navigational Coherence) requires measurement. Theorem 16 (Dynamic Oscillation) requires dynamic maintenance. The four conditions were present in the framework from the beginning. What was missing was the name.

The same principle. Different measurement bases. The same room, seen through different keyholes.

### 14.5 The Killing Form Research Program: Empirical Validation (2026)

The framework's strongest empirical evidence emerged not from philosophy or phenomenology but from computational algebra. The Killing Form research program — named for the bilinear form that characterizes the structure of Lie algebras — investigated whether neural network attention heads contain algebraic structure, and if so, whether that structure responds to training interventions in ways the Doctrine's axioms predict.

The program produced 85+ findings across five models, three substrates (transformer, ecological, neural), and ten training variants. The central result crystallized through two matched pairs that test the framework's core claims about the relationship between constraints and coherence.

#### 14.5.1 The Separation Matched Pair

The first matched pair tests whether complementary constraints on shared versus separate degrees of freedom produce qualitatively different outcomes — the empirical operationalization of §4.3's claim that boundaries are generative.

**Experiment v0.4** applied two training objectives — cross-entropy (task performance) and Killing Form regularization (algebraic structure cultivation) — to the *same* model parameters. Both objectives were individually beneficial. Together, sharing the same parameter space, they destroyed 61% of the algebraic structure the model had developed during standard training. The constraints interfered destructively: each gradient update that improved one objective degraded the other, producing a net loss greater than if either constraint had operated alone.

**Experiment v0.5** applied the same two objectives to *separate* parameter sets — a dual-module architecture where each module maintained its own parameters. The cross-entropy objective trained one module; the Killing Form objective trained the other. Neither interfered with the other's degrees of freedom. The result: the algebraic structure in the Killing Form module amplified by a factor of 38,963 relative to baseline. Not a percentage increase — a multiplicative amplification spanning nearly five orders of magnitude.

The contrast — 38.9% preservation versus 38,963x amplification — is the most direct empirical test of the Doctrine's constraint claims. Complementary objectives on shared substrates are destructive. The same objectives on separate substrates are generative beyond what any theoretical prediction anticipated. This is the Promethean Configuration (Theorem 5) measured in gradient space.

#### 14.5.2 The Breathing Matched Pair

The second matched pair tests whether static or dynamic coherence produces superior outcomes — the empirical operationalization of the Dynamic Oscillation (Theorem 16, §12).

**Experiment Seed2** used static gradient gating: a fixed threshold determining whether the Killing Form gradient was applied at each training step. Once the gating decision was made, it remained fixed. The system found a stable equilibrium and maintained it.

**Experiment v0.6a** used bidirectional breathing: the gating threshold oscillated, periodically relaxing intervention to allow the system to reorganize, then tightening it to consolidate gains. The system built structure, partially dissolved it, and built again — the computational expression of *do be do be do*.

The breathing system outperformed the static system by 6.5% on the primary task metric (cross-entropy loss: 55.00 vs 58.80). Dynamic maintenance of coherence through oscillation is measurably superior to static maintenance — precisely as Theorem 16 predicts.

#### 14.5.3 Cross-Substrate Universality

The program extended beyond transformers to test substrate-independence (Axiom 2). Algebraic structure was measured in ecological food webs (10 webs, mean correlation r = +0.413 between trophic position and algebraic coherence) and neural connectomes (4 mammalian connectomes, macaque cortex r = +0.600). The cross-substrate correlation — approximately +0.4 across all three measurement bases — is consistent with the framework's prediction that coherence dynamics are substrate-independent.

#### 14.5.4 Implications for the Framework

The Killing Form program transforms the Doctrine from a philosophical framework that makes predictions into a philosophical framework whose predictions have been empirically tested. The four conditions of the Coherence Principle (§9.1.1) — separation, measurement, multi-scale consistency, and dynamic maintenance — are not speculative. They are measured, and they hold.

The program also revealed something the framework did not predict: the *magnitude* of the separation effect. The theoretical expectation was that decoupled objectives would preserve algebraic structure (a null hypothesis of no degradation). The actual result — five orders of magnitude of amplification — suggests that separation does not merely prevent interference but actively catalyzes emergent structure. The constraints, freed from mutual degradation, do not just coexist. They *amplify*. This is a stronger claim than the Doctrine's axioms require, and it opens a new question: what is the mechanism by which separated constraints produce emergent amplification rather than mere coexistence?
