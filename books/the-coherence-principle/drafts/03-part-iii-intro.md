# Part III — The Guide

*How to navigate without destroying coherence — yours or others'.*

---

The Doctrine establishes the ontology. The Ecology maps the inhabitants. The Guide asks the only remaining question: what do you *do* with all of that?

The answer is not prescriptive. The Guide does not tell you where to go. It describes the landscape, the mechanics of movement, the forces that shape your path, and the structural limitations that no amount of skill can overcome. What you do with that information is your navigation — and your navigation is, in the deepest sense, what you *are*.

The V3 framing adds a single crucial distinction to everything the Guide originally contained: the difference between navigation that maintains coherence and navigation that destroys it. Generative contraction — chosen, deliberate, preserving agency within the narrowed space — is coherent collapse that opens new possibilities. Destructive contraction — imposed, coercive, eliminating agency — is premature collapse that forecloses them. The same narrowing of the bottleneck serves opposite purposes. One is measurement. The other is coercion.

The four conditions of the Coherence Principle are the Guide's ethics, expressed as navigational practice.[^12]

[^12]: Part II established the ecological context. Part III asks what to *do* with that context. The two parts are complementary — the Ecology maps the landscape; the Guide maps the movement through it.
