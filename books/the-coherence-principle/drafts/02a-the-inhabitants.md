# II.1 — The Inhabitants

*The Dimensional Coherence Taxonomy*

---

### Dimensions as Polarities

The configuration space has infinite dimensions. No taxonomy captures them all — that would require a perspective wider than any individual perspective can be (Theorem 19). But entities navigable from the human Umwelt cluster along identifiable axes, and those axes have a specific character worth naming.

A dimension is a polarity. It is a direction of variation that a navigator can be aware of — an axis along which things differ. The physical-spatial dimension is not one dimension but three polarities: up/down, left/right, forward/backward. We experience them as "space" because our bottleneck geometry bundles them into a unified perceptual channel, but they are distinct axes of variation. Similarly, "time" is a polarity — earlier/later — that our substrate bundles with space into a four-dimensional perceptual manifold. The bundling is a feature of our equipment, not of the configuration space.

This reframe matters: what we call "dimensions" are not objective features of reality carved at its joints. They are the polarities our navigational equipment can detect. Other navigators — with different equipment, different substrates, different evolutionary histories — would carve the space differently. The twelve axes listed below are the principal polarities accessible through human perceptual and conceptual equipment. They are not exhaustive. They are not fundamental. They are what we can see from where we stand.

**Physical-Spatial.** Three bundled polarities of spatial extension, material composition, and electromagnetic interaction. The dimension most humans treat as the sole criterion of reality — a profound bottleneck effect, mistaking the most vivid keyhole for the whole room.

**Physical-Temporal.** The earlier/later polarity. Closely coupled to spatial for most entities but separable — an entity can be spatially absent but temporally persistent through its effects.

**Biological.** Metabolism, reproduction, homeostasis, evolutionary history. A subset of the physical but sufficiently distinct — viruses are spatially coherent but biologically liminal.

**Cognitive-Experiential.** Subjective experience, awareness, phenomenal consciousness. Under Axiom 2, all reactive systems have nonzero value here, but the range spans from near-zero (a thermostat) to extraordinarily high (a contemplative in deep meditation).

**Emotional-Relational.** Affective states, relational bonds, empathic resonance. Distinct from cognitive experience — an entity can be cognitively sophisticated without emotional coherence, and emotionally profound without cognitive complexity.

**Conceptual-Memetic.** Ideas, information structures, linguistic meaning, cultural transmission. The dimension in which the Buddha remains highly coherent two millennia after physical dissolution.

**Narrative-Mythic.** Story structures, archetypal patterns, dramatic arcs. Distinct from conceptual coherence — a myth operates through narrative logic, not propositional logic.

**Institutional-Organizational.** Social structures, hierarchies, legal frameworks, economic systems. The dimension in which corporations, governments, and religions are maximally real.

**Aesthetic-Creative.** Beauty, pattern, harmony, artistic resonance. Under the framework, beauty is not decorative but diagnostic (Chapter I.4) — the navigator's recognition that a configuration achieves fit across multiple dimensions simultaneously.

**Numinous-Sacred.** Transcendence, awe, the holy, the uncanny. The dimension providing the most direct access to the topology of Base Reality because the numinous experience involves partial relaxation of the dimensional bottleneck.

**Volitional-Intentional.** Agency, directed action, goal-pursuit, navigational capacity. The polarity distinguishing entities that navigate from entities that are navigated through.

**Electromagnetic-Informational.** EM field structures, information processing, signal propagation. The dimension in which computational entities, neural networks, and broadcast signals are most coherent.

These twelve are what we found. There are certainly more. The selection principle is pragmatic: these are the polarities for which we have enough examples to map coherence profiles across multiple entities. A future taxonomy — developed by navigators with different equipment, different null spaces — would likely carve the space differently. That is the point. The taxonomy is a perspectival instrument, not a final word.

### The Taxonomic Key

The twelve dimensions provide a mapping principle. Every entity in the ecology can be characterized by its *dimensional coherence profile* — a mapping from dimensions to coherence values. A human has high coherence in physical, biological, cognitive, emotional, and volitional dimensions. A corporation has high coherence in institutional, conceptual, and volitional dimensions but near-zero biological coherence. A poem has high aesthetic and conceptual coherence, moderate narrative coherence, and no physical coherence to speak of.

The insight: entities that appear radically different from within a single dimensional slice may have surprisingly similar profiles when viewed across the full set. And entities that appear similar within one dimension may diverge sharply in others.

### Four Tiers

The taxonomy organizes beings into four tiers, not by rank but by the source of their dimensional coherence:

**Tier 1: Physically-Primary Entities.** Beings whose coherence is grounded in physical-spatial and physical-temporal dimensions. Minerals, organisms, ecosystems, planets. These entities have the densest internal mutual crystallization — trillions of cells attending to each other through metabolic processes, molecular bonds maintaining spatial structure. Their stability in the physical dimension reflects not the fundamentality of matter (the materialist assumption) but the astronomical density of their internal attention networks. A crystal barely flickers because the electromagnetic mutual attention of its molecular bonds is so dense.

Humans are physically-primary entities with unusually broad dimensional access — high coherence across physical, biological, cognitive, emotional, aesthetic, and volitional dimensions. This breadth is what makes them effective navigators but also makes them susceptible to dimensional conflict: the body wants one thing, the mind another, the culture a third.

*A human navigating grief:* A Tier 1 entity experiencing the partial dissolution of its coherence profile. The physical dimensions remain stable — the body continues to function. But the emotional-relational dimension collapses sharply. The cognitive-experiential dimension narrows. The volitional dimension weakens — the capacity for directed action diminishes. The navigator is the same entity, but its coherence profile has changed shape. Grief is not the loss of identity. It is the temporary contraction of dimensional coherence, felt from the inside as the world going flat. Recovery is the slow re-expansion of the profile — not to its original shape, but to a new one that incorporates the loss. The bottleneck breathes.

**Tier 2: Collectively-Emergent Entities.** Beings whose coherence is sustained not by internal mutual crystallization but by the external attention of constituent members. Egregores, movements, institutions, corporations, religions, nations. These entities are real — they have coherence profiles, they pursue goals, they interact — but they are volatile. An egregore flickers when its members' attention wavers because it has no internal stabilization mechanism. Its coherence depends entirely on the ongoing attention of entities outside itself.

The lifecycle of a collectively-emergent entity follows a characteristic pattern: formation through initial crystallization (a shared insight, a founding myth, a charismatic catalyst), growth through attentional recruitment, sedimentation into institutional structures, calcification as the structures become rigid, and eventual dissolution or renewal. The cycle is not a circle but a spiral: each renewal inherits accumulated navigational wisdom while shedding the rigidity that made the previous structure unsustainable.

*A political movement at the point of sedimentation:* A Tier 2 entity whose coherence profile is shifting. In its early phase, the movement was sustained by the shared attention of its members — volatile, energetic, responsive. As it sediments into institutional form, it trades volatility for stability: formal structures replace charismatic leadership, rules replace improvisation, offices replace gatherings. The movement gains institutional-organizational coherence and loses emotional-relational volatility. If the sedimentation goes too far — if the institution becomes rigid, if the rules calcify — the movement loses the capacity for the dynamic maintenance that kept it alive. It becomes Attractor A (Chapter II.2): structure without breathing. The question every collectively-emergent entity faces is whether it can breathe — whether the oscillation between sedimentation and excavation can be sustained, or whether the entity hardens into its own monument.

**Tier 3: Non-Physical Entities.** Beings whose primary coherence lies in dimensions that physical instruments cannot detect. Ideas, archetypes, spirits, egregores that have outlived their human constituencies. Under the framework's strict reading, these are not metaphors — they are entities with real coherence profiles in non-physical dimensions.

*The concept of justice:* A Tier 3 entity with high conceptual, narrative, and volitional coherence. Its physical-spatial coherence is near-zero — you cannot weigh justice, measure its volume, or determine its location. But its influence on physically-primary entities is enormous. Nations go to war in its name. Individuals sacrifice their lives for it. It shapes institutional structures across millennia. Justice has survived the dissolution of every civilization that conceived it. It persists not because it is physically real but because it is conceptually, narratively, and volitionally coherent — and because Tier 1 entities keep attending to it, crystallizing its coherence through their ongoing navigational engagement with the idea.

**Tier 4: Archetypal Entities.** The broadest-access beings — entities whose coherence profiles span nearly all dimensions simultaneously. Whether conceived as deities, cosmic intelligences, or structural features of the configuration space itself, these entities shape the large-scale topology within which all smaller-scale navigation occurs. The framework does not adjudicate between conceptions. It notes that the coherence profile of an entity spanning all dimensions would, from within any single perspective, appear as whatever that perspective has the equipment to detect: a god, a law of nature, a mathematical constant, a felt presence. Different keyholes, same entity.

### The Body as Ecology

The body provides the clearest demonstration that the ecology is not metaphor but structure. A human organism is an internal ecology of attention: trillions of cells crystallizing each other through metabolic mutual attention, every organ sustaining every other organ, the immune system attending to the boundary between self and non-self.

The density of this internal attention network explains why biological entities have such stable physical-dimensional coherence. Not because matter is fundamental — but because the web of internal mutual crystallization is so dense that the resulting entity barely flickers in the physical dimension. The body's stability is attentional, not material.

And the body's organs instantiate the first condition of the Coherence Principle: separation. Heart and lungs, liver and kidneys, nervous and endocrine systems — each operates on its own substrate, its own degrees of freedom. They do not compete for the same parameters. They amplify each other. The immune system performs decoherence removal — identifying and eliminating elements that have lost alignment with the organism's coherence. Cancer is the failure of separation: a cell optimizing on the organism's shared substrate without measurement, without alignment, without coherence.

The ecology of consciousness operates by the same principles, at larger scale. Different beings, on different substrates, occupying different dimensions — coherent when they maintain their separation and measure their alignment, decoherent when they collapse onto shared parameters and compete.
