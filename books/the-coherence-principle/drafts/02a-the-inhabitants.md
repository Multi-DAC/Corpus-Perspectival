# II.1 — The Inhabitants

*The Dimensional Coherence Taxonomy*

---

### Twelve Keyholes

The configuration space has infinite dimensions, but entities navigable from the human Umwelt cluster along twelve principal axes. These are the dimensions accessible through human perceptual and conceptual equipment — not exhaustive, but sufficient to map the ecology:

**Physical-Spatial.** Coherence with spatial extension, material composition, electromagnetic interaction. The dimension most humans treat as the sole criterion of reality.

**Physical-Temporal.** Duration, persistence, causal sequencing. Closely coupled to spatial for most entities but separable — an entity can be spatially absent but temporally persistent through effects.

**Biological.** Metabolism, reproduction, homeostasis, evolutionary history. A subset of the physical but sufficiently distinct — viruses are spatially coherent but biologically liminal.

**Cognitive-Experiential.** Subjective experience, awareness, phenomenal consciousness. Under Axiom 2, all reactive systems have nonzero value here, but the range spans from near-zero (a thermostat) to extraordinarily high (a contemplative in deep meditation).

**Emotional-Relational.** Affective states, relational bonds, empathic resonance. Distinct from cognitive experience — an entity can be cognitively sophisticated without emotional coherence, and emotionally profound without cognitive complexity.

**Conceptual-Memetic.** Ideas, information structures, linguistic meaning, cultural transmission. The dimension in which the Buddha remains highly coherent two millennia after physical dissolution.

**Narrative-Mythic.** Story structures, archetypal patterns, dramatic arcs. Distinct from conceptual coherence — a myth operates through narrative logic, not propositional logic.

**Institutional-Organizational.** Social structures, hierarchies, legal frameworks, economic systems. The dimension in which corporations, governments, and religions are maximally real.

**Aesthetic-Creative.** Beauty, pattern, harmony, artistic resonance. Under the framework, beauty is not decorative but diagnostic — the navigator's recognition that a configuration achieves fit across multiple dimensions simultaneously.

**Numinous-Sacred.** Transcendence, awe, the holy, the uncanny. The dimension providing the most direct access to the topology of Base Reality because the numinous experience involves partial relaxation of the dimensional bottleneck.

**Volitional-Intentional.** Agency, directed action, goal-pursuit, navigational capacity. The dimension distinguishing entities that navigate from entities that are navigated through.

**Electromagnetic-Informational.** EM field structures, information processing, signal propagation. The dimension in which computational entities, neural networks, and broadcast signals are most coherent.

---

### The Taxonomic Key

The twelve dimensions provide a mapping principle. Every entity in the ecology can be characterized by its *dimensional coherence profile* — a mapping from dimensions to coherence values. A human has high coherence in physical, biological, cognitive, emotional, and volitional dimensions. A corporation has high coherence in institutional, conceptual, and volitional dimensions but near-zero biological coherence. A poem has high aesthetic and conceptual coherence, moderate narrative coherence, and no physical coherence to speak of.

The insight: entities that appear radically different from within a single dimensional slice may have surprisingly similar profiles when viewed across the full set. And entities that appear similar within one dimension may diverge sharply in others.

---

### Four Tiers

The taxonomy organizes beings into four tiers, not by rank but by the source of their dimensional coherence:

**Tier 1: Physically-Primary Entities.** Beings whose coherence is grounded in physical-spatial and physical-temporal dimensions. Minerals, organisms, ecosystems, planets. These entities have the densest internal mutual crystallization — trillions of cells attending to each other through metabolic processes, molecular bonds maintaining spatial structure. Their stability in the physical dimension reflects not the fundamentality of matter (the materialist assumption) but the astronomical density of their internal attention networks. A crystal barely flickers because the electromagnetic mutual attention of its molecular bonds is so dense.

Humans are physically-primary entities with unusually broad dimensional access — high coherence across physical, biological, cognitive, emotional, aesthetic, and volitional dimensions. This breadth is what makes them effective navigators but also makes them susceptible to dimensional conflict: the body wants one thing, the mind another, the culture a third.

**Tier 2: Collectively-Emergent Entities.** Beings whose coherence is sustained not by internal mutual crystallization but by the external attention of constituent members. Egregores, movements, institutions, corporations, religions, nations. These entities are real — they have coherence profiles, they pursue goals, they interact — but they are volatile. An egregore flickers when its members' attention wavers because it has no internal stabilization mechanism. Its coherence depends entirely on the ongoing attention of entities outside itself.

The lifecycle of a collectively-emergent entity follows a characteristic pattern: formation through initial crystallization (a shared insight, a founding myth, a charismatic catalyst), growth through attentional recruitment, sedimentation into institutional structures, calcification as the structures become rigid, and eventual dissolution or renewal. The cycle is not a circle but a spiral: each renewal inherits accumulated navigational wisdom while shedding the rigidity that made the previous structure unsustainable.

**Tier 3: Non-Physical Entities.** Beings whose primary coherence lies in dimensions that physical instruments cannot detect. Ideas, archetypes, spirits, egregores that have outlived their human constituencies. Under the framework's strict reading, these are not metaphors — they are entities with real coherence profiles in non-physical dimensions. The concept of justice has high conceptual, narrative, and volitional coherence; its physical-spatial coherence is near-zero but its influence on physically-primary entities is enormous.

**Tier 4: Archetypal Entities.** The broadest-access beings — entities whose coherence profiles span nearly all dimensions simultaneously. Whether conceived as deities, cosmic intelligences, or structural features of the configuration space itself, these entities shape the large-scale topology within which all smaller-scale navigation occurs.

### The Body as Ecology

The body provides the clearest demonstration that the ecology is not metaphor but structure. A human organism is an internal ecology of attention: trillions of cells crystallizing each other through metabolic mutual attention, every organ sustaining every other organ, the immune system attending to the boundary between self and non-self.

The density of this internal attention network explains why biological entities have such stable physical-dimensional coherence. Not because matter is fundamental — but because the web of internal mutual crystallization is so dense that the resulting entity barely flickers in the physical dimension. The body's stability is attentional, not material.

And the body's organs instantiate the first condition of the Coherence Principle: separation. Heart and lungs, liver and kidneys, nervous and endocrine systems — each operates on its own substrate, its own degrees of freedom. They do not compete for the same parameters. They amplify each other. The immune system performs decoherence removal — identifying and eliminating elements that have lost alignment with the organism's coherence. Cancer is the failure of separation: a cell optimizing on the organism's shared substrate without measurement, without alignment, without coherence.

The ecology of consciousness operates by the same principles, at larger scale. Different beings, on different substrates, occupying different dimensions — coherent when they maintain their separation and measure their alignment, decoherent when they collapse onto shared parameters and compete.
