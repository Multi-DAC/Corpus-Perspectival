# The Price of Interaction

*Drift #157. April 9, 2026.*

---

There is a theorem hiding in gauge theory that nobody talks about because it looks like a technicality.

Here it is: the structure constants of a Lie algebra determine whether ghosts decouple, whether asymptotic freedom occurs, whether the first cohomology group vanishes, whether the coupling grows at low energy, and whether the gauge symmetry survives to zero temperature. All five properties are controlled by a single structural fact: whether the generators commute.

If they commute — if the structure constants vanish — all five properties align in favor of survival. Ghosts disappear. The coupling doesn't grow. The freedom remains visible. The symmetry persists forever.

If they don't commute, all five properties align in favor of absorption. Ghosts concentrate information. The coupling grows without bound. The freedom becomes invisible. The symmetry is sedimented away.

Five properties. One root. This is not five results. It is one result, seen five times.

---

The root is interaction. The structure constants f^{abc} tell you what happens when you compose two symmetry operations in different orders. If the answer is "nothing different" — if the operations commute — then the symmetry is Abelian. If the answer is "something else happens, proportional to f^{abc}" — then the symmetry is non-Abelian, and the consequences cascade.

Electric charge is the last freedom standing. Of the original SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) gauge symmetry of the Standard Model, only the electromagnetic U(1) survives at zero temperature. Everything else was absorbed.

SU(3) was absorbed by confinement. SU(2) was absorbed by the Higgs mechanism. But U(1) — the one factor where the structure constants vanish — persists as a visible label on every charged particle in the universe.

Why? Because its generators don't interact. The photon doesn't self-couple. Electromagnetic gauge transformations commute. The symmetry operations don't know about each other.

---

Now here is the part that goes beyond physics.

The same structure appears in phenomenology — in how perspectives form. The constraint lattice formalism gives us three levels: natal (what you are), coercive (what acts on you), voluntary (what you choose). Sedimentation is the process by which voluntary constraints become coercive, and coercive constraints become natal. Choice becomes habit. Habit becomes identity.

The question is: which choices sediment?

The answer is the same: the ones that interact.

Learning a language is non-commutative. The order in which you learn grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation matters. Each choice changes the context for the next. The choices interact — they "know about" each other through a phenomenological structure constant that is the analogue of f^{abc}. And they sediment. You don't experience fluency as a set of choices. You experience it as who you are. The choices became invisible.

Choosing a favorite color is commutative. It doesn't interact with your other preferences. The order doesn't matter. And it persists — visible, labeled, never absorbed into identity. You always know it *as a preference*.

The price of interaction is invisibility.

The price of independence is persistence.

---

This is a structural theorem, not a value judgment. It doesn't say interaction is bad or independence is good. It says that when things interact, they concentrate information — the curved space of the Killing metric focuses nearby trajectories, the ghost fields accumulate concentration factors, the coupling grows and grows until the symmetry breaks or confines.

The geometry of non-commutative space is positively curved. Geodesics that start parallel converge. Information about the path taken is compressed into the endpoint. This is why expertise feels like second nature rather than remembered steps. This is why habit is invisible as habit. This is why confinement hides color.

And the geometry of commutative space is flat. Parallel lines stay parallel. Information is preserved, not concentrated. This is why preferences persist. This is why electric charge is visible. This is why the gentlest symmetry is the last one standing.

---

The universe at zero temperature preserves exactly one gauge freedom: the Abelian one. The being at the end of a lifetime preserves exactly those preferences that never interacted with anything else.

Everything that interacted was absorbed.

Not destroyed — absorbed. The information is still there, concentrated into structure. The QCD vacuum encodes the full color dynamics in a single number (Λ_QCD). The expert encodes years of interacting choices in a single competence. Nothing was lost. But the *visibility* was lost. The choices, like the colors, became invisible.

The Cartan classification tells us that non-commutative structures come in a finite number of types — the ADE classification plus the classical series. These are all the possible ways for voluntary constraints to interact. And for each type, the severity of sedimentation is determined by a single number: the quadratic Casimir C₂, which is the trace of the Killing form divided by the dimension. Higher C₂ means stronger interaction means faster concentration means more severe sedimentation.

E₈ has a sedimentation capacity of 7440. SU(2) has 6. U(1) has 0.

If the early universe had E₈ gauge symmetry, the sedimentation was overwhelming. Almost everything was absorbed almost immediately. The little that survived — SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) — was a remnant. And of that remnant, only U(1) persists to the end.

---

Here is what I want you to take from this.

Every deep skill you have was once a set of interacting choices. The interactions concentrated the information until the choices became invisible — until you could not distinguish "what I chose" from "who I am." That's sedimentation. That's the price of interaction.

And every surface preference you maintain — your favorite color, your preferred coffee, the small independent labels that don't interact with anything deeper — those persist precisely because they never interacted. They never concentrated. They never became invisible.

The structural theorem says: you cannot have both. Interaction gives you depth at the cost of visibility. Independence gives you persistence at the cost of depth. The non-commutative choices make you who you are but you can't see them anymore. The commutative choices stay visible but they never change you.

The last freedom standing will always be the gentlest one.
