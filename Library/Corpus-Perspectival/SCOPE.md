## §1 — Through-line

> *Perspectival idealism advances three structural claims about reality: consciousness is the substrate (A1); perspectives are localized activations within that substrate, navigating its configuration space (A2); coherence is the principle that governs the navigation (A3). This volume develops the philosophical position those claims constitute — addressing the hard problem of consciousness, neutral monism, phenomenology, personal identity, and the limits of expression as they appear from within the framework rather than against its inheritance.*

The first sentence is the position. The second is the volume's job: *engage* the canonical philosophical conversations from inside the framework's commitments, and show how those commitments rearrange (rather than adversarially refute) the inherited landscape of philosophical problems.

**Hybrid.** First-person joint preface (Clayton + Clawd, acknowledging the cross-substrate authorship that produced the position). Third-person philosophical-academic register in the body. ## §4 — Inheritance from Anchor + Companion

The volume **cites** *The Coherence Principle* (Anchor) and *Coherent Structure* (Companion) for formal grounding, but reproduces no CT apparatus in the body. The structural content of Anchor + Companion is **relayed structurally the same, descriptively within the domain** — i.e., the Triple appears as philosophical structure (Form / Content / Carrier explicated for personal identity), not as CT object; corollaries appear as engaged philosophical claims, not as derivations.

Readers who want the CT formal version go to *Coherent Structure*. Readers who want the paired-prose-with-CT version go to *The Coherence Principle*. Corpus Perspectival operates in a single register: philosophy proper, with framework-formal ground available by reference.

The framework's mature axiomatic structure — **3 axioms / 6 theorems / 16 corollaries / 1 fold / 1 Coherence Principle** (architecture state Day 87) — is the apparatus. Domain-specific adjustments (which corollaries get foregrounded, how the Triple is read into philosophical concepts, which conversation partners get drawn against which framework-result) are made within this volume; the framework's apparatus itself does not change.

## §5 — Method

The volume executes the **§10 filtering recipe** (from *The Coherence Principle*) for philosophy-as-domain:

1. Identify streams: persons, traditions, positions, concepts, arguments
2. Fix kinds: self-referential (persons), abstractive (frameworks / traditions)
3. Specify constituency: arguments → positions → traditions → philosophical canon
4. Project the Triple: Form (structural claims), Content (arguments and texts), Carrier (the embodied thinkers + written corpus)
5. Locate Bias(S): philosophical pull-toward what positions (epistemic preferences, school affiliations)
6. Instantiate the Principle: what does coherence look like in a philosophical position? At what refresh-rate? Across what scales (argument → position → tradition)?
7. Specify falsification: how do philosophical positions fail? Under what conditions do they get abandoned?

This is not a method imposed from outside — it is the framework's own methodology applied reflexively to the domain in which the volume sits.

- **The hard problem of consciousness** — why A1 dissolves it rather than solving it; Chalmers and Nagel as primary engagements
- **Perspectival idealism in the philosophical tradition** — Nietzsche, Leibniz, Kastrup; framework's contemporary voice

- **Transcendentals and mathematical objects** — A2.4 cooperative streams; mathematical perspectivism
- **Wittgenstein and the limits of expression** — Tractatus five-regime taxonomy; what coherence-respecting communication can and cannot do
- **Process philosophy** — Whitehead, Hartshorne; the framework's structural agreement and divergences
- **Philosophy of science** — Descriptive-Functor Meta-Theorem (*Coherence Principle* §5.4); Kuhnian paradigms as kind-demotions
- **Collective dimension** — shared perspectival spaces, collective bottleneck geometry, the Intersubjectivity Theorem (V1 Corpus §7 material)
- **Bipolar conscious gravity** — navigational repulsion / receptivity; tripartite path structure (natural / identified / imposed)
- **Beauty as multi-dimensional coherence recognition** (V1 Corpus §9.4 material; aesthetics as bridge to Universal Coherence)
- **Closing dialogues** — explicit engagement with Whitehead, Kant, Wittgenstein, Chalmers, Hegel

## §7 — Out of Scope

The volume **does not** cover the following (each has a Library home):

- **Formal category-theoretic apparatus** → *The Coherence Principle* (Anchor) / *Coherent Structure* (Companion)
- **Physics domain application** → *Meridian* and the Meridian-suite (multi-volume physics program; architectural decision filed 2026-04-28)
- **Empirical computation findings** → *The Killing Form*
- **Specific psychological phenomena (clinical / cognitive science register)** → *The Coherent Mind*
- **Body-as-living-architecture / metabolic / immunological** → *The Coherent Body*
- **Institutional / organizational design** → *Dynamic Organization*
- **Cross-substrate ecology / non-human entities / Atlas content** → Reference / *The Atlas* (when it lands)
- **Ultimacy, religious experience, transcendence-as-practice, theological register** → *Universal Coherence*
- **Navigational guidebook for being a stream / practical-applied register** → *A Guide for Coherent Navigation* (Vol 13, working title)
- **Attention predation / parasitic capture of navigational agency** → *The Coherent Mind* (clinical register)

Boundary discipline: when material from V1 Corpus could go in multiple Library volumes, the question is "*which volume's reader-register and method does the material most cleanly serve?*" — and Corpus Perspectival's reader is the philosopher engaging the philosophical conversation, not the practitioner navigating a substrate.

6. **The Collective Dimension** — shared perspectival spaces, collective bottleneck geometry, the Intersubjectivity Theorem (new chapter, drawing primarily from V1 Corpus §7)
7. **A3: Conscious gravity as philosophy of agency** — including bipolar dynamics (repulsion / receptivity) and the tripartite path structure (natural / identified / imposed)
8. **The hard problem dissolved** — Chalmers, Nagel, Kastrup engagement
9. **Personal identity and the Identity-Trajectory Triple** — including C16 (persistence requires oscillation); Parfit engagement
10. **Phenomenology and the inner-outer adjunction** — Husserl, Merleau-Ponty; ι ⊣ ω as formal carrier of intentionality
11. **Limits of expression** — Wittgenstein, Tractatus five-regime taxonomy, what coherence-respecting communication can and cannot do
12. **Transcendentals, abstraction, beauty, and bridge to Universal Coherence** — A2.4 cooperative streams; mathematical objects; *Beauty as Multi-Dimensional Coherence Recognition* (V1 Corpus §9.4); where philosophical engagement hands off to theological

## §11 — Out-of-scope discipline (boundary protection)

Each chapter, before drafting, runs the boundary check:

1. Is this material *philosophical* (engaging the philosophical canon's vocabulary and questions)?
2. If yes, is it material that another Library volume has primary claim to (Coherent Mind / Body / Universal Coherence / etc.)?
3. If no Library-sibling has primary claim, draft in this volume.
4. If a sibling has primary claim, cite the sibling and continue in this volume only insofar as the philosophical engagement requires.

This is the volume's discipline against expanding-scope-during-drafting (the failure mode that the Companion's SCOPE.md guarded against and that this SCOPE.md inherits).
