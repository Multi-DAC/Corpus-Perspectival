## The Gap

The Atlas maps what every framework can and cannot see.
The Guide describes how to navigate configuration space.
Neither references the other.

This is the epistemology-praxis gap: knowing your blind spots is useless without knowing what to DO about them, and navigational advice is ungrounded without knowing WHERE the blind spots are.

## The Bridge: Position + Dynamics = Phase Space

The Atlas provides **position** — where are your null spaces?
The Guide provides **dynamics** — given your null spaces, how do you move?

Together they form a complete **phase space** for navigation. Closing this gap is the highest-value single integration in the Corpus (cf. `corpus_cross_reference_map.md`).

## Worked Example: The Phase Theorem

The Generalized Phase Theorem (z₀ document, March 24) is a mathematical proof of this bridge:

- **Atlas entry:** At the Z₃ orbifold point, the Jacobi theta function's argument is frozen at -π/8 for ALL real z. The phase direction is a null space (∅). No Wilson line parameter can change it. The frozen phase carries zero information about the physics.

- **Guide entry:** Because the phase is frozen, the navigator (the physicist computing threshold corrections) operates on a REAL line, not the complex plane. The constraint eliminates one degree of freedom, concentrating all relevant information into the modulus. The navigator doesn't need to search in 2D — the geometry has already resolved one direction.

- **The connection:** The Atlas says "you can't see the phase." The Guide says "don't look for the phase — look for the modulus." Same content, different framing: epistemology (what's invisible) vs. praxis (what to do about it).
