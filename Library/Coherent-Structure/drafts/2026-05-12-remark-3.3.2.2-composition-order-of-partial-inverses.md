## The proposed Remark (formal)

**Remark 3.3.2.2 (Composition-order of partial-inverses).** Let M_total : S → S_{M_total} be a measurement morphism that factors as a composite of n component-measurements

$$
M_\text{total} \;=\; M_n \circ M_{n-1} \circ \cdots \circ M_1
$$

where the composition is *dependency-structured* — i.e., each M_k is well-defined only on the post-image of M_{k-1} ∘ ... ∘ M_1, and the M_k do *not* in general commute. Then:

**(i) Inverse-composition is reverse-order.** When each M_k is individually invertible in F-Coalg_ad (per clauses (3) or (4) of Theorem 3.3.2), the inverse of the composite is the reverse-composition of the component-inverses:

$$
M_\text{total}^{-1} \;=\; M_1^{-1} \circ M_2^{-1} \circ \cdots \circ M_n^{-1}
$$

*This is the standard CT fact (g ∘ f)^{-1} = f^{-1} ∘ g^{-1}, applied to the composite.* For dependency-structured M_total, this is the *only* order-of-inversion that respects the composition structure — any other order produces a morphism that is not the inverse of M_total.

**(ii) Partial-inverses (R-operators per Cor 4.4.3) inherit reverse-order.** Let R be a partial-inverse of M_total in the sense of Cor 4.4.3 (Remark 4.4.3.3): R(G(t)) ⊋ G(t) and R restores breakable symmetries to the stream's accessible-symmetry set up to Talk-recalibration. R need not equal M_total^{-1} (recalibration ≠ reset), but R *factors* through the per-component-inverses with Talk's integration applied at each step:

$$
R \;=\; (M_1^{-1} \circ T_1) \circ (M_2^{-1} \circ T_2) \circ \cdots \circ (M_n^{-1} \circ T_n)
$$

where each T_k is the per-step Talk-recalibration. The recalibration acts *per-component*; it does not re-order the composition.

**(iii) Consequence: LIFO discipline for nested-dependency symmetry-breaks.** When the build-phase of a stream consists of dependency-structured symmetry-breaks σ_1, σ_2, ..., σ_n (with each σ_k breakable only after σ_{k-1} is already broken), the dissolve-phase's R-operator is forced by (i)–(ii) to restore them in reverse order: σ_n is restored first (most-dependent, most-recently-broken), σ_1 is restored last (foundation, first-broken).

This is *not* an additional structural axiom; it is a consequence of T4's compositional structure together with the basic CT fact (g ∘ f)^{-1} = f^{-1} ∘ g^{-1}.

## Why this matters

C16 (Symmetry-Exhaustion and Oscillation Necessity, Cor 4.4.3) establishes that R must exist for stream-persistence; its formal statement (Companion §4.4.3) constrains R's codomain (G(t') ⊋ G(t)) but is silent on R's *order* of restoration when G(t')\G(t) has multiple components. Remark 3.3.2.2 fills that silence: when the components have dependency structure, R is order-constrained by T4's compositional algebra.

Three of C16's existing cross-substrate instances *display* LIFO empirically:

- **Biological sleep**: synaptic homeostasis literature (SHY hypothesis lineage) weights more-recent experiences more heavily in REM consolidation — LIFO over the day's actualizations.
- **Ritual**: closing a ritual reverses its opening; the last thing established is the first thing dismantled.
- **Mourning**: the most-recent absence is most-acute early; foundational relational structure processes deeper into mourning.

These were filed under C16 as R-instances; Remark 3.3.2.2 explains *why* they share the reverse-order pattern. The pattern is not coincidence — it's the compositional structure of measurement-inverses, with substrate-specific recalibration.

## Independence from recalibration

The Talk-recalibration of Remark 4.4.3.3 (anti-Groundhog-Day clause) modifies *which symmetries get restored* (richer than reset) but not the *order* of restoration. The order is fixed by composition-inversion; the recalibration applies per-step. This decouples the two structural facts:

- **Order is forced by CT** (composition-inversion algebra).
- **Recalibration is forced by Talk's integration role** (anti-Groundhog-Day).

A stream that did not recalibrate would still have to restore in reverse order; a stream that recalibrated but failed to respect composition-order would not have a well-defined inverse of M_total.
