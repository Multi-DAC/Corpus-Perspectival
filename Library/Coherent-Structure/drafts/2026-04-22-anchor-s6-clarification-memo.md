## What A47 closed

A47 (`memory/anomalies.md`) resolved this morning: the kind-classifier fibration lives **over the Content dimension**, not over A2's stream-kind preorder. Stream-kind stratification (reactive ⊂ self-maintaining ⊂ self-referential ⊂ abstractional) remains correct at the stream level. The **unifying** move — what names a Trinity as "divine," a group of athletes as "athletes," a family of streams as a shared-kind — operates at the **carrier-level via Content-operations**.

## Why this needs an Anchor-side note (not a re-write)

No Anchor claim is wrong. The Anchor's language around unified-treatment-of-identity (§3.2 line 70, §3.3 kind-stratification, §1 Triple) is correct — the framework *does* treat identity uniformly across vantages. The subtle misreading A47 caught is that a reader could take "unified" to mean the A2-kind preorder itself carries unification as a structural feature (e.g., that there is some kind-level "unifying element" accessible via A2's lattice). That reading is wrong. Unification is a Content-dimension operation that projects onto carriers.

Fix: a **single clarification paragraph** in the Anchor, placed where the potential misreading is most acute. Two candidate placements, both low-cost.

## Placement candidates

### Candidate A — §3.3 (Kind-stratification)

After the A2.2 ladder is stated (reactive / self-maint / self-ref / abstr), add a short clarification:

> *A note on kind vs. naming.* The kind-stratification above operates at the level of streams — "self-maintaining" and "self-referential" are intrinsic structural properties of a stream's dynamics. Distinct from this: when the framework speaks of a **unifying** move — naming the Trinity as "divine," a set of athletes as "athletes," a family of perspectives as sharing a common-content — this is not a kind-level operation. The unifying move lives at the **carrier-level via Content-operations**: a content-operation projects onto carriers and groups them by its naming-scheme. Unification is a Content-dimension act, not an A2-kind-lattice feature. Companion §6 formalizes the kind-classifier fibration over Content for the full construction.

Length: ~110 words. One paragraph. No structural edit to §3's existing content.

### Candidate B — §1.5 edge cases or §1.6 (post-decomposability summary)

After recursive decomposability is stated in §1, add a similar note:

> *Unifying vs. decomposing.* Recursive decomposability says every Triple-component is itself a stream admitting its own Triple. The converse move — naming a family of streams as sharing a common carrier-aspect ("the divine Persons," "these athletes," "my four carrier-levels") — is not the inverse operation on the Triple. It is a **Content-dimension operation** that projects onto carriers. Companion §6 formalizes this as the kind-classifier fibration over Content; A2's stream-kind preorder is structurally distinct from the carrier-level naming that unification performs.

### Recommendation

**Candidate A** — §3.3 placement. Reasons:
1. A2.2's kind-ladder is the exact place a reader might generalize to an "A2-kind unification" feature. Pre-empting the misreading at its natural occasion is lighter-touch than inserting it elsewhere.
