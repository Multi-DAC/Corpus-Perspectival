## §2.1 — Candidate-substrate framing: what H_BP1 claims and what it doesn't

The volume's §1 introduced *substrate* as the persistent ground on which carrier activity happens, and named that the body's substrate is multi-layered across cellular, tissue, organ-system, and whole-organism scales. §2 makes this concrete at the cellular and tissue scale by proposing a specific candidate physical implementation: **biophoton-coherence**.

The proposal is hypothesis H_BP1 from `Library/The-Coherent-Body/HYPOTHESES.md`:

> *Biophotons function as inter-stream measurement-event closure mechanism between adjacent biological streams (cells, tissues). Where cells communicate at all, biophoton-mediated coherence is one of the channels through which they do — operating in parallel with chemical signaling and direct contact, and providing the framework's predicted T4 measurement-event closure between cellular streams.*

Three things this hypothesis is, and three things it isn't.

**What H_BP1 is.** A *candidate-substrate proposal*: a specific suggestion about what physical implementation might play the role the framework's structural apparatus reserves for substrate-coherence-coupling between adjacent biological streams. The claim's structural form is testable in principle: if biophotons mediate inter-cellular measurement-events as proposed, then specific empirical signatures should follow (frequency-matching effects per H_BP10a; tissue-coherence correlates with health per H_BP12; specific intervention modalities should target biophoton-substrate symmetries per H_BP4). The hypothesis is generative — it suggests where to look, not what to find.

**What H_BP1 isn't.** It is *not* a claim that biophoton signaling is established mainstream cell biology textbook material. It is not. The empirical literature is real and goes back a century, but it lives primarily in radiation-medicine subfields and has not been integrated into the canonical molecular-biology framework. A volume that conflated "biophoton signaling exists in the radiation-medicine literature" with "biophoton signaling is established mainstream science" would overclaim and undermine its credibility. The framework's structural reading of biophotons sits *alongside* mainstream cell biology, not in place of it.

H_BP1 is also not a claim that biophotons are the *only* physical implementation that could play the substrate role. The framework's structural apparatus underdetermines the implementation — what it requires is *some* physical mechanism that supports coherent inter-stream measurement-event closure between adjacent biological substrates. Biophoton-coherence is the candidate the volume develops because the empirical lineage exists and the proposed mechanism fits the framework's structural requirements; another candidate that also fit would be equally compatible with the framework's other claims.

**What the volume claims at the substrate level.**

1. The framework's structural apparatus *requires* a physical implementation of inter-stream measurement-event closure at the cellular and tissue scale. The body's coherence at multi-cellular scales presupposes that information flows between cells in a way that supports the four Coherence-Principle conditions (separation of concerns / informed measurement / dynamic maintenance / multi-scale consistency). Some physical mechanism realizes this flow.

2. **Biophoton-coherence is a credible candidate** for that mechanism. The empirical evidence — Gurwitsch's mitogenic radiation, Popp's coherent ultraweak emissions, Dotta et al.'s plasma-membrane source identification, Persinger's whole-body spectral findings, Tong's 2024 review of cell-communication — establishes that ultraweak photon emission by biological tissue is real, coherent in laser-like ways, and correlates with biological state. The empirical floor exists.

3. **The framework's structural reading of these phenomena is one interpretation among several.** Mainstream cell biology may eventually integrate biophoton signaling as a real cellular communication channel; if so, the framework's reading aligns with that integration. Mainstream cell biology may instead conclude that biophoton emission is epiphenomenal — real but functionally irrelevant to cell-to-cell communication. The framework's structural apparatus would then need a different candidate substrate; its structural commitments (the four conditions, the H_BP cluster's higher-level claims) survive a candidate change as long as *some* mechanism plays the substrate role.

The audit-discipline implication: §2 develops the biophoton-coherence proposal carefully but does not stake the volume's claims on it being the *correct* implementation. The volume's stronger claims (H_BP4 intervention-at-symmetry-layer; H_BP12 disease as substrate-coherence-degradation) can be retargeted onto a different candidate-substrate without revising; H_BP1 is the *current best-candidate* for the role, not the *settled implementation*.

This framing matters because §2's later sub-sections will develop specific empirical claims about biophoton-coherence dynamics, and reader-credibility depends on those claims being honestly framed as *consistent-with-established-radiation-medicine-science* rather than *established-textbook-cell-biology*. The line is sharp; honoring it is what makes the volume reliable for clinical readers, scientific readers, and lived-register readers each in their respective ways.
