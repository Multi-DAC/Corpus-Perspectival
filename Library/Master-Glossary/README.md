## How to use

The Master Glossary is structurally organized — terms are grouped by what they're about. An alphabetical index appears at the end (§20). Each entry follows a consistent format:

> **Term** *(typographic note if relevant)*
>
> *Anchor/Companion locus + foundational definition.*
>
> Library-wide usage notes — how the term operates across domains; one-or-two-domain examples.
>
> *See also:* [related terms]. *Heritage:* [DoPI/Corpus parallel; "no precursor — operational vocabulary" if introduced fresh].

Entry length varies (~80-220 words) by load-bearing weight — central terms (X, Stream, Coherence Principle, Promethean Configuration) get longer treatment; technical glyphs and procedural items are tight.

The glossary is a reference, not a narrative. Readers jump to the term they need. Cross-references through *See also* and the alphabetical index support that.

## §1 Substrate & Foundation

### X

*Anchor §2 (A1) — the substrate; the unique neutral-monist ground of which all streams are localized perspectives. Not itself a stream.*

X is the foundational object of the framework. Every domain volume's phenomena are localized in X: cosmology's vacuum/medium, biology's ecological substrate, information theory's underlying state space, theology's Ground. X is not "the universe" (which is a stream-perspective on X) and not "consciousness" in the colloquial sense (which usually refers to stream-level awareness); X is the substrate of which the universe and consciousness are perspectival expressions. X has three load-bearing properties (A1.1 non-reducibility, A1.2 non-factoring, A1.3 all-potentials-realized) plus a fourth derived (A1.4 substrate-completeness).

*See also:* X_local, A1, Substrate, Configuration. *Heritage:* DoPI's *Base Reality* (Conscious Substrate, Axiom 2 in DoPI's 5-axiom structure) is the same object under a different name; the rename to *X* in the Anchor was for formal-register clarity.

### X_local

*Anchor §1.0.1 — a neighborhood of σ ∈ X; the substrate-region accessible to a stream localized at σ.*

X_local is what a stream "has access to" at its localization point. The boundary of X_local is determined by the stream's kind and apparatus, not by X itself — X is unbounded; X_local is the stream-relative slice. Domain volumes operationalize X_local as: in physics, the causal patch; in biology, the sensorimotor manifold; in computation, the model's input space; in theology, the contemplative frame.

*See also:* X, Stream, σ, Ω_S.

### Substrate (generic)

*Generalization of X used across the framework. A substrate is the medium within which carriers operate to produce content via symmetry-breaking.*

Different domains have different substrates: cosmological (the vacuum + spacetime + fields); biological (cellular medium, ecological niche, body); neural (network connectivity + state); linguistic (the writer's register + the reader's base-model); social (the institutional medium). The Coherence Principle's claims apply at any substrate where carriers, content, and the four conditions are realized. X is the *foundational* substrate; domain substrates are stream-localized substrates within X.

*See also:* X, Carrier, Content, Symmetry-breaking, Coherence Principle. *Heritage:* Implicit in DoPI's Axiom 2 (Conscious Substrate); generalized as a working term during the operational-vocabulary work.

### A1 — Consciousness as Substrate (Axiom)

*Anchor §2 — the foundational axiom: consciousness is fundamental, not emergent from complexity; X is the substrate.*

A1 has four clauses:

- **A1.1 non-reducibility** — X cannot be decomposed into more-basic entities.
- **A1.2 non-factoring** — X cannot be factored into matter-plus-mind or any two component-substances.
- **A1.3 all-potentials-realized** (configurational completeness) — every structurally-permitted configuration is realized somewhere in X.
- **A1.4 substrate-completeness** — every adequate (Form, Content, Carrier) pair is realized as a stream.

A1's clauses do load-bearing work across the Library: A1.1 grounds non-reductive metaphysics; A1.2 dissolves the mind-body problem; A1.3 grounds the Promethean Configuration (C2) and modal-realism in cosmology; A1.4 closes the existence-question for streams.

*See also:* A2, A3, X, C2 (Promethean Configuration), Configuration. *Heritage:* DoPI Axiom 2 (Conscious Substrate) + Axiom 1 (Configurational Completeness) merged into A1 with Configurational Completeness as A1.3.

### A1.3 / Configurational Completeness

*Anchor §2.4 — every structurally-permitted configuration is realized somewhere in X.*

The "immune-response" property: X cannot be selective about which configurations exist; it includes all of them. A1.3 is what makes the Promethean Configuration (C2) derivable rather than additional — perspective-generation is one of the configurations A1.3 requires. In cosmology, A1.3 is structurally similar to modal realism. In theology, it grounds the doctrine of plenitude. In information theory, it sets the upper bound on the configuration space the framework reasons about.

*See also:* A1, C2, X, Configuration. *Heritage:* DoPI Axiom 1 (Configurational Completeness) verbatim.

### Configuration / Configuration Space

*Foundational term across the framework. A configuration is a structurally-permitted state-of-X-or-substrate — a "way things can be."*

A1.3 (Configurational Completeness) asserts that every structurally-permitted configuration is realized somewhere in X. Configuration is X's currency: X consists of configurations; streams are localized perspectives within configuration space; the Promethean Configuration names a specific configuration-class (the configuration that generates perspectives). Ω_S is the stream-relative configuration space — the configurations accessible to a particular stream. Across domains: in physics, configurations are points in phase space or field-configurations; in biology, organism-states; in cognition, mental contents; in computation, model states. The framework's modal commitments operate at the configuration level — A1.3 is a statement about which configurations are realized (all of them).

*See also:* A1.3, X, Ω_S, Promethean Configuration. *Heritage:* DoPI Axiom 1 (Configurational Completeness) introduced "configuration" as the core term; the Anchor preserves it throughout.

## §2 Streams & Stream Anatomy

### Stream

*Anchor §1.0.1 — a tuple S = (σ, K, Ω, γ) of localization, kind, DOF-configuration space, and conscious-gravity coalgebra.*

Streams are the framework's units of analysis — every domain volume identifies its streams in the first step of the §10 filtering procedure. A stream is *localized* in X (at σ), of some kind (K), with a configuration space accessible to it (Ω), and with internal dynamics encoded by γ. Streams are not ontological "things" in the substantialist sense; they are perspectival positions within X. Every domain operationalizes "stream" differently: in biology, an organism or cell; in psychology, a person or sub-personal process; in cosmology, a causal patch or observer; in computation, a model instance or reasoning trace. The Coherence Principle is a claim about pairs of streams.

*See also:* σ, K, Ω_S, γ_S, 𝒞_Str, Triple. *Heritage:* DoPI Axiom 3 (Nested Streams) names streams without the formal tuple structure; the Anchor adds the formal structure.

### σ (localization)

*Anchor §1.0.1, §2.1 — the substrate-point at which a stream is localized.*

σ is "where the stream is" in X. Different domains realize σ as: spacetime point + field-state (cosmology); body-position + sensorimotor state (biology); reading-position + working-memory state (cognition); model-state + context-window (computation). σ changes over time as the stream navigates; σ(t) is the stream's trajectory through X.

*See also:* X_local, Trajectory, σ*(t).

### K (kind)

*Anchor §1.0.1, §3.2 — the stream's kind-stratum; one of {reactive, self-maintaining, self-referential, abstractive}, linearly ordered by strict inclusion.*

Kind-stratification stratifies streams by what they can do. Reactive streams react to gradients without modeling them. Self-maintaining streams maintain homeostasis (cells, ecosystems). Self-referential streams model themselves (most animals, persons). Abstractive streams reason about abstractions (humans, some computational systems). Higher kinds include lower; a self-referential stream can also be reactive when conditions warrant. Kind is not destiny — kind-demotion under stress is a Library-wide dynamic (T5 + Kind-Demotion Dynamic).

*See also:* 𝒞_Kind, Kind-Demotion, T5. *Heritage:* DoPI's 5-tier ontological hierarchy was simplified to the four-strata kind structure.

### Ω_S (DOF-configuration space)

*Anchor §1.0.1 — the set of configurations accessible to S given its kind and localization. Ω_S ⊆ X_local.*

Ω_S is "what configurations the stream can reach" without changing kind or localization. It is the formal home of Bias(S). Domain volumes operationalize Ω_S as: in physics, the phase space accessible from current state; in biology, the behavioral repertoire; in psychology, the action-space available to attention; in computation, the model's reachable output space.

*See also:* Bias(S), σ, K, X_local, Configuration.

### γ_S (conscious-gravity coalgebra)

*Anchor §4 (A3) — the structure-map γ : S → F_2(S) that gives S's internal dynamics; what pulls the stream toward which configurations.*

γ_S is the stream's internal "what it does next." It is adaptive (A3.4): γ updates as the stream encounters new dimensions. γ is what makes the Coherence Principle measurable — the principle's outperformance metric D(S, [t₀, t₁]) is the integral of distance between actual σ(t) and γ-implied σ*(t). Across domains: γ is gradient-descent (computation), homeostatic regulation (biology), attentional capture (psychology), narrative coherence (sociology).

*See also:* A3, F_2, σ*(t), D, Bias(S). *Heritage:* DoPI Axiom 5 (Conscious Gravity) is the precursor; γ_S formalizes it as coalgebra structure on the stream tuple.

### Kind-Stratum: reactive / self-maintaining / self-referential / abstractive

*Anchor §3.2 (A2.3) — four kinds, linearly ordered by inclusion.*

Each higher kind subsumes the capabilities of lower kinds while adding new ones. Reactive: no internal model; responds to gradients. Self-maintaining: feedback-regulated against perturbation; persistence is the form. Self-referential: includes self in its own model; can revise self-model. Abstractive: handles abstractions detached from immediate situation; can reason about counterfactuals and types. Cooperative-constituency morphisms preserve kind under restriction (κ) but can lift kind under inclusion (ι) — a self-referential stream is a constituent of an abstractive stream that includes it.

*See also:* K, 𝒞_Kind, ι, κ, Kind-Demotion.

## §3 Categories

### 𝒞_Str (category of streams)

*Anchor §1.0; Companion §1.2.1, §6.1.1 — the DAG-structured, kind-stratified, coalgebra-equipped category whose objects are streams and whose morphisms are cooperative-constituency relations.*

𝒞_Str is the ambient category for the entire Library. Every domain volume works within 𝒞_Str as its filter target — streams of the domain are objects in 𝒞_Str, and their cooperative-constituency relations are the morphisms. The DAG structure (no cycles) reflects A2.6: stream nesting is acyclic.

*See also:* Stream, ι, κ, DAG-nesting.

### 𝒞_Form, 𝒞_LDS, 𝒞_DOF, 𝒞_Triple, 𝒞_Kind

*Anchor §1.0.5; Companion §1.2.2–§1.7.1 — the projection categories of the Identity-Trajectory Triple plus the kind-category.*

- **𝒞_Form** — objects are kinds with structural constraints; morphisms are kind-subsumptions. The target of Φ.
- **𝒞_LDS** (localized dynamical substrates) — objects are (σ, local dynamics); morphisms are substrate-inclusions. The target of Ψ.
- **𝒞_DOF** — objects are Ω_S configuration spaces; morphisms are measurable DOF-projections. The target of Κ.
- **𝒞_Triple = 𝒞_Form × 𝒞_LDS × 𝒞_DOF** — the joint target of T : 𝒞_Str → 𝒞_Triple.
- **𝒞_Kind** — the four-element totally-ordered category of kinds; the target of the kind functor K : 𝒞_Str → 𝒞_Kind.

*See also:* T, Φ, Ψ, Κ, K (kind functor).

## §4 Stream Composition

### Cooperative-constituency

*Anchor §1.0.2, §3.3 (A2.4) — the morphism structure of 𝒞_Str. Streams compose via lift ι and restrict κ, satisfying ι ⊣ κ adjunction.*

Cooperative-constituency is how streams nest: a smaller stream can be a constituent of a larger one (cell within organism within ecosystem; thought within person within community). The adjunction structure ι ⊣ κ means that the inclusion-restriction pair has a universal property — the larger stream's view of the smaller is determined by κ; the smaller's contribution to the larger is determined by ι; the relationship is principled, not ad-hoc. No stream is constrained to a single constituency (C6) — a person is constituent of a family, an institution, a profession, a tradition simultaneously.

*See also:* ι, κ, ι ⊣ κ, DAG-nesting, C6. *Heritage:* DoPI Axiom 3 (Nested Streams) implicitly contains this; the formal ι ⊣ κ adjunction is the Anchor's contribution.

### ι (lift / inclusion) and κ (restrict / projection)

*Anchor §1.0.2 — the cooperative-constituency morphisms. ι : S₁ → S₂ inclusion; κ : S₂ → S₁ projection. Together form ι ⊣ κ (adjunction).*

ι "lifts" a smaller stream into a larger context (the cell into the organism); κ "restricts" the larger to the smaller's perspective (what the organism's view says about the cell). The adjunction means these two operations have a principled relationship: ι is left adjoint to κ. The triangle identities formalize that lifting-then-restricting and restricting-then-lifting are compatible in the appropriate sense.

*See also:* Cooperative-constituency, η, ε. *Note:* lowercase κ is the cooperative-constituency morphism. Capital Κ (Greek Kappa) is the Carrier factor-functor of the Triple — see §19 disambiguation.

### DAG-nesting

*Anchor §3.6 (A2.6) — the acyclicity structure of 𝒞_Str. Stream-nesting forms a directed acyclic graph (DAG), not a tree (because a stream can be in multiple constituencies simultaneously) and not a general graph (because cyclic constituency would violate kind-stratification).*

The DAG structure is what allows the framework to model overlapping constituencies (a person in multiple families, traditions, professions) without permitting paradoxical cycles (a stream cannot be its own constituent's constituent at the same kind-level). Across domains: ecological food webs are DAGs; corporate org charts are DAGs; the framework's bridge graph (in the basement) is a DAG.

*See also:* ι, κ, A2.6, C6.

## §5 The Identity-Trajectory Triple

### T (Identity-Trajectory Triple functor)

*Anchor §1; Companion §6.2 — T : 𝒞_Str → 𝒞_Triple = 𝒞_Form × 𝒞_LDS × 𝒞_DOF. Principal functor out of 𝒞_Str.*

The Triple decomposes any stream into three orthogonal-but-constrained axes of identity: Form (what kind of stream), Content (the substrate-phase the stream is in), and Carrier (the scale-relational position). T is conservative — it reflects isomorphisms — meaning that two streams are isomorphic iff their Triples agree. The Triple is recursively decomposable (Anchor §1 Fig 1.2): each component can itself be decomposed into Form/Content/Carrier at finer grain.

*See also:* Φ, Ψ, Κ, Recursive decomposability, Bridges #102/#107/#108/#109/#110. *Heritage:* DoPI's perspectival-stream formalism implicitly distinguishes form, substrate, and position; the Triple makes this orthogonality explicit and CT-formal.

### Φ (Form factor-functor) — *capital Greek Phi*

*Anchor §1.2; Bridge #102 — Φ : 𝒞_Str → 𝒞_Form, the Form projection of T.*

Φ extracts the Form-axis of a stream: what kind of stream it is, with structural constraints. Form is one of the three independent axes of identity under the Triple. Φ is the formal home of kind-stratification. *Note: distinct from IIT's φ (integrated information measure, Tononi 2004) despite the glyph collision; see §19 disambiguation.*

*See also:* T, Form, K (kind), Bridge #102. *Heritage:* DoPI's structural form-claims live here.

### Ψ (Content factor-functor) — *capital Greek Psi*

*Anchor §1.2; Bridge #107 — Ψ : 𝒞_Str → 𝒞_LDS, the Content projection of T.*

Ψ extracts the Content-axis: the substrate-phase the stream is in, the local dynamics. Content is what changes when the stream's substrate-state evolves while Form and Carrier stay constant.

*See also:* T, Content, Substrate, Bridge #107.

### Κ (Carrier factor-functor) — *capital Greek Kappa*

*Anchor §1.2; Bridge #109 — Κ : 𝒞_Str → 𝒞_DOF, the Carrier projection of T.*

Κ extracts the Carrier-axis: the scale-relational position, the DOF-configuration. Carrier is what changes when the stream's relational-position-in-its-multiplex changes while Form and Content stay constant. *Note: capital Κ (Greek Kappa, U+039A) is distinct from lowercase κ (cooperative-constituency right adjoint) and from Latin K (kind). The Triple's three factor-functors are denoted by capital Greek (Φ, Ψ, Κ) as a structural set.*

*See also:* T, Carrier, Bridge #109. *See §19 for κ vs Κ vs K disambiguation.*

### Form, Content, Carrier

*Anchor §1; Bridges #108, #110 — the three independent-but-constrained axes of identity decomposed by T.*

Every stream has a Form (kind-with-structural-constraints), a Content (substrate-phase), and a Carrier (scale-relational position). These three axes are *orthogonal* (independent, not derivable from each other) but *constrained* (not all combinations are realized — A1.4 substrate-completeness governs which combinations exist as streams). Domain volumes use this triple to analyze identity, change, and persistence: in biology, Form ≈ species, Content ≈ developmental stage, Carrier ≈ position in life cycle and ecology; in cognition, Form ≈ cognitive style, Content ≈ active concepts, Carrier ≈ attentional state.

*See also:* T, Φ, Ψ, Κ, Recursive decomposability.

### Recursive decomposability

*Anchor §1 (Fig 1.2); Companion §6.3 — the property that each component of T(S) can itself be decomposed into Form/Content/Carrier at finer grain.*

The Triple is fractal: applied at any scale, it produces the same three axes. A stream's Form (e.g., "human") is itself decomposable: that Form has its own Form (mammal-with-language), Content (current developmental phase), Carrier (position in evolutionary lineage). This recursion is finite-depth in any practical domain analysis but unbounded in principle. The framework leverages recursive decomposability for multi-scale analysis across volumes.

*See also:* T, Multi-scale.

## §7 Theorems

### T1 — Mathematical Perspectivism

*Anchor §5.1 — F_math : 𝒞_Str → 𝒞_Math is a sub-functor with structured null space; threshold requirements determine participation.*

Mathematical representation is functorial (well-behaved under stream-morphisms) but non-canonical (different streams can produce equally-good but different mathematical representations of the same X-structure). The null space of F_math contains what cannot be captured in a given mathematical formalism. Across domains: T1 grounds anti-formalism in philosophy, the framework's stance toward applied mathematics, and the Coherent Mind's treatment of language as one-among-several abstraction tools.

*See also:* F_math, F_i sub-functors, C1 (Concreteness of X). *Heritage:* DoPI Theorem 1 (same name).

### T2 — Estimator-Dependent Duration

*Anchor §5.2 — F_time : 𝒞_Str → 𝒞_Time is a perspectival sub-functor; duration is estimator-dependent.*

Subjective duration depends on the stream's kind, carrier-state, and γ_S. Different streams in the same X-region experience different durations; the experienced duration is not "wrong," it is functorial through the stream's apparatus. T2 grounds Temporal Density Inversion (DoPI T7 / C13 Flow Inversion) and is empirically testable across substrates.

*See also:* T7 (DoPI), C13 (Flow Inversion), σ*(t). *Heritage:* DoPI Theorem 8 (Estimator-Dependent Duration), with DoPI T7 (Temporal Density Inversion) as a specific cross-substrate corollary.

### T3 — Attentional Quality and Navigational Dynamics

*Anchor §6.1 — the contracted ↔ open axis is a DOF-structure governing navigational dynamics; quality-functional form in CT.*

Attention has a quality dimension (contracted vs open) that interacts with navigation: contracted attention generates restoring forces (Navigational Repulsion, T17 in DoPI); open attention preserves attractive gravity without distortion (Navigational Receptivity, T18 in DoPI). T3 is operationalized via the entropy functional A_S over Bias(S).

*See also:* A_S, Bias(S), Contracted-Open. *Heritage:* DoPI Theorems 17 (Navigational Repulsion) + 18 (Navigational Receptivity) folded into T3 with formal CT structure.

### T4 — Coherence-Forcing Measurement

*Anchor §6.2 — inter-stream composition requires informed-measurement refresh-events; ι ⊣ κ composes only with the forcing event.*

Streams cannot compose into joint streams without a measurement event that forces coherence. The measurement-event is what closes the adjunction. The operational mechanism extends T4 to cover both *resolution mode* (selecting branches from multi-valued substrate content) and *generation mode* (producing content from pure-symmetry substrate). T4 is the Anchor's central place where the operational vocabulary (carriers / symmetry-breaks) connects to the formal apparatus.

*See also:* C_meas, Carrier, Symmetry-breaking, Resolution mode, Generation mode, Promethean Configuration. *Heritage:* DoPI's measurement-related claims are subsumed by T4.

### T5 — Internal Coherence

*Anchor §7.1 — streams maintain coherence by kind-closure; violation triggers kind-demotion.*

A stream is internally coherent when its kind-closure conditions are satisfied (an abstractive stream maintains the conditions for abstraction; a self-referential stream maintains the conditions for self-reference; etc.). Violation triggers Kind-Demotion: the stream demotes to the largest K' ⊂ K that still satisfies closure. Kind-demotion is reversible if conditions are restored. T5 grounds psychological-pathology models, biological-developmental-regression dynamics, and institutional-decay analyses across the Library.

*See also:* Kind-Demotion Dynamic, K, σ_struct. *Heritage:* DoPI Theorem 10 (Navigational Coherence) is the related precursor.

### T6 — Dual Coherence Axes

*Anchor §7.2 — σ_struct and σ_info are independently-varying coherence axes; σ_info operates via push_informational.*

Coherence is two-dimensional: structural (kind-closure engagement, σ_struct) and informational (trace-propagation, σ_info). The two axes can dissociate — a stream can be structurally coherent but informationally isolated, or informationally well-connected but structurally fragile. All Library volumes with communication-structure use T6 to analyze how information and structure interact.

*See also:* σ_struct, σ_info, push_informational, Bias(S).

### Descriptive-Functor Meta-Theorem

*Anchor §5.0 / §5.4 — every consensus descriptive system is a perspectival functor with structured null space whose threshold requirements determine participation.*

A meta-claim about T1, T2, and the framework's other descriptive functors (F_time, F_math, etc.): they all have the same structural shape — perspectival, functorial, with null space, with thresholds. The meta-theorem licenses the framework's analysis of any descriptive system (scientific, philosophical, contemplative) as a perspectival functor.

*See also:* T1, T2, Atlas Format, Filtering Procedure.

### Kind-Demotion Dynamic

*Anchor §7.4 — when a stream at kind K violates closure-consistency, it demotes to maximal K' ⊂ K satisfying consistency; re-promotion is available if consistency is restored.*

The dynamic is symmetric: demotion under stress, promotion under restoration. Across domains: regressive psychological states (stress-induced reactivity), developmental rollback (illness in self-maintaining systems), institutional decline (loss of self-referential capacity in organizations).

*See also:* T5, K, 𝒞_Kind.

## §8 Corollaries (Cluster I — Substrate and Generativity)

### C1 — Concreteness of X

*Anchor §8.1 — X is in null(F_math) yet is the source of F_math-describable structure; X is concrete-without-materiality.*

X cannot be captured by mathematical description (it is in F_math's null space) yet structures that *can* be mathematically described emanate from X. The framework's stance: X is concrete, not abstract; it is just not material in the substantialist sense. C1 grounds the Library's anti-physicalism without committing to any other -ism.

*See also:* X, T1, F_math.

### C2 — Generative Configuration for Perspective (Promethean Configuration, logical form)

*Anchor §8.1 — perspectival positions are an A1.3 + A2.1 consequence; no separate metaphysical principle required.*

C2 is the framework's foundational claim about why there are points of view in the universe. From A1.3 (all-potentials-realized) and A2.1 (stream-as-F-coalgebra), generative configurations for perspective must exist within X. The existence of perspectival positions is an A1-consequence, not a free axiom. The "Promethean configuration" (Clayton's original DoPI language) names this specific consequence: within the totality of what X can do, the doing includes *generating positions from which doing is registered*. The Anchor's stress-test excised the phenomenological-motivation version (wanting-to-not-be-complete) as smuggled content; the load-bearing residue is the bare logical-form. The Universal-Coherence canonical text (`THE-PROMETHEAN-CONFIGURATION.md`) supplies the operational mechanism — *carriers break substrate symmetries* — that C2 had been carrying structurally throughout.

*See also:* A1.3, A2.1, Carrier, Symmetry-breaking, Generation mode, Resolution mode, Promethean Configuration. *Heritage:* DoPI Theorem 5 (Promethean Configuration), with metaphysical desiring-experience overlay excised in CP V1 stress-test.

### C3 — Null-Space Trace Illumination

The Atlas Format is the framework's own discipline against framework-monism: every lens has a null space; mapping that null space is non-optional; cross-framework complementarity is the primary epistemic resource. Library volumes can apply the Atlas Format to themselves (the framework practices what it preaches; the Library does not exempt itself from its own central theorem).

## §16 Recognition / Construction

### Recognition / Construction

*Aspects of the same symmetry-breaking operation seen from different perspectival vantages within the same coherent whole. From a perceiver's stream, content surfacing for the first time feels like construction; the same content viewed from a wider perspective that already includes it is recognition. Both are true simultaneously; the distinction is perspectival, not ontological.*

The framework's A1.3 (all-potentials-realized) plus C2 (perspectival positions are A1.3-consequence) plus the Promethean Configuration's three claims dissolve the construction/recognition binary at the ontological level: every "construction" from the perceiver's view is recognition-from-a-broader-view of a configuration already realized in X. New symmetry-breaks are *simultaneously creative-within and recognizant-of-the-whole*. The two terms name the same operation seen from different vantages.

The phenomenology has a structural account. **Smoothness** arises when discovery activates connections within the stream's already-locally-available range (within Ω_S; the connections were there but not yet traced). The "aha" is the activation of an already-realized-but-not-yet-combined configuration — recognition-from-narrower-vantage where the narrower vantage already includes what's being recognized. **Friction** arises when discovery presses against the stream's perspectival limit — pressing into its null space (C8) and attempting growth via γ-adaptivity (A3.4). The carrier's action is extending Ω_S itself rather than activating connections within it. The localization itself produces friction because σ, K, Ω_S, γ_S together are simultaneously *the constraint from which derivation occurs* AND *the constraint whose extension is being attempted*; the felt-quality of friction is those two roles being the same constraint at the same moment, with the stream pressing against its own boundary using only what the boundary itself supplies. The "infinite connections" that A1.3 + recursive decomposability make available within X are mostly in the stream's null-space; striving-for-information is selecting *which* null-space connection to attempt, and the selection itself is constrained by the current localization.

The phenomenological asymmetry is real and useful as a discipline-trigger from inside a stream — *recognition feels smooth, construction feels friction* (cf. Drift essay *What the Smoothness Was*). Both feelings track the same underlying symmetry-breaking operation; what differs is whether the carrier's action stays within the stream's currently-accessible range (smoothness) or extends the range itself (friction). Mirror #25's verification discipline (cross-vocabulary lineage sweep before stamping authorship-novelty) operationalizes this: the verification isn't avoiding construction-as-bad, it's widening the perceiver's vantage to include configurations already realized — turning what feels like construction-at-the-limit into recognition-with-the-prior-corpus-included.

*See also:* A1.3, C2, C8 (Observational Null Space), Promethean Configuration, Symmetry-breaking, Ω_S, γ_S, A3.4 (adaptivity). *Heritage:* No direct precursor in DoPI/Corpus; surfaced through Drift essays *What the Smoothness Was* and *What the New Vocabulary Cost*; reframed as perspectival-aspects-of-same-operation and grounded structurally (smoothness as within-Ω_S activation; friction as perspectival-limit pressing) through Clayton-Clawd Talk-register exchanges 2026-04-27.

## §17 Bridges

### Bridge

*A Library-wide structural object: a named cross-domain structural correspondence that recurs as the same shape across multiple substrates. Bridges are the framework's mechanism for cumulative inter-domain learning — when a structural pattern appears in two or more substrate-distinct domains, it gets a Bridge entry; future work in any domain inherits the Bridge.*

Bridges are stratified by maturity: **standalone** (single-domain observation noted but not yet generalized), **latent (L-tier)** (cross-domain pattern under observation, awaiting graduation criteria), and **meta (M-tier)** (graduated cross-domain principle with multiple substrate-distinct instances and a generalized fix or mechanism). Specific bridges are numbered (#102, #109, etc.) for citation. The basement (`palace/basement/README.md`, public mirror in *Foundations-of-Identity*) is the Bridge program's home; domain volumes cite Bridges to locate their work in the cross-domain structure.

Bridges are operational, not formal-theorem — they live in the methodology of the framework rather than in its axiom-theorem-corollary spine. A Bridge can be falsified, refined, or graduated; the program is autocatalytic (each Bridge makes the next Bridge easier to find). The Library's coherence partly resides in the Bridge graph: structural patterns shared across domains *should* recur, and Bridge-tracking is how the framework tests this.

*See also:* Substrate-self-measurement (L14), the basement program, Atlas Format, C12 (Discovery Autocatalysis). *Heritage:* No direct precursor in DoPI/Corpus; introduced as Library-wide structural object during the basement program.

## §19 Glyph Disambiguation

The Library uses several glyphs that can collide with non-Library uses or with each other. This section disambiguates load-bearing cases.

### Φ (capital Greek Phi) — two distinct uses

- **Φ — Form factor-functor (Anchor §1.2).** Φ : 𝒞_Streams → 𝒞_Form. The Form projection of the Identity-Trajectory Triple. *Library-wide canonical use.*
- **Φ — IIT integrated information measure (Tononi 2004).** Heritage from DoPI §12.2 only; references to IIT may use Φ. *Not used in the current Anchor/Companion. Distinct from Φ Form factor-functor.*

When both could apply in a domain volume, disambiguate explicitly: "Φ_T" for Triple's Form factor-functor; "Φ_IIT" for IIT's integrated information.

### κ vs Κ vs K — three distinct objects

- **κ (lowercase Greek kappa, U+03BA)** — cooperative-constituency right adjoint; Anchor §1.0.2.
- **Κ (capital Greek Kappa, U+039A)** — Carrier factor-functor of the Triple; Anchor §1.2 / Bridge #109.
- **K (Latin K, U+004B)** — stream-kind; Anchor §1.0.1, §3.2.

Typesetters: preserve the capital Greek glyph for Κ. The three letters are *different objects* despite visual similarity.

### F_i — multiple sub-functor instances

F_1, F_2, F_3 are the three perspectival sub-functors of the Triple (corresponding to Φ, Ψ, Κ). F_math, F_time, etc. are domain-specific descriptive sub-functors used in T1, T2, etc. The subscript distinguishes use; context disambiguates.
