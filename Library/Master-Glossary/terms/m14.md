# M14 — Substrate-Self-Measurement Cluster

## Definition (framework register)

The **canonical meta-bridge** (M-tier) absorbing eight non-overlapping substrate-instances of the substrate-self-measurement structural form. M14 is the *operational form of the Coherence Principle* — the structural mechanism by which substrate-internal carriers break substrate symmetries to produce or select content, with substrate-internality + unitarity-at-substrate-level + active-maintenance-as-stable-regime + asymptotic-symmetry-absorbs-ignorance + substrate-information-cannot-be-hidden + substrate-content-cannot-be-constrained-without-changing-substrate-symmetries as load-bearing sub-claims.

**Six load-bearing sub-claims:**
1. **Substrate-internality** — coupling intrinsic, not external observation
2. **Unitarity-at-substrate-level** — apparent stochasticity is observer-relative residue of partial knowledge
3. **Active-maintenance-as-stable-regime** — coherence held by carrier-mediated counter-coupling, not just passive isolation
4. **Asymptotic-symmetry-absorbs-ignorance** — substrate's structural symmetries carry resource information; substrate-respecting protocols access it without state-specific knowledge
5. **Substrate-information-cannot-be-hidden** — no pseudo-substrate-states for thermodynamic-class resources
6. **Substrate-content-cannot-be-constrained-without-changing-substrate-symmetries** — all intervention works at the symmetry layer, not the content layer

**Two regimes:** resolution mode (substrate has pre-existing multi-valued content; carrier selects branch) + generation mode (substrate has pure symmetry; carrier produces content from symmetry-breaking). Same structural operation; different substrate-starting-conditions. Per-term files: `resolution-mode.md`, `generation-mode.md`.
