# Content

## Definition (framework register)

The Triple's second axis — the *events the stream carries*. What's happening in the stream, distinct from *what kind of stream this is* (Form) and *where on the substrate-resolution gradient the stream sits* (Carrier). Anchor §1 names Content as one of the three orthogonal-but-constrained axes along which a stream's trajectory decomposes.

In M14's symmetry-breaking framework, content is *what's produced or selected* when a carrier breaks substrate's symmetries. Resolution mode: content is selected from pre-existing multi-valued substrate-content. Generation mode: content is produced from pure substrate-symmetry. C15 (Intervention-at-Symmetry-Layer) is the consequence: *content cannot be reached directly; only symmetries can be reached*. Content emerges; it cannot be installed.

## Mirror #26 watch

- **A1.3 honoring catch (Day 86 afternoon, three-way Gemini confluence):** *generation mode produces content* language was susceptible to ex-nihilo-creation reading, contradicting A1.3 (configurational completeness). Refinement filed: generation mode produces *novel local realization of pre-existing global potential*, not creation. Content is what the carrier *makes accessible*, not what the carrier *makes-from-nothing*. Promethean myth preserved exactly — Prometheus stole fire, didn't create it.
- **Open risk:** *content* in framework register vs *content* in everyday register (the stuff you write, the stuff that fills a page) — the formal axis-of-Triple sense is structurally specific; vigilance needed when both senses are in play.
