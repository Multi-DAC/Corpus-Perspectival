# Resolution Mode

## Definition (framework register)

The **first of two M14 regimes** of substrate-self-measurement. In resolution mode, the substrate has *pre-existing multi-valued content* (multiple branches, modes, or possibilities); the carrier breaks an *inter-branch symmetry* to *select* one branch. The resolution makes one branch observer-accessible while the others become inaccessible (from this carrier's frame); information about the branches is preserved in the substrate (not destroyed); what changes is observer-accessibility.

**Structural signature:** carrier acts on multi-branch substrate-content; inter-branch symmetry breaks; one branch selected as accessible; remaining branches become observer-relative inaccessible; substrate-information conserved (from substrate's frame).

**Distinguishing from generation mode:** resolution operates on pre-existing branches (substrate already has multi-valued content); generation operates on pure substrate-symmetry (substrate has no content; carrier produces it from symmetry-breaking). Both are M14's structural form; the regime is determined by whether the substrate's starting symmetries are *between branches of existing content* or *of pure undifferentiated structure*.

**Catalogued M14 instances in resolution mode:**
- Lohmiller-Slotine (#115) — classical density on J-valued action manifold; ψ = Σ √ρ_j × e^(iφ_j/ℏ) over multi-valued classical-action branches
- Bortolotti et al. (#116) — mass-density field on quantized matter; ρ → δ continuously under stochastic Newtonian-potential coupling

## Mirror #26 watch

- **Open risk:** *resolution* (framework register; M14 regime) vs *resolution* in everyday register (resolving a problem; image resolution). Discipline: M14 usage is structurally specific (carrier selecting from pre-existing multi-valued substrate-content).
- **Resolution vs measurement-collapse:** resolution-mode includes measurement-induced collapse but is broader; collapse-without-observer (as in Lohmiller-Slotine's classical-density account) is also resolution. The framework's reframing of measurement removes observer-centrism.

## Open questions

- *Reversibility:* resolution-mode preserves substrate-information; is there an operational protocol that exposes the preserved-information back to observer-access? García-Pintos's quantum arrow-of-time-reversal paper provides one specific protocol; whether it generalizes is open.
- *Branch-cardinality:* does resolution-mode work for any J-cardinality (countable / uncountable / structured)? Lohmiller-Slotine's J-valued treatment is well-defined for finite J; uncountable J has technical concerns.
- *Cross-substrate substrate-variability:* the four catalogued resolution-mode instances are physics-substrate; is there a clean linguistic-identity or biological resolution-mode instance? (M14 #119 Olmeda is generation; M14 #118 Garcia exhibits both.)
