# Configuration

## Definition (framework register)

A specific point in a stream's configuration space Ω — the *occupied state* of the stream at a given moment, including both its kind-constraints (Form) and its current Content-occupancy. Anchor §1.4 + §3.5 develop Ω as the measurable space of configurations a stream can occupy; trajectory σ*(t) is the actual sequence of configurations across time.

The term is load-bearing in two distinct ways:
1. **Stream-internal:** a stream's configuration at time t (what specific state it's in within its Ω)
2. **Generative-existential:** *generative configurations for perspective* (Anchor C2) — the structural conditions under which perspective can exist at all; this is the Promethean Configuration's referent at the operational layer

The two senses are not contradictory: C2's *generative configuration for perspective* is a particular configuration-type in the substrate-Ω; specific streams localize within configurations of that type.
