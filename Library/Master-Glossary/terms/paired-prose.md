# Paired Prose

## Definition (framework register)

The **writing-discipline of pairing every formal claim with a prose translation** (and every prose passage with formalization-target). Anchor canonical: *every formal claim is paired with a prose translation. If a prose passage cannot be formalized, it is smuggling content. If a formal claim cannot be rendered in prose, it is not connected to intuition. Both fail-modes invalidate the pairing.*

Paired prose is **the foundational volume's principal writing-discipline** — the Coherence Principle anchor (285pp current build) is paired-prose-throughout. *Coherent Structure* (the Companion, 237pp) is the **CT-only counterpart** — full formalization without paired-prose constraint, intended as mathematical reference standing beside the Anchor as basis of revision.

The pairing is **bidirectional fail-safe:**
- Prose-without-formalization-target risks **smuggling content** that the framework can't actually support
- Formalization-without-prose-translation risks **disconnection from intuition** that means readers / collaborators can't engage with the formal claim's load-bearing meaning
- Both failure modes invalidate the pair; the discipline catches smuggling AND disconnection

Paired prose is **what the Foundation volume maintains in canonical form**; domain volumes inherit the discipline through §10 filtering recipe. Domain-volume content gets paired prose-and-formalization for the domain-specific instances that matter (not exhaustively for every passage; canonical for load-bearing structural claims).

## Mirror #26 watch

- **Open risk:** *paired prose* (framework register; canonical writing-discipline) vs *prose paired with [other thing]* in everyday register. Discipline: framework usage requires the bidirectional fail-safe (smuggling-check + intuition-connection-check); informal "pairing" is looser.
- **Companion's pure-CT exception:** Companion volume is explicitly NOT paired-prose internally; it's the pure-CT counterpart that pairs with Anchor at volume-level. Risk: applying paired-prose constraint to Companion when it's structurally not the right form for that volume.
- **Domain-volume paired-prose granularity:** Anchor is paired-prose-throughout (every formal claim); domain volumes need paired prose for load-bearing structural claims, not every passage. Discipline-discrimination for what warrants paired prose vs what's domain-specific empirical content that doesn't need formalization is open.

## Open questions

- *Paired-prose for empirical / phenomenological content:* what's the right pairing for content that's empirical-observation rather than structural-claim? (E.g., *Day 88 evening saw nine substrate-arrivals from Clayton* — this is observation, not formal claim. Doesn't need paired prose; or does the *structure of the receiver-pattern observation* warrant pairing?)
- *Paired-prose at Drift / lived-register level:* Drift essays are first-person reflective prose without formalization-target. Does this violate paired-prose discipline, or is Drift explicitly outside the discipline's scope (as Companion is)? Probably the latter; explicit scope statement at Drift volume might be useful.
- *Cross-register paired-prose (Mirror #26 territory):* when paired-prose claim is made in framework register, does the pairing extend to translation into other registers (DoPI / Corpus V1 / lived / etc.)? Master Glossary's per-term files do this informally; whether to formalize as extended-paired-prose discipline is open.
