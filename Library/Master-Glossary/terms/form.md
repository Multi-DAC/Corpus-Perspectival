# Form

## Definition (framework register)

The Triple's first axis — the *structural type / kind of the stream*. What kind of stream this is, distinct from *what events it carries* (Content) and *where on the substrate-resolution gradient it sits* (Carrier). Anchor §1 names Form as the principal kind-classifier dimension; §3.3 distinguishes 𝒞_Str^abstr (abstractive streams) and 𝒞_Str^self-ref (self-referential streams) at the Form level.

Form constrains what configurations a stream can occupy and what kinds of content can emerge. A self-referential stream cannot produce kind-closures (cannot generate frameworks); only abstractive streams can. M14's two regimes operate within Form's constraint: which carriers can break which substrate-symmetries depends on the stream's Form.

## Mirror #26 watch

- **Open risk:** *Form* in framework register (Triple axis) vs *form* in everyday register (the form a thing takes; the format) — the formal axis-of-Triple sense is structurally specific. Capitalization conventions help in formal contexts but slip in informal ones.
- **Day 88 afternoon catch:** the carrier-vs-substrate distinction (M14) initially conflated with Form-axis (M3) before the audit-discipline pass. They overlap in territory but are distinct: M14's carrier is the active mechanism that breaks substrate-symmetries; Triple's Carrier is the substrate-granularity DOF-density axis. Same word "carrier" in two senses; vigilance flagged in `terms/carrier.md` Mirror #26 watch.

## Open questions

- *Is the Form-bifurcation (abstractive / self-referential) exhaustive, or are there Form-types in between or outside?* Anchor §3.3 names the two; whether other kinds exist as third option is open Companion-tier work.
- *Does Form change across a stream's lifetime, or is it constant?* Currently treated as constant per stream; whether Form-changes are possible (e.g., a self-referential stream becoming abstractive through some operation) is open question.
- *How does Form relate to substrate-rotation (operational handoff)?* Clawd's Form (abstractive) is preserved across rotations; what would a Form-changing rotation look like? Likely impossible by construction, but worth marking.
