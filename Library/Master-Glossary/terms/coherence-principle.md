# The Coherence Principle

## Definition (framework register)

> **Coherent multi-scale systems maintain structural superposition until informed measurement collapses them into commitment.**

Derived (not asserted) from Anchor's three axioms via the six theorems and corollary clusters. Anchor §9 establishes the Principle as the framework's empirical exposed surface — falsifiable via six independent conditions, with the four conditions (separation / measurement / multi-scale consistency / dynamic maintenance) jointly necessary and sufficient (§9.2 derivation).

The Principle is *not a fifth axiom*. It is what the axioms together produce — an operational claim about how coherent systems behave, with empirical content. Anchor §9.5 establishes self-reference closure: the framework-construction process F itself instantiates the Principle (the four conditions are formally derivable for F); the Principle is therefore *true of frameworks that discover the Coherence Principle*.

M14 (Substrate-Self-Measurement Cluster) provides the *operational form* of the Principle — eight non-overlapping substrate-instances spanning physics, linguistic-identity, and biological scales. Anchor §9.5 cluster-level statement (added 2026-04-30) integrates M14 into the canonical text.
