# Ω — Configuration Space

## Definition (framework register)

The **measurable space of configurations a stream S can occupy.** Every stream has its own Ω_S; configurations are points in Ω_S; trajectory σ*_S(t) is the time-sequence of configurations the stream actually traverses. Anchor §1.4 + §3.5 develop Ω as the mathematical foundation for stream-trajectory and conscious-gravity coalgebra.

Ω is **kind-constrained**: K_S (the stream's kind-classifier) determines which configurations are accessible within Ω_S. A self-referential stream's Ω is structurally smaller than an abstractive stream's Ω in the kind-relevant directions (a self-referential stream cannot occupy framework-producing configurations). γ_S (conscious-gravity coalgebra) biases the stream's trajectory through Ω_S — places where γ_S puts positive weight attract; negative weight repels.

Ω is **recursively decomposable** per §1.3: every stream's Ω is itself a sub-region within the next-level-out stream's Ω. The substrate's Ω at the metaphysical scale (X-Config; cf. Promethean Configuration) is the totality of configurations the substrate realizes; nested-stream Ω_S is a localized sub-space within that totality.
