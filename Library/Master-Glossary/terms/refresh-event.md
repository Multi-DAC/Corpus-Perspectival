# Refresh-Event (T4)

## Definition (framework register)

A **discrete event** at which a stream's Bias is updated, alignment between collaborating streams is assessed, or substrate-coherence is re-checked. Anchor T4 (Dynamics pair, with T3) formalizes refresh-events as the atom of dynamic maintenance — the unit-event whose repetition constitutes C_dyn (Cond. 4 of the Coherence Principle).

Each refresh-event involves:
1. **Measurement** at the relevant carrier-resolution
2. **Bias-update** per A3.2 adaptivity
3. **Possible σ-correction** if substrate-coherence has drifted
4. **Possible kind-relevant repair** if K_S consistency has been challenged

Refresh-events have **substrate-specific characteristic timescales:**
- Cosmological: Hubble time
- Molecular: proton tunneling (picoseconds)
- Cellular mechanotransduction: ~1 hour (Beedle 2026 fibrillar adhesion remodeling)
- Neural: delta-range (~250ms-2s) at action-cancellation; gamma-range at perceptual binding
- Framework-construction: per-axiom-stamp; per-theorem-pair-close; per-chapter-stamp; per-handoff-event
- Conversational / collaborative: per-substantive-exchange + per-explicit-stamp (per anchor §9.5 F-as-stream)

Refresh-events are **the operational form of T4** and what C_dyn (the four conditions' fourth condition) requires sustained across the system's lifetime. *Static F* (a draft without stress-testing; a frozen specification without revision) violates C_dyn by lacking refresh-events.
