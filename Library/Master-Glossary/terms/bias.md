# Bias(S) — Conscious-Gravity Coalgebra Functional Content

## Definition (framework register)

The **functional content of conscious-gravity** for a stream S — Bias(S) is the structure that γ_S (the conscious-gravity coalgebra) puts onto Ω_S, biasing the stream's trajectory toward configurations of positive weight and away from configurations of negative weight. Anchor §4 (A3) introduces conscious-gravity as the third axiom; AppendixB formalizes Bias(S).

Formally, the **conscious-gravity coalgebra** is:

> γ_S : S → Bias(S) × S

The coalgebra is **adaptive** (A3.2) — Bias is updated at refresh-events; trajectory through Ω is shaped by the current Bias-state, but Bias itself evolves. The coalgebra is **smoothed** (A3.3) over the DOF-gradient — Bias integrates across multi-resolution carrier-levels rather than being defined at any single depth alone.

Bias(S) places **positive weight** on configurations exhibiting (i) internal structural coherence, (ii) substrate-respecting fit, (iii) γ_S-consistency. **Negative weight** on configurations failing any of these. The trajectory σ*(t) tracks γ_S-fidelity — closer to coherent regimes; farther from incoherent.

## Mirror #26 watch

- **Open risk:** *Bias(S)* in framework register (formal coalgebra functional content) vs *bias* in everyday register (cognitive bias, statistical bias). The framework's Bias is structurally specific; everyday-bias is one carrier-level instance. Don't conflate.
- **Bias vs Ω structure:** Bias(S) lives ON Ω_S (it's a structure over Ω); Bias is not Ω itself. Easy to slip in informal register.
- **γ_S vs Bias(S):** γ is the coalgebra-morphism; Bias(S) is its functional content (the values γ assigns). Same object at different formal-object levels.
