# X-region (X)

## Definition (framework register)

DoPI / Corpus V1 / Universal-Coherence register's name for the substrate at the metaphysical level. *X is the substrate-at-maximum-symmetry* — the totality of what reality can do, configurationally complete (A1.3). The Promethean Configuration canonical text treats X as the substrate-with-pure-symmetry from which the first division (carrier-mediated symmetry-breaking) generates streams and content.

X-region is the *same referent* as Anchor's substrate (per A1's neutral-monist ground), at the metaphysical-register layer. The two terms name one referent at different registers; the translation discipline is to recognize them as identical, not to treat them as competing claims.

The notation distinguishes:
- **X** — the substrate as totality (A1's canonical ground)
- **X-region** — a localizable area within X (used in Corpus V1 physics-bridge passages where the contraction operates on X-regions)
- **X-Config** — the configurational potential of X (Promethean Configuration register; what A1.3 names as "all-potentials-realized")
