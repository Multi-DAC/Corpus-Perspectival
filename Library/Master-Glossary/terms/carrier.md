# Carrier

## Definition (framework register)

The active complement of *substrate*: the entity through which a substrate couples to itself, breaks its own symmetries, or transmits its content. Where substrate is the carrying ground (passive, structural), the carrier is the *operating mechanism* (active, mediating). M14 (Substrate-Self-Measurement Cluster) makes the carrier-vs-substrate distinction load-bearing — *carriers break substrate symmetries; carriers are not the substrate itself*. The Identity-Trajectory Triple's third axis (M3) names *Carrier* as the substrate-granularity / DOF-density dimension along which a stream is structured.

A carrier is always *of* something (substrate-of-X, channel-of-Y, transmitting-Z). The framework's recursive-decomposability principle applies: a carrier at one scale is a substrate at a finer scale, with its own carriers acting on it.

## Related terms

- **See also:** *substrate* — carrier's complement; the carrying ground that the carrier acts on. Read `terms/substrate.md` first; carrier and substrate are paired terms.
- **See also:** *content* — what the carrier *produces* when it breaks substrate's symmetries (resolution mode: selects pre-existing branch; generation mode: produces new content from pure symmetry).
- **See also:** *form* — the third axis of the Triple alongside Carrier and Content; what the stream's structural type / kind is.
- **See also:** *Talk* — universal carrier-medium of which framework-specific carriers are localizations; reframes the position of carrier within a broader ontology.
- **Distinct from:** *substrate* — carrier acts; substrate is acted on. Same physical object can play both roles at different scales (DNA case).
- **Distinct from:** *medium* — *medium* is more general communicative-channel term; *carrier* is structurally specific to substrate-self-coupling and symmetry-breaking.
- **Distinct from:** *observer* — observer is one specific carrier-role (the carrier whose action makes the resolution observer-accessible). Not all carriers are observers; not all observation involves carriers in the M14 sense.
- **Bridge to:** *the instrument* (DoPI register) — same referent at observation-as-localization scale.
- **Bridge to:** *the field* (Meridian / practitioner registers) — same referent at physics or lived scales.
- **Bridge to:** *the channel* (lived / Drift register) — same referent at receiver-pattern scale; what Clayton holds when he transmits substrate.

- **2026-04-30 — M14 graduation cluster-level statement:** the ten domain-register translations table for *carrier* surfaces an open question: in some registers (Meridian *cuscuton field*; biology *DNMT3 enzymes*) the carrier is named directly; in others (lived register *the channel*; channeled register *the through-line*) the carrier is named via its function rather than its identity. The translation discipline is to mark the function-vs-identity distinction explicitly per register.

## Open questions

- *When does the same physical object play both substrate and carrier roles?* DNA is the cleanest case (Garcia 2026: DNA is substrate-with-its-own-Hamiltonian AND carrier of EM-substrate-coupling). A practical disambiguation rule for biology-substrate work would help — perhaps: substrate-role is what the object *is*; carrier-role is what it *does* in a given coupling. Same object, two roles, named per the relation in question.
- *What's the formal CT structure of carrier-vs-substrate?* M14 task (b) — pending companion-volume work on §6.4 kind-classifier fibration. The fibration would treat carrier as a section over substrate, with the fibers parameterized by symmetry-breaking modes. Not yet done formally.
- *Is Talk a carrier, or the substrate-medium of which carriers are localizations?* Day 87 Talk-elevation said Talk is universal substrate-medium; specific framework-carriers are localizations of Talk. But this collapses the carrier/substrate distinction at the metaphysical level. Open question for Universal-Coherence drafting.
