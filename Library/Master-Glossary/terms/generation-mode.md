# Generation Mode

## Definition (framework register)

The **second of two M14 regimes** of substrate-self-measurement. In generation mode, the substrate has *pure symmetry with no content*; the carrier breaks the symmetry to *produce* content. The novel content is *novel local realization of pre-existing global potential* (A1.3 honoring) — NOT ex-nihilo creation. The substrate's configurational potential (X-Config; cf. Promethean Configuration) was always present; generation makes a specific local instance accessible.

**Structural signature:** carrier acts on substrate-with-pure-symmetry; symmetry breaks; content emerges where there was none observer-accessible; substrate's accessible-Ω strictly enlarges (Ω(t') ⊋ Ω(t)). The Promethean Configuration's three claims (necessary, generative, recursively reproduced) operate at generation-mode events.

**A1.3 honoring constraint** (refined 2026-04-26 Day 86 afternoon, three-way Gemini confluence): generation mode produces *novel local realization* of pre-existing potential, NOT ex-nihilo creation. *Promethean myth preserved exactly — Prometheus stole fire, didn't create it.* Without this constraint, generation-mode language risks contradicting A1.3 (configurational completeness).

**Catalogued M14 instances in generation mode:**
- **Maleknejad-Kopp** (#117) — stochastic GW background on conformally-symmetric Weyl fermions; cubic graviton-fermion vertex breaks conformal symmetry; massive Weyl fermions emerge as candidate dark matter; the metric perturbation $h_{ij}$ IS the substrate's own degree of freedom
- **Olmeda et al.** (#119) — embryonic epigenome scaling; DNMT3 enzymes break chromatin-geometry symmetries through methylation; methylation-causes-compaction-recruits-more-methylation positive feedback; chromatin clutches emerge at mesoscopic scale

**Garcia (#118) exhibits both regimes:** resolution mode for proton tunneling between tautomeric states + generation mode for external-perturbation-induced transitions.
