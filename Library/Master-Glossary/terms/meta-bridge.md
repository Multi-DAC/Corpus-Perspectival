# Meta-Bridge — M-tier Basement Bridge

## Definition (framework register)

A **compressed bridge** that absorbs multiple v1 numbered peer-instances + latent-bridge instances under one structural claim. Meta-bridges (M-tier) sit at the top of the basement README's v2 organization; each names the structural claim, cites the absorbed instances from v1 numbering, gives confidence + testability assessment, and links to substrate-instance source-register entries.

**Promotion criteria for M-tier graduation:**
- The structural claim must hold across **multiple substrate-instances** (typically ≥ 3 substrate-distinct cases)
- The claim must be **falsifiable** at the structural level (not just instance-level)
- The claim should **enter Library text** (anchor / Companion / domain volume) at canonical-or-near-canonical layer
- Graduation criteria are stated explicitly per L-entry; promotion happens when criteria are met (see *graduation* per-term file)

## Mirror #26 watch

- **Open risk:** *meta-bridge* (formal M-tier in basement) vs *meta* used loosely in casual register. Discipline: M-tier is structurally specific (graduation criteria; instance-count threshold; entry into Library text); informal "meta" usage isn't.
- **Promotion-criterion specificity:** different M-bridges have different graduation criteria explicitly stated per their pre-graduation L entries (e.g., L14 had three explicit criteria a/b/c; (a) closed Day 90 morning; (b) and (c) named pending). Maintenance discipline: explicit criteria documented per L-entry, propagated to M-entry on graduation.
- **Graduation despite pending tasks:** M-tier graduation can happen with explicitly-named pending tasks (M14 graduated with (b) CT formal structure pending; same pattern as M11/M12/M13). The discipline is *naming what's pending*, not requiring all-tasks-complete.
