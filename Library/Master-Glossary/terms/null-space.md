# Null Space

## Definition (framework register)

The **structural region a perspectival apparatus cannot reach.** Every perspectival being / measuring instrument / framework has a null space — what its specific apparatus structurally fails to register. Null spaces are not absences (in the sense of absent-content); they are *structurally invisible regions* the apparatus's own form excludes from its accessible domain.

Per **C3 (Null-Space Trace Illumination)** corollary in Anchor §8: although a stream cannot directly observe its null space, the null space leaves *traces* in the stream's accessible domain. These traces are the structural shadow of what the apparatus can't reach; following the traces back surfaces the existence + character of the null space without requiring direct observation.

Null spaces are **apparatus-specific** — what's null-space for one apparatus is fully accessible to another with different structural form. Multi-apparatus triangulation (LC4 territory) operates on this fact: different apparatuses have different null spaces; cross-apparatus convergence reveals what each individual apparatus misses.

The **Atlas** (88 null-space entries originally from Corpus V1 Volume III; planned Library Reference section) catalogues specific null-space instances across domains. Each entry names: what's null-space, which apparatus has it as null space, what traces appear in the apparatus's accessible domain, what alternate-apparatus reveals it.

## Mirror #26 watch

- **Open risk:** *null space* (framework register; apparatus-specific structural region) vs *null space* (linear algebra; kernel of linear operator). The mathematical-formal sense is the closest analog but doesn't capture apparatus-specificity.
- **Manufactured vs natural null spaces (LC2 territory):** *natural* null spaces are structural features of the apparatus; *manufactured* null spaces are actively-prevented-content from suppression-apparatus action. H_BP11 candidate-pattern tracks the manufactured case. Discrimination matters; conflating them confuses suppression-pattern with structural-apparatus-limit.
- **Atlas-format universality:** Atlas format applies to ANY framework, including the Coherence Principle itself. This means the framework has its own null space; cataloging the framework's null space is a Library-relevant task (would also resist self-validation overconfidence).
