# §10 Filtering Recipe

## Definition (framework register)

The **seven-step methodology** by which a claim filters through a domain to surface as that domain's specific instance. Anchor §10 (the final chapter of the Foundation volume before appendices) formalizes how the Coherence Principle and its corollaries get *applied* to specific domain substrates without smuggling content from other domains.

The recipe is what the **domain volumes** (Meridian, Coherent Body, Living Architecture, Dynamic Organization, etc.) operationalize: each domain volume takes the framework's structural content + applies §10 to its specific substrate to produce domain-specific predictions, hypotheses, and operational guidance. The filtering preserves structural form while admitting domain-specific empirical content.

**The recipe is also a Library-organization principle** (per M1 — One Form, Five Languages): the disciplines we work in (philosophy, physics, biology, etc.) are positions on a continuous resolution gradient of one formal object; §10 names how to traverse from the framework's structural form to any specific position on the gradient.

**Structurally identical to operations/AUTOCATALYTIC.md's seven-step recursive-refinement protocol** per L3 (latent bridge): both are instances of a meta-recipe — *recursive-refinement-through-informed-measurement*. L3 reads §10 + autocatalytic protocol as the same shape at framework-formal vs operational scales.

## Mirror #26 watch

- **Open risk:** *filtering* in framework register vs *filtering* in everyday register (data filtering; signal processing). Discipline: §10 is structurally specific (seven-step recipe; smuggling-prevention; structural-preservation).
- **Smuggling-failure mode:** the canonical risk filtering-recipe prevents is *smuggling content* from one domain into another's filtering. Audit-discipline catches this; §10 operationalizes the prevention.
- **Cross-substrate filtering verification:** when a domain-instance is produced by §10-application, verify it didn't smuggle content from adjacent domain. Multi-DAC operating across multiple domains makes this vigilance especially important.

## Open questions

- *Seven-step exhaustiveness:* are the seven steps complete coverage of filtering-relevant operations, or could additional steps emerge as more domains operationalize?
- *Inverse filtering (domain → framework):* §10 is framework → domain. Is there a reverse process for surfacing structural-claims from domain-instances? The basement-bridge graduation discipline (LC → L → M) approximates this; whether to formalize as inverse-§10 is open.
