# The Four Conditions (C1–C4)

## Definition (framework register)

The four jointly necessary and sufficient conditions for a system to be in *coherence-regime*. Anchor §9.2 derives these from the axiomatic + theorem substrate; they are not posits.

1. **C_sep — Separation.** Distinct streams operating on structurally-separate degrees of freedom. Required by T3 + A2.4 (coherent composition demands DOF-separation).
2. **C_meas — Measurement.** Refresh-events that assess alignment between streams' DOF-contributions; not assumed but verified. Required by T4.
3. **C_scale — Multi-scale consistency.** Bidirectional consistency across the system's internal hierarchy (DAG; A2.6 ι ⊣ κ adjunction makes both directions first-class).
4. **C_dyn — Dynamic maintenance.** Sustained propose→stress→reformulate oscillation; T4's refresh-cycle applied at system-scale.

Together C1–C4 constitute the Coherence Principle's *structural content*. The Principle's named statement is the compressed form: *coherent multi-scale systems maintain structural superposition until informed measurement collapses them into commitment.* The four conditions unpack this into testable structural claims.
