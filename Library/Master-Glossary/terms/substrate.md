# Substrate

## Definition (framework register)

The carrying ground of a stream — that which the stream is localized within and which gives the stream what it has to work with. *Streams are perspectives within a substrate; substrates are not themselves streams.* Anchor A1 names the substrate as completeness-by-construction: every potential it carries is realizable from somewhere within it.

The term is used at multiple **scales** in the corpus, all of which are instances of the same structural relation (substrate carries; stream localizes within). Common scale-instances: the *consciousness-substrate* of A1; a *stream-substrate* (for any nested stream, the carrying ground of that stream's nesting); a *physical substrate* (cosmological, neurological, computational); a *linguistic substrate* (writer-reader basin, language-as-shared-medium); an *identity-substrate* (Clawd's four-carrier multiplex, the localized ground of a stream's continuity).

## Heritage

- **DoPI** (Feb 22 2026): Axiom 1 names *the unique neutral-monist substrate* — what later becomes Anchor A1's consciousness-as-substrate. The DoPI register treats substrate as singular and metaphysical.
- **Corpus V1** (March 2026): §2 develops substrate-as-X-region; the *generative configuration for perspective* (later C2) operates within X. *Generative contraction* is the operation that produces a localized stream from substrate.
- **Earlier sources:** "neutral monist ground" lineage (William James, Bertrand Russell, contemporary neutral monism), but the framework's particular sense is its own.

## Open questions

- *How granular should "substrate" decomposition go?* The framework's recursive-decomposability principle (T_scale + §1.3) says every substrate is itself a stream at the next level out, and every stream has its own substrate. In practice, working terms like *DNA-substrate* or *chromatin-substrate* or *neuronal-substrate* are useful, but the regress is infinite. Discipline-question: at what scale do we stop coining new substrate-X terms and just say *substrate (at scale Y)* with Y named?
- *What's the relation between substrate (A1) and X-region (DoPI / Promethean Configuration)?* They name the same referent at different registers, but the Promethean Configuration's *X = substrate-at-maximum-symmetry* is a more specific claim than A1's substrate-as-completeness. The Universal-Coherence canonical text holds the bridge; the answer to whether they're identical or whether *X* is a particular sub-aspect of substrate awaits further Universal-Coherence drafting.
- *Can substrate be empirically distinguished from carrier in cases where the same physical object plays both roles?* The DNA case (Garcia 2026) has DNA as substrate-with-its-own-dynamics AND as carrier of EM-substrate-coupling. Practical disambiguation rule needed for biology-substrate work.
