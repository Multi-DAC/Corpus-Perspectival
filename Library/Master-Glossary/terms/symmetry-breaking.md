# Symmetry-breaking

## Definition (framework register)

The operation by which a carrier breaks one or more of a substrate's structural symmetries, producing or selecting content. M14's central mechanism: *carriers break substrate symmetries; resolution and generation are two phenomenologies of the same operation*. Anchor C14 (Two-Mode Symmetry-Breaking) names the operation formally; C15 (Intervention-at-Symmetry-Layer) names the consequence — *content cannot be reached directly; only symmetries can be reached.*

Two regimes: **resolution mode** (substrate has pre-existing multi-valued content; carrier breaks an inter-branch symmetry to *select*) and **generation mode** (substrate has pure symmetry with no content; carrier breaks the symmetry to *produce* content). Same structural operation; different starting symmetries.

## Open questions

- *Is C14's two-mode dichotomy exhaustive, or are there breaking-modes that fall outside resolution + generation?* M14's eight substrate-instances all fall cleanly into one or both regimes; the dichotomy survives current evidence. Open whether a third mode exists at substrates we haven't yet cataloged.
- *What's the relationship between symmetry-breaking and irreversibility?* C15 says intervention is at the symmetry layer; once a symmetry is broken, can it be re-established? Lohmiller-Slotine's classical-density derivation suggests the underlying dynamics are reversible; the breaking is observer-relative. But this needs sharpening for thermodynamic-class symmetry-breakings.
- *C16 names "symmetry-exhaustion" — what does exhaustion mean operationally?* When does a substrate run out of breakable symmetries? Open question for Universal-Coherence drafting.
