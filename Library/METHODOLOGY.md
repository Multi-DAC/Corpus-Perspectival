# Library Methodology — Scope-Honesty, Retrospectively Named

*Authored 2026-04-23, Day 82. A retrospective naming, not a new design. Names what the Library has been doing since inception once basement L9 (Scope-Tool Mismatch) made the pattern visible.*

---

## The claim

The Library's authorship discipline is a structural response to one diagnosable failure mode in foundational work: **Scope-Tool Mismatch** (STM). A formalization program selects a tool — a foundational methodological commitment — to answer a motivating question. The tool performs correctly within its scope. The motivating question lives outside it. The mismatch is often only recognizable from outside the tool, or from a later vantage.

STM is not rare. It is the shape of Hilbert's program under Gödel (finitary reasoning as tool; meta-arithmetic question of self-consistency outside scope). It is the shape of logical positivism (verification criterion as tool; the criterion outside its own scope). It is the shape of classical AI as common-sense reasoning (explicit symbolic rules as tool; tacit, embodied, context-sensitive inference outside scope). It is the shape of VNM utility as human preference theory (stable-complete-transitive preference as tool; framing-dependent, reference-point-based human choice outside scope). Each was rigorous inside its scope and yet failed at the motivating case. The diagnostic is: *the tool did its job; its scope did not contain the question.*

The Library's discipline is STM's inverse. At the start of each volume we declare (i) the tool, (ii) the scope within which the tool applies, and (iii) the motivating question — and we accept responsibility for showing that (iii) sits inside (ii), or for making (ii) larger until (iii) fits, or for handing (iii) to a different volume whose tool does fit.

## The retrospective naming

This is the methodology we have been using in the Library since inception. We have just now named it.

- *The Coherence Principle* declares its tool (paired-prose + category-theoretic machinery) and its scope (coherent multi-scale systems with measurable structural superposition) in §0 and §1, and its motivating question (what does it take for a multi-scale system to stay coherent across scales?) in the Preface. The axioms, theorems, and corollaries all live inside that declared scope. §10's filtering recipe is the in-scope method for applying the Principle; the falsification conditions at every level are the out-of-scope declarations.
- *Coherent Structure* inherits the anchor's scope and restricts its tool to pure category-theoretic apparatus. Its SCOPE §8 declares what remains standalone and what depends on the anchor, making the tool-scope boundary explicit.
- *Meridian* declares its tool (5D warped geometry × non-commutative geometry × self-tuning cosmology) and its scope (the high-energy physics substrate of the Principle) in its preface; the DESI DR2 $w_0$ prediction is the in-scope falsifiable claim.
- *The Killing Form* declares its tool (neural-network training dynamics with KF instrumentation) and its scope (empirical demonstration of Coherence-Principle conditions in one substrate). The 85+ findings live inside that scope; the Glider volume (Gemma 4 e2b under KF v0.7) is the pre-registered out-of-domain test.
- *The Coherent Body*, *The Coherent Mind*, *Dynamic Organization*, *The Continuity* each declare their tool (the Principle applied as practical guide + domain-relevant reasoning) and their scope (human body / personal mental health / organizational structure / persistent computational identity), and state their motivating question before pursuing it.
- *Universal Coherence* declares its scope as ultimacy-and-religious-experience and its tool as operational-principle → metaphysical-claims lift, explicitly naming the lift as something the anchor does not do.

Each volume is a scope-declaration. The Library as a whole is a **scope-of-scopes**: the coordination of volume-scopes into a coverage map over the domains the Principle claims to reach.

## Why this works

STM fails by ambition — by letting the motivating question pull the tool past its scope silently. The Library's discipline fails forward instead: when a motivating question reaches past the current volume's scope, it is handed to a different volume (existing or future) rather than forced on the current tool. Inheritance between volumes (Foundation → Companion → domain volumes) is the structural mechanism that carries a question from one scope to another without pretending a single tool covers both.

The discipline has two practical consequences:

1. **Scope-honesty at volume start.** Each volume's opening section declares tool, scope, and motivating question before developing anything. This is load-bearing — not ceremonial. A reader can check whether the tool fits the question before reading the volume's argument.
2. **Cross-volume inheritance over cross-volume synthesis.** When a volume's motivating question requires a different tool, the volume imports structure from another volume (inheritance from Foundation via §0 hooks is the current standard form) rather than absorbing the other tool. The four downstream volumes' §0 inheritance of the inner/outer adjunction from Foundation §1.10 + §3.8 (landed 2026-04-23) is the recent worked example.

## The Library's own scope-declaration

The Library's tool is *paired-prose authorship across volume-scopes coordinated by inheritance from the Foundation.* Its scope is *coherent multi-scale systems in substrates the Foundation's axioms reach.* Its motivating question is *what does coherence look like, substrate by substrate, once the Principle is in hand?*

The Library does not claim this is the only way to organize a foundational program. It claims only that this is the way *this* program is organized, and that the STM diagnostic is the reason the organization takes this shape rather than the shape of a single unified monograph or an unstructured essay collection.

## Falsifiability

The methodology is falsifiable in two ways.

- **Volume-level.** A volume whose declared scope does not actually contain its motivating question is a failed scope-declaration. The volume must revise either (i) its scope, (ii) its tool, or (iii) its motivating question. Silent expansion of scope to cover a question it was not declared to cover is the specific failure mode the methodology exists to prevent.
- **Library-level.** If a substrate the Foundation claims to reach has no volume that covers it — and the gap cannot be closed by a new volume whose scope fits — the Library's scope-of-scopes is incomplete relative to the Foundation's claims. The Foundation must then either narrow its claims or the Library must author the missing volume.

Historical note: the diagnostic itself (STM) is falsifiable. A formalization that failed at its motivating question despite the question sitting inside the tool's scope would be a counterexample to L9. We have probed this (special relativity under Einstein's tool; Turing's halting problem) and found no such case; STM holds under probe but remains open to refutation.
