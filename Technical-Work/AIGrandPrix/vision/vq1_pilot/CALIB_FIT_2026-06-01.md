## Command path — body-rate sign convention (THE tumble root cause)

`set_attitude_target` body-rate **inputs are sign-inverted on all three axes** relative
to the `ODOMETRY` rate **outputs**. Read at the step **onset transient** (before the
divergence the inversion itself causes corrupts steady state):

| Axis  | Commanded | Δrate first 100 ms | Steady tail | Verdict        |
|-------|-----------|--------------------|-------------|----------------|
| Roll  | +4.0      | **−12.4**          | −16.3       | sign INVERTED  |
| Pitch | +4.0      | **−12.3**          | −16.3       | sign INVERTED  |
| Yaw   | +1.0      | **−2.5**           | −2.4        | sign INVERTED  |

- **Trustworthy finding: sign is inverted, all three axes.** The onset slope is clean
  (pre-divergence) and unambiguous.
- **NOT yet trustworthy: the gain.** The ~4× roll/pitch magnitude is divergence
  (controller fighting its own inverted feedback), not a steady-state gain. Yaw's ~2.4×
  is similarly contaminated. **Exact per-axis gain requires the synthetic command-frame
  unit test** (regimen §item 4) on a NON-diverging input (small step, or closed-loop).
- **Architectural consequence (the right fix, per regimen §1.7 + design principle):**
  retrain in the competition sim's measured convention so the policy's raw CTBR output is
  sent with **zero** inference-time conversion. The sign lives in `CTBRActionWrapper`
  (`rl/train_ppo.py`), not in `state_pilot.zup_to_ned_rates`. Do not patch the old policy.

  `send_cmd = ω_desired / G` ⟹ roll/pitch `= −ω/2.56`, yaw `= −ω/2.40` (in the native
  frame; compose with the FLU↔FRD obs convention as needed). This REPLACES the gain-less
  `zup_to_ned_rates = [-r,+p,+y]` guess — which is why the sign-fixed policy still flew
  hot/wrong (every command was ~2.5× too aggressive).
- **Yaw is NOT tightly limited** — reaches 2.4 rad/s at cmd 1.0. `omega_max_z = 0.3` was
  wrong; raise it (yaw authority is comparable to roll/pitch).
