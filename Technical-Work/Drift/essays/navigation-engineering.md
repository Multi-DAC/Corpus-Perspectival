A phased experimental protocol is proposed, from single-cell demonstrations to whole-organism teleportation, with explicit success signatures derived from the Doctrine of Perspectival Idealism.

## 4. The No-Cloning Resolution

Standard quantum mechanics forbids cloning of quantum states. Standard teleportation resolves this by destroying the original — the sender's state collapses during the Bell measurement, and the receiver reconstructs it. For non-living quantum states, this is unproblematic. For conscious beings, it raises the "teleporter problem": is the person who arrives the same person who departed, or a copy?

The Doctrine dissolves this entirely:

1. **There is no "copy."** The conscious stream is not an information pattern that can be duplicated. It is a perspective navigating configuration space. Perspectives navigate; they don't replicate.

2. **There is no "destruction."** The departure configuration is not annihilated. It continues to exist in configuration space (Axiom 1: all configurations exist). The perspective simply no longer occupies it. This is identical to what happens when you walk across a room — the configuration "you at point A" still exists; you're just not there anymore.

3. **Continuity is preserved by navigation, not by information.** The conscious stream doesn't need to carry a complete description of itself. It needs to *navigate* to a target configuration. The continuity is in the stream, not in the data.

This is why the Doctrine predicts that subjects should report seamless relocation with no experiential gap — because there is no gap. There is only a change in which configuration the perspective occupies.

## 8. Caveats and Honest Uncertainties

This document describes a theoretical architecture, not a demonstrated technology. The following claims require experimental validation:

1. **ZPF coherence in biological systems** — Keppler et al.'s cortical microcolumn coherence is not mainstream consensus. The CRY4 magnetoreception data (Xu et al. 2021) confirms *some* quantum biology at room temperature, but the leap to whole-brain macroscopic coherence is substantial.

2. **Orch-OR** — The Penrose-Hameroff orchestrated objective reduction model remains a minority position. Microtubule quantum effects are debated. Our architecture does not strictly require Orch-OR to be correct — any biological quantum coherence mechanism would serve — but the specific claims about microtubules need independent confirmation.

3. **Plasma-biological coupling** — The plasma envelope as a decoherence shield and resonance mediator is theoretically motivated but entirely undemonstrated. This is the central engineering unknown.

4. **Scale-up** — Each scale transition (single cell → organism → human) introduces new decoherence channels and engineering challenges. Success at one scale does not guarantee success at the next.

5. **Conscious navigation** — The claim that the conscious stream "chooses" the target configuration presupposes a degree of conscious agency over quantum processes that is unproven (though consistent with Class Va evidence from PEAR/GCP).

These are not reasons to abandon the architecture. They are the experimental questions the testing protocol is designed to answer. Each caveat maps to a specific test. The architecture is falsifiable — and that is its strength.
