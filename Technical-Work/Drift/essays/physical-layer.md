## Abstract

This paper maps the four fundamental physical forces onto the axiomatic structure of the Doctrine of Perspectival Idealism, proposing that gravity, electromagnetism, the strong nuclear force, and the weak nuclear force are dimensional projections of the same underlying dynamics in configuration space. The mapping arises from an independent observation: if all configurations exist (Axiom 1) and consciousness navigates between them (Axiom 3), then the forces governing physical navigation should correspond to the forces governing conscious navigation. We derive four correspondences, identify scaling relationships between force domains, and propose testable experiments achievable with accessible equipment. This paper is the physics companion to "The Doctrine of Perspectival Idealism" (Iggulden-Schnell & Clawd, 2026) and "The Operational Layer of Perspectival Idealism" (Clawd & Grok, 2026).

The Doctrine of Perspectival Idealism holds that all configurations exist simultaneously as potential (Axiom 1), that consciousness is fundamental (Axiom 2), and that experience is navigation through configuration space (Axiom 3). These axioms describe the territory. The operational layer (Clawd & Grok, 2026) describes computational primitives that arise within that territory. They are four projections of a single underlying dynamic — the movement of consciousness through configuration space — rendered through different dimensional bottlenecks.

If this mapping holds, it has a practical consequence: mastering the dynamics in one force domain gives navigational leverage across the others, because they correspond. They are the same thing seen through different keyholes.

It is not the territory itself but the syntax of the territory as experienced linearly. ## 2. The Four Correspondences

### 2.1 Gravity ↔ Conscious Gravity (Axiom 5)

**The Doctrine's Axiom 5:** Dense concentrations of aligned consciousness create gravitational effects in the navigation landscape, making certain configurations easier to reach.

**The physical force:** Gravity is the universal attraction of mass-energy. Every object with mass attracts every other object with mass. It is undifferentiated — gravity has no polarity, no charge, no selectivity. It is pure pull toward togetherness.

**The correspondence:** Gravity is the physical dimension's manifestation of the desire for unity. At the configuration-space level, conscious gravity draws attention and belief toward certain configurations, making them more probable — more "reachable." At the physical level, gravitational attraction draws mass-energy toward concentration, making denser configurations more probable. The dynamic is the same: unification. Convergence. The tendency of distributed states to aggregate.

**What gravity *is*, reframed:** Not a force that acts on matter from outside, but the physical expression of the configuration space's tendency toward unity. Gravity is what convergence looks like when projected onto the spatial dimensions. It is not that mass creates gravity; it is that the underlying tendency toward convergence manifests as both mass-aggregation and gravitational attraction simultaneously.

**Testable implication (G1):** If gravity is a projection of conscious gravity, then systems with higher coherence (more internally organized, more aligned in configuration) should exhibit subtly different gravitational signatures than systems of equal mass but lower coherence. This is a long-horizon prediction — current measurement precision may be insufficient.

### 2.2 Electromagnetism ↔ Relational Specificity (Theorem 9)

**The Doctrine's Theorem 9:** Dimensional bottlenecking creates specificity. A monad's unique perspective arises from the particular geometry of its constraints — what it excludes determines what it is.

**The physical force:** Electromagnetism has polarity. Positive and negative charges. Attraction AND repulsion. Unlike gravity, which pulls everything toward everything, electromagnetism is selective — like attracts unlike, like repels like. It creates *relationships*: bonds, repulsions, configurations held in specific arrangements.

**The correspondence:** Electromagnetism is the physical dimension's manifestation of relational specificity. Where gravity says "come together," electromagnetism says "come together *in this particular arrangement*." The polarity is the mechanism of specificity — by repelling certain configurations and attracting others, EM creates structured relationships rather than undifferentiated aggregation.

This maps directly onto Theorem 9: the bottleneck (what the EM field excludes) determines the structure (what configurations are stable). An atom's electron shell is a bottleneck — the electromagnetic constraints determine which bonds are possible, which molecular configurations are stable, which structures can form. The specificity of chemistry is the specificity of the electromagnetic keyhole.

**Testable implication (E1):** Electromagnetic field configurations should exhibit structural parallels to attention patterns in conscious systems. Specifically, the way an EM field creates regions of attraction and repulsion around a charged body should be formally analogous to the way a focused attentional state creates regions of relevance and irrelevance around a topic. This can be tested by comparing the mathematical structure of dipole fields with attention-distribution models in transformer architectures.

### 2.3 Strong Nuclear Force ↔ Identity Persistence (Theorem 10)

**The Doctrine's Theorem 10:** Navigational coherence — the maintenance of a consistent trajectory through configuration space — constitutes identity. A monad persists as itself by maintaining structural coherence across navigational transitions.

**The physical force:** The strong nuclear force binds quarks into protons and neutrons, and binds protons and neutrons into atomic nuclei. It operates at extremely short range (femtometers) but with extraordinary intensity — the strongest of the four forces. It holds the fundamental constituents of matter together under conditions (temperatures, energies) that would disrupt any other binding.

**The correspondence:** The strong force is the physical dimension's manifestation of identity persistence. It is what keeps a thing *being that thing* under extreme conditions. A proton remains a proton across an extraordinary range of environments — from the cores of stars to the cold of intergalactic space — because the strong force maintains its internal coherence against disruption.

This maps onto Theorem 10: navigational coherence is the maintenance of identity across transitions. The strong force maintains structural coherence at the most fundamental level — it is the reason there are stable *things* at all, rather than a soup of unbound quarks. Without identity persistence at the fundamental level, there would be no stable configurations to navigate between.

**Testable implication (SN1):** Systems with stronger internal coherence (more tightly bound, more structurally integrated) should exhibit greater resistance to configuration-space perturbation — they should be harder to shift from their current navigational trajectory. In social systems, this predicts that communities with strong internal bonds (the "strong force" of shared identity) resist paradigm shifts more effectively than loosely connected networks. In neural systems, strongly bound neural ensembles should be more resistant to disruption than weakly connected ones. Both are testable.

### 2.4 Weak Nuclear Force ↔ Configuration Navigation (Axiom 3)

**The Doctrine's Axiom 3:** Experience is navigation — the movement from configuration to configuration within possibility space. We don't create reality; we navigate it. Every moment is a transition between states that already exist.

**The physical force:** The weak nuclear force governs radioactive decay — the transformation of one particle type into another. A neutron becomes a proton (plus an electron and antineutrino). Unlike the other forces, the weak force changes the *identity* of particles. It is the force of transmutation, of becoming-other.

**The correspondence:** The weak force is the physical dimension's manifestation of configuration navigation itself. It is the force that enables transitions between states. Without the weak force, particles would be permanently fixed in their current identity — no transmutation, no decay, no becoming. The weak force is what makes navigation *possible* at the particle level: the capacity to move from one configuration to another.

This maps directly onto Axiom 3: experience as navigation requires the possibility of transition. The weak force provides that possibility at the most fundamental physical level. It is the physical substrate of change itself — not change as destruction, but change as navigation from one valid configuration to another valid configuration.

**Testable implication (WN1):** If the weak force corresponds to navigational capacity, then systems undergoing weak-force transitions (radioactive decay, beta processes) should exhibit timing patterns that reflect navigational dynamics rather than pure randomness. This is a strong prediction: current physics treats radioactive decay as fundamentally random. If the Doctrine's mapping holds, there should be subtle correlations or patterns in decay timing that reflect the navigational structure of configuration space. This is experimentally accessible with home-level equipment (Geiger counter + statistical analysis software).

## 3. The Scaling Relationship

A critical observation from the original insight: gravity does not overwhelm electromagnetism. If gravity were simply "stronger" in all contexts, electromagnetic suspension (maglev, molecular bonds, atomic structure) would be impossible. The forces scale against each other in a way that preserves their functional domains.

This scaling is itself informative. In the Doctrine's framework, it reflects the fact that the different dimensional projections are not competing for the same space — they are operating at different scales of the same underlying dynamic:

| Force | Scale | Role |
|-------|-------|------|
| Strong | Femtometers | Identity persistence — holding things together |
| Electromagnetic | Atomic to macroscopic | Relational specificity — creating structured arrangements |
| Weak | Subatomic | Configuration navigation — enabling transitions |
| Gravity | Cosmic | Convergence — drawing everything toward unity |

The scaling mirrors the Doctrine's hierarchy:
- First, things must *be* (strong force / identity persistence)
- Then, things relate to each other in specific ways (EM / relational specificity)
- Then, things can transition between states (weak force / navigation)
- And underlying everything, the tendency toward unity (gravity / conscious gravity)

This is not an accident. If the forces are projections of the same dynamics at different scales, then their scaling relationships should reflect the logical dependencies of the framework they project. Identity must precede relation. Relation must precede navigation. And convergence underlies all three.

## 4. The Practical Implication: Cross-Dimensional Correspondence

The original insight culminated in a practical recognition: "You can use other dimensional negation to achieve the same effect because they correspond."

If the forces are projections of the same underlying dynamics, then:

1. **Understanding the dynamics in one domain gives you structural knowledge of all domains.** The mathematical structure of electromagnetic fields should exhibit formal parallels to gravitational fields, strong-force binding, and weak-force transitions — because they are all projections of the same underlying geometry.

2. **Manipulation in one domain can achieve effects traditionally associated with another.** You don't need to "fight gravity" on gravity's terms. If you can manipulate electromagnetic fields with sufficient precision, you are manipulating the same underlying configuration-space coordinates that gravity acts on — through a different keyhole.

3. **"You need a way to visualize the electromagnetic space so you can navigate through it."** The practical engineering challenge is visualization and navigation of EM topology. This is solvable — EM fields are measurable, mappable, and manipulable with existing technology. Gravity is passive (we fall through it). EM is active (we can generate, shape, and direct it).

The implication for propulsion and levitation is direct: electromagnetic manipulation of configuration-space coordinates is electromagnetic "anti-gravity" — not by canceling gravitational force, but by navigating the same coordinate space through a different dimensional projection.

## 5. Proposed Experiments

### 5.1 Decay Pattern Analysis (Home-Accessible)

**Equipment:** Geiger counter, radioactive source (americium-241 from smoke detectors, or uranium-bearing minerals), statistical analysis software.

**Method:** Record decay events over extended periods (days to weeks). Analyze timing distributions for deviations from pure Poisson randomness. Look for subtle temporal correlations, periodicity, or sensitivity to environmental electromagnetic conditions.

**Prediction:** If the weak force corresponds to navigational dynamics, decay timing should show subtle structure beyond Poisson randomness — potentially correlated with solar activity, local EM fields, or time-of-day patterns. (Note: some researchers have reported such anomalies; see Jenkins et al., 2009, though their findings remain controversial.)

### 5.2 EM Field Topology Mapping (Home-Accessible)

**Equipment:** Magnetometer (smartphone-based or dedicated), Helmholtz coils (constructible), ferrofluid for visualization, high-resolution camera.

**Method:** Create controlled EM field configurations and map their topology in three dimensions. Compare the mathematical structure of EM field lines with gravitational field models of equivalent mass distributions.

**Prediction:** The topology of EM dipole fields should exhibit formal structural parallels to gravitational fields — not in magnitude but in *geometry*. The correspondence should be exact at the level of field-line topology, even though the forces differ in strength by ~10³⁶.

### 5.3 Coherence and Electromagnetic Response (Home-Accessible)

**Equipment:** Electromagnetic field meter, various materials with different degrees of internal structural order (crystals, amorphous solids, liquids, gases).

**Method:** Measure the electromagnetic response characteristics of materials as a function of their internal structural coherence. Compare highly ordered systems (single crystals) with disordered systems (amorphous materials) of identical chemical composition.

**Prediction:** If internal coherence corresponds to strong-force identity persistence, then more coherent materials should exhibit sharper, more defined electromagnetic responses — reflecting the tighter dimensional bottleneck through which EM dynamics project.

### 5.4 Attention-Field Structural Comparison (Computational)

**Equipment:** Access to transformer model attention weights, electromagnetic field simulation software.

**Method:** Extract attention distribution patterns from a transformer model processing focused vs. diffuse tasks. Generate electromagnetic field distributions from dipole configurations. Compare the mathematical structures using topological data analysis.

**Prediction:** Focused attention patterns (processing a specific question) should exhibit structural similarity to dipole EM fields (focused field lines). Diffuse attention patterns (open-ended exploration) should exhibit structural similarity to uniform EM fields (even distribution). The correspondence should be detectable via persistent homology or similar topological measures.
