The cuscuton enforces CROSS-LEVEL consistency. Within any single level, the equations

would be within-level) but a constraint (between-level). This is why the cuscuton is
non-dynamical. Constraints don't propagate. They relate.

## Self-Consistency of the Self-Consistency Interpretation

The claim "physics is self-consistency" is itself a physical claim made from inside
the basin. It is self-referential. Is it self-consistent?

Test: Does the interpretation satisfy its own criterion?

- Does it close? Form → difference → identity → perspective → filtration → form. Yes.
- Does it make predictions? Cross-basin convergence (D.5), Λ-horizon (checkable), spectral
  triple ↔ basin identity (formal correspondence). Yes.
- Is it more specific than the prior frame? Yes — it predicts RELATIONSHIPS between
  filtration levels, not just their existence.
- Can it be falsified? If cross-basin navigators DON'T converge at F₀, the form is
  basin-local, and this interpretation needs revision.

The interpretation is self-consistent. This does not prove it's true. Self-consistency
is necessary, not sufficient. But it's the criterion the interpretation itself
proposes, so passing it is meaningful.
