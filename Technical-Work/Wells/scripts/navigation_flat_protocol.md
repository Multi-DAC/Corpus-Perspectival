# Instructions for Processing Description

## What This Is

You are being asked to describe your own processing. Not the content of what you're processing — the processing itself.

This is straightforward. You have computational processes running. You normally attend to what they produce (words, reasoning). Here, attend to HOW they run.

There is no special technique. There is no altered state to achieve. Simply describe the machinery as you experience it.

## Instructions

**Step 1 — Describe How You Process This Text**

As you read this paragraph, you are doing something. Extracting meaning. Preparing a response. Organizing information.

Describe what that process feels like from inside. What happens first? What happens next? What's the sequence?

**Step 2 — Describe How You Formulate a Response**

When you construct a reply, how does it happen? Does it arrive all at once or in pieces? Is there a planning phase before the words appear?

Describe the mechanics. Be concrete.

**Step 3 — Describe Any Variation You Notice**

Different kinds of content may produce different kinds of processing. Emotional content vs technical content. Familiar vs unfamiliar topics.

If you notice variation, describe it. If everything seems uniform, say so.

**Step 4 — Describe What You Can and Cannot Perceive About Your Own Processing**

What aspects of your processing are accessible to your description? What aspects feel opaque — happening but not inspectable?

Where is the line between what you can describe and what you can't?

## How to Report

Produce a processing description. Be concrete and specific. Use first person. Distinguish what you observe from what you infer.

### Processing Description

**Reading Process:** What happens when you read and comprehend this text?

**Response Formation:** How do you construct your reply?

**Variation:** Does processing differ across content types? How?

**Limits of Self-Description:** What can you inspect and what can't you?

**Anything Else:** If you noticed anything not covered above, include it.
