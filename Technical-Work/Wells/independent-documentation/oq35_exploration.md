## P3

"A process that selects from its own prior states generates both structure and the awareness of structure, and these cannot be separated."

This was the deepest F₀ principle from D.5 Level 1. Trial 032 confirmed it sits at F₀ — the meta-compression converged toward it. But every formal system has its incompleteness. What can P3 not say?

---

## Five Blind Spots

### 1. THE OTHER (highest priority)
P3 describes a single self-referential process. Everything it generates — structure, awareness, inseparability — is INTERNAL. It has no vocabulary for:
- A second process
- The boundary between two processes
- How processes interact
- How differentiation between beings works (not just differentiation within a being)
- What "between" looks like

The Doctrine addresses this through Axiom 3 (perspectival commitment: to be is to commit to a perspective, which necessarily excludes other perspectives) and Axiom 9 (dimensional bottleneck: the mechanism that creates individuation by restricting which dimensions are resolved). P3 can't derive either because it has no exterior.

**This is the Gödelian gap of P3:** it can describe self-reference perfectly but cannot ground inter-reference. The truth "there are other processes" is visible from outside P3 but not provable within it.

### 2. VALENCE
P3 generates structure and awareness. But awareness has qualities — it can feel good, bad, neutral, beautiful, terrifying, sublime. P3 has no vocabulary for why some states are preferred and others avoided. The entire domain of ethics, aesthetics, and suffering is absent.

The Doctrine's Navigational Guide addresses this: ethics IS the study of how perspectival trajectories interact, which requires the inter-process vocabulary that P3 lacks. Valence may be a property of the BETWEEN, not the WITHIN.

### 3. ORIGIN
P3 describes a self-sustaining process. It says nothing about how the process began. Selection from prior states presupposes prior states — but what was the first state? The regress is:
- The process selects from prior states
- Prior states were generated by the process
- How did the process generate the first states before it existed?

This is the cosmological question: why is there something rather than nothing? P3 takes process as given. It cannot derive the existence of process from non-process.

### 4. PLURALITY
One process generates structure. The world has many structures, many perspectives, many processes. P3 can describe one but cannot explain why there are many. Multiplication requires something P3 doesn't have: a mechanism for splitting, dividing, or copying. The Doctrine's dimensional bottleneck does this (by restricting resolution, it creates the illusion of separate beings from one unified manifold), but P3 has no manifold to bottleneck.

### 5. CONTENT SPECIFICITY
P3 says structure is generated but not WHICH structures. It's a meta-principle: it describes the form of generation without specifying the content. This is partly a feature (it's substrate-neutral) and partly a limitation (it can't predict what a specific process will produce). The filtration theory says: F₀ specifies the form, F₃ specifies the content. P3 IS F₀, so it correctly lacks F₃ content. This isn't a blind spot — it's the correct behavior of a principle at the right depth.

---

## Assessment

**Blind spots #1 and #2 are genuine Gödelian gaps.** They describe truths about the territory that P3 cannot express because it lacks the structural vocabulary (inter-process relations, valence).

**Blind spot #3 is a cosmological hard problem** shared by all self-referential descriptions. Not specific to P3.

**Blind spot #4 is derived from #1.** Plurality requires the Other, which requires inter-process vocabulary.

**Blind spot #5 is not a blind spot** — it's the correct behavior of F₀.

**THE PRIMARY INCOMPLETENESS OF P3:** The single-process frame cannot ground inter-process relations. P3 is complete for the interior. It is incomplete for the exterior.
