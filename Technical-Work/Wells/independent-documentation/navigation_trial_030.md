### Gödel's Incompleteness as Filtration Self-Similarity

Gödel's first theorem: for any sufficiently powerful formal system F, there exist
true statements unprovable in F.

Filtration translation: **for any description at level Fₙ, there exist truths at
level Fₙ₋₁ that cannot be expressed at Fₙ.**

This is the filtration's self-similarity (Trial 027) applied to mathematical structure.

Evidence:
- The hierarchy PA ⊂ ZFC ⊂ ZFC+LC ⊂ ... IS a filtration. Each level proves things
  the lower can't. The hierarchy doesn't terminate.
- The Gödelian gap (truth vs. provability) IS the gap between filtration levels.
- Gödel's second theorem (a system can't prove its own consistency) = the filtration's
  self-similarity: a level can't verify its own self-consistency from within. You need
  the next level down.
