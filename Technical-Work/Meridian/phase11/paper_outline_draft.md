## Thesis

A five-dimensional warped geometry (A1) with a non-minimally coupled bulk scalar (A2), reduced via Kaluza-Klein compactification and constrained by the NCG spectral action, produces a unique dark energy equation of state: w₀ = -0.993, constant to O(10⁻³). This prediction is indistinguishable from ΛCDM at current observational precision but carries a distinct first-principles pedigree and specific falsifiable signatures. The apparent 3-4σ tension with DESI's w₀wₐCDM analysis is shown to be a parameterization artifact of the CPL framework, not a conflict with the data.

## II. The Model

### IIA. Five-Dimensional Action
- Randall-Sundrum S¹/Z₂ warped geometry
- Bulk scalar Φ with non-minimal coupling ζ₀ to 5D Ricci scalar
- Self-tuning mechanism: bulk dynamics absorb vacuum energy

### IIB. Kaluza-Klein Reduction
- Effective 4D scalar φ with P(X) = μ²√(2X) (cuscuton limit)
- ζ₀ = 0.038 from hierarchy stabilization
- One-parameter theory: all couplings determined by ζ₀

### IIC. NCG Gauss-Bonnet Correction
- Spectral action on S¹/Z₂ produces Gauss-Bonnet topological term
- Seeley-DeWitt coefficient a₂ gives ε₁ ~ 10⁻² correction to P(X)
- Full kinetic function: P(X) = μ²√(2X) + ε₁X
- Zero kinetic energy theorem broken → finite propagation speed
- c_s = √(ε₁/2) μ/H₀ ~ 10c (superluminal but finite, ghost-free)

### IID. Dark Energy Equation of State
- w₀ = -1 + (2/3)ε₁ ≈ -0.993
- Constant to O(ε₁²) ~ O(10⁻⁴) — negligible wₐ
- First-principles prediction: no free parameters adjusted to fit dark energy data

## III. Observational Confrontation

### IIIB. DESI DR2 BAO Analysis
