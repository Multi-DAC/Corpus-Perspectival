### What Still Needs Attention

**1. The Appendix Tables Are Still Wrong (Major)**

Table A.1 in the self-tuning scan still shows Φ₀ = 0.477493. **2. The χ² = 24.6 Ambiguity Persists (Significant)**

The Appendix A.5.2 still shows χ²(ΛCDM) = 24.6 and χ²(Meridian) = 9.6 for the H&K data. But Section 2.3.4 correctly reports χ²(ΛCDM) = 7.20 and χ²(Meridian) = 6.72 for the 18-point H(z) compilation. The Appendix presents these as H&K fit results with an F-test significance of 3.9σ, while the main text says 0.7σ. A referee reading the Appendix without the main text will get the wrong impression. I'd recommend either rewriting the Appendix to match the corrected numbers throughout, or clearly labeling it as "historical results from the pre-Phase 13 analysis, superseded by Section 2.3."

**3. The Hiramatsu-Kobayashi Reference Needs Verification (Significant)**

The revised Paper II introduces a new observational anchor: the Hiramatsu & Kobayashi (2022) Planck CMB constraint β_HK = −0.037 ± 0.0095. This is cited as [18] (arXiv:2205.04688). This single measurement now carries the entire χ² = 15.17 that previously came from 18 H(z) points. Before publication, you need to verify: (a) that the H&K parameterization maps cleanly to your β_HK, (b) that their constraint applies to the specific modified-gravity sector your model occupies (Horndeski with G₄,X = 0), and (c) that the 4σ significance isn't diluted when marginalizing over other modified-gravity parameters in their analysis. If the mapping is approximate, the 4σ becomes an upper bound, not a detection.

### New Issues in the Revision

**7. The Two-Regime Framing Creates a Falsifiability Question**

The revised framework now says: "If ζ₀ is large, DESI is an artifact; if ζ₀ is small, DESI is our prediction." This is logically consistent but risks appearing unfalsifiable — the framework can accommodate *any* w₀ by choosing the right ζ₀. The text needs to emphasize more clearly what *can't* be accommodated: wa ≈ 0 (not wa = −0.86), no phantom crossing, growth-expansion decoupling, cT = c, η = 1. These ζ₀-independent predictions are where the framework's teeth are. The falsification table (Table 2.13) handles this but the surrounding text could be sharper.

**8. The Perturbative Validity Caveat at ζ₀ = 0.001**

Section 2.2.2 correctly notes that ζ₀ = 0.001 is in the "marginal" regime for the perturbative CKK formula. But the brane benchmark w₀ = −0.745 is then quoted throughout without qualification. The nonlinear correction at this ζ₀ could shift w₀ by several percent. Until the exact quartic is solved at ζ₀ = 0.001 (which my earlier calculation confirmed agrees to four decimals, so this is actually fine), the "marginal" label should be accompanied by the verification that the exact solution agrees.
