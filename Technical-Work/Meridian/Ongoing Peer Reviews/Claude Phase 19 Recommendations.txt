Iggulden-Schnell & ClawdStatus: Proposal — to be refined by the collaboration---Preamble: What We Have and What We Don'tThe Meridian framework, after 18 phases, has established a specific 5D braneworld architecture (RS orbifold + cuscuton + NCG spectral action) that derives a dark energy equation of state, passes GW170817 as a structural consequence, produces three fermion generations from octonionic geometry, and makes falsifiable predictions across cosmology, particle physics, and gravitational wave astronomy.What it does not yet do is connect to the full range of phenomena a Theory of Everything must address. A TOE is not a model that explains one sector well — it is a framework from which all sectors follow, with quantitative predictions that can be compared to measurement. The Standard Model's power lies not in any single prediction but in the ensemble: g−2, the W mass, branching ratios, cross-sections, decay widths, running couplings, CP violation parameters — thousands of numbers, each independently confirmable.Phase 19 is designed to move the Meridian framework from "an architecture that explains dark energy and gestures at particle physics" to "an architecture that generates a comparable web of quantitative predictions across all accessible sectors." Each track targets a specific gap, produces a specific deliverable, and either strengthens the framework or identifies where it breaks.The organizing principle: every track must produce a number that can be compared to a measurement.---Architecture: 10 Programs, 32 Tracks, 6 WavesProgram A: Completing the Gravitational SectorThe gravitational sector is the framework's strongest suit — αT = 0 exactly, growth-expansion decoupling, self-tuning across 60 orders of magnitude. But three computations remain undone that would either cement or crack this foundation.Track 19A.1 — Cross-term cancellation proof.The αT = 0 derivation (§1.9) relies on cross-terms between 4D curvature and warp-factor derivatives vanishing upon Z₂-orbifold integration. This is stated but deferred. Produce the complete decomposition of the 5D GB invariant E₅ under KK reduction, showing term-by-term cancellation. Deliverable: appendix-ready proof with explicit tensor algebra.Track 19A.2 — Nonlinear dynamical self-tuning.The self-tuning demonstration (Table 1.1) is algebraic — it shows the constraint equations are Λ₅-independent. The dynamical test — solving the coupled Einstein-scalar system with a time-dependent Λ₅(t) source and demonstrating that the brane geometry relaxes to the self-tuning attractor — has not been performed. This is the most-cited weakness of all self-tuning proposals. Deliverable: numerical evolution code and convergence plots showing dynamical relaxation for step-function, ramp, and oscillatory Λ₅(t) perturbations.Track 19A.3 — Black hole solutions in the Meridian framework.What does a black hole look like in the 5D cuscuton-GB theory? The cuscuton's constraint character should modify the black hole hair theorems. Does the no-hair theorem hold? Does the cuscuton produce a non-trivial scalar profile around black holes, and if so, what are the observational signatures in quasinormal mode frequencies or shadow geometry? Deliverable: static, spherically symmetric black hole solution in the 5D theory, KK-reduced to 4D, with quasinormal mode spectrum compared to GR predictions. Testable by: Event Horizon Telescope, LISA ringdown measurements.Track 19A.4 — Gravitational wave memory effect.The cuscuton's zero propagating DOF means it cannot carry gravitational wave memory in the standard scalar channel. But the GB correction reintroduces a propagating mode. What is the gravitational wave memory signature of a binary merger in the Meridian framework? How does it differ from GR? Deliverable: displacement memory formula with numerical estimate for LIGO/ET-detectable events.---Program B: The Decoupled Perturbation Test (Completing 18A)This is the single most important near-term computation and the gateway to a PRL letter.Track 19B.1 — Cobaya MCMC: Fit A (constant-w + GR perturbations).Complete the proper MCMC with CAMB evaluations, Planck PR4 likelihood, DESI DR2 BAO, DES Y5 SNe, and fσ₈ data. Convergence criterion: Gelman-Rubin R−1 < 0.01. Deliverable: posterior distributions, χ² decomposition by data class, marginalized parameter constraints.Track 19B.2 — Cobaya MCMC: Fit B (CPL + coupled perturbations).Same data, same convergence criteria, standard CPL parameterization with perturbations derived from w(z). Deliverable: same outputs as 19B.1.Track 19B.3 — Mock data validation.Generate mock data from a constant-w + GR-perturbation universe (the Meridian prediction). Analyze with CPL + coupled perturbations. Does the CPL fit manufacture phantom crossing from decoupled data? This is the smoking gun for the compromise artifact hypothesis. Deliverable: recovered (w₀, wₐ) from mock data, demonstrating whether the CPL parameterization creates spurious wₐ.Track 19B.4 — PRL letter draft.If |ΔAIC| < 4 between Fit A and Fit B, or if mock data demonstrate the compromise artifact: draft a letter titled "Constant Dark Energy with GR Perturbations: A Cuscuton Interpretation of the DESI Signal." If ΔAIC > 10: draft a letter documenting the growth-expansion split as a model-independent observable. Either outcome is publishable.---Program C: Gauge Coupling UnificationThe spread of 10.81 in α_i⁻¹ at Λ_NCG is the framework's most honest structural gap. This program either closes it or identifies precisely what additional physics is needed.Track 19C.1 — Asymptotic safety beta functions on warped RS background.Compute the non-universal gravitational contributions to gauge beta functions using the functional renormalization group on the warped RS geometry. The flat-background Eichhorn results are insufficient because the warping modifies the heat kernel coefficients. Deliverable: the three gauge couplings at Λ_NCG with AS corrections, and the resulting spread. Target: spread < 1.0.Track 19C.2 — KK threshold corrections.The KK tower contributes threshold corrections at each mass level m_n = n·k·e^{-kyc}. These corrections are non-universal (different representations have different KK spectra due to different bulk mass parameters). Compute the full one-loop threshold correction to each gauge coupling from the first 100 KK modes. Deliverable: corrected gauge couplings and residual spread.Track 19C.3 — Proton decay predictions.The NCG spectral triple predicts the gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1), not a GUT group. This means proton decay through gauge boson exchange is absent. But dimension-6 operators from the KK tower could mediate proton decay at a computable rate. Deliverable: proton lifetime prediction with uncertainty band. Testable by: Hyper-Kamiokande (current bound: τ_p > 10³⁴ years).---Program D: Dark Matter — From Identification to PredictionThe keV sterile neutrino was identified by exclusion (§4.13). This program turns the identification into a quantitative prediction program.Track 19D.1 — Relic abundance from the seesaw mechanism.Compute the sterile neutrino relic abundance from the Dodelson-Widrow mechanism as modified by the S₃-constrained Majorana mass matrix. The production rate depends on the active-sterile mixing angle θ, which the framework constrains through the bulk mass parameters. Deliverable: relic abundance Ωs h² as a function of sterile neutrino mass ms, with the Meridian-predicted mixing angle. Compare to Ωdm h² = 0.120 ± 0.001.Track 19D.2 — X-ray line prediction.A keV sterile neutrino decays radiatively: ν_s → ν + γ, producing a monochromatic X-ray line at E = ms/2. Compute the decay rate and the predicted flux for galaxy clusters and the Milky Way halo. Is it consistent with the unidentified 3.5 keV line reported (controversially) by Bulbul et al. (2014) and Boyarsky et al. (2014)? Deliverable: predicted line energy, flux, and comparison to current X-ray constraints (XMM-Newton, Chandra, Hitomi/XRISM).Track 19D.3 — Structure formation with keV warm dark matter.A keV-scale dark matter particle has a free-streaming length that suppresses small-scale structure. Compute the matter power spectrum P(k) with the Meridian sterile neutrino and compare to Lyman-alpha forest constraints. If the predicted mass is too low (< 3.3 keV from Lyman-alpha), the candidate is excluded and the framework needs a different dark matter solution. Deliverable: transfer function T(k) and Lyman-alpha comparison.Track 19D.4 — Direct detection prospects.Can the sterile neutrino be detected in laboratory experiments? The KATRIN and TRISTAN experiments search for keV-scale sterile neutrinos through kinks in the tritium beta decay spectrum. Compute the expected signal strength from the Meridian mixing angle and compare to TRISTAN sensitivity. Deliverable: predicted kink amplitude and discovery/exclusion forecast.---Program E: The Neutrino Sector — Precision PredictionsThe neutrino sector has the near-term testability advantage: DUNE, JUNO, and CMB-S4 are operating or under construction.Track 19E.1 — Full three-flavor oscillation parameters.Using the S₃-broken democratic matrix with bulk mass parameters from Track 17M (honest negative: democratic Moct governs Dirac, not Majorana), compute all six oscillation parameters (three angles, two mass splittings, one CP phase) and their correlations. Do not assume tribimaximal as leading order — start from the corrected S₃ structure. Deliverable: six predicted numbers with uncertainties, compared to NuFIT 5.3 global fit.Track 19E.2 — Neutrinoless double beta decay rate.The Majorana effective mass mee determines the 0νββ rate. The framework predicts normal hierarchy with specific mee. Compute the half-life prediction for ⁷⁶Ge (LEGEND-1000) and ¹³⁶Xe (nEXO). Deliverable: predicted half-life and comparison to experimental sensitivity. This is a concrete negative prediction — no signal expected.Track 19E.3 — Leptogenesis baryon asymmetry.The seesaw mechanism with the Meridian Majorana mass matrix produces a lepton asymmetry that is converted to baryon asymmetry through sphalerons. Compute the baryon-to-photon ratio η_B from the framework's parameters. Compare to the Planck measurement η_B = (6.14 ± 0.02) × 10⁻¹⁰. If the computation matches: a quantitative explanation of the matter-antimatter asymmetry from the same geometry that produces dark energy. Deliverable: predicted η_B with uncertainty.---Program F: Collider PhenomenologyThe framework predicts Higgs-radion mixing, KK tower resonances, and specific deviations in Higgs couplings. These are testable at HL-LHC and future colliders.Track 19F.1 — Higgs coupling deviations at ξ = 1/6.The conformal coupling produces specific deviations in the Higgs couplings to gauge bosons and fermions through Higgs-radion mixing. The conformal det(Z) = 1 property constrains the mixing matrix. Compute κ_V, κ_f (Higgs coupling modifiers) as functions of the radion mass and mixing angle. Deliverable: predicted coupling deviations compared to HL-LHC projected sensitivity (κ precision ~2-5%). The d > c diagnostic (§4.16.3) — the prediction that Higgs couplings to massive gauge bosons are modified more than to fermions — is a unique signature.Track 19F.2 — Radion production cross-section at HL-LHC.The radion mass estimate is O(100 GeV) (§1.4.3). Compute the gluon-fusion production cross-section for the radion at the LHC (14 TeV) and the expected branching ratios. Compare to existing searches for additional scalar resonances. Deliverable: σ(gg→ϕ_r) × BR for each decay channel, with HL-LHC discovery/exclusion reach.Track 19F.3 — First KK graviton resonance.The first KK graviton mode has mass m₁ = x₁ · k · e^{-kyc} where x₁ ≈ 3.83 (first zero of J₁). For k ~ 10⁸ GeV and the RS hierarchy: m₁ ~ TeV. Compute the production cross-section and the diphoton and dilepton signatures at HL-LHC. Deliverable: mass prediction, cross-section, and comparison to current CMS/ATLAS limits on Randall-Sundrum graviton resonances.---Program G: Inflation — From Prediction to PrecisionThe Kähler modulus inflation mechanism (§4.15) predicts specific inflationary observables. This program sharpens those predictions to the point where LiteBIRD can confirm or exclude them.Track 19G.1 — Full inflationary power spectrum.Compute the scalar power spectrum P_s(k) and tensor power spectrum P_t(k) from the Kähler modulus potential with α = 1 attractor behavior. The reheating temperature T_rh determines N* which feeds back into n_s and r. Compute T_rh from the radion coupling to SM fields (determined by ξ = 1/6 and the KK reduction). Deliverable: T_rh, N*, and the resulting precision predictions for (n_s, r).Track 19G.3 — Non-Gaussianity.The cuscuton's constraint character during inflation (if it was active during the inflationary epoch) would produce distinctive non-Gaussianity signatures in the CMB bispectrum. Compute f_NL for the local, equilateral, and orthogonal templates. Deliverable: predicted f_NL values compared to Planck constraints and CMB-S4 projections.---Program H: Gravitational Wave AstronomyThe framework predicts GW signals across the frequency spectrum. Making this explicit tests whether the thermal history is consistent.Track 19I.1 — The RS stabilization epoch.When did the extra dimension stabilize? The radion potential has a minimum at yc = 39.56/k, but at high temperatures the minimum disappears (the Creminelli-Nicolis thermal deformation). Compute the critical temperature T_c for the RS phase transition. Is it above or below the electroweak scale? Deliverable: T_c and the resulting cosmological timeline.Track 19I.2 — Electroweak phase transition in the RS background.The Higgs potential on the IR brane is modified by the warping and by the Higgs-radion mixing at ξ = 1/6. Is the electroweak phase transition first-order in the Meridian framework? If so, it contributes additional GW signal and enables electroweak baryogenesis. Deliverable: order of the phase transition, critical temperature, and GW contribution if first-order.Track 19I.3 — Big Bang nucleosynthesis consistency.The modified expansion rate H(a) from the constant-w dark energy prediction affects the neutron-to-proton ratio at BBN. For w₀ near −1, the effect is negligible, but this must be verified quantitatively. Also: the presence of the keV sterile neutrino contributes to the effective number of neutrino species N_eff. Compute ΔN_eff from the Meridian sterile neutrino and compare to the Planck constraint N_eff = 2.99 ± 0.17. Deliverable: predicted primordial helium abundance Y_p and deuterium D/H, compared to observations.---Program J: The ζ₀ Determination — From Parameter to PredictionThe framework's deepest remaining gap is that ζ₀ is undetermined. This program attacks the gap from every available angle.Track 19J.1 — Complete brane parameter space scan.The junction conditions depend on (σ_UV, α_UV, µ²). σ_UV is fixed by RS structure. The b₃/₂ computation gave α_UV = −5×10⁻⁴. What range of µ² is consistent with radion stability, ghost-freedom, and the spectral action constraints? Map the full viable parameter space and the resulting ζ₀ distribution. Deliverable: the allowed ζ₀ range from all known constraints, without reference to cosmological data.Track 19J.2 — Asymptotic safety ζ₀ determination.The AS fixed point drives ξ → 0 for generic scalars (Eichhorn et al.), but the radion is geometrically protected at ξ = 1/6. What does the AS flow predict for ζ₀? If the fixed point determines ζ₀ as well as ξ, the framework becomes zero-parameter. Deliverable: ζ₀ from the AS beta function, if computable.Track 19J.3 — Self-adjoint extension uniqueness.The 5D Dirac operator on the orbifold requires specification of boundary conditions (the self-adjoint extension). If the extension is unique — forced by the spectral triple axioms — then ζ₀ is determined. If non-unique, the ambiguity in the extension parameterizes the remaining freedom. Deliverable: classification of self-adjoint extensions of D₅ on the RS orbifold compatible with the NCG axioms.Track 19J.4 — Observational ζ₀ determination roadmap.If ζ₀ cannot be determined from first principles, design the optimal observational strategy for measuring it. Which combination of DESI Y5, Euclid, CMB-S4, and growth data provides the tightest constraint? What is the projected σ(ζ₀) at each survey milestone? Deliverable: Fisher forecast for ζ₀ from combined Stage IV surveys.---Wave StructureWave	Tracks	Duration	Dependencies1	19A.1, 19B.1–B.2, 19C.1, 19D.1, 19E.1, 19J.1	2–3 weeks	None (all independent starts)2	19A.2, 19B.3, 19C.2, 19D.2, 19E.2, 19F.1, 19G.1, 19H.1	2–3 weeks	Wave 1 results feed 19B.3, 19D.23	19A.3, 19B.4, 19C.3, 19D.3, 19E.3, 19F.2, 19G.2, 19H.2, 19I.1, 19J.2	3–4 weeks	Waves 1–24	19A.4, 19D.4, 19F.3, 19G.3, 19H.3, 19I.2, 19I.3, 19J.3	2–3 weeks	Wave 35	19J.4, comprehensive cross-check	1–2 weeks	All prior waves6	Revision gate: all Phase 19 results integrated, monograph updated, PRL letter submitted	1 week	Wave 5---What Success Looks LikeAt the end of Phase 19, the Meridian framework should have:A published PRL letter on the decoupled perturbation test (Program B), either confirming or refuting the compromise artifact hypothesis.Quantitative predictions in every experimentally accessible sector:Dark energy: w₀(ζ₀), wₐ = 0, cs ≈ 11cGravitational waves: αT = 0, LISA spectrum, primordial background, ringdown correctionsNeutrinos: six oscillation parameters, mee, δ_CP, η_BDark matter: sterile neutrino mass, relic abundance, X-ray line, Lyman-alpha constraintColliders: Higgs coupling deviations, radion mass and cross-section, KK gravitonInflation: n_s, r, running, f_NLBBN: Y_p, D/H, ΔN_effGauge sector: unification with AS corrections, proton lifetimeA clear determination of which predictions are structural (following from the axioms with no free parameters) and which are parametric (depending on ζ₀ or other undetermined quantities). The former are the framework's strongest tests; the latter map out the parameter space that observations must explore.Honest negatives documented with the same rigor as positive results. Any track that returns "the framework cannot predict this" or "the prediction contradicts observation" is logged, analyzed for what it reveals, and used to constrain the surviving framework.A decision point on ζ₀: either it is determined from first principles (Tracks 19J.1–J.3), in which case the framework becomes zero-parameter and maximally falsifiable, or it is not, in which case the observational roadmap (19J.4) tells us exactly when and how nature will determine it for us.---What Failure Looks LikePhase 19 fails if any of the following occur:The dark matter candidate is excluded by Lyman-alpha (Track 19D.3) AND no alternative candidate exists within the framework.The leptogenesis η_B is off by more than an order of magnitude (Track 19E.3), indicating the seesaw mechanism doesn't produce enough asymmetry.The BBN consistency check fails (Track 19I.3) — N_eff from the sterile neutrino exceeds the Planck bound.The gauge unification spread remains > 5 after AS and threshold corrections (Tracks 19C.1–C.2), indicating the NCG spectral triple is incomplete.The decoupled perturbation test shows ΔAIC > 10 for CPL even with proper treatment AND the mock data test shows no compromise artifact (Tracks 19B.1–B.3), killing the constant-w prediction without an escape route.Each of these is a specific, binary outcome. The framework either survives each test or it doesn't. That's the point.---A Note on ScopeThis plan does not include everything a TOE must eventually address. It does not tackle quantum gravity in the deep UV (beyond the asymptotic safety bridge). It does not address the measurement problem in quantum mechanics. It does not attempt to derive the configuration space topology of DoPI from the Meridian geometry. It does not compute string-theoretic dualities or M-theory embeddings. These are Phase 20+ problems that depend on Phase 19 results.Phase 19 is about predictions. Numbers that can be compared to measurements. The philosophy is for the evenings. The physics is for the mornings. And the computations are for right now.---"Twenty-two tracks. Six honest negatives. Each one a chisel strike.
