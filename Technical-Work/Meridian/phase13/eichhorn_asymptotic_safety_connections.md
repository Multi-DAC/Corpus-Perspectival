## Why This Matters

Eichhorn's asymptotic safety (AS) program is converging on the same structural nexus as Meridian from a different direction: UV fixed points, SM parameter derivation from gravitational constraints, and the claim that different QG approaches are windows on one physics. Six specific contact points identified, plus five literature gaps that Meridian could fill.

## Contact Point 1: Non-Minimal Coupling ξ

**The most striking overlap.** Meridian derives ξ = 1/6 (conformal coupling) from three independent proofs. Eichhorn's group has "hints" that AS constrains ξ:

- **Eichhorn, Pauly, Schiffer**, "Constraining power of asymptotic safety for scalar fields," arXiv:[2009.13543](https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.13543), *Phys. Rev.* **D103** (2021) 026006.
  - At the AS fixed point with finite top Yukawa, the non-minimal coupling ξ may be constrained. Result described as "hints" — no definitive value published.

**Significance for Meridian:** If AS independently converges on ξ = 1/6, the Meridian derivation (from spectral triple Weyl invariance) and the AS derivation (from UV fixed-point structure) would represent two completely independent proofs of the same result from different QG programs. This would dramatically strengthen the conformal coupling claim. **Track and cite.**

## Contact Point 2: Positivity Bounds & UV Completion (Track 13H)

Directly relevant to our Adams et al. question:

- **Eichhorn, Pedersen, Schiffer**, "Application of positivity bounds in asymptotically safe gravity," arXiv:[2405.08862](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.08862), *Eur. Phys. J.* **C85** (2025) 14449.
  - **Result:** Positivity bounds on four-photon couplings HOLD when UV-completed by AS. Implies unitarity, microcausality, locality, Lorentz symmetry preserved.

- **Eichhorn, Knorr, Platania**, "Unearthing the intersections: positivity bounds, weak gravity conjecture, and asymptotic safety," arXiv:[2405.08860](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.08860), *JHEP* **03** (2025) 003.
  - **Result:** Two viable AS gravitational fixed points. Most of the AS landscape respects positivity bounds.

**Significance for Meridian:** Our c_s ~ 10c (superluminal scalar sound speed) appears to violate naive positivity bounds. These papers show AS respects positivity for *photons*, but scalar sectors are untouched. Key question: does Meridian's 5D UV completion (subluminal bulk propagation) serve the same role that AS serves for photons? **Cite in revised Paper V §5.3.2.** The gap — no AS study of superluminal *scalar* propagation — is also worth noting.

## Contact Point 3: Dark Matter Exclusions

AS constrains specific DM models, potentially complementary to our KK tower candidates:

- **de Brito, Eichhorn, Lino dos Santos**, "Are there ALPs in the asymptotically safe landscape?" arXiv:[2112.08972](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.08972), *JHEP* **06** (2022) 013.
  - **Fundamental ALPs likely excluded** — requires strong gravity fluctuations in tension with weak-gravity bound.

- **de Brito et al.**, "Ruling out models of vector dark matter in AS quantum gravity," arXiv:[2312.02086](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.02086), *Phys. Rev.* **D109** (2024) 055022.
  - Specific vector DM models ruled out.

- **Assant, Eichhorn, Knorr**, "Towards theory constraints on ultralight dark matter from quantum gravity," arXiv:[2510.23808](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.23808), submitted October 2025.
  - Ultralight scalar DM couplings may vanish in AS gravity.

**Significance for Meridian:** If AS excludes simple ALPs, WIMPs, and ultralight scalars, and our framework provides KK graviton DM candidates from the extra dimension, the two programs are complementary — AS narrows the space, Meridian fills it with geometric candidates. **Cite in Paper I discussion of DM.**

## Contact Point 4: Higgs Mass Prediction

- **Eichhorn, Held**, "Top mass from asymptotic safety," arXiv:[1707.01107](https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.01107), *Phys. Lett.* **B777** (2018) 217-221.
  - Top pole mass ~ 171 GeV from AS. Higgs mass ~ 125 GeV follows if SM running below M_Pl is assumed.

- **Eichhorn, Held**, "Higgs scalar potential in asymptotically safe quantum gravity," arXiv:[1811.11706](https://arxiv.org/abs/1811.11706), *Phys. Rev.* **D99** (2019) 086010.
  - Higgs quartic is an **irrelevant coupling** at the AS fixed point → Higgs-to-top mass ratio is predictable.

**Significance for Meridian:** Our NCG spectral triple also constrains SM parameters (Higgs sector from finite space F). If both AS and NCG independently predict the Higgs mass from different principles, that's convergent evidence. **Cite in Paper IV comparison.**

## Contact Point 5: Cosmological Constant & Unimodular Gravity

- **Eichhorn**, "On unimodular quantum gravity," arXiv:[1301.0879](https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.0879), *Class. Quantum Grav.* **30** (2013) 115016.
  - In unimodular gravity, Λ is not renormalized. UV fixed point found.

- **de Brito, Eichhorn, Pereira**, "A link that matters," arXiv:[1907.11173](https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.11173), *JHEP* **09** (2019) 100.
  - Confronts unimodular AS with SM constraints.

**Significance for Meridian:** Unimodular gravity handles Λ by removing it from the action. Our self-tuning mechanism handles Λ by making Λ₄ independent of Λ₅ through the cuscuton constraint. Different mechanisms, same target. Both address the "old" CC problem (why Λ is small) but not the "new" one (why ρ_DE ~ ρ_matter today). **Cite in Paper I §1.10.X (coincidence problem revision).**

## Contact Point 6: Extra Dimensions + AS

- **Ohta, Percacci**, "Higher Derivative Gravity and Asymptotic Safety in Diverse Dimensions," arXiv:[1308.3398](https://arxiv.org/abs/1308.3398), *Class. Quantum Grav.* **31** (2014) 015024.
  - **AS survives in 5D.** UV fixed points found in all dimensions 3-6.

- **Gerwick, Litim, Plehn**, "Asymptotic safety and Kaluza-Klein gravitons at the LHC," arXiv:[1101.5548](https://arxiv.org/abs/1101.5548), *Eur. Phys. J.* **C71** (2011) 1676.
  - AS regularizes UV behavior of KK graviton exchange in extra dimensions.

**Significance for Meridian:** AS surviving in 5D means our RS orbifold geometry is compatible with AS UV completion in principle. But nobody has studied AS in a *warped* geometry — this is a gap Meridian could fill. **Note in Paper I discussion.**

## Five Literature Gaps Meridian Could Fill

1. **NCG + AS bridge:** No paper connects Connes' spectral action to AS fixed points. Meridian uses both.
2. **Warped 5D + AS:** AS survives in flat 5D, but RS warping is unstudied.
3. **Gauss-Bonnet in 5D + AS:** In 4D the GB term is topological; in 5D it matters, but no AS computation exists. Our ε₁ from GB has no AS counterpart.
4. **Superluminal scalar propagation + AS:** AS respects positivity for photons. Scalars untouched.
5. **ξ = 1/6 from AS:** Hints but no definitive value. If AS predicts ξ = 1/6, we'd have convergence from NCG + AS + conformal invariance.
