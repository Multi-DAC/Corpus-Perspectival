## 2. Key Results

1. **All KK modes unconstrained by LHC.** At standard RS1 parameters, the lightest KK mode (gauge bosons at 3.7 TeV) is above the LHC kinematic reach. Current ATLAS/CMS limits (m > 4.3 TeV for k/M_Pl = 0.1) do not apply to k/M_Pl = 1.

2. **Gauge-graviton mass ratio is a geometric prediction:** m_1^gauge / m_1^grav = x_1(J_0) / x_1(J_1) = 0.628. This is determined solely by the RS geometry and is independent of k, ky_c, or any Meridian-specific parameters.

3. **Non-uniform Bessel spacing:** The KK tower spacing follows the Bessel zero pattern, not equal spacing. Observation of multiple KK modes with the correct spacing pattern uniquely identifies RS geometry.

4. **Zeta_0 mass shift:** The non-minimal coupling shifts all KK masses by +2.2% (zeta_0 = 0.022 from 16R). At m_1 = 5.9 TeV, this is Delta_m = 129 GeV — measurable in principle at FCC-hh.

5. **Discovery hierarchy:** Radion (lightest) -> KK gauge bosons -> KK graviton -> higher KK modes. The radion provides the first evidence; the KK tower confirms the extra-dimensional origin.
