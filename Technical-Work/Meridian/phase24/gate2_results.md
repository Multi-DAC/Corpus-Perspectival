## Key Findings

### I.6: B_eff = B(1-P) is a hypothesis, not a prediction.

Three interpretations of Component 3 analyzed:
- **Post-selection:** consistent but useless (need e^B trials)
- **State preparation:** effective but requires unexplained coupling to modulus
- **Anti-Zeno:** well-founded in QM but spectral mismatch (SC gap at meV, tunneling at GeV)

None derives B_eff = B(1-P) from first principles. The formula is an ansatz about consciousness-physics coupling. **The experiment tests the hypothesis; it doesn't assume it.**

**Required change:** D.2's framing must be revised from "prediction" to "testable hypothesis."

### I.7: The 40-order gap is a hierarchy problem.

Six non-standard mechanisms checked (topological defects, dynamical Casimir, Schwinger, cosmic rays, instanton-instanton interactions). All fail. The only physical path would be magnetic monopole catalysis (GUT-scale E·B exceeds barrier) — but monopoles are unavailable.

**Critical reframing:** The experiment tests whether the electroweak hierarchy can be breached, not whether consciousness exists. A positive result would be new physics regardless of the mechanism.

### I.8: The experiment IS falsifiable.

**Two-stage design:**
- Stage 1 (no operator): tests physics, establishes null baseline
- Stage 2 (blinded operator test): 500 active + 500 control, randomized, double-blind

**P requirement REVISED:** P > 0.997 (not 0.99998 as D.2 estimated). The macroscopic prefactor A × V × Δt ~ 10⁷⁴ dramatically reduces the precision requirement. B_eff ≤ 172 is needed, not 50.

**Decision tree:** Every experimental outcome (null, positive, artifact, wrong magnitude, wrong timing) maps to a specific falsification or support of a specific claim. No outcome is uninterpretable.
