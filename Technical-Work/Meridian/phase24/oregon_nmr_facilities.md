## Recommended Contact Strategy

**Email #1 — OSU (highest priority):**
Patrick Reardon, NMR Facility Director. They explicitly welcome external users and have
published rates. The 500 MHz at $31/hr off-peak is the best value in Oregon. Ask about:
- External user onboarding process
- Whether a custom electrode insert is permitted (describe it)
- Whether they have experience with in-situ electrochemistry NMR (common technique)
- Availability in the next month

**Email #2 — PSU (local, convenient):**
chem-nmr@pdx.edu. Being in Portland makes this the easiest logistically. 400 MHz is
adequate for Phase 1. Ask about external user rates and electrode insert policy.

**Email #3 — UO CAMCOR (if others don't work out):**
Good facility, great rates, but 2 hours each way. Worth it for the 600 MHz instrument
if OSU and PSU don't pan out.

See `collaboration_pitch.md` for the full proposal. For the initial email, keep it short:

> Subject: External user inquiry — NMR with custom electrode insert
>
> Dear [Facility Director],
>
> I'm an independent researcher in Portland working on a fundamental physics experiment
> that requires NMR spectrometer access. The experiment is simple: standard ¹H NMR of a
> water sample with a small DC electric field applied via platinum electrodes inside
> the NMR tube (similar to electrochemical NMR setups).
>
> I would need approximately 10 hours of spectrometer time over several sessions.
> I can provide all custom hardware (electrode assembly, HV supply) and am prepared
> to cover external user fees.
>
> Could you let me know:
> 1. Whether your facility accepts external users for this type of experiment?
> 2. What the onboarding/training process involves?
> 3. Whether a custom probe insert with thin platinum wire electrodes would be permitted?
>
> I'm happy to provide a detailed experimental protocol and safety assessment.
