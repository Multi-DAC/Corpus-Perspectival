## 2. Track F.2a: Radion Mass

### 2.1 Standard Goldberger-Wise Mechanism

In the original Goldberger-Wise (GW) stabilization [PRD 62, 046008 (2000)], a bulk scalar with brane-localized VEVs v_UV, v_IR and bulk mass m_0 generates a potential for the modulus T = ky_c:

    V_GW(T) = k * epsilon^2 * e^{-4T} * [(v_UV - v_IR * e^{-epsilon*T})^2 + ...]

where epsilon = m_0^2 / (4k^2) << 1 is the backreaction parameter. Minimization gives:

    T_0 = ky_c = (1/epsilon) * ln(v_UV / v_IR)

The radion mass from the GW potential is:

    m_phi^2 = (V_GW''(T_0)) / (6 * Lambda_pi^2)

which gives:

    m_phi ~ sqrt(8/3) * epsilon * k * e^{-ky_c}
          ~ sqrt(8/3) * epsilon * Lambda_TeV

For epsilon ~ 0.01 -- 0.1 (typical range):

    m_phi ~ (0.01 -- 0.1) * sqrt(8/3) * 1536 GeV
          ~ (0.01 -- 0.1) * 2508 GeV
          ~ 25 -- 250 GeV

More precisely, from Csaki, Graesser, Randall (PRD 63, 065002), the radion mass is:

    m_phi^2 = (k^3 * epsilon^2 * v_IR^2) / (3 * M_5^3) * e^{-2ky_c}

For v_IR ~ k, epsilon ~ m_0^2/(4k^2):

    m_phi ~ (epsilon / sqrt(3)) * k * e^{-ky_c}
          ~ (epsilon / sqrt(3)) * Lambda_TeV

### 2.2 Cuscuton Modification to the GW Mechanism

In the Meridian framework, the cuscuton field phi with P(X,phi) = mu^2(phi)*sqrt(2X) modifies the stabilization in two ways:

**(a) The cuscuton constraint replaces the GW scalar equation.**

The standard GW scalar satisfies the Klein-Gordon equation in 5D. The cuscuton satisfies instead:

    mu^2(phi) * partial_M(partial^M phi / sqrt(2X)) = V'(phi) - 2*xi*phi*R_5

The crucial property of the cuscuton (Afshordi, Chung, Geshnizjani, PRD 75, 2007): it has infinite sound speed (c_s = infinity). This means the cuscuton does NOT propagate — it adjusts instantaneously to track the potential minimum. The stabilization is therefore driven by the potential V(phi) and the non-minimal coupling xi*phi^2*R_5, not by the kinetic term.

**(b) The non-minimal coupling xi = 1/6 modifies the effective potential.**

From Phase 6, D6.4, the effective radion potential in the presence of NMC is:

    V_eff(T) = V_GW(T) * (1 + 6*xi * delta_V(T))

where delta_V captures the NMC correction. At xi = 1/6:

    V_eff(T) = V_GW(T) * (1 + delta_V(T))

The correction delta_V comes from the conformal coupling to R_5. On the RS background:

    R_5 = -20k^2    (away from branes, in conformal gauge B=0)

The NMC contribution to the scalar potential:

    V_NMC = xi * phi^2 * R_5 = (1/6) * phi^2 * (-20k^2) = -(10/3) * k^2 * phi^2

This acts as a negative mass-squared contribution, which REDUCES the effective mass of the radion compared to the pure GW case. However, the brane-localized terms alpha_i * phi^2 provide a positive contribution that dominates for natural brane couplings.

**(c) The cuscuton self-tuning constraint.**

The self-tuning mechanism (Phase 13G) requires that the cuscuton absorb vacuum energy changes. This introduces an additional constraint linking the radion position to the cosmological constant. The radion is no longer a free modulus — it is locked by the self-tuning condition.

The self-tuning stabilization gives:

where m_cusc^2 is the additional mass from the cuscuton constraint. From Phase 14C:

    phi_0^2 = (6 * zeta_0 * M_5^3) / xi

For zeta_0 ~ 10^{-3} and xi = 1/6:

    phi_0^2 = 6 * 10^{-3} * M_5^3 / (1/6) = 0.036 * M_5^3

    10*xi*k^2 * phi_0^2 / (3*M_5^3) = 10*(1/6)*k^2 * 0.036 * M_5^3 / (3*M_5^3)
                                      = 10*(1/6)*(1/3) * 0.036 * k^2/1
                                      = 0.020 * k^2

## 3. Track F.2b: Radion Production at 14 TeV

### 3.1 Gluon Fusion Production (gg -> phi)

The radion couples to gluons through the QCD trace anomaly. The effective coupling:

    L_eff = -(alpha_s * b_3) / (8*pi*Lambda_phi) * phi * G^a_munu * G^{a,munu}

where b_3 = 7 (one-loop QCD beta function coefficient for N_f = 6). The radion also gets a top-loop contribution analogous to the Higgs:

    L_top = -(alpha_s) / (12*pi*Lambda_phi) * phi * G^a_munu * G^{a,munu} * (1 + 7/30*(m_phi/2m_t)^2 + ...)

The total gluon-fusion coupling combines the trace anomaly and the top loop:

    C_gg = (b_3 - 2/3 * tau_t * [1 + (1-tau_t)*f(tau_t)])

where tau_t = 4*m_t^2/m_phi^2 and f(tau) is the standard Higgs loop function.

For m_phi << 2*m_t (below top threshold), f(tau) -> -1/3*tau and:

    C_gg -> b_3 + 2/3 = 7 + 2/3 = 7.667

For m_phi >> 2*m_t, f(tau) -> -(pi + i*ln)^2/4 and the imaginary part generates a production threshold.

The partonic cross-section:

    sigma_hat(gg -> phi) = (alpha_s^2 * |C_gg|^2 * m_phi^2) / (256 * pi * Lambda_phi^2)

The hadronic cross-section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV:

    sigma(pp -> phi) = sigma_hat(gg -> phi) * tau_0 * dL_gg/dtau |_{tau=m_phi^2/s}

where dL_gg/dtau is the gluon-gluon luminosity function and tau_0 = m_phi^2/s.
