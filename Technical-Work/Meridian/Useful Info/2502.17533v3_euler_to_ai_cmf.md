## Key Mathematical Concepts

### Conservative Matrix Field (CMF)
A set of matrices M_x, M_y, M_z on a discrete lattice satisfying path-independence:

    M_x(x,y,z) * M_y(x+1,y,z) = M_y(x,y,z) * M_x(x,y+1,z)
    (and cyclic permutations for M_z)

This is a **discrete flat connection** — zero curvature on a lattice. Different formulas are different trajectories through the lattice.

### Coboundary Equivalence
Two recurrence matrices A(n), B(n) are coboundary equivalent if:

    A(n) * U(n+1) = U(n) * B(n)

for some coboundary matrix U(n). This is the discrete analogue of a gauge transformation.

### UMAPS Algorithm
Novel coboundary solver: uses empirical coboundary matrices (computed from recurrence limits) fitted to rational functions. No nonlinear equations needed. Proven sufficient for degree-d rational entries with N >= 2d+1 steps.

### Dynamical Metrics
- Convergence rate r: how fast the formula approaches the constant
- Irrationality measure delta: INVARIANT under coboundary transformations (the "fingerprint" of a formula's CMF position)
