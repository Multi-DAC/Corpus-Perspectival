**This is the primary connection.**

In our 5D framework, electromagnetism arises from the metric component G_μ5. The full 5D metric with the KK gauge field:

**The EPS claim in Meridian language:** Capacitance uncoupling = modifying the boundary condition for A_μ at the brane. HV oscillation = driving A_μ at high amplitude. "Weightlessness" = local reduction of e^{2A} (the warp factor contribution to gravitational strength).

### 3.7 Magneto-Lattice Bandgaps → KK Spectrum + Soliton Stability ★★

**Paper:** arXiv 2602.00649 — MHD wave bandgaps in periodic magnetic fields.

**Connection:** The mathematics of wave propagation in a spatially periodic magnetic field produces bandgap structures — frequency ranges where MHD modes cannot propagate. This is structurally identical to the KK spectrum in a warped extra dimension: the warp factor A(y) creates a potential well for KK modes, and the eigenvalue spectrum has gaps.

**EPS link:** The EPS mesoscopic interface describes magnetoacoustic waves propagating through a plasma with structured magnetic fields. If the magnetic structure is periodic (e.g., a standing wave pattern on the vehicle surface), then magneto-lattice bandgap physics applies. The bandgap blocks certain return modes after a vacuum transition — this is the mechanism behind the EPS claim of "soliton vacuum-sealed energy with small dissipation rate."

**For Meridian:** The Floquet/Bloch analysis in this paper provides the mathematical template for computing KK spectra in non-standard warp factor profiles (e.g., the cuscuton soft-wall). The soft-wall effect (F → 0 near IR brane) is a kind of "impedance mismatch" that creates effective bandgaps in the KK tower. Phase 3 numerical work should use this analogy explicitly.
