## 2. The Three Candidates

### 2.1 Topology A: Compact Interval / S¹/Z₂ Orbifold

    y ∈ [0, y_c]     with Z₂ orbifold at both endpoints

    UV brane at y = 0:   tension σ_UV > 0, Standard Model or Planck-scale physics
    IR brane at y = y_c:  tension σ_IR < 0, TeV-scale physics (SM matter here)

This is the **Randall-Sundrum 1 (RS1)** setup. The S¹/Z₂ orbifold is topologically equivalent: start with a circle, impose y → −y symmetry, the two fixed points become the two branes.

**Boundary conditions:** Junction conditions J1, J2, J3a, J3b from D1.3 at both branes. The ODE system {S1, S2} is a two-point boundary value problem on [0, y_c].

**Hierarchy:** Generated by the exponential warp factor:

    Λ_IR / Λ_UV = e^{A(y_c)} = e^{∫₀^{y_c} p(y) dy}                ... (2.1)

For p* ≈ −k (constant warp rate), this gives Λ_IR/Λ_UV = e^{−ky_c}. With ky_c ≈ 37: Λ_IR/Λ_UV ≈ 10^{−16}, explaining the weak/Planck hierarchy.

**4D gravity:** The graviton zero mode is normalizable:

    M_Pl² = 2 ∫₀^{y_c} F(φ) e^{2A} dy < ∞                          ... (2.2)

Finite by construction (finite interval). ✓

**Modulus stabilization:** The inter-brane distance y_c must be dynamically fixed. In standard RS1, this requires the Goldberger-Wise mechanism (a bulk scalar with brane-localized potentials). In our framework, the cuscuton + non-minimal coupling naturally stabilizes y_c:

- The scalar constraint (D1.3, eq 6.3) locks φ to the geometry
- The junction conditions (J3a, J3b) fix φ at both branes
- The shooting problem has a unique solution for y_c

The cuscuton IS the modulus stabilizer. No additional mechanism needed.

### 2.2 Topology B: Semi-Infinite Ray

    y ∈ [0, ∞)     with one brane at y = 0

    UV brane at y = 0:   tension σ_UV, all matter localized here
    No IR brane:          extra dimension extends to infinity

This is the **Randall-Sundrum 2 (RS2)** setup.

**Boundary conditions:** Junction conditions at y = 0 only. At y → ∞, regularity requires:

    A(y) → −k·y + O(1)      (asymptotically AdS₅)                   ... (2.3)
    φ(y) → φ_∞              (scalar approaches a constant)           ... (2.4)

The ODE system {S1, S2} must have a trajectory that starts at the UV brane data and asymptotes to a stable fixed point (p*, φ*) as y → ∞.

**Hierarchy:** NOT generated. There is no IR brane — the Standard Model lives on the single UV brane at the Planck scale. The weak scale must be put in by hand or generated by a different mechanism. ✗

**4D gravity:** The graviton zero mode is normalizable if A(y) decreases fast enough:

    M_Pl² = 2 ∫₀^∞ F(φ) e^{2A} dy

For A → −ky: M_Pl² ≈ M₅³/k < ∞. ✓ But there are also continuum KK modes (no mass gap), leading to corrections to Newton's law at distances ~ 1/k.

**Self-tuning:** Compatible with the cuscuton — the trajectory in (p, φ) phase space must approach the fixed point. The cuscuton constraint ensures regularity. But without an IR brane, the self-tuning has only one boundary condition (UV), and the asymptotic condition replaces the IR junction condition.

### 2.3 Topology C: Circle S¹ (Periodic)

    y ∈ [0, L]     with y ~ y + L (periodic identification)

    No branes. All fields periodic in y.

The original **Kaluza-Klein compactification**.

**Boundary conditions:** Periodicity: A(0) = A(L), A'(0) = A'(L), φ(0) = φ(L), φ'(0) = φ'(L). The ODE system {S1, S2} must have periodic orbits (limit cycles) in the (p, φ) phase plane.

**Hierarchy:** No natural mechanism. Without branes, there is no distinction between UV and IR scales. A warped periodic solution (A(y) not constant) would require A(0) = A(L), which means the net warp ∫₀^L A'dy = 0. The warp factor goes down and comes back up — no net hierarchy. ✗

**4D gravity:** For constant A: M_Pl² = 2M₅³L. For warped periodic: M_Pl² = 2∫₀^L F e^{2A} dy. Both finite. ✓

**Self-tuning:** The periodic orbit requirement is more restrictive than the fixed-point behavior of topologies A and B. The Poincaré-Bendixson theorem guarantees limit cycles exist in 2D systems under certain conditions (no fixed points in a trapping region), but our system HAS fixed points — so periodic orbits would need to encircle them. This is possible but constrains (μ², c, ξ) more tightly.

**Matter localization:** Without branes, there is no natural location for Standard Model matter. Fermions must be bulk fields, and their zero modes are determined by the topology + gauge structure (this is where NCG in Phase 5 would enter). More complex but less phenomenologically motivated.

### 4.1 Why Not B or C?

**Topology B (semi-infinite)** is viable as a *limit* of A (sending y_c → ∞). It may be relevant for the RS2-like regime where the KK continuum matters (e.g., short-distance gravity corrections). But it does not generate the hierarchy.

**Topology C (circle)** is relevant for the original KK mechanism (electromagnetism from the 5th dimension). It reenters in Task 2.4 when we restore the KK gauge field — the U(1) gauge symmetry comes from the S¹ isometry. But for the background geometry and the scalar sector, the orbifold interval is the correct setting.

### 4.2 Topology B as Limiting Case

Note that Topology A contains B as a limit:

    y_c → ∞:  Topology A → Topology B                                 ... (4.1)

In this limit, the IR brane moves to infinity, the IR junction conditions become asymptotic boundary conditions, and the discrete KK tower becomes a continuum. The self-tuning analysis goes through with the shooting condition replaced by asymptotic regularity.

We keep this limit in mind for Phase 3 (when we study the KK spectrum and graviton localization), but for Phase 1, the finite interval is the primary setting.
