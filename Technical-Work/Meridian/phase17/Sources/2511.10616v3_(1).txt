I.
INTRODUCTION
Since the discovery of dark energy (DE) in 1998 [1],
cosmological data seemed to favor the existence of a cos-
mological constant ╬ø, a key element of the standard
paradigm of cosmology, the ╬øCDM model. Cosmolog-
ical data from cosmic microwave background (CMB) [2],
large-scale structure (LSS) [3], or supernovae [4] ap-
peared to favor a dark energy equation of state param-
eter close to w = ŌłÆ1, with no compelling evidence for
evolving dark energy. However, since the beginning of
2024, this picture has been challenged by new measure-
ments from the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument
(DESI) [5] and supernovae compilations [6ŌĆō8]. The first
DESI data release (DR1) suggests a mild preference for
evolving dark energy when combined with CMB and su-
pernovae data, deviating from a cosmological constant
by 2.5Žā ŌłÆ3.9Žā [9, 10] (depending on the supernova data
used). This raises concerns about the consistency of su-
pernova dataŌĆōfor instance, Ref. [11] pointed out that a
systematic offset in the magnitude between low and high
redshift supernovae could mimic an evolving dark energy
signal. However, with the second DESI data release, a
preference for evolving dark energy at 3.1Žā was observed
without supernovae measurements [12], along with a 2.3Žā
discrepancy between DESI DR2 BAO and Planck (as-
suming the ╬øCDM model) in the 2D posterior distribu-
tion of {Ōä”m, rd ┬Ę h}, where rd is the sound horizon at
baryon drag. This discrepancy is exacerbated to 3.7Žā in

Interestingly, the reconstructed dark energy equation

We can also invoke axion-like scalar field models [21],

or non-minimally coupled scalar field models [23]. An al-

31], in which two scalar fields (one of quintessence type
and the other of phantom type) combine to allow a cross-
ing of the w = ŌłÆ1 limit without instability.
On the other hand, the potential preference for evolv-
ing dark energy is a good motivation for studying modi-
fied gravity models: is the evolving dark energy, in fact,
the manifestation of an extension or deviation from gen-
eral relativity [32ŌĆō35]?

scribed by the general time-dependent functions ╬▒K, ╬▒B,

B.
EFTofDE framework
EFTofDE provides a physically motivated model-
independent description of the DE perturbations around
an FLRW background for theories with a single scalar
degree of freedom [103, 104]. Working in unitary gauge
and including all operators that are invariant under time-
dependent spatial diffeomorphisms, the quadratic ac-
tion for linear scalar and tensor perturbations can be
parametrized by a small set of time-dependent functions.

equations of motion contain at most second-order deriva-
tives, it is customary to introduce four dimensionless
EFT functions: ╬▒K, ╬▒B, ╬▒M, and ╬▒T , which control the
kinetic term of the scalar field, the braiding between the
kinetic terms of the scalar and the metric, the running of
the Planck mass, and tensor speed excess, respectively.
