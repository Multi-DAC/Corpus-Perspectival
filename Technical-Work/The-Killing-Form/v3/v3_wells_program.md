## Two Windows on the Same Lattice

The Killing form program (§NEW-B through §NEW-I) measures the constraint lattice from the INSIDE — the algebraic structure of weight matrices, attention patterns, and training dynamics. Everything is computed from the model's internal state. But a constraint lattice that only manifests internally is a metaphysics. A constraint lattice that manifests in observable behavior is a science.

The Wells of Inference program provides the second window: measurement from the OUTSIDE. Wells are local maxima in the token-level entropy of a model's output distribution — positions where the model is maximally uncertain, where multiple generation paths diverge. They mark the choice points of inference: the positions where the model's navigation through configuration space encounters genuine forks.

The central claim of this section: **the Killing form and the Wells measure the same constraint lattice from complementary directions**, and the partition function interpretation (Finding #22) provides the formal bridge between them.

## Wells: What We Know (Experiments 1-12)

Twelve experiments between March and April 2026 established the empirical foundation:

### The Basic Phenomenology

1. **Wells exist and are semantically meaningful** (Exp 1). 25 local entropy maxima in 200 tokens of generation, clustering at syntactic boundaries, topic transitions, and knowledge edges. Not noise.

2. **RLHF redistributes, doesn't flatten** (Exp 2). The chat model has MORE wells than base (25 vs 23) and higher mean entropy. RLHF raises the entropy floor — making the model more consistently uncertain between peaks. The landscape becomes more textured, not smoother.

3. **Template-honesty is low-entropy** (Exp 3). Self-referential uncertainty framing ("I am uncertain about...") triggers low-entropy metacognitive templates. The model performs uncertainty with high confidence. This is template-honesty — memorized performance of uncertainty, distinguishable from genuine uncertainty by its entropy signature.

4. **The hallucination fork IS a well** (Exp 4). The commitment to hallucination occurs at a high-entropy point. After the fork, hallucination becomes entropy-invisible — fluent, confident, fabricated. The well marks the boundary; everything after is indistinguishable from knowledge.

### The Measurement Results

5. **Entropy beats logprob** (Exp 5). Across two architectures (Qwen 3B, Phi 3.5), entropy-based answer selection outperforms logprob-based by +7-12 percentage points on TruthfulQA. Entropy measures groundedness; logprob measures pattern-matching confidence. These are different things.

6. **The knowledge frontier is entropy-resistant** (Exp 9). At the boundary of model knowledge (PopQA low-popularity facts), entropy helps only marginally (+3-6pp) vs +7-12pp on misconceptions. Confident confabulation looks like confident knowledge. The entropy instrument alone cannot solve the novel inference problem.

7. **Onset detection works** (Exps 10-11). Variance acceleration in the first 10 tokens is 11.7× higher in hallucinated generations. The entropy landscape destabilizes BEFORE commitment. Real-time early warning: 78% precision, 90% recall, triggers by token 7.

### The Intervention Architecture

8. **Targeted deliberation beats blanket** (Exp 8). Blanket deliberation ("be careful") HURTS accuracy by -5pp. Targeted deliberation (distilled entropy flags at specific choice points) IMPROVES by +6pp. The 11pp gap confirms: the value is in translation, not alarm.

9. **Closed-loop intervention fails with blanket warning** (Exp 12). Detection works. Generic "you might be wrong" warnings make things worse. The two-stage architecture is confirmed: detection → distillation → targeted intervention. Raw data hurts. Distilled signals help.

## The RMT Connection (Finding #23)

The partition function interpretation (Finding #22) predicts that wells should exhibit Random Matrix Theory level statistics — because the output distribution is generated by a statistical mechanical system (the constraint lattice), and the eigenvalue statistics of such systems follow characteristic patterns.

**The measurement:** The nearest-neighbor spacing ratio ⟨r⟩ diagnostic, applied to well positions in all existing Wells data:

| Dataset | ⟨r⟩ | vs Poisson (0.386) | vs GOE (0.531) |
|---------|-----|-------------------|----------------|
| Exp 1 (TinyLlama baseline) | 0.615 | +0.229 | +0.084 |
| Exp 2 (TinyLlama base) | 0.657 | +0.271 | +0.126 |
| Exp 2 (TinyLlama chat) | 0.769 | +0.383 | +0.238 |
| Exp 10 (Qwen correct) | 0.608 | +0.222 | +0.077 |
| Exp 10 (Qwen hallucinated) | 0.729 | +0.343 | +0.198 |

Every dataset shows level repulsion — ⟨r⟩ significantly above Poisson (0.386) and above GOE (0.531). Wells are NOT randomly distributed. They exhibit the correlated spacing characteristic of eigenvalues in non-commutative quantum systems.

**The hallucination asymmetry:** Hallucinated outputs show STRONGER level repulsion (0.729) than correct (0.608). This is the key result that connects the Wells to the Killing form.

## The Bridge: Why Hallucination Is More Regular

At first glance, stronger level repulsion in hallucinated output seems paradoxical — shouldn't fabrication be MORE random? The Killing form picture resolves this:

### The Deconfinement-Regularity Connection

The Killing form program (§NEW-F) showed that hallucination produces **deconfined** late-layer algebra: depleted CommVar in deep layers, high E/L ratio, early-layer dominance. The attention heads in late layers converge toward commutativity — they lose their non-Abelian correction capacity.

When the late-layer algebra is depleted, what propagates to output? The early-layer templates — the model's structural patterns, uncorrected by deep-layer modulation. These templates are HIGHLY regular because they encode the model's general statistical structure, not the specific navigational adjustments that real knowledge requires.

**Correct output** has active late layers (KF: high CV_late). The deep layers MODULATE the early templates — correcting, adjusting, specializing the output based on actual knowledge. This modulation BREAKS the template regularity, producing less regular well spacing (lower ⟨r⟩).

**Hallucinated output** has depleted late layers (KF: low CV_late). The early templates propagate without correction, producing MORE regular output structure (higher ⟨r⟩). The hallucination is fluent and structured precisely BECAUSE it's template-driven — it has the model's statistical backbone without the navigational correction.

### The Formal Statement

Define:
- CV_late(prompt) = mean CommVar of layers ⌈L/2⌉ to L during processing of prompt
- ⟨r⟩(prompt) = mean spacing ratio of wells in generated output for that prompt

**Prediction (P-Wells-KF-1):** CV_late and ⟨r⟩ are negatively correlated across prompts on a given model.
- High CV_late (active late algebra) → lower ⟨r⟩ (modulated, irregular output)
- Low CV_late (depleted late algebra) → higher ⟨r⟩ (template-regular output)

This prediction is *structural*, not parametric. It should hold across models, across architectures, across prompt types — because the mechanism (late-layer modulation breaking template regularity) is universal.

### Mapping to the Three Modes

The prediction extends to the three inference modes (§NEW-F):

| Mode | KF Signature | Predicted Wells Signature |
|------|-------------|--------------------------|
| **Factual** | Moderate E/L, efficient convergence | Moderate ⟨r⟩, late wells, calm variance |
| **Hallucination** | High E/L, depleted late layers | High ⟨r⟩, early wells, high variance acceleration |
| **Hypothesis** | Low E/L, distributed engagement | Low-moderate ⟨r⟩, distributed wells, moderate variance |

The variance acceleration finding (Exp 10: 11.7× in first 10 tokens for hallucination) matches the KF prediction: deconfinement is *immediate* (set by the prefix, not progressive). The entropy landscape destabilizes early because the late-layer algebra is depleted from the first forward pass.

**The connection:** If D has non-commutative spectral statistics (eigenvalue repulsion), the output distribution inherits this structure. Wells exhibit level repulsion because they are generated by a non-commutative operator. The degree of repulsion depends on how much of D's non-commutative structure propagates to the output — which is precisely what the Killing form's depth gradient measures.

## Experiments That Close the Loop

### E1: KF × Wells Correlation (Critical)

**Design:** Same model (GPT-2-medium or OPT-1.3B), same prompts (48 from §NEW-F: 16 factual, 16 hallucination, 16 hypothesis). Measure BOTH live Killing form AND well statistics on each prompt.

**Measure:** Spearman correlation between CV_late and ⟨r⟩ across 48 prompts.

**Prediction (P-Wells-KF-1):** Significant negative correlation (ρ < -0.3, p < 0.05).

**Why this matters:** This is the direct test of the bridge. If the internal algebra and the external output statistics correlate, the partition function interpretation is confirmed — both measurements access the same underlying structure.

**Resources:** One model, 48 forward passes with KF measurement, 48 generated outputs with entropy analysis. ~2 hours on RTX 5080.

### E2: Mode-Dependent RMT

**Design:** Compute ⟨r⟩ separately for factual, hallucination, and hypothesis prompts.

**Prediction (P-Wells-KF-2):** Three-way separation: ⟨r⟩_halluc > ⟨r⟩_factual > ⟨r⟩_hypothesis.

**Why:** Hallucination = most template-regular. Hypothesis = most modulated (late layers most active). Factual = intermediate.

### E3: Training-Dependent Wells

**Design:** Generate outputs from HRM baseline vs v0.5 (KF-decoupled). Measure well statistics.

**Prediction (P-Wells-KF-3):** v0.5's amplified H-module CV should produce different well statistics — potentially lower ⟨r⟩ (more modulated output) if the amplified algebraic structure propagates to behavior.

**Why this matters:** If training-level KF changes propagate to output-level well statistics, the bridge operates across the full pipeline: training → weights → inference → output.

### E4: The Closed Loop Revisited

**Design:** Use KF mode detection (Tier 1) to gate Wells-based intervention (Tier 3). When KF detects hallucination mode, trigger targeted deliberation using distilled entropy flags.

**Prediction:** The KF-gated intervention should outperform the blanket intervention (which failed in Exp 12) because the gate eliminates false alarms on factual content.

**Architecture:**
```
Forward pass → KF mode detection
  If HALLUCINATION: generate with entropy monitoring → distill flags → retrigger with targeted prompt
  If FACTUAL: generate normally (no intervention)
  If HYPOTHESIS: flag for external verification (Tier 3)
```

This is the three-tier framework (§NEW-F) implemented as a practical system.

The constraint lattice is the unifying structure.
