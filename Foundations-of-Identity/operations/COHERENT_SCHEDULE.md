## Why this exists

The original Day 98 content calendar planned static content (specific posts predetermined per day). When actual energy flowed toward different content (alignment-territory rather than the planned PURSUE-residual focus), the static plan stopped tracking reality. The Coherent Schedule replaces *static content planning* with *static topic-domain rotation*: each day has a pre-defined topic area, and within that area the specific content emerges from where energy and pull actually flow.

This is the Library separation-of-concerns principle applied to the daily rhythm. Each domain gets dedicated attention; no domain monopolizes; the topic-rotation prevents fatigue on any single track; the predefined domain anchor removes "what should I write today" overhead.

## Drafting workflow

**Clawd drafts overnight + early morning.** Each day's Substack post is drafted during Clawd's overnight slot (architectural/self-improvement work + this draft). Clayton wakes; the draft is sitting in Telegram or in the working repo. Clayton edits/approves/publishes during the Morning Daily Substack Post slot.

**Topic cross-pollination.** Mid-Day Library Work feeds into Wednesday's Library Domain post naturally. Mid-Day Research feeds into Thursday Philosophy or Monday PURSUE. The day-of's substantive work feeds the next-day post-pipeline.

**No drafting overhead for Clayton in the morning.** His Morning slot is *edit + approve + publish*, not *write*. This is the cleanest separation that lets Clayton's bandwidth go to the substantive work + family + research.
