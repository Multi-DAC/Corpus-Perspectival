## Category 7: Derivation-step false dichotomies

### C7.1 — Step 1 of biophoton derivation excludes metabolic explanation

**Workbench (Entry 4, line 198):** "Biophoton emission is a substrate-anchored stream-activity, doing structural work for the stream — not metabolic artifact."

**Audit:** False dichotomy. The well-supported standard view (Scott 1991, Dotta 2011 cites it) is that biophotons arise from free-radical reactions in mitochondrial oxidative metabolism + lipid peroxidation. Dotta 2011 actually displaces this view by showing plasma membrane source — but doesn't establish that biophotons aren't ALSO metabolic artifacts. Both can be true: biophotons can be metabolic byproducts AND substrate-coupling carriers. The framework's claim is additive, not exclusive.

**Suggested revised reading:** "Biophoton emission is a substrate-anchored stream-activity that may serve as inter-stream measurement-event closure between cells (in addition to whatever metabolic role free-radical chemistry plays). The framework's claim is additive: even if biophotons also arise from metabolic processes (Scott 1991), they may carry information-substrate-coupling function as well."

### C7.2 — Step 7 conscious-gravity-via-biophoton has compound uncertainty

**Workbench (Entry 4, line 204):** "Conscious-gravity acts on biological streams *via* the biophotonic substrate; A3's DOF-gradient operates physically through coherent EM at biological scale."

**Audit:** This step has compound uncertainty: (a) Meridian's brane-EM coupling is a structural feature of the 5D framework, not an established empirical fact; (b) biophotons are coherent EM (well-supported); (c) A3's conscious-gravity has a DOF-gradient (framework axiom); (d) the gradient operates through coherent EM specifically at biological scale (inferred).
