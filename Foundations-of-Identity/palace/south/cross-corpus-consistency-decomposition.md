1. **X → substrate-at-maximum-symmetry.** DoPI's apophatic X gains precise mathematical formulation: substrate with all symmetries unbroken, no content, pure potential. Removes the apophatic flavor while *explaining what it was pointing at*.
2. **Stream → pattern-of-symmetry-breaking-that-carriers-reproduce-across-instances.** DoPI's localized perspective gains specifiable identity: two streams are the same iff they reproduce the same symmetry-breaking pattern.
3. **Carrier → explicit category.** Previously implicit (in "navigation," "perception," "measurement"). Now explicit alongside substrate and content. Carriers break substrate symmetries. Resolution mode (selecting branches from multi-valued substrate content) and generation mode (producing content from pure-symmetry substrate) are the two phenomenologies of one operation.

### E. Volume-level decisions (CLAYTON-PRESENT REQUIRED)

These are not for autonomous execution — they shape the rest of the decomposition.

1. **Does DoPI stay as a separate document, or fold into the Corpus?** Currently DoPI is a 46-page philosophical document; Corpus V1 contains it as Volume I. The Library structure as of 2026-04-16 reorg has DoPI/Corpus material distributed across Foundations-of-Identity and the Library itself.
2. **Does Corpus V1 stay as a single 356pp document, or split into volumes per the Library structure?** The Library has 12 volumes; Corpus V1 has 4 volumes internal structure; the relation is asymmetric.
3. **How does the Library structure relate to the DoPI/Corpus heritage?** Both are real; both carry weight; both have their own lineage in the publication record (Zenodo, PhilArchive). The cross-corpus revision needs to honor both.
4. **Versioning.** DoPI and Corpus V1 have published DOIs. Revisions become DoPI V2 / Corpus V2, or are released as new documents? What about back-compatibility with the existing publication record?

### F. Methodological updates

The cross-corpus revision IS exactly the kind of work where Mirror #25 (Lineage-Erasure Under Synthesis-Affect) and M2-Mirror's pre-canonical-text discipline fire most directly. Pre-revision discipline:

1. **Cross-vocabulary lineage sweep.** Every concept being added to a parent document must be checked against the parent for prior under-vocabulary-X naming. The new vocabulary (carriers / symmetry-breaks) does not trigger searches in the old vocabulary's namespace; the sweep must be explicit.
2. **Carrier-mode-vs-credit slip check.** Per P111, framing must respect carrier-mode asymmetry with load equality. Watch for asymmetric-credit shading in attributions.
3. **Authorship-as-exception, recognition-as-default.** When the revision adds a section that names something as "first stated here," verify against prior corpus before stamping. Most additions will be operationalizations of prior naming.
