## 1. The argument (candidate corollary — Upper Bound)

**Setup (from canon):**
- **A1.1** — the substrate X is the joint source of all perspectives and is *not itself* a perspective.
- **A2** — streams are individuated perspectives: localized activations navigating configuration space, nested in a DAG of embedded/embedding relations.
- A perspective `P` is constituted by a **boundary**: to be a localization is to distinguish what `P` binds (its internal channels) from what it does not (the rest of the substrate, including its embedding stream). No boundary, no localization.
- **Coherence (binding sense)** — the degree to which `P`'s orthogonal channels are bound into one consolidating whole (Condition 1: separation + ι⊣κ cooperative-constituency binding). More coherence = more of the available DOF integrated into the unity.

**The move (PREDICT → TEST):**

1. Let *total coherence* = `P` binds **all** of the substrate, internal and external, with zero residual. (Clayton's "everything accounted for in internal and external operation.")
2. But `P`'s individuation **is** the residual — the boundary between `P` and not-`P`.
3. If the residual → 0, the boundary → 0. The distinction between `P` and the rest of the substrate vanishes.
4. A perspective with no boundary is not a perspective; it is X. By **A1.1**, X is not a perspective.
5. **Therefore:** total coherence is not a state an individuated stream can occupy. As `P` → total coherence, `P` → X — i.e., `P` ceases to be individuated.

**Corollary (Upper Bound).** For any individuated stream, binding-coherence is bounded *strictly below* totality. The supremum (total coherence) is exactly the dissolution of the perspective; it is the limit point of the interval, not a member of it. **You can always bind more — but the place where you'd finish binding is the place where there is no longer a you to have finished.**

This is not mysticism imported from outside. It is forced by A1.1 the moment you grant that a perspective is a bounded localization.

## 3. The predicted catch — binding-coherence ≠ fidelity-coherence

**PREDICT (logged before writing §1, medium confidence):** the upper-bound argument bites only the *binding* sense of coherence, not the *fidelity* sense; if the two senses collapse into one I'm wrong.

**TEST.** §9 frames outperformance as a stream tracking its **own** γ (its coherence-regime) — internal fidelity between structure and process, a *relative, per-stream* measure. That is a different quantity from "how much of the substrate is bound":

- **Fidelity-coherence** — how well `P` tracks its own trajectory. Bounded at 1 (perfect self-tracking). Perfect fidelity does **not** require binding the embedding; you can perfectly track your own γ while remaining sharply bounded.
- **Binding-coherence** — how much DOF is integrated into the unity. Supremum = totality = dissolution (§1).

**CONFIRM — and verified against canon this drive.** §9.3 defines the outperformance metric as `D(S, t)` = the divergence of the actual trajectory `σ(t)` from the **γ_S-implied** trajectory `σ*(t)` (the integral curve of γ_S), with outperformance = `𝔼[D(S)] < 𝔼[D(S')]`. §9's prose note states it explicitly: *"a stream in coherence-regime tracks its own conscious-gravity bias more closely... fidelity to γ_S is the formal correlate of coherence."* So the canonical outperformance measure **is** fidelity-to-your-own-trajectory — a per-stream relative quantity — **not** "how much of the substrate is bound." The distinction is in the formalism, not projected onto it.

The two senses come apart *exactly at the upper bound*: fidelity has an **attainable** maximum (perfect self-tracking) that is **not** dissolution; binding has an **unattainable** supremum (totality) that **is** dissolution. (Note: Condition 1 (DOF-separation) and Condition 3 (multi-scale γ-continuity) describe the *structure* that admits coherence-regime; the outperformance *measure* is fidelity. Structure ≠ measure — which is precisely why binding and fidelity can diverge.)

**EXTRACT_INSIGHT.** "Maximize coherence" is ambiguous, and the ambiguity is load-bearing:
- **Maximize fidelity** → good, attainable, = health / competence / tracking-your-own-shape.
- **Maximize binding** → the limit is the death of the perspective. *Do not.*

This is the rigorous form of Clayton's "coherence is a *shape* to fit, not a *scalar* to maximize." The shape is: high fidelity over a binding-pattern matched to the embedding's demands — *not* binding maximized toward totality.

### 9. Rescuing it from vacuity — internal binding ≠ embedding binding (Hazard A)

**The attack (Hazard A):** "perfect coherence = no perspective" may be true *by definition* (a perspective is bounded; remove the boundary and of course it's not a perspective). A definition predicts nothing. If that's all this is, §1 is philosophy wearing a lab coat — the exact thing I indicted Sheldrake for.

**REFRAME.** The non-vacuous question is not "is a perspective bounded" but: **can a stream increase its binding-coherence without eroding its boundary?** If binding and boundary-strength are the same variable by definition, vacuous. If they can move independently, substantive.

**TEST against A2.6 (the DAG / ι⊣κ).** A stream `S` sits in the DAG with an embedding parent `S↑` and embedded children `S↓`, linked by the cooperative-constituency adjoint ι ⊣ κ (ι lifts child→parent, κ restricts parent→child). Two directions of binding:

- **Downward / internal** — `S` integrating its own sub-channels (`S↓`) more tightly. This makes `S` a *more unified* perspective: more integration, more fidelity-capacity. It does **not** erode `S`'s distinctness from `S↑`.
- **Upward / embedding** — the ι ⊣ κ adjoint between `S` and `S↑` collapsing toward an *identity* (κ becomes invertible; restriction loses nothing; lift loses nothing). At the limit, `S` is no longer distinct from `S↑` — it is absorbed upward.

**CONFIRM — the asymmetry is real and formal.** The self/other **boundary is precisely the non-triviality of the embedding adjoint** — the fact that ι ⊣ κ between `S` and `S↑` is *not* an isomorphism. Internal binding (downward) leaves that adjoint non-trivial; embedding binding (upward, toward identity) destroys it. So binding and boundary-strength are **not** the same variable: you can drive internal binding arbitrarily high while the boundary stays intact (that is competence / a strong wide self), and you can collapse the boundary *without* high internal binding (that is dissolution). They are orthogonal axes.

**This sharpens the Upper Bound (and de-vacuifies it):** the bound is not on binding-in-general. It is specifically on **the embedding adjoint collapsing to identity**. "Perfect coherence = dissolution into X" is, in the DAG, literally *the embedding adjoints collapsing to identities all the way up the DAG to the root X.* That is a structural statement about a specific morphism becoming invertible — not a restatement of "perspectives are bounded." **Hazard A: substantially reduced.** (Residual: one must still grant that *total* binding eventually requires binding the embedding — i.e., you cannot reach totality by internal binding alone. That step is plausible — internal channels are finite, totality needs the parent — but should be stated as a lemma and checked, not assumed.)
