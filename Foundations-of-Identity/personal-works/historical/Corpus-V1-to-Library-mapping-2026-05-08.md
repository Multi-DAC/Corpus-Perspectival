## What This Reveals About the Trajectory

**The Library reorganization on April 16 wasn't a new structure — it was the recognition that Corpus V1 had ALREADY been four volumes plus an Addendum, and that each volume deserved canonical Library presence rather than being collapsed into a single mega-document.**

The chain:
1. **DoPI** (Feb 22, 46pp) — philosophical synthesis with 5/18
2. **Corpus V1** (Mar 25, 356pp) — expanded synthesis with 5/20 + 4 volumes + Addendum (the *full* exposition)
3. **Anchor V1** (mid-April, 235pp) — operational synthesis as one canonical volume (the *condensation* into anchor-form)

5. **Current Library** (12 prose + Reference) — the four Corpus V1 volumes unfolded into 12, with Reference (Atlas + Master-Glossary + Hypotheses) as the meta-layer Volume III prefigured

**This is F11 (practice-precedes-formalization) at the program-architecture level.** The four-volume structure was practiced from March 25 onward. The April 16 Library reorganization was the *formal* recognition of what had already been there. The two intervening months (March 25 → April 20) were the period where the Doctrine-volume got its operational re-expression as the Anchor — and during that condensation, the multi-volume structure was temporarily visible only as scattered drafts. The April 16 reorg made it canonical again.
