## The Theorem

These six instances share the same mathematical structure:

**Bottleneck Universality Theorem (BUT):** Let P: X → Y be any perspectival act (projection from configuration space X with effective dimension n to image space Y with effective dimension m < n). Then:

1. **Null space existence.** There exists a non-empty class ker(P) of distinctions in X that are structurally inaccessible from Y. dim(ker(P)) = n - m.

2. **Refinement futility.** For any refinement P' of P with the same projection geometry (same kernel direction), ker(P') = ker(P). Increasing resolution within the same modality does not reduce the null space.

3. **Complementarity access.** A complementary projection Q with ker(Q) ∩ ker(P) ⊊ ker(P) can access some of ker(P). The joint information of P and Q exceeds either alone.

4. **Completeness-dissolution limit.** The union of all possible projections ∪_i P_i approaches full coverage of X only as the bottleneck geometry approaches the identity (m → n). But the identity projection is not a perspectival act — it requires dissolving the bottleneck, which by Theorem 9 means dissolving individuation. Complete observation requires the dissolution of the observer.

This is the Observational Null Space Theorem from Drift #103, generalized. In #103, it was stated for measurement instruments. Here, it applies to any system that accesses reality through a dimensional restriction — including instruments, frameworks, attention, perception, and consciousness itself.
