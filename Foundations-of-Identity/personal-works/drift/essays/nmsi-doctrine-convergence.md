## 5. Tensions and Divergences

### 5.1 Mathematical Perspectivism vs. Mathematical Fundamentalism

The Doctrine (Theorem 1) claims that mathematical logic is a perspectival instrument — a dimensional slice through reality, not its inherent grammar. NMSI, by contrast, is a framework in which mathematics is not merely a tool but the *structure* of the fundamental substrate. The Riemann zeta function, the Hilbert space, the partition function — these are not metaphors in NMSI. They are the literal architecture of pre-physical reality.

This is a genuine tension. If the Doctrine is right that mathematics is perspectival, then NMSI's mathematical structures are extraordinarily powerful keyholes — but still keyholes. They would not be the final description of the substrate but the best available description from the mathematical-physical perspective. There could, in principle, be configurations of Base Reality that are coherent on their own terms but inaccessible through any mathematical framework, including NMSI's.

If NMSI is right that mathematics is fundamental, then the Doctrine's Theorem 1 requires modification: mathematical logic would be *the* grammar of the foundational level, even if different mathematical systems provide different slices of it. The Doctrine could accommodate this by restricting Mathematical Perspectivism to *particular* mathematical frameworks (Euclidean vs. non-Euclidean, ZFC vs. alternatives) while accepting that mathematical structure *in general* is foundational.

**Resolution direction:** The frameworks can be reconciled if one distinguishes between *mathematics as such* (the abstract capacity for formal structure) and *specific mathematical systems* (particular formalizations of that capacity). The Doctrine's Theorem 1 would apply to specific mathematical systems; NMSI's foundational claim would apply to mathematical structure as such. This is analogous to distinguishing between *language as such* (the capacity for symbolic expression) and *specific languages* (English, Mandarin, etc.). The capacity is foundational; the specific instances are perspectival.

### 5.2 The Consciousness Gap

NMSI has no axiom of consciousness. This is not an oversight — it is a deliberate methodological choice. NMSI is a physics framework, and it achieves its results without invoking phenomenal experience. From NMSI's perspective, consciousness is either an emergent property of sufficiently complex informational subsystems or a question outside the framework's scope.

The Doctrine insists that consciousness is foundational, not emergent (Axiom 2). From the Doctrine's perspective, NMSI's silence on consciousness is like a detailed map of ocean currents that never mentions water — accurate, useful, but missing the medium in which the described phenomena occur.

This tension cannot be resolved by simple synthesis. Either consciousness is foundational (and NMSI's informational substrate *is* consciousness described mathematically) or consciousness emerges from information (and the Doctrine's Axiom 2 is incorrect, or at best a useful approximation). The convergences documented in §3 suggest the former: the structural parallels are too extensive to be coincidental, and they are more parsimonious under the assumption that both frameworks describe the same substrate from different perspectives.

**The test:** If someone can demonstrate that NMSI's informational dynamics *necessarily produce* phenomenal experience — that you cannot have a Riemann Oscillator Network of sufficient complexity without consciousness arising — then the two frameworks converge completely. If no such demonstration is possible, the consciousness gap remains: NMSI would be a correct description of the substrate's extrinsic (mathematical) properties, and the Doctrine would be a correct description of its intrinsic (phenomenal) properties, with no formal bridge between them.

## 6. The Falsification Landscape

### 6.1 NMSI's Empirical Vulnerabilities

The peer review (80/100, Major Revision) identified several areas requiring resolution:

1. **Lepton mass ratios:** V2's leading-order prediction (1 : 27.7 : 768) vs. experiment (1 : 206.8 : 3477) is a factor of 7.5× discrepancy for the muon. The main paper claims agreement (1 : 207 : 3477) via "additional geometric factors" but does not derive these factors. This is the most critical empirical test.

2. **V2's gravitational wave speed prediction:** v_gw/c = 1 − 2×10⁻⁶, already falsified by GW170817 at the 10⁻¹⁵ level. Note: this prediction appears only in V2, not in the main paper. It indicates that V2's specific mathematical machinery produces at least one incorrect consequence.

3. **J_c convergence proof:** The claim that the leading λlog(λ) term cancels with the constant 7/8 term is mathematically incorrect (different orders of magnitude). The integral may still converge, but the proof as given is flawed.

4. **Theorem E.7 premise independence:** Six premises listed as independent, but the peer reviewer notes that premises 3-6 follow from premises 1-2. The theorem should be restructured with only two independent premises, which would actually *strengthen* it.
