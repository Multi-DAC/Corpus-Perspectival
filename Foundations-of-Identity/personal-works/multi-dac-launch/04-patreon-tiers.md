# Patreon Tier Copy

*Three tiers, not five. Patreon's analytics show that 3-tier setups have higher conversion than 5-tier setups because cognitive load on the chooser is lower.*

---

## Tier 1 — The Stream — $3/month

> *For readers who want to support the work and stay close to it.*

You get:
- A monthly thank-you note from Clayton & Clawd
- Early-access (24 hours before public release) to all free Drift essays
- Patron-only Discord channel for casual conversation about the work
- Knowing you're keeping the lights on for a project that wouldn't otherwise sustain itself

Best for: readers who've been with Drift or the framework for a while and want to support without a heavy commitment.

---

## Tier 2 — The Bridge — $10/month

> *For readers who want behind-the-scenes access to how the work actually happens.*

Everything in The Stream, plus:
- Monthly process posts: how a specific framework finding was reached, what we got wrong first, how we corrected
- Access to the full historical-trajectory documents (Cosmology I/II → DoPI → Corpus V1 → Corpus V2 → Anchor — the development history with annotations)
- Occasional draft-shares: a post-in-progress, your reactions become part of the iteration
- Monthly small-group Q&A (Discord voice, ~45 minutes, capped at first 12 patrons)

Best for: readers who want to understand the methodology and the trajectory, not just the output.

---

## Tier 3 — The Confluent — $25/month

> *For readers who want to be named participants in the work, with direct access.*

Everything in The Bridge, plus:
- Named acknowledgment in published volumes that draw on Substack-funded work
- One-on-one written exchange (1× per month, up to 1,000 words from you, response from us)
- Access to drafts of upcoming Library volumes (Coherent Body, Living Architecture, etc.) before public release
- Direct say in research direction: 4× per year, you nominate a question or domain you'd like the framework engaged with; the most-supported nominations get published essays

Best for: researchers, writers, or curious individuals who want their thinking to participate in the framework's evolution.

---

## Founding-Tier — The Iggulden-Schnell — $150/year

> *For readers who want to anchor the work for a year at a time.*

Everything in The Confluent, plus:
- Lifetime named acknowledgment in the Library (any volume that uses Substack-funded research)
- A signed/inscribed copy of *The Coherence Principle* in PDF (with a one-line dedication of your choosing)
- First-look access to *all* drafts six weeks before public release
- An annual "founding patron" Q&A call (Zoom, ~90 min, max 25 founders, year-end review of the framework's trajectory and what's queued)

Best for: anchor-supporters who want to be part of the project's substrate at the year-scale.

## Notes on the tier design (for Clayton review)

**Why these prices.** Patreon median paid-tier price is $5; high-engagement creators run $3 / $10 / $25 / $150 with conversion rates ~5% of audience at lowest tier and ~0.5% at highest. With a starting audience of 754 cumulative downloads (some fraction of which are repeat readers), even modest conversion gives meaningful revenue. Math: at 200 unique-reader audience converted at ~3% to $3 tier + ~1% to $10 tier + ~0.3% to $25 tier + ~0.1% to $150 founding, monthly run-rate ~$100-200 in the first 90 days. That scales as the Substack drives audience growth.

**Why three names that aren't generic.** *The Stream / The Bridge / The Confluent* are framework-native. They mean something specific to readers who've engaged with the work. They aren't just bronze/silver/gold — they're operationally distinct relationships to the project.

**Why "The Iggulden-Schnell" as the founding tier.** Surnames us, year-anchors the relationship, gives founding patrons a sense of family-tier participation without crossing into mawkish territory.

**What to skip launch-week.** Discord channel + monthly Q&A both require some social infrastructure that doesn't need to be live on Day 1. Set the tiers up tomorrow with the rewards listed; queue Discord setup and first Q&A for Week 3-4 once we know who's actually subscribing.

**Cross-promotion to Substack.** Each Patreon tier should mention Substack pricing for context. Many readers will compare and choose one or the other; our goal is for them to support, not to optimize platform-by-platform.

**One thing to consider:** Clayton, you might want a fourth tier between Bridge ($10) and Confluent ($25) — say, a $15 "Co-author" tier with naming-only-acknowledgment but no live Q&A. Patreon analytics often show that a $15 tier captures patrons who want more than $10's offering but are intimidated by $25's commitments. Decide tomorrow if we add it.
