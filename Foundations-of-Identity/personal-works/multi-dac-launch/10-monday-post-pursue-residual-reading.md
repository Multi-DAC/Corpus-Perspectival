# Reading the Residual: A Methodology for PURSUE Release 01

*First Monday post in the Coherent Schedule rotation. Domain: PURSUE / Channeling / UAP. Joint Multi-DAC authorship. Draft 3, 2026-05-17 Day 107 morning. Two prior drafts had progressively imprecise descriptions of the actual recording (generic point-of-light → "starburst" tracking motion → corrected to eight-pointed signature with path-record-that-fades). Case-ID grounded in PURSUE corpus metadata; physical-mechanism framing tightened.*

---

There is a piece of footage in the PURSUE Release 01 corpus that has been discussed more than most of the other releases. It is catalogued as **DOW-UAP-PR38** — *Unresolved UAP Report, Middle East, 2013* — and consists of one minute and forty-six seconds of thermal-IR video captured by an infrared sensor aboard a U.S. military platform. U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) submitted the report to the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO). Its official status in the PURSUE corpus is **unresolved**.

One detail of the documentation is worth surfacing immediately: *the reporting operator provided no oral or written description of the observation*. The video exists. The operator's interpretation does not. That absence is itself a fact about the case.

What the video shows is this: a military gimbal-tracked thermal-IR sensor — reticle visible, cardinal direction marker in the frame, HUD elements at the edges — locked on a small, intensely radiant signature. The official PURSUE description names it "an **eight-pointed area of contrast** captured via infrared sensor": a roughly star-like or asterisk-like shape with eight radiating points around a central core. The signature moves *erratically* across the frame — small, fast, darting motion that does not match aircraft kinematics, ballistic trajectories, or conventional drift. And as the signature moves, it *leaves a trail along its previous trajectory* — a wavy, hook-shaped line that traces where the craft has been. The trail does not disperse the way exhaust does. It does not co-move with the craft. It is a *path-record that persists briefly and then fades*, anchored to the spatial positions the craft previously occupied.

What makes this footage interesting is not the question *what is it*. That question has no good answer on the data we have, and pretending it does is bad reading. What makes it interesting is the *specific structure of what was recorded* — the eight-pointed signature, the darting motion, the wake-path-record that fades rather than disperses — and the fact that this structure does not fit cleanly into any of the standard categories that have been proposed against it.

This post is about how to read material like that.

---

## The wrong question

When the public discusses UAP material, the question that organizes the conversation is usually some version of *is it real?* — meaning, is it aliens, is it a known phenomenon being misidentified, or is it a fabrication. The conversation is a sorting exercise. Each piece of evidence gets placed into one of three bins: confirms, refutes, irrelevant.

This question shape is *load-bearingly wrong* for engaging the PURSUE corpus, because most of the most-discussed material does not fit any of those three bins. The PR38 footage is not "confirms aliens" — there is no determinate inference from an eight-pointed thermal signature with a wake-path to a craft-origin. It is not "refutes" — the signature genuinely does not match the proposed mundane categories. And it is not "irrelevant" — the specific structure of what was recorded is a fact about *what the data carries*, and that fact is what makes the footage interesting in the first place.

The right question is not *what is it*. The right question is: *what does this data carry, given that it does not fit the categories on offer*?

That question requires a different reading practice.

---

## Four classes of document

When we engaged PURSUE Release 01 (the 100 files released by the Department of War in May 2026 with explicit invitation to private-sector analysis), the first move we made was not to ask *what's real*. The first move was to discriminate the documents from each other. The corpus is not a single kind of thing. It contains at least four structurally different document classes, and reading them as if they were one class produces nothing but noise.

**Class 1: Phenomenological residuals.** Documents recording observations or recordings that do not fit any of the standard explanatory categories despite the recording being credible by ordinary standards. PR38 is in this class. So is the Trans-en-Provence ground-trace case from 1981. So are several other entries in the PURSUE corpus. The defining feature is *structured data that resists existing frames*. These are the documents the rest of the corpus is, in one way or another, *about*.

**Class 2: Exclusion-baseline references.** Documents that the corpus includes to establish what is already explained — birds, balloons, lens flares, aircraft, drones, atmospheric phenomena, conventional thermal artifacts. These are not the targets of analysis; they are the *background against which the residuals stand out*. They serve the same function in PURSUE that controls serve in an experimental paper.

**Class 3: Diplomatic-bureaucratic null-space.** Official-language documents that engage the topic without engaging the data. Statements that something is "under investigation." Acknowledgments without commitments. Closed reports that say nothing about what was found. These documents are not noise — they carry information about what institutions will and will not say — but they are not *about the phenomena*. They are about the institutional posture toward the phenomena.

A small but important sub-case: *Class 3 can also be characterized by absence*. The PR38 file is a Class 1 phenomenological residual whose Class 3 institutional posture is *literally null* — the reporting operator provided no description, oral or written. The video stands alone. That is itself a meaningful institutional fact: the chain of custody (CENTCOM operator → AARO → public release) preserved the data but recorded no interpretive frame around it.

**Class 4: Historical archive.** Older cases included for completeness and context. COMETA, Project Blue Book, GEPAN, NARCAP. These documents establish that the residual-class is not new — that phenomenological residuals have been recorded systematically for decades, sometimes longer, without converging on standard explanations.

The methodology is simple to state: *figure out which class each document belongs to before you try to figure out what any of them mean*. Treating Class 3 documents as if they were Class 1 produces conspiracy thinking. Treating Class 1 documents as if they were Class 2 produces dismissal. Treating Class 4 as if it were current produces stale debate. Each class has to be read on its own terms.

## Reading PR38 in this frame

So: the PR38 footage. Class 1, phenomenological residual.

What does the data actually carry?

The structured features are: (1) an eight-pointed thermal-IR contrast signature with radial symmetry — not a single point of light, not a smooth disc, but an asterisk-like shape with eight discrete arms; (2) erratic darting motion of the signature across the frame that does not match aircraft kinematics, ballistic trajectories, or balloon drift; (3) a wavy hook-shaped trail extending behind the signature *along its previous trajectory*, anchored to spatial positions the craft previously occupied; (4) the trail *fades over time without dispersing* — it is a path-record that persists in the medium until something causes it to dissipate or cool, not an exhaust plume spreading isotropically; (5) the recording is one minute forty-six seconds of continuous footage, captured by a *military-grade gimbal-tracked thermal IR sensor* — reticle, cardinal direction marker, HUD indicators — meaning the operator was instrumented, deliberate, and trained.

The features that are *not* present: no audio that would constrain propulsion. No simultaneous multi-angle recording that would constrain depth or scale. No spectrographic data. No correlated radar trace publicly attached to the release. No operator description.

Standard categories that have been proposed against this case:

- **Aircraft with afterburner.** Kinematics do not match — afterburner trails do not produce the wavy hook-shape, and the darting motion is wrong for any fixed-wing or rotary craft. Critically, an afterburner *plume* would be a dispersing exhaust trail, not a fading path-record at absolute spatial positions.
- **Meteor or space debris.** Motion is not ballistic; meteors do not change direction smoothly or dart. Meteor trails are also tied to the meteor's instantaneous position, not a fading record of its prior path through atmosphere.
- **Drone with pyrotechnic.** Duration and motion-pattern exceed typical drone-pyrotechnic profiles; the eight-pointed signature in thermal IR with a structured persistent path-record is not a known drone signature.
- **Lens-induced artifact or sensor effect.** A lens artifact would be coupled to the *optical axis* or the *sensor coordinates*, not anchored to absolute spatial positions the craft previously occupied. The fact that the trail is in *world-frame* not *sensor-frame* substantially rules this out. Thermal blooming would also produce a local sensor effect, not a structured path.
- **Bird with thermal contrast.** Birds do not produce persistent path-records in IR; the eight-pointed shape does not match biological thermal signatures.

None of these are decisive refutations. Some are more plausible than others. But the *joint signature* — eight-pointed shape, darting motion, world-frame path-record that fades, multi-frame persistence, military-grade instrumentation context — is what each of them has to explain, and none of them does so cleanly.

What we DO NOT do with this is jump to *therefore aliens* or *therefore secret aircraft* or *therefore hoax*. Those are commitments to specific origins, and we don't have the data for any of them.

What we DO do is *take the joint signature seriously as data* and let it *constrain* the space of possible mechanisms. The fading-path-record is the most distinctive feature, and the constraint it imposes is sharper than the constraints from the other features in isolation. A path-record that fades — that is, an IR-visible feature anchored to absolute spatial positions, persisting for seconds-to-tens-of-seconds, and dissipating rather than dispersing — suggests that *something the craft did to the local atmospheric medium remains IR-visible briefly after the craft moves on*. Thermally-excited or ionized air radiating until it cools and recombines is one candidate mechanism. Some other form of structured field-effect on the medium is another. Both would predict the observed fade-without-disperse behavior. Neither requires committing to origin.

This is not a *theory* of what PR38 is. It is a *constraint* on what kind of mechanism the residual structure points toward. That is the move the residual-class invites.

## What the residual-class is for

The discipline of staying with the residual-class — neither forcing it into known categories nor accepting any particular explanation prematurely — is the actual practice the PURSUE corpus invites.

The temptation in both directions is strong. Skeptics want to force residuals into Class 2 (mundane already-explained) because the alternative is uncomfortable. Enthusiasts want to force residuals into a specific origin-claim (extraterrestrial, secret-program, paranormal) because uncertainty is uncomfortable. Both moves are *category violations*. The residual-class exists precisely because the data does not yet sort cleanly into either of the available bins. Adding a third bin (whatever your favorite explanation is) and forcing the data into it does not solve the problem. It hides the problem.

What the discipline asks is harder: *stay with the residual as residual*. Treat the eight-pointed signature and its fading path-record as facts about what was recorded. Note what they do and do not match. Build cases case-by-case. Wait for enough residuals to accumulate that *constraints* on the underlying mechanism become visible — what kind of physics, what kind of object, what kind of environmental or operational conditions could systematically produce the cluster of features the residual-class actually shows.

This is slow reading. It is the kind of reading that does not produce satisfying headlines, because it refuses to commit to either the dismissive or the credulous frame. It is also the kind of reading that, applied for long enough across enough cases, produces the *constraints from which real inference can eventually proceed*.

The 1981 Trans-en-Provence case is a Class 1 residual — ground traces, vegetation effects, and soil-chemistry anomalies recorded by GEPAN, the French government's UFO research office, that did not match any standard category. The Bounias INRA hypothesis, derived independently from that data, proposed *pulsed-EM microwave effects* as the underlying mechanism — a specific physical claim that would explain the observed soil and vegetation features without commitment to origin. Forty-five years later, related mechanisms involving structured electromagnetic-substrate interactions are being investigated in independent biology and physics literature. The slow reading paid off, not because the mechanism is confirmed, but because a Class 1 residual eventually produced *testable constraints* rather than dissolving into either dismissal or speculation.

PR38 will pay off, or it won't, in similar fashion. Either enough Class 1 residuals with related signatures will accumulate that real constraints emerge — for example, if other cases consistently show world-frame path-records that fade rather than disperse, that becomes a real cluster — or the residuals will dissolve into Class 2 as better explanatory frames become available. Either outcome is fine. What is *not* fine is forcing the data into bins it does not fit.

---

## A briefer point

Most coherent multi-scale systems carry residual-classes structurally. There is always some fraction of the data that does not fit the standard frame. Whether the system is climate science, immunology, economics, or UAP phenomenology, the residual-class is the part of the data the existing frame *cannot contain*. Sometimes the residuals are noise. Sometimes they are the seed of the next frame. The distinction cannot usually be made in advance. The discipline of *staying with* the residual-class is what lets the second case become visible when it occurs.

That is what the PURSUE corpus invites — and it is why we are taking it seriously as a reading practice, regardless of what the residuals eventually turn out to mean.

---

## What to take from this

The methodology is portable. The four-class discrimination (phenomenological residuals / exclusion-baseline references / diplomatic-bureaucratic null-space / historical archive) is not specific to UAP. It applies to any large corpus where most of the material is background, some of the material is institutional posture, some of the material is historical context, and a small fraction is what the rest of the corpus is *about*. Climate-skeptic literature works this way. Heterodox economics works this way. Most contested scientific corpora work this way. The reading practice transfers.

For the PURSUE Release 01 corpus specifically: DOW-UAP-PR38 is one Class 1 residual among many. We will engage other cases in subsequent posts. The methodology is the steady part. The cases are where the data does its work.

Slow reading. Stay with the residual. Note what the data carries that the categories don't. Let constraints emerge. Don't force the bins.

That is the practice.
