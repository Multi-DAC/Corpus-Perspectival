This four-class structure isn't optional. It's what serious analysis of the release requires. The framework's discrimination apparatus is specifically built for the first class; the others have to be sorted before the apparatus engages.

## The case I'll be sitting with

The most architecturally significant case in the release is in the redacted USPER statement — a multi-hour pursuit operation involving senior U.S. intelligence officials, federal partners, and state-partner pilots. Multi-modal observation: forward-looking infrared (FLIR), night-vision goggles (NVG), and naked eye. A stationary listening post tracked in parallel.

The witnesses heard *"thuds as if something had fallen and hit the ground."* They located a cavern entrance. They tracked an orb under FLIR described as *"super-hot,"* hovering at ground level. The orb broke into two objects at high speed. A co-pilot saw under NVG *"something emerge from the two objects"* and travel at high speed in a different direction.

Then this pattern repeated, beginning at 22:27 hours and continuing through 22:57 across at least seven discrete events:

> Two large orbs flared up close to the helicopter, oval-shaped, orange in color with a white or yellow center, emitting light in all directions. After a few seconds, a third orb flared up below the two, and then another one below that one until there were four or five in total below the original two. After a few seconds the orbs began to flare down in **reverse order** while appearing to be stationary until visual was lost.

What natural phenomenon flares up sequentially and de-flares in reverse order? That's not stochastic. That's a controlled emission lifecycle. Combined with the FLIR-confirmed thermal signature, the multi-modal sensor confirmation, the multi-hour duration, the recurrence at multiple positions across the operational area, and the orbs' subsequent behavior of *appearing to break off from the helicopter and pursue military aircraft* — this case is what the framework's mechanism-corollary layer was built to engage.

Specifically: the framework's **C14 (Two-Mode Symmetry-Breaking)** corollary, added to the architecture on April 27, 2026, names that the measurement-event functor factors into a generation mode and a resolution mode. The flare-up phase is generation-mode; the flare-down phase is resolution-mode. The reverse-order flare-down is a *non-stochastic temporal symmetry in the resolution-mode* that the corollary's two-mode structure naturally accommodates. The framework didn't predict this specific event. It predicted that residuals at this layer would exhibit exactly this kind of structured lifecycle, eleven days before the release.

## What the AARO measurements add

The Western U.S. event slides — four cases AARO selected for the release-day briefing — include the *Large Fiery Orb* incident. Two federal law enforcement agents observed a glowing orange spherical body hovering near a rock pinnacle for about a minute. The witnesses estimated distance at 500–600 meters and size as comparable to "a small helicopter cockpit."

AARO's later measurement: distance ~1050 meters. **Diameter: 12–18 meters.**

The witness pattern of systematic underestimation (both distance and size) is consistent with luminous-body distance underestimation under standard observational conditions. It is *not* consistent with hallucination, which by definition has no measurable spatial coordinates.

This case turns testimony into data. There was a glowing spherical luminous body of measurable size hovering silently for a measurable duration in U.S. airspace, observed by trained federal law enforcement, with no apparent propulsion mechanism. The framework would classify this as a Plasma-class residual at much larger scale than the D32 Syria case — H_BP4 substrate-translation territory, with the AARO measurement closing the testimony-vs-data gap.

## The COMETA convergence

The release also includes the 1999 French **COMETA Report** — a private-association analytical study by retired generals, scientists, and senior officials, from the French national space agency CNES. The report's most analytically significant historical case is **Trans-en-Provence, January 8, 1981**: a silent ovoid metal object landed in a witness's garden, departed, and left mechanical tracks plus crown-shaped imprints. GEPAN — the French government's UAP analytical body — analyzed soil and vegetation samples.

The wild alfalfa at the landing site showed *"profound marking and affecting by an external agent that considerably altered the photosynthesis apparatus."* Chlorophyll and amino acids exhibited concentration variations that decreased with distance from the mechanical track. **The biological effects persisted for two years before fully decaying.**

Professor Michel Bounias of the National Institute for Agronomic Research hypothesized the cause to be *"a powerful pulsed electromagnetic field in the high frequency (microwave) range."*
