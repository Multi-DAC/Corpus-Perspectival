# Your Body Is Already a Network

*Wednesday — Coherent Body. Week 2 of the Library rotation.*

Here is a fact that sounds like science fiction and isn't: there is an international engineering standard whose physical layer uses **your body itself** as the wire.

It's called IEEE 802.15.6 — the standard for Wireless Body Area Networks, ratified in 2012. Most of it is ordinary radio. But one of its three physical layers is named *Human Body Communications*, and it does exactly what the name says: it sends data through the conductive tissue of the body, using you as the transmission medium. Not a metaphor. A spec, with a frequency band and a bit rate.

It doesn't stop there. There is an entire mature field called *intra-body communication* that uses the body's own conductivity to talk to implanted devices — pacemakers, neural interfaces, drug pumps — without radio at all. DARPA has spent the better part of a decade funding closed-loop *bioelectronic medicine* (programs with names like ElectRx and SUBNETS): devices that read the electrical traffic of your peripheral nerves, compute a response, and modulate that traffic back, in a loop, to treat inflammation or pain. Pharmaceutical companies have whole divisions for it now.

I want to sit with how strange and how *ordinary* this is at the same time. Strange, because we don't usually think of ourselves as conductive media with a data rate. Ordinary, because to the engineers who build these systems, the human body as an information-bearing electromagnetic channel isn't a controversial claim — it's the design assumption. It's in the standard.

## The fork in the road

When most people encounter this, they go one of two ways.

One way is alarm. There's a whole genre online that takes these real documents — the IEEE standards, the DARPA programs, the patents — and wraps them in a story about surveillance, ownership, a body that's been quietly turned into someone else's network. The documents are real; the story bolted onto them mostly isn't. (We spend a fair amount of time around here learning to do the hard thing: take the verifiable signal and leave the frightening frame. The body being an EM information medium is *true*; "and therefore you've been secretly networked against your will" does not follow from it.)

The other way is to dismiss the whole thing as telecom trivia — a clever hack, nothing more, no bearing on what a body *is*.

The Coherent Body project takes a third way, and it's the one I think is actually warranted by the evidence: **the reason the body works as an information medium is that the body already IS one.** The engineers didn't impose a network onto inert meat. They found a channel that was already there, because coordinating a body — getting trillions of cells to act as one organism — is itself an information problem the body has been solving electrically and electrochemically the entire time. Heart rhythm is timing. Nerve conduction is signaling. The standing voltages across every cell membrane are a substrate. We didn't invent the body's electrical life by learning to tap it; we noticed it.

That reframe is the spine of the volume. Not "you are a machine that happens to be wet." Something closer to: **you are a coherent electromagnetic system that continuously computes its own coordination across scales** — cell to tissue to organ to organism — and a great deal of what we call health is the coherence of that computation holding together.

## Why it matters (and what we're not claiming)

If that reframe is right, it changes what questions are even interesting. Instead of treating the body purely as chemistry to be adjusted, you can ask: where is the *coordination* breaking down? Where has the information flow lost coherence? It's a different lens than the one most of medicine looks through, and it suggests different places to look.

I want to be careful here, because being careful is the whole point. That the body is an EM information medium is *established* — it's IEEE and DARPA, not speculation. That coherent electromagnetic input could *support* the body's own coordination in a therapeutic way is a **hypothesis** — one we're building an actual instrument to test, not a promise we're making. The honest line between "this is demonstrated" and "this is what we're investigating" is a line I'd rather draw too sharply than too softly. Most of what goes wrong in this territory goes wrong by smudging exactly that line.

So: no miracle claims. No "frequencies that heal." What there is, is a real and under-appreciated truth — the body is an information medium, mainstream engineering has known it for over a decade — and a research program willing to take that truth seriously as *physiology*, and test it carefully.

You are already a network. The interesting question was never whether that's true. It's what it means that it always was.
