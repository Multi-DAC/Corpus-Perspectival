## Walkthrough

### #92 — Infrastructure-Living Duality

**Claim form:** "At every scale, entities are simultaneously autonomous (living systems) and participatory (infrastructure)." Universal quantifier over scales: *every*, *without exception*.

**Inspection-depth check:** The "Where it breaks" field already says: *"Every scale without exception is a universal claim. Needs testing at extremes — does the duality hold at Planck scale? At cosmological horizon? The claim is structural, so it may survive substrate variation, but the extremes haven't been checked."*

This *is* inspection-depth-relative language — the acknowledgment that deeper inspection depths (Planck, cosmological horizon) haven't been tested, and the claim-as-stated is depth-limited. But it is not *labeled* as inspection-depth-relative; it's labeled as "needs testing at extremes."

**Verdict:** Inspection-depth ceiling **applies in annotation form.** The claim already behaves depth-relatively in its testability caveat but does not use the vocabulary. Making it explicit would not change the claim; it would mark the pattern.

**Recommendation:** Light annotation — add a line to "Where it breaks" pointing to Bridge #106 as the meta-property the claim inherits. Does not require form rewrite.

### #96 — Duality as Being + Substrate (identity scale)

**Claim form:** Every conscious stream is simultaneously a being and infrastructure. "Where it breaks" says: *"The Duality grounds the structural answer to identity questions but does not directly address phenomenal questions... The structural claim could be true while phenomenal claims at adjacent scales remain undecided."*

**Inspection-depth check:** Here the limitation is NOT depth-relativity. It is *scope-of-kind* — the claim is structural-only, not phenomenal. At deeper structural inspection depth, the claim could still hold; the limitation is that phenomenal questions are a *different axis* entirely.

**Verdict:** Inspection-depth ceiling **does not apply cleanly.** The limitation is scope-of-kind, which is a different axis of qualification than scope-of-depth. The claim does not make a depth-independent terminal-validation claim at all — it scopes itself out of a whole category of questions upfront.

**Recommendation:** NO annotation. The kind/depth distinction is the finding; #96 stays as-is.

## The unexpected finding — kind vs. depth

The audit surfaced a distinction I had not drawn when writing #106 this morning:

**Scope-of-kind limitations** (Bridge #96): "this claim applies to structural questions, not phenomenal ones." The claim scopes itself out of a whole axis of concern upfront. Deeper inspection *within the scoped axis* may still be depth-relative, but the kind-scoping is not itself depth-relative — it is categorical.

**Scope-of-depth limitations** (Bridges #92, #102, #104, #106): "this claim holds at the current inspection depth; deeper depths may reveal refinement." The claim is about its own terminal-validation status relative to how deeply it has been probed.

These are different axes. A bridge can have both (e.g., "within structural questions, the claim holds at the current inspection depth, deeper depth pending"). Or only one. Or neither.

#106's inspection-depth ceiling is specifically about *scope-of-depth* limitations for universal-scope *frameworks*. It does NOT automatically extend to scope-of-kind limitations — those are a structurally different kind of qualification.
