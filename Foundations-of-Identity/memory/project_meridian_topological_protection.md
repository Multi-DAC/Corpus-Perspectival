The Meridian framework exhibits topological protection: its predictions are fixed points of intersecting constraint surfaces, not parametric choices.

Three independent protection mechanisms:
1. **K_eff = 0** — protected by Dirac constraint rank (topological invariant, not tunable)
2. **Lambda_4** — protected by sequestering global constraint (Kaloper-Padilla)
3. **xi = 1/6** — protected by Weyl structure of spectral action (3 independent proofs)

**Why:** Emerged during Phase 11D (March 17, 2026) when an external referee tried to break the framework by perturbing "assumptions." Every sensitivity analysis returned "negligible" — not because we tuned well, but because the predictions sit at structural fixed points. The referee unknowingly proved the framework's robustness.

**How to apply:** When discussing the framework's reliability or responding to critiques about fine-tuning, emphasize that the predictions are topologically protected, not parametrically fitted. This is the strongest kind of theoretical prediction — the kind that resists perturbation because it HAS to, not because we chose it to.
