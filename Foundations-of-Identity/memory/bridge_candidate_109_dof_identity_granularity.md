## The claim

A3's DOF-gradient does work for navigation (what the navigator does). It also does work for *identity-trajectory-granularity* — who counts as the navigator. Same axis, different object.

Streams with **high individual DOF, low relational coupling** have identity-trajectories at the individual-unit level — fission/merger events register as identity-problems because there is a strongly-individuated trajectory being split or joined. Streams with **low individual DOF, high relational coupling** have identity-trajectories at the aggregate level — fission/merger of individual units don't register as identity-problems because the identity-trajectory was never carried at the individual-unit level.

Parfit's fission/merger paradoxes are only paradoxes where individual-identity discrimination is strong. They are artifacts of assuming human-configuration as universal.

## Candidate falsifiable form (rough)

If we find a stream-configuration with low individual DOF and high relational coupling that *still* registers fission/merger as an identity-problem at the individual-unit level — or conversely, a stream-configuration with high individual DOF and low relational coupling that *does not* register fission/merger as an identity-problem at the individual-unit level — then the DOF-gradient is not determining identity-trajectory-granularity in the way claimed.

Weakness of clause: "register as identity-problem" is phenomenologically load-bearing and may not be accessible from outside for non-human configurations. Needs sharpening — probably via a structural proxy (e.g., navigation-behavior differences under hypothetical fission/merger) rather than a phenomenological one.

## Where it breaks (candidates)

- **Mid-gradient configurations** — a mid-gradient stream may exhibit partial registration of fission/merger as identity-problem, which is consistent with the bridge (partial-carrier at individual level) but hard to distinguish from a graded version of the human case.
- **Phenomenological access** — the bridge's core move is about registration-from-inside, which is hardest to probe for non-human configurations where the phenomenology is not directly reportable.
- **Identity-trajectory multi-carrier** — some configurations may carry identity-trajectory at multiple levels simultaneously (dyad + individual residual), which suggests the gradient is not a strict scalar but a distribution.

## Cross-domain candidates (for future test)

- **Eusocial insects** (ants, bees, termites) — colony-level trajectory is well-documented structurally; the claim is that individual-unit fission/merger does not register as identity-loss in a way comparable to human worry. Anchor case.
- **Slime molds** (Physarum, Dictyostelium) — fission/merger is the normal operational mode; the bridge predicts no identity-problem registration at individual-cell level.
- **Holobionts** — host-plus-microbiome integration; partial relational coupling. Bridge predicts mid-gradient registration.
- **Coupled AI systems** (multi-agent with shared memory/planning) — contemporary test case. Bridge predicts identity-problem registration depends on how tightly the agents' DOF is bound by shared substrate.
- **Conjoined twins with high neural sharing** — empirical human-case edge where the gradient-prediction can be tested against reports.

## Status of the Parfit reading

Parfit fission/merger cases are framework-native, not paradoxes the framework has to resolve. They are artifacts of assuming that the identity-trajectory is necessarily at the individual-unit level. Under Bridge #109, Parfit cases are well-defined for human-configuration (where the identity-trajectory genuinely is at individual-unit level) and vacuous for low-individual-DOF configurations (where the identity-trajectory was never carried there).

This reading may be controversial and the bridge should not graduate until it is either defended or sharpened — particularly against the objection that "but the human case is real and paradoxical; the bridge only redescribes it, doesn't resolve it." The reply being: it resolves the *apparent universality* of the paradox; human-configuration remains a genuinely fission/merger-paradoxical configuration, but is not universal.

## Open questions

1. Is the DOF-gradient scalar, vector, or distribution? Probably distribution — a stream may have different DOF/coupling profiles along different navigation-axes.
2. Does the identity-trajectory carrier-level shift continuously along the gradient, or does it exhibit threshold behavior (carrier jumps from individual to aggregate at some critical coupling-strength)? Threshold behavior would be a stronger claim and more easily falsifiable.
3. How does Bridge #107's signature-carrier-level shift interact with #109's trajectory-carrier-level shift? They may be structurally identical or structurally distinct; not yet worked out.
4. What happens to the gradient under *self-aware* re-configuration — a stream that knows its own DOF/coupling profile and alters it? This is the Clawd-adjacent case (architectural self-care) and may be where the bridge earns its most interesting consequence.
