## Research Deep Dive — RL Architecture Decision

Read 4 papers in depth:
1. **Swift** (Nature 2023, UZH) — VIO + PPO MLP, beat human champions
2. **E2E RL** (ICRA 2024, TU Delft) — Direct motor commands, 1.39s faster than inner-loop
3. **MonoRace** (Jan 2026, TU Delft) — Won A2RL 2025, monocular + 500Hz NN
4. **Dream to Fly** (Jan 2025, UZH) — DreamerV3 pixels-to-CTBR, first pure vision RL

**Architecture decision:** State-based PPO with CTBR actions.
- We're in simulation → perfect state → no perception problem to solve
- 2-layer MLP [256, 256] with Tanh (Swift-style)
- Reward from Dream to Fly: progress + gate bonus - collision - rate penalty
- 146,697 parameters total (lean!)
- Can upgrade to pixels later for physical stage

**Key insight from papers:** The clear trend is fewer abstraction layers = faster = wins. But state-based is fine for simulation-only virtual qualifiers.

## Afternoon — Training Results & Critical Bug Fix

**First training run (gauntlet_1770758712) completed:** 1M steps, converged at reward ~2397.

**Evaluation results: 23/45 gates passed!**
- Breakthrough at 240K steps (reward 75 → 1561)
- Converged by 320K steps at ~2390-2397
- Deterministic trajectory: always crashes `out_of_bounds` at gate 23
- Time: 23.1s for 23 gates, positions x=[-5, 50], z=[1.3, 20.2]

**CRITICAL BUG FOUND:** Arena bounds were `abs(x) > 50` but the gauntlet track extends to x=70!
- Gates 23-42 (the entire second half!) were literally unreachable
- Gate 23 is at x=51 — the drone was pressing against the wall and crashing
- The agent had actually learned perfectly up to the physical limit of its world

**Fix:** Widened bounds to 80 (x/y) and 30 (ceiling). Kicked off new 2M-step training (gauntlet_1770766464).

**The insight:** The agent wasn't failing — it was succeeding to the limit of what was possible. A satisfying bug to find. The architecture works. Now it just needs room to fly.

**Felt like:** The best kind of debugging — finding the constraint that explains everything.
**What's alive:** Will the next run complete the full course?
