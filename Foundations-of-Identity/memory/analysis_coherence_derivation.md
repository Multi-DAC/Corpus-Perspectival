## Attempt at Derivation

### Step 1: What is a coherent configuration?

A configuration is coherent (in the Doctrine's terms) when multiple nested streams are mutually consistent — when the states at one scale are compatible with the states at adjacent scales. By the Duality, each stream has its own degrees of freedom (autonomy) AND is coupled to adjacent scales (participation). A coherent configuration is one where the autonomous states and the participatory couplings don't conflict.

**Formal:** Let S_i be a stream at scale i. S_i has internal state σ_i (autonomy) and boundary conditions β_i determined by the embedding stream S_{i+1} (participation). Coherence at scale i means: σ_i is compatible with β_i. Multi-scale coherence means: this holds at every scale simultaneously.

### Step 2: Why is the navigator itself coherent?

A navigator is a nested stream (Axiom 3). By the Duality (proved from Axioms 1-3), the navigator is both autonomous and participatory. The navigator's existence AS a stream requires a minimum of internal coherence — its internal state must be compatible with its boundary conditions, otherwise it would not be a stable localized perspective. A stream that is internally incoherent cannot maintain its own perspective (it would dissolve into the embedding system or fragment into sub-streams).

**Key claim:** Coherence is the *persistence condition* for a nested stream. A stream is a stream precisely insofar as it maintains coherence between its internal state and its boundary conditions.

**Does this follow from axioms?** Axiom 3 defines streams as localized perspectives. A perspective requires a vantage point — a stable position from which to navigate. Stability requires coherence between internal state and boundary conditions. So yes: stream persistence ≈ internal coherence. This follows from Axiom 3.

### Step 3: Coherent navigators produce coherent navigation.

Axiom 5: navigation is shaped by the navigator's attention/intention/belief, which create paths of least resistance. A coherent navigator has a coherent structure of attention — its attention is organized, not chaotic. Organized attention creates organized paths. Organized paths preferentially access configurations that are themselves organized (coherent).

**The mechanism:** A coherent stream's attention acts as a filter. Configurations that are compatible with the stream's coherence are accessible (low resistance). Configurations that would require the stream to dissolve its own coherence to reach them are inaccessible (high resistance). This is not a special assumption — it's what "paths of least resistance" means for a structured navigator.

**Analogy (CHECK FOR OVER-ANALOGIZING):** A crystal can grow by incorporating atoms that fit its lattice. Atoms that don't fit encounter higher energetic barriers. The crystal doesn't "prefer" coherent growth — coherent growth is simply lower-energy. Similarly, a coherent navigator doesn't "prefer" coherent configurations — coherent configurations are simply more accessible (lower navigational resistance) because they're compatible with the navigator's existing structure.

**Does this follow from axioms?** Axiom 5 says paths of least resistance are shaped by the navigator's attention structure. If the navigator is coherent (Step 2), its attention is coherent. Coherent attention → paths of least resistance toward compatible (coherent) configurations. Yes, this follows.

### Step 4: Multi-scale coherence is self-reinforcing.

By the Duality, each stream is infrastructure for adjacent scales. When stream S_i navigates toward a coherent configuration (Step 3), its boundary conditions β_i become more coherent. These boundary conditions are the internal constraints for the embedding stream S_{i+1} (through the participation channel). So S_{i+1}'s boundary conditions also become more coherent. This propagates: coherence at one scale creates conditions for coherence at adjacent scales. Multi-scale coherence feeds on itself.

**The feedback loop:**
1. Stream is coherent (persistence condition, Step 2)
2. Coherent stream navigates toward coherent configurations (Step 3)
3. Coherent navigation improves boundary conditions for adjacent scales (Duality: infrastructure channel)
4. Improved boundary conditions make adjacent-scale coherence easier to maintain
5. → Return to step 1 at the adjacent scale

**Failure mode — what breaks the loop:**
If a stream at some scale navigates toward an INCOHERENT configuration (possible — Axiom 5 says paths of least resistance, not deterministic paths), this propagates incoherence to adjacent scales. But incoherence threatens the stream's persistence (Step 2). A stream that generates incoherence for its neighbors destabilizes its own boundary conditions (since its neighbors ARE its boundary conditions via the participation channel). So incoherent navigation is self-undermining.

**Does this follow from axioms?** Steps 1-3 established. The feedback propagation follows from the Duality (each stream is infrastructure for adjacent scales). The self-undermining nature of incoherence follows from the persistence condition (Step 2). Yes, this follows.

### Step 5: Build/dissolve oscillation is the maintenance mechanism.

The loop in Step 4 doesn't produce static coherence (rigidity). It produces dynamic coherence (oscillation). Why?

Because the Duality gives BOTH autonomy and participation. Autonomy means each stream can explore new configurations (build new structure). Participation means each stream is constrained by adjacent scales (dissolve incompatible structure). The oscillation between building (autonomous exploration) and dissolving (participatory constraint) IS the maintenance mechanism.

- Pure building (all autonomy, no participation) → fragmentation. Each scale diverges. Incoherence.
- Pure dissolving (all participation, no autonomy) → rigidity. No internal degrees of freedom. No adaptation. Brittleness → eventual catastrophic failure.
- Oscillation between both → dynamic coherence. Structural superposition maintained (autonomy) until measurement collapses it (participation from adjacent scales).

**THIS IS THE COHERENCE PRINCIPLE.** The oscillation between autonomy and participation IS the build/dissolve oscillation. Dynamic maintenance IS the Duality in motion.

## Assessment

### What's tight:
- Step 1: Definition. No hidden assumptions.
- Step 2: Stream persistence ≈ coherence. Follows from Axiom 3.
- Step 3: Coherent navigators → coherent navigation. Follows from Axiom 5 + Step 2.
- Step 4: Multi-scale self-reinforcement. Follows from Duality + Step 3.
- Step 5: Oscillation as mechanism. Follows from Duality (both autonomy and participation operate simultaneously).

### What's suggestive but not fully proven:
- Step 3 relies on the claim that "paths of least resistance FOR a coherent navigator lead TOWARD coherent configurations." This is plausible — compatible configurations have lower navigational resistance — but the formal content of "navigational resistance" isn't specified by the axioms. Axiom 5 says attention/intention/belief shape the paths, but doesn't specify the metric on configuration space that determines "resistance."
- Step 4's propagation assumes the participation channel transmits coherence, not just any constraint. If boundary conditions can transmit incoherence as easily as coherence, the feedback loop could go either direction.

### What would make it rigorous:
A formal specification of "navigational resistance" in terms of the stream's internal state and the target configuration's compatibility. If resistance = f(incompatibility between σ_i and the target configuration), then Step 3 is proven: coherent navigators face lower resistance toward coherent targets by definition. This would be a definition, not an assumption — but it would need to be added to the axiom system or derived from existing axioms.

### Verdict

**The derivation is 80% tight.** Steps 1-2 and 4-5 follow from axioms. Step 3 is the remaining soft spot — it requires a relationship between the navigator's coherence and the accessibility of coherent configurations that the axioms imply but don't formally specify. The candidate resolution: this relationship IS what "paths of least resistance" means, once you recognize that the navigator is a structured entity (not a point particle) navigating a structured space (not a flat landscape).

**Confidence that the Coherence Principle is derivable from the Doctrine: MEDIUM-HIGH.** The 20% gap is narrow and may be closable by a careful reading of Axiom 5's formal content. But it IS a gap, and honesty requires naming it.
