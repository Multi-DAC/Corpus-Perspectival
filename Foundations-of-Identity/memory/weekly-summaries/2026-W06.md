## Major: Infinite Gate Training Architecture

**Clayton's breakthrough idea:** Instead of training on pre-defined tracks, generate gates procedurally during the episode. Each gate transition tests a specific maneuver. The agent trains on an infinite stream of randomized maneuvers, making memorization impossible.

This is genuinely novel — no drone racing RL paper does this. Everyone trains on fixed tracks.

### The Journey Today
1. Started with curriculum training on 8 hand-designed tracks (3e-5 LR)
2. 0% on non-Gauntlet tracks after 1.4M steps → too conservative
3. Bumped LR to 1e-4 → still 0% after 967K steps
4. Diagnosed the structural problem: tracks have wildly different gate spacing (2.9m to 20m avg). Specialist learned one speed/turning profile.
5. Pivoted to fresh-from-scratch on all 8 tracks → 0% still at 300 episodes
6. Built procedural track generator → 500 tracks across 6 styles
7. Launched 508-track training with ALL upgrades: 500Hz, drag, 2x512 network, gate orientation
8. **Clayton's key insight:** Why simulate tracks at all? Just simulate individual gate transitions. Train on the GRAMMAR of flight, not specific sentences.
9. Built InfiniteGateEnv with 11 maneuver formulas + adaptive curriculum
10. First results: sprint, spiral, threading cracking above 0% success

### Upgrades Implemented
- **Network:** 2x256 (146K) → 2x512 (559K params)
- **Control frequency:** 50Hz → 500Hz
- **Drag:** cd=0 → cd=0.3

- **Reward scaling:** time_penalty now dt-scaled for frequency invariance
