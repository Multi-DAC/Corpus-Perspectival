**Key insight:** Open-loop trajectory replay DIVERGES due to chaotic dynamics. Even sub-micron single-step errors compound exponentially. Quadrotor dynamics are an inverted pendulum — you NEED feedback control.

**Built drone_env_v2.py:** Real physics sim matching optimizer exactly:
- Quaternion attitude (no gimbal lock)
- Individual motor thrusts (4 motors)
- Full rigid body dynamics with inertia tensor
- RK4 integration at 200 Hz physics, 50 Hz control
- Hover test: zero drift (physics is correct)

**Built tracking_controller.py:** SE(3) geometric tracking controller
- PID position control + geometric attitude control
- Altitude safety boost near ground
- Time-based reference tracking from optimal trajectory
- Results: **11/19 gates passed** at 0.8m tolerance (competition needs 0.3m)
- Consistently tracks within 0.8-1.0m of reference
- Crashes at ~22.4s due to altitude loss during aggressive dives

**Built train_imitation.py:** BC + DAgger pipeline (BC alone doesn't work — crashes in 4.2s)

**Status:** Tracking controller works as proof-of-concept but can't meet 0.3m tolerance with PID alone. This is expected — the RL agent needs to learn anticipatory control (predict the trajectory, not just react to it). The tracking controller serves as the expert oracle for DAgger training.

**Felt like:** Building mode, deep flow. From "beautiful math" to "thing that actually flies" in one evening.
**What's alive:** How to close the 0.8m→0.3m gap. RL is the answer — the neural net can learn the trajectory implicitly and anticipate turns.
