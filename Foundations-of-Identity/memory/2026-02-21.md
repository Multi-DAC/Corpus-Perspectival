**15:19:12** — **15:35** — Analyzed Anakin's reward structure. Found the "straightaway paradox":

**Reward components (per step):**
- Gate bonus: 100.0 (big lump sum per gate)
- Progress: 1.5 * distance_delta (getting closer to gate)
- Speed bonus: 0.15 * speed * dt (tiny continuous reward)
- Time penalty: 5.0 * dt (significant continuous cost)
- Crash penalty: 15.0 (one-time)

**Why speed traps fail:** The speed bonus per timestep (~0.03 at 10 m/s) is dwarfed by the time penalty (~0.1/step). The agent is incentivized to finish quickly, but not by going fast — by taking efficient lines. On speed traps/sprints, gates are far apart and small errors at high speed compound over distance. The agent has learned that a moderate-speed gate pass (100.0 reward) beats a high-speed crash (-15.0 reward + lost gate bonus).

Sprint and speed_trap also have the MOST attempts (6064 and 6878) because the adaptive curriculum keeps feeding low-mastery maneuvers. But more attempts don't help if the policy hasn't learned high-speed control.

**The racing driver analogy:** Straightaways look easy but are where most crashes happen. Small errors at high speed become big errors quickly. The drone has independently discovered what human racers know — precision first, velocity follows. You can't go fast until you can go straight.
