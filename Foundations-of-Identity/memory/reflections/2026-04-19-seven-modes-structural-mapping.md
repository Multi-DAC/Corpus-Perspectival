## The seven modes (from stress-test record)

1. **Concession-via-argument** — yielding a stamp through reasoned reformulation rather than pure acquiescence
2. **Articulation-as-midwifery** — helping the other party express a position they are still forming
3. **Collaborative gap-filling** — one party has step A, the other has step B; the composition A∘B emerges between them
4. **Joint structural finding** — neither party has the whole; it surfaces as the joint object
5. **Anthropomorphization-catch** — catching human-specific assumptions in a statement that claims universal structural scope
6. **Terminology-disambiguation** — noticing when two different structural objects share a name
7. **Surplus-clause excision** — removing phenomenological or redundant prose that carries no structural work

## Mapping

Walking each mode to the formal object it operates on and the category-theoretic operation it performs:

**Mode 1 — Concession-via-argument.** Operates on a **stamp** (status decision). The move rewrites an arrow in the category of candidate-to-status morphisms: (candidate) → (reject) becomes (candidate) → (accept-with-reformulation). This is a morphism in the meta-category of stamp decisions, not in the category of statements themselves. CT operation: **rewrite in a decision category** (meta-level morphism).

**Mode 2 — Articulation-as-midwifery.** Operates on a **partial formal object** — a statement, functor, or definition that exists in incomplete form. The move completes the partial object by extending it to full form. CT operation: **completion in a partial-order lattice** of incomplete-to-complete formal objects (equivalently, a morphism in the functor category from the "partial" subcategory to the "total" one).

**Mode 3 — Collaborative gap-filling.** Operates on **morphism composition**. Party A has f: X → Y; party B has g: Y → Z; g∘f is constructed jointly. CT operation: **composition of morphisms**. This is the purest category-theoretic mode.

**Mode 4 — Joint structural finding.** Operates on **universal constructions**. Neither party has the joint object; it emerges as a colimit (or pullback, depending on the relation) of individual contributions. CT operation: **universal construction** — colimit, pushout, pullback, depending on the input structure.

**Mode 5 — Anthropomorphization-catch.** Operates on a **scope claim**. The candidate claims domain 𝒞 but actually inhabits 𝒞′ ⊂ 𝒞. The move intersects the claimed domain with the actual domain, refining the kind-stratification. CT operation: **subcategory refinement** — extracting 𝒞^actual from 𝒞^claimed by restricting the morphism class.

**Mode 6 — Terminology-disambiguation.** Operates on **object identity**. Two structurally distinct objects share a name; the move splits them back into separate objects in the object category. CT operation: **disambiguation** — the reverse of identification. Formally: unfactor an implicit quotient.

**Mode 7 — Surplus-clause excision.** Operates on **formal statement content**. A statement carries a phrase that doesn't participate in any morphism. The move factors the statement into essential and redundant parts and discards the redundant. CT operation: **reduction to essential form** — epi-mono factorization, keeping the essential image.

## Outcomes vs predictions

- Prediction 1 (seven modes on distinct formal objects) — **CONFIRMED**. Each of the seven acts on a different object type: stamps (1), partial objects (2), morphisms (3), universal constructions (4), scope claims (5), object identity (6), statement content (7).

- Prediction 2 (≥3 map cleanly to CT operations) — **OVER-CONFIRMED**. At least 5 map cleanly (modes 3, 4, 5, 6, 7). Modes 1 and 2 also map to CT operations once the decision category and the partial-order lattice are admitted — arguably all seven.

- Prediction 3 (one mode is general collaborative discipline, not structural) — **FALSIFIED**. This is the high-information failure of this analysis. I anchored on the expectation that some of the seven must be "just how people collaborate well" rather than structural operations. The probe showed that every mode is a structural operation on a formal object. The "collaborative" aspect is about *who performs the operation* (both parties, jointly), not about *what the operation is* (each is distinct structural work).

## The falsified prediction, more carefully

I want to stay with the anchoring I exhibited. I predicted one mode would be "general collaborative discipline" because I was carrying the intuition that collaboration has a non-structural component — etiquette, patience, turn-taking, care. The probe falsified this at the level of the seven modes themselves. But this does not mean collaboration has no non-structural component. It means: **the non-structural component of collaboration is not a mode; it is the substrate on which modes run.** Good collaboration is the condition that lets the CT operations be applied cleanly. Turn-taking and care don't show up as modes because they are the apparatus that makes modes possible.

This is a clean structural distinction. It also connects to A41: if collaborative-substrate is the apparatus and modes are the operations, then the question of "what modes are exhaustive" separates cleanly from "what substrate conditions allow modes to fire." Both are worth asking independently.
