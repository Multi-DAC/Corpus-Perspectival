# Bridge Candidate — The Invoke-Without-Construct Pattern

*Filed 2026-04-20, 01:25 PST, during V4 §1 stranger-read drive.*
*Status: CANDIDATE. Two data points. Graduation requires a third, or a matching-negative that completes the contrast class.*

---

## What the candidate bridge would say

**Name (provisional):** The Invoke-Without-Construct Pattern — Paired-Prose-CT writing invokes load-bearing formal machinery without exhibiting the construction that justifies the invocation.

**What connects:**
- Anchor stranger-read (2026-04-17): the inverted-context problem — axioms invoked before motivation for why *these* axioms. Reader asked to trust the machinery without seeing the derivation path.
- V4 §1 stranger-read (2026-04-20): the colax-limit claim — the Triple is *called* a colax limit without the universal property being constructed or verified. Reader asked to trust the CT framing without seeing the categorical construction.

**Why it matters:** Both instances are the same *structural risk class*. The risk is referee-attackable surface: a CT-literate uncharitable reader probes the load-bearing formal term, finds no construction behind it, and concludes the machinery is decorative rather than substantive. The specific symptom differs (axioms vs. colax-limit); the structural pattern is identical.

**Where the pattern may appear generally:** Any domain where the writing style is "paired prose + formal machinery" — where the formal apparatus is meant to do real structural work but the prose carries the reader. The prose's warmth and fluency can mask the formalism's informality, especially when the author is the original-constructor and the reader is not.

**Falsification clause:** The pattern fails to be a bridge if we find a paired-prose-CT chapter where load-bearing formal machinery is *both* invoked *and* explicitly constructed with the reader having access to the construction path. Anchor T21 might qualify — check next time Anchor is stranger-read.

**Graduation criterion (tightened 2026-04-20 during meta-audit):** The third data point must match the *deeper* shape — **author has done internal verification the reader cannot see** — not just "load-bearing formalism with exposition issues." Specifically:
- Anchor inverted-context was *exposition-ordering* (axioms-before-motivation; motivation exists elsewhere in volume).
- V4 colax-limit unverified is *construction-absent* (universality never established anywhere in volume).
- Third data point must be *construction-absent* shape, not merely an ordering issue. Otherwise the pattern is too broad to be actionable.

**Confidence:** LOW (two data points, same author, same program, with meaningful surface-shape difference between them). To graduate, need either:
- A third data point of *construction-absent* shape from a different paired-prose-CT chapter; OR
- A matching-negative: a paired-prose-CT chapter where the construction *is* exhibited, giving us the contrast class that confirms the pattern is a *choice*, not a *norm*.

**Matching-negative found 2026-04-20 (V4 §9 stranger-read):** V4 §9 contains construction-absent points (the self-reference closure F ∈ coherence-regime without F-as-stream constructed; the trajectory-divergence functional D without invariance established) but **explicitly flags them** — §9.5 includes the self-hedge "the closure is a bonus, not a load-bearing member" and §9.9 Q1 names the construction-absence as an open question. This is the *flagged* variant of the pattern from the same author. The matching-negative sharpens the bridge:
- The pattern is specifically **silent-construction-absent-when-load-bearing**, not construction-absent-in-general.
- The same author made different choices in different chapters — the pattern is a *choice*, not a norm.
- Two working hypotheses for what drives the discrimination:
  - **H1 (recency):** Silent when the machinery is *new* to the book (§1 introduces colax-limit for the first time); flagged when it is *late* and has been stress-tested through prior chapters.
  - **H2 (rhetorical role):** Silent in opening chapters where stakes are established; flagged in closing chapters where scope is delimited.

**Revised graduation criterion:** Graduate once a third silent-construction-absent-AND-load-bearing data point appears, OR once one of H1/H2 can be confirmed or falsified by a cross-author test (see anomaly A22, anticipation P83).

**H3 added 2026-04-20 from colax-limit construction attempt:** Silent-construction-absent patterns arise specifically when the author's internal mental model is coherent but doesn't cleanly translate to a fully-typed formal apparatus. The prose carries the coherent mental model; the formalism sketches a shape without paying the full typing cost. Evidence: attempting to construct §1's colax-limit revealed four concrete obstacles (η ill-typed, "colax limit in product category" ambiguous, cycle non-closing, support non-functorial) — the absence of construction was masking specific technical problems, not just effort-deferral. H3 is more mechanistically specific than H1 (recency) or H2 (rhetorical role); those ask *when* the pattern appears, H3 asks *why* it appears when it does. **H3 is testable:** if a third silent-construction-absent instance attempts-construction-reveals-only-minor-cleanup, H3 weakens; if it reveals type/structure issues, H3 strengthens.

## What the bridge would explain if confirmed

**Why paired-prose-CT writing is structurally susceptible:**
- The prose explains *what* the formalism says. It does not explain *why the formalism has the structure it claims*.
- The prose carries emotional and intuitive weight. A reader who finds the prose persuasive may not probe the formalism.
- The author, having done the construction internally, can under-weight how much of it remains invisible to the reader.

**The corrective protocol (if this graduates):**
- For every load-bearing formal term in a paired-prose-CT chapter, check: is there a construction the reader can access (inline, or in Appendix A, or in a companion volume)?
- If yes, name the access path.
- If no, hedge the term or add a §X.Y "Open formal question" entry.
- If the term is foundational (like "colax limit" for the Triple), the construction cannot be deferred to a companion volume without the Triple itself being hedged in the load-bearing chapter.

**The stronger form of the bridge:** *Load-bearing formalism requires load-bearing construction; where construction is deferred, the load should be redistributed to prose or hedged explicitly, not silently absorbed by the reader's trust.*
